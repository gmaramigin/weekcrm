## How we picked

Outlook integration is a spectrum, not a checkbox. The CRMs below all ship a native Outlook add-in (not just an IMAP connection), two-way email sync, two-way calendar sync, and contact/deal context in the Outlook sidebar. We also weighed reliability — many CRMs advertise Outlook support but break on Outlook Web vs. Outlook Desktop vs. Outlook Mobile. The picks below work across all three.

## The picks

**1. HubSpot — best free Outlook CRM.** The Outlook add-in is polished, fast, and works in desktop, web, and mobile. Email tracking, templates, sequences, and meeting booking are all accessible from the inbox. The free tier covers unlimited users and 1M contacts — for most small teams, this alone is the answer.

**2. Microsoft Dynamics 365 — deepest native integration.** Built by Microsoft. Teams, SharePoint, Power Platform, and Outlook share the same identity and data fabric. For 100+ employee organizations already standardized on Microsoft, the integration depth is unmatched. The catch: Dynamics requires a partner-led implementation and per-app pricing that adds up fast.

**3. Nutshell — best value with all-inclusive plans.** Outlook sync is included on every tier including the $13/user/mo Foundation plan. Unlimited contacts, unlimited storage, and a native Outlook add-in put Nutshell ahead of competitors that gate Outlook sync to higher tiers.

**4. Salesflare — best automated in-inbox CRM.** Salesflare's Outlook add-in turns the inbox itself into the CRM — contact records, deal stages, and pipeline activity all available from Outlook's reading pane. Auto-capture of contacts, emails, and meeting attendance is the standout feature. Best for small B2B sales teams (1–10 people).

**5. Pipedrive — best simple pipeline CRM with Outlook sync.** Pipedrive's Smart Email BCC and Outlook add-in cover the basics cleanly. Pipedrive is best when you want a visual pipeline CRM rather than a full marketing+sales suite, and you don't need Outlook-native automation rules.

## What to verify before you commit

- **Outlook Web vs. Desktop vs. Mobile parity.** Some CRMs ship a polished Outlook Desktop add-in and a broken Outlook Web experience (or vice versa). Test all three before you buy.
- **Calendar two-way sync.** A CRM that reads your calendar but doesn't write back is half a tool. All five above support proper two-way sync.
- **Microsoft 365 enterprise SSO.** If you're on Microsoft 365 Business or Enterprise, the CRM should support Azure AD SSO. HubSpot, Dynamics, and Nutshell all do.
- **Tracking pixel reliability.** Outlook's enhanced privacy features in 2024–2026 broke many CRM open-tracking pixels. Verify the CRM still reports open rates in Outlook with current versions.

## What to skip

- **Gmail-first CRMs that "also support" Outlook.** Streak, NetHunt, and Copper are excellent Gmail CRMs but their Outlook stories are weaker. If you're 80%+ Outlook, pick from this list instead.
- **Generic IMAP-based "Outlook integration."** A real Outlook integration is a native add-in. IMAP-only connections lose calendar sync, contact context, and reliable tracking.

## Bottom line

For most Outlook teams, **HubSpot Free** is the right starting point — the Outlook add-in is the best in the free tier and the upgrade path is clean. **Dynamics 365** wins above 100 seats with deep Microsoft ecosystem use. **Nutshell** is the best paid SMB pick when you also want email marketing and forms in the same tool. **Salesflare** is the right answer for small teams that want the inbox to *be* the CRM.
