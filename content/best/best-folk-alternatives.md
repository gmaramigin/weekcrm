## Why look for a folk alternative

folk is a genuinely good relationship CRM — it pulls contacts from LinkedIn and Gmail in one click and brings structure to network-heavy work like fundraising, recruiting, and partnerships. But it's intentionally narrow. It's not built for complex sales pipelines or heavy reporting, it's better for people than for opportunity tracking, and several useful features only unlock on paid tiers. Teams that start doing real deal management usually hit those edges fast.

## How we picked

We looked for tools that keep folk's contact-first simplicity but add what folk leaves out: pipeline depth, reporting, automation, or stronger automatic enrichment. We also weighted ease of adoption and price, since folk users tend to value lightweight tools over feature maximalism.

## What to consider

- **You want folk's feel but real CRM power** → **Attio**. The cleanest upgrade path. It's modern and contact-first like folk, but adds pipelines, automations, and AI-native enrichment, with a free plan and paid tiers from $29/user/month.
- **You sell through your network** → **Nimble**. A social CRM that auto-aggregates contact data from email, calendar, and social profiles, with a one-click Prospector extension. Flat pricing at $24.90/user/month annually — no tier confusion.
- **You want the cheapest simple contact CRM** → **Capsule**. Clean, lightweight, with a free plan and paid tiers from $18/month. It does contacts, tasks, and a basic pipeline well without the clutter.
- **You want AI to maintain relationships for you** → **Cloze**. It auto-logs emails, calls, and meetings into a self-updating timeline and surfaces a daily follow-up agenda. From $17/user/month — but note its heavy pivot toward real estate, and test its contact-merge behavior before importing.
- **Your team lives in Gmail** → **Copper**. The only CRM officially recommended by Google, built natively inside Gmail, Calendar, and Drive. Most teams need the $59/user Professional plan for full sales features.

## Bottom line

If folk feels too thin, **Attio** is the safest upgrade — same modern DNA, far more depth. Choose **Nimble** or **Cloze** for relationship- and network-led selling, **Capsule** for the cheapest no-fuss contact CRM, and **Copper** if your whole team works inside Google Workspace. Trial two against your actual workflow before committing.
