## How we picked

Junk removal is a speed-and-repeat business. Most jobs are booked over the phone or online within minutes of the inquiry, the work happens at the customer's location, pricing is quick (by the truckload or item), and the real margin lives in two places most single-truck operators neglect: recurring commercial accounts (property managers, realtors, contractors, storage facilities) and online reviews that feed the next wave of residential leads. We judged these CRMs on the things that matter for a hauling business: (1) **fast lead capture and booking** so no inquiry goes unanswered, (2) **automated follow-up** on quotes and no-shows, (3) **recurring/commercial account tracking** to build a predictable route, (4) **review generation** triggered on job completion, and (5) **QuickBooks-friendly billing**. Pure inbound-SaaS CRMs are excellent tools but the wrong shape for on-demand field work, so they're not here.

## What to consider

- **You want everything in one tool** → **Thryv**. All-in-one for residential haulers — CRM, online booking, reminders, invoicing, and automated review requests at a flat monthly price rather than per-seat.
- **You're a one- or two-truck operation** → **vcita**. Online booking, quotes, reminders, and light CRM in one simple tool an owner-operator can run from a phone without office staff.
- **You run on QuickBooks** → **Method CRM**. Native, real-time QuickBooks sync so jobs flow straight to invoices — the best fit for an established hauler already invoicing in QuickBooks.
- **You're building a commercial route** → **HubSpot**. A free CRM core plus real account tracking and marketing tools for landing and keeping recurring property-management and contractor accounts.
- **You quote at high volume** → **Salesmate**. A sales-first pipeline with built-in calling, texting, and automation — strong when you're fielding and following up on dozens of inquiries a day.

## Pricing snapshot

Junk removal CRM pricing sits at the affordable end. **Free / entry**: HubSpot Free, vcita from ~$29/mo, Method CRM from ~$28/user/mo, Thryv at a flat monthly rate. **Mid**: HubSpot Sales tiers and Salesmate from ~$29/user/mo when you want heavier automation and commercial account management. For most haulers running one to five trucks, the sweet spot is $30–$80/month per user, plus a QuickBooks connection if you invoice there. Enterprise field-service dispatch platforms are overkill unless you're running a large multi-truck franchise with dedicated dispatchers.

## The two margins single-truck operators miss

Most junk removal owners run their whole business on speed of the first call — and they're right that fast quoting wins jobs. But the operators who scale past one truck do it by capturing two margins the notebook can't. The first is **recurring commercial work**: a property manager who needs monthly cleanouts, a realtor who preps listings, a contractor who fills a dumpster every job. A CRM like **HubSpot** or **Salesmate** turns those into tracked accounts with automated check-ins, so instead of hoping they call again you're booking them on a route. The second is **reviews**: junk removal is chosen on a phone, and the top-rated hauler in the area gets the click. **Thryv** and **vcita** fire off a review request the instant a job is closed, while the customer is happiest, turning satisfied residential jobs into the lead source that fills tomorrow's schedule. Capture both and a single-truck operation becomes a repeatable business — without ever buying enterprise dispatch software.

## What's missing from this list

This list covers CRMs in the WeekCRM directory. Purpose-built junk-removal and field-service platforms — like Jobber or industry-specific hauling apps — bundle routing, dispatch, and payment in ways a general CRM doesn't, and if truck routing is your main bottleneck they're worth a separate look alongside Method or HubSpot.
