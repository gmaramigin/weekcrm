## How we picked

Data enrichment isn't a single feature — it's a spectrum from basic auto-completion to AI-driven inference. The CRMs below were chosen for **breadth of enrichment** (firmographic + technographic + contact + intent), **depth of automation** (fields filled automatically, not just on demand), and **modernness of approach** (AI-inferred vs database-lookup). For each, we note the strongest enrichment use case.

## Use-case fit guide

- **B2B SaaS startup running ICP-targeted inbound** → Attio. Built-in AI enrichment plus a clean data model means new leads get auto-classified into ICP segments without setup work.
- **Marketing-led growth motion** → HubSpot with Breeze Intelligence. Form-fill enrichment, account-based marketing data, and intent signals all live in one platform.
- **Enterprise sales org with strict data governance** → Salesforce Data Cloud + AppExchange. Most third-party data providers (Bombora, 6sense, ZoomInfo, Cognism) plug in natively.
- **Sales team that wants the CRM to autonomously update itself** → Day.ai. Enrichment is inferred from email/calendar activity, not just static data lookups.
- **Budget-conscious team wanting decent enrichment** → Zoho CRM with Zia. Built-in enrichment is competent and included.

## Types of enrichment that matter

CRM enrichment isn't a monolithic capability. The fields below tend to be the highest-leverage:

- **Firmographic**: company name, size, revenue, industry, headquarters, founding year. Standard table-stakes data.
- **Technographic**: tech stack the company uses (Salesforce, HubSpot, AWS, Shopify, etc.). High value for ICP-driven outbound.
- **Hierarchical**: parent company, subsidiaries, M&A activity. Critical for enterprise sales.
- **Contact-level**: verified email, direct dial, LinkedIn URL, job seniority. Outbound-sales necessity.
- **Intent data**: third-party signals indicating active research on a problem you solve. Bombora, 6sense, G2 buyer intent.
- **AI-inferred**: company positioning summary, ICP score, buying-stage prediction. Newer, probabilistic but powerful.

The best CRMs cover the first 3–4 categories natively; intent data and AI inference are increasingly built in but still often paired with specialist tools.

## Attio — modern AI-native enrichment

Attio's enrichment is the most modern approach in 2026:

- **Auto-enrichment on record creation**: new company records get firmographic data, AI summaries, and inferred ICP fit within seconds.
- **AI-generated fields**: any field can be defined as "AI-extract from website" or "AI-summarize from public data" — fields fill automatically.
- **Agentic enrichment**: Attio's CRM agents can run scheduled enrichment passes ("for every account with no tech stack, attempt to enrich") without manual triggers.

Strongest fit: modern SaaS startups, AI-native teams, anyone who wants enrichment that updates itself rather than requiring point-in-time refreshes.

## HubSpot Breeze Intelligence

HubSpot's enrichment in 2026 runs through Breeze Intelligence (the successor to Clearbit after HubSpot's acquisition):

- **Form enrichment**: reduce form fields by 80% — Breeze fills the rest from the email.
- **Account-based marketing data**: enriched accounts get TAM/SAM/ICP analysis.
- **Intent data**: buying signals from Bombora and HubSpot's own behavioral data.
- **Reveal**: identifies anonymous website visitors as companies (B2B website de-anonymization).

Strongest fit: marketing-led B2B growth motions where enrichment fuels segmentation, lead scoring, and ABM.

## Salesforce Data Cloud + AppExchange

Salesforce's enrichment story is the broadest, anchored on AppExchange:

- **Data.com successor** (rolled into Salesforce Data Cloud): firmographic and contact enrichment.
- **AppExchange integrations**: ZoomInfo, Cognism, LeadIQ, Apollo, Lusha, Clearbit, Bombora, 6sense, G2 — almost every enrichment vendor plugs in natively.
- **Data Cloud + AI Cloud**: unified customer data with AI-driven segmentation and inference.

Strongest fit: enterprise sales orgs with strict data-governance requirements and a Salesforce-anchored stack.

## Day.ai — autonomous enrichment

Day.ai's approach is unique: enrichment isn't a separate process, it's a continuous side-effect of ingesting your email and calendar:

- **Contact records auto-build** from email signatures and meeting attendees.
- **Companies auto-enrich** with website data, public news, and LinkedIn activity.
- **Relationship graph** auto-builds from communication patterns.

Strongest fit: sales teams whose biggest pain is stale CRM data — Day.ai's autopilot specifically targets that problem.

## Zoho CRM with Zia AI

Zoho's Zia AI provides reasonable built-in enrichment at a budget-friendly price:

- **Contact enrichment**: pulls public data (LinkedIn, company website, social profiles) into records.
- **Lead scoring**: AI-driven probability of conversion based on profile + behavior.
- **Anomaly detection**: flags unusual deal patterns for review.

Strongest fit: cost-conscious teams that want decent enrichment included rather than paying for a separate tool.

## When built-in enrichment isn't enough

Built-in CRM enrichment has improved dramatically but still has gaps:

- **Verified direct dials and personal emails**: dedicated tools (Apollo, ZoomInfo, RocketReach) remain stronger.
- **Hyper-fresh data**: tools that crawl LinkedIn or scrape job boards in real time outperform CRM-bundled enrichment.
- **Niche datasets**: industry-specific data (healthcare provider directories, real estate property records, automotive VINs) needs specialty providers.
- **Privacy-compliant outbound**: GDPR/CCPA-compliant European contact data (Cognism, Kaspr) is a specialist's game.

Most growth-stage sales teams run built-in enrichment + 1 specialist tool. Larger enterprises run 3–5 specialist tools through Salesforce.

## Pricing snapshot

Built-in CRM enrichment is typically bundled or sold as credit-based add-ons:

- **Attio Pro**: $69/seat/mo with enrichment credits included.
- **HubSpot Breeze Intelligence**: $30/month for 100 credits; enterprise tiers in the $1,000s.
- **Zoho CRM Enterprise with Zia**: $50/seat/mo, enrichment included.
- **Salesforce Data Cloud**: enterprise pricing, often $100k+/year.

Standalone tools:

- **Apollo**: $79/seat/mo for the full dataset.
- **ZoomInfo**: $14,000+/year for the lowest tier; serious investment.
- **Clay**: $349/month for 5,000 credits; popular with growth teams blending enrichment from many sources.

## Trial advice

Pick a CRM with enrichment + 1 dedicated tool. Run them against your real outbound list for 30 days. Measure: enrichment accuracy (verify a sample of 100 records manually), data freshness, and the cost per validated contact. The combination that gives you the best cost-per-meeting-booked is the right architecture — not the tool with the most marketing about AI.
