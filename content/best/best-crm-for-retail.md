## How we picked

Retail and DTC CRM requirements diverge from B2B SaaS in three concrete ways: customer volume is orders of magnitude higher, every interaction is downstream of an order or product question, and customer lifetime value depends on fast resolution and personalized outreach more than deep account management. The vendors below either ship native Shopify/BigCommerce/WooCommerce integrations that surface orders, refunds, and subscriptions inside every CRM record, or they pair cleanly with the data layer a retail team actually needs.

## What to consider

- **DTC brand running inbound marketing and lifecycle email alongside a support team** → HubSpot. The marketing automation, email flows, and contact-level purchase history integrations make it the strongest unified platform for DTC growth.
- **Omnichannel retail or enterprise consumer brand** → Salesforce. Salesforce Commerce Cloud, Marketing Cloud, and Service Cloud are the most complete enterprise stack for large retail operations with complex omnichannel requirements.
- **Mid-market DTC with a sales team focused on wholesale or B2B retail accounts** → Freshsales. Clean pipeline management, AI lead scoring, and affordable pricing for teams that need CRM for the B2B side of a DTC brand.
- **Multi-channel retail with a broad toolset** → Zoho CRM. Strong Shopify and WooCommerce connectors, multi-currency support for international retail, and the broader Zoho ecosystem for inventory and fulfillment.
- **Wholesale-focused retail brand with field sales reps calling on buyers** → Pipedrive. Clean pipeline management for B2B wholesale accounts; integrates with common retail ERPs.

## Shopify integration depth

For DTC brands, the Shopify integration is often the deciding factor. Key questions to evaluate:

- Does the CRM pull in orders, refund status, and subscription status at the contact level (not just as a data feed)?
- Can support reps see recent orders without switching tabs?
- Does the integration sync abandoned cart data for marketing automation triggers?
- Is the sync real-time or batch?

HubSpot's Shopify integration is the most robust for marketing use cases. For support-focused retail, a dedicated helpdesk (Gorgias, Reamaze) often supplements the CRM for order management — the two can run in parallel with different data layers.

## Loyalty and LTV considerations

Retail CRMs should surface customer lifetime value, purchase frequency, and loyalty status alongside the contact record. This data typically lives in a loyalty platform (Yotpo, LoyaltyLion, Smile) or the ecommerce platform itself. Evaluate whether your chosen CRM can pull this data in via native integration or API, and whether it can trigger retention campaigns based on LTV thresholds.

## Trial advice

For DTC brands: run a pilot with your top 10 customer service scenarios. The CRM should let a rep resolve an order inquiry, process a return request, and flag a loyalty customer for a proactive retention offer — all from the same interface. If they're switching between the CRM, Shopify, and a spreadsheet to handle those three tasks, the integration isn't deep enough.
