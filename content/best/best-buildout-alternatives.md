## Who should leave Buildout

Buildout earns its price for commercial brokers who ship offering memorandums and brochures, track occupancy with stacking plans, and think in CRE deal stages (LOI, under contract, due diligence, close). If your team genuinely uses the marketing-document engine and the CRE-native data model — properties, spaces, tenants, leases as real objects — the consolidated stack is worth more than the sum of the three products it replaced. Don't move for the sake of moving.

Leave if any of three things is true. First, the cost math stopped working: the quote-only Rethink/Apto tiers plus the setup fee add up, and a transparent competitor may do the same job for less. Second, you're feeling the post-acquisition seams — features you relied on in legacy Apto or Rethink that haven't fully carried over. Third, your business isn't really CRE-shaped anymore, and you're bending a specialist tool to fit general sales or residential work it was never built for. Each of those points to a different alternative below.

## What to consider

- **Best CRE-native swap (often cheaper)** → AscendixRE. Same commercial-real-estate DNA — commission splits, stacking plans, Ascendix Search geomapping, Composer OM templates — but built on Salesforce with the license bundled at $79-99/user/mo. Transparent per-seat pricing versus Buildout's quote wall.
- **Best for enterprise real estate** → Propertybase. Salesforce-based, franchise-grade reporting across offices, deep customization. From $69/user/mo before Salesforce and implementation costs; a fit for multi-office brokerages, overkill for a small team.
- **Best build-it-yourself platform** → Salesforce Sales Cloud. Maximum flexibility to model your own CRE processes, $25-350/user/mo, huge AppExchange. Choose it only if you have (or will hire) admin capacity — TCO runs well above list.
- **Best for marketing-led brokerages** → HubSpot CRM. Free tier plus paid from $20/seat/mo, with email campaigns, landing pages, and pipeline in one place. You'll model deals with custom objects since it isn't CRE-shaped.
- **Best lightweight pipeline** → Pipedrive. Visual, activity-based deal tracking at $14-99/user/mo with almost no learning curve. Right when you've decided you don't need stacking plans or lease comps at all.
- **Best if you've pivoted to residential** → BoldTrail. All-in-one residential real estate CRM (CRM + IDX website + 20+ lead-gen tools) powering RE/MAX and eXp. From ~$499/mo — a team-scale tool, not a solo product, and a completely different model from CRE deal management.

## Picking by why you're unhappy

The trap when leaving Buildout is treating "another CRE tool" and "a cheaper tool" as the same search — they aren't. If you still need CRE workflows and are leaving on price or product-consolidation friction, AscendixRE is the obvious move: same category, cleaner pricing, and no separate Salesforce contract. If you're leaving because your deals have gotten simpler, don't pay for any CRE platform — Pipedrive or HubSpot's free tier will hold a pipeline for a fraction of the cost. And if your business has shifted from commercial to residential real estate, note that BoldTrail solves a genuinely different problem (buyer/seller leads, IDX websites) and none of the CRE tools do. Confirm data export from Buildout before you commit — migrating property, tenant, and lease records out of a specialist schema is the part teams underestimate.
