## How we picked

A dance studio fills classes through **trial offers** and keeps them full through **retention**. Like other lesson businesses, the student dances but a **parent pays**, so the CRM has to link both and bill the household cleanly. The biggest, quietest leak is the trial class that converts to nothing and the mid-season dancer who drifts away. We weighted trial-to-enrollment automation, family billing, and retention triggers over enterprise reporting a studio owner will never open.

## What to consider

- **All-in-one for a single studio** → vcita. Booking, household billing, reminders, and a family CRM in one tool.
- **Trial conversion + retention** → Keap. Best automation for enrolling trials and re-engaging drifting dancers.
- **Reviews + parent messaging** → Thryv. Pulls in Google reviews and runs two-way SMS with parents.
- **Visual enrollment pipeline** → Pipedrive. Inquiry → trial booked → attended → enrolled, at a glance.
- **Start free** → HubSpot. Unlimited students and parents on the free tier.

## Pricing snapshot

Studio-appropriate plans run $0–$60/month for a single location. HubSpot starts free; vcita, Keap, and Thryv sit in the $30–$60 range with billing and messaging bundled, usually replacing a separate booking or email subscription.

## Trial advice

Test the trial-class follow-up and a retention flag together. Set one automation to nudge unconverted trials after 48 hours, and one to alert you when a current student misses two classes. A CRM that quietly converts a few more trials and saves a few wavering dancers each season earns back its cost long before recital.
