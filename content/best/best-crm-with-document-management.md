## How we picked

"Document management" in a CRM spans three jobs: **storing** files against contacts and deals (no more digging through email), **generating** quotes, proposals, and contracts from CRM data with templates, and **e-signature plus tracking** so you know when a prospect opens or signs. The best CRMs do all three natively or via tight integrations with tools like PandaDoc and DocuSign. We weighted native document features, template/merge quality, and how cleanly files stay attached to the right record.

## What to consider

- **Best all-round document + quoting CRM** → HubSpot. Document storage on records, a built-in quotes tool, document tracking (see when prospects view a proposal), and tight PandaDoc/DocuSign integrations — strong across all three jobs.
- **Best for clean deal-attached documents** → Pipedrive. Smart Docs lets you generate quotes and proposals from deal data, track opens, and collect e-signatures, all anchored to the deal.
- **Best value all-in-one** → Zoho CRM. Native quotes, sales orders, and invoices plus Zoho Sign and a document library — a lot of document tooling inside an affordable suite.
- **Best for projects plus documents** → Insightly. Stores documents against contacts, projects, and opportunities, so files follow the relationship from sale into delivery.
- **Best for configurable enterprise documents** → SugarCRM. Document repository with revision tracking and quote/proposal generation, customizable for teams with specific document workflows.
- **Best for deep CPQ and contracts at scale** → Salesforce. With CPQ and the AppExchange (DocuSign, Conga, PandaDoc), it offers the most powerful document generation and contract management for complex, high-volume sales.

## Native vs integrated

Decide how much you need built in. **Native** quote/proposal tools (HubSpot, Pipedrive Smart Docs, Zoho) keep everything in one place and one bill — best for most teams. **Integrated** setups (Salesforce + DocuSign/Conga, or any CRM + PandaDoc) unlock far more powerful document automation and CPQ but add cost and configuration. Heavier contract volume and approval workflows push you toward the integrated/enterprise end.

## Pricing snapshot

Zoho CRM, Insightly, and Pipedrive start affordable (~$14–$29/user/mo), with document features arriving on mid tiers. HubSpot and SugarCRM scale up as you add Sales Hub or document modules. Salesforce plus CPQ and a contract tool is the most expensive but the most capable. Note that e-signature volume and advanced document generation are often metered or gated to higher tiers.

## What to prioritize

Prioritize: documents that auto-attach to the right contact/deal (and stay searchable), template-driven generation that pulls live CRM fields (so quotes aren't retyped), e-signature and open-tracking, and version control if contracts get revised. If you send a lot of proposals, document tracking — knowing exactly when a buyer opened page three — is a quietly powerful sales signal.

## Trial advice

In a trial, run a real document end to end: generate a quote from a live deal, send it for signature, and watch it track back to the record. Confirm the merged fields are correct and the signed copy lands on the right contact. If a proposal goes out and comes back signed without you touching another app, the CRM's document management is doing its job.
