## Why look for a Veeva Vault CRM alternative

Let's be clear up front: Veeva is dominant in life-sciences CRM for good reasons. It was built specifically for pharma field reps, HCP and HCO engagement, closed-loop marketing, sample management, and the compliance reality of selling regulated products. For a global biopharma field force, that depth is hard to match, and most large pharma will stay.

So why are teams evaluating alternatives in 2026? A few real reasons:

- **Cost.** Veeva is a premium, enterprise-priced platform. For a 10- or 30-person commercial team at a biotech, medtech, or diagnostics company, the per-seat economics can be hard to justify against what the team actually uses.
- **Heaviness.** Veeva is engineered for large, pharma-specific field operations. Smaller commercial teams frequently find it's overkill — more process, configuration, and compliance machinery than their go-to-market actually needs.
- **The Salesforce-to-Vault migration.** Veeva moved its CRM off the Salesforce platform onto its own Vault platform. Every Veeva customer faces a re-platforming and re-validation project on that timeline whether they like it or not. When you're already absorbing that disruption, it's a natural moment to ask whether you're on the right platform at all.
- **A desire for something more general or affordable.** Companies whose commercial motion isn't purely field-rep-and-HCP — or who want one platform across sales, marketing, and support — often want a more general CRM they can shape to fit.

If any of those describe you, the picks below are worth a look. The honest framing: none of them is a pre-validated, drop-in Veeva replacement for a regulated field force. Choose by team size and how much pharma-specific compliance you genuinely need.

## How we picked

We weighted four things for a life-sciences buyer: how directly the platform maps to field, medical, and HCP-engagement use cases; how much compliance and regulated-data handling it can support (natively or via partners); cost relative to Veeva; and fit by team size, since a 500-rep biopharma and a 15-person diagnostics launch team need very different things. We were deliberately honest about where each option needs configuration or a partner to approach what Veeva ships out of the box.

## Salesforce — the most direct enterprise alternative

Salesforce is the obvious starting point, and not by accident: Veeva CRM was originally built *on* the Salesforce platform before Veeva moved to Vault. Salesforce's own Life Sciences Cloud targets the same territory — field engagement, medical, HCP/HCO relationship management, and connected commercial data. For a large pharma or biopharma that wants enterprise scale, a vast partner ecosystem, and AI/automation depth, it's the most credible like-for-like alternative. The trade-offs are familiar: enterprise pricing in Veeva's neighborhood, and you'll lean on a specialized life-sciences implementation partner to build and validate the pharma-specific workflows Veeva delivers as product. Best for enterprise teams that want to stay on a Salesforce-class platform without Veeva.

## Creatio — mid-market with no-code compliance workflows

Creatio's strength is a no-code platform that lets you model complex, regulated processes without a heavy development team. For a mid-market life-sciences organization — a growing biotech or a medtech with a real but not massive field force — that flexibility matters: you can build HCP engagement flows, approvals, and audit-friendly processes visually and adapt them as the business changes. It won't arrive pre-loaded with Veeva's validated pharma modules, but its workflow engine and BPM roots make it well-suited to encoding compliance logic yourself. Best for mid-market teams that want enterprise-grade process control without enterprise-grade cost or rigidity.

## SugarCRM — configurable mid-market platform

SugarCRM is a mature, highly configurable CRM that fits commercial life-sciences teams that need a solid sales and account platform but don't require Veeva's full pharma-validation stack. It offers strong customization, on-premise or cloud deployment options (useful when data-residency or control matters), and predictable pricing. Think of it as a capable general CRM you shape to your commercial process — territory and account management, pipeline, and customer engagement — rather than a purpose-built HCP-compliance system. Best for mid-market commercial teams that value configurability and deployment flexibility over industry-specific modules.

## Zoho CRM — best value for cost-sensitive small commercial teams

For small biotech, medtech, and diagnostics teams watching every dollar, Zoho CRM is the value leader. It's deeply customizable, integrates with a broad suite of business apps, and costs a fraction of an enterprise life-sciences platform. A small field or commercial team can model accounts, contacts, and territories, and automate the basics, without an enterprise budget. The caveats are honest ones: regulated HCP data handling, Part 11-style controls, and validation are on you and your implementation partner. For a lean team that needs a real CRM — not a validated pharma field system — that's an acceptable trade. Best for cost-sensitive small commercial and field teams.

## HubSpot — best for early biotech and diagnostics commercial launch

HubSpot shines when a company is standing up its first commercial motion. An early-stage biotech approaching launch, or a diagnostics company building out sales and marketing, gets a clean, fast-to-deploy platform that unifies CRM, marketing, and customer engagement — and it starts free. The appeal is speed and simplicity: a small team can be productive in days, not a quarter. As with the other general CRMs, it isn't a pharma-validated environment; you'll configure compliance and likely bring in a partner as you scale into regulated HCP territory. Best for early-stage life-sciences companies launching a commercial team for the first time.

## How to choose

Anchor the decision on two questions: how big is the team, and how much pharma-specific compliance do you truly need?

- **Enterprise field force, staying on a Salesforce-class platform** → **Salesforce** (Life Sciences Cloud) with a life-sciences partner.
- **Mid-market, process-heavy, wants to own its compliance workflows** → **Creatio**.
- **Mid-market commercial team, configurability and deployment control** → **SugarCRM**.
- **Small, cost-sensitive commercial or field team** → **Zoho CRM**.
- **Early-stage launch, first commercial motion** → **HubSpot**.

## A note on compliance and validation

This is where intellectual honesty matters most. Veeva's durable advantage is that it ships *validated*, life-sciences-specific functionality: HCP/HCO data models, consent and sample management, closed-loop marketing, audit trails, and an environment built around 21 CFR Part 11 and GxP expectations. None of the alternatives here arrive that way. They are general (or general-purpose-with-BPM) platforms that *can* be configured to meet many regulated requirements — but the validation, e-signature and audit controls, and HCP data governance become your responsibility, usually with a specialized implementation partner. For a small commercial team, that's a manageable scope. For a large regulated field-force operation, it's a serious program of work you should budget and staff for deliberately. Don't let a lower sticker price hide the validation cost.

## The bottom line

Veeva earned its dominance, and for global biopharma it will keep it. But the cost, the heaviness for smaller teams, and the unavoidable disruption of the Salesforce-to-Vault migration make 2026 a fair moment to re-evaluate. If you want an enterprise platform without Veeva, **Salesforce** is the most direct path. For mid-market, weigh **Creatio** for workflow and compliance control or **SugarCRM** for configurability. For small and early-stage commercial teams, **Zoho CRM** delivers the best value and **HubSpot** the fastest launch. Just go in clear-eyed: you're trading Veeva's pre-validated, pharma-specific depth for lower cost and flexibility — and the compliance work that depth represented doesn't disappear, it moves to you.
