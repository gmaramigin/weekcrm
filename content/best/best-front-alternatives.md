## Who should leave Front

Front's core insight — that customer conversations shouldn't live in one person's inbox — was genuinely novel when it launched and the execution is still strong. Multi-channel shared inboxes, routing rules, SLA alerts, and the ability to comment on messages without the customer seeing them are features that improve team coordination on high-volume communication workflows. The CRM-lite layer and analytics on top make it a plausible alternative to a full helpdesk for some teams.

The value proposition strains at the edges. For teams primarily doing customer support, the cross-team routing that makes Front powerful is overhead they never use. For email-light teams, Front's pricing feels high for a glorified shared inbox. The mobile app has historically lagged desktop. And unlike Hiver (which lives in Gmail) or Missive (which is multi-provider and fast), Front requires adopting a new interface that not all team members embrace fully. Teams where only 20% of messages actually need the routing logic often realize they're paying for infrastructure that a simpler tool would have handled.

## What to consider

- **Best Gmail-native equivalent** → Hiver. The most compelling Front alternative for Google Workspace shops: shared inboxes, collision detection, assignments, SLA tracking, CSAT surveys, and automations — all inside Gmail with no new UI to learn. If your team already lives in Google Workspace, Hiver's adoption barrier is essentially zero. From $19/user/month, with analytics and automation matching Front's core use cases at lower cost.
- **Best collaborative email client** → Missive. Unlike Front and Hiver, Missive is genuinely multi-provider — Gmail, Outlook, IMAP, SMS, WhatsApp, Instagram DMs — treated as a unified collaborative email client. Internal comments on any message, real-time co-authoring of drafts, team inbox rules, and a chat layer built alongside email. From $14/user/month. The best pick for small teams (2–20 people) where the whole company works from shared inboxes.
- **Best for pure support focus** → Help Scout. Where Front tries to be a platform for all customer-facing communication, Help Scout is explicitly built for support. Shared inbox that feels like email, a knowledge base (Docs), Beacon live chat, and an AI assistant that handles routine requests — without the cross-department routing complexity Front brings. Free for five users, then $25/user/month. Faster to onboard and simpler to operate for support teams that don't need sales-to-logistics handoffs.
- **Best when you need real ticketing** → Freshdesk. The right move when a shared inbox no longer fits your volume or SLA obligations. Multi-channel ticketing (email, phone, chat, social), SLA automation, CSAT reporting, and a free plan for unlimited agents on email. A bigger UX shift from Front than the inbox-native alternatives, but necessary when you need ticket numbers, formal escalation paths, and multi-tier SLA tracking.
- **Best affordable upgrade from basics** → Zoho Desk. At $14/agent/month with a free three-agent tier, Zoho Desk offers formal ticketing, SLA management, Zia AI, and multi-channel support at a fraction of Front Growth's price. Less collaborative-inbox-feel than Front, but significantly more powerful for volume ticket management. If your team is leaving Front because routing logic grew beyond what it handles, Zoho Desk's workflow engine is more appropriate at the price.
- **Best for multi-channel European teams** → Trengo. A WhatsApp-first shared inbox platform with strong multi-channel support (WhatsApp Business API, Instagram, Facebook, email, live chat) and a team inbox model similar to Front's. Better WhatsApp integration than Front out of the box, GDPR-compliant data handling, and pricing from $19/user/month. The standout choice for European businesses where WhatsApp is a primary support and sales channel.

## Migrating from Front

Front supports full data export from Settings → Company → Export Data: messages, tags, contacts, and teammates. Conversations export in EML format and can be imported into most email clients; structured data (contacts, tags, conversation metadata) exports as CSV. The Front API also allows custom exports for teams with large conversation histories.

The migration asset that requires the most planning is your rule set. Front's routing rules (sender domain → assign to team A, subject line contains "invoice" → tag Finance) don't transfer — you'll rebuild them in the new tool's automation language. Export a list of all active rules before canceling, and use the migration as a chance to audit which rules are actually firing versus which accumulated and went stale. Most teams find that 30–40% of their routing rules are redundant on review.

For teams moving from Front to Hiver specifically, Google Workspace users keep their existing Gmail labels and filters — only the shared inbox layer changes. The transition typically takes a single day and requires no migration of historical data since Hiver reads from your existing Gmail account.

## Picking the right size tool

The sharpest decision in replacing Front is matching tool size to actual team size and workflow complexity. A two-person team sharing a support inbox needs Missive or Help Scout, not an enterprise routing platform. A 30-person support org handling multi-channel at SLA needs Freshdesk or Zoho Desk. Front's niche — teams of 5–50 with cross-functional handoffs — is where it earns its price. If you're outside that band in either direction, the alternatives above will feel like a better fit from the first week.
