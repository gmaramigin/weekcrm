## How we picked

Marketplace support is support conducted under someone else's rules. Amazon, eBay, Walmart, Etsy, and the rest each impose their own response clocks, messaging restrictions, dispute processes, and account health metrics — and violating them costs you selling privileges, not just goodwill. We weighted **native, policy-aware channel integration** above almost everything else: a tool that merely forwards marketplace messages to email will get your response times measured wrong and eventually get you in trouble.

We then weighted **order context across every channel simultaneously**. A multi-channel seller has the same SKU moving through Shopify, Amazon FBA, eBay, and a wholesale account, each with different fulfillment, different return rules, and different buyer expectations. The agent needs order, tracking, fulfillment method, and return eligibility in one view regardless of where the sale originated, or handle time balloons and errors follow.

Finally, we weighted **deflection through self-service**. Marketplace buyers ask about order status and returns at a rate that makes staffing to volume economically absurd against marketplace-thin margins. The winning pattern is a self-service portal that resolves the majority of contacts without an agent, with the remaining volume routed to people who can actually add value. We also included Zendesk separately for the different problem of operating a two-sided marketplace, where buyer and seller support are genuinely different businesses.

## What to prioritize

- **Native marketplace messaging with SLA clocks per channel.** Reply inside Amazon's and eBay's systems, with each marketplace's own timer tracked and surfaced before it breaches. eDesk's account-health awareness is the differentiator; generic tools will report an average response time that means nothing to Amazon.
- **A self-service order and returns portal.** Lookup by order number or email, live tracking, self-initiated returns, printable labels. Richpanel's portal model targets exactly the contact types that make up the majority of your volume. This is where the ROI is.
- **Unified order view across every sales channel.** Shopify, Amazon, eBay, Walmart, and your own site in one agent view with fulfillment method and return eligibility. Re:amaze and eDesk both handle broad channel spreads; verify each specific channel you sell on is fully supported.
- **Dispute and A-to-z claim workflow.** Chargebacks, INR claims, and marketplace disputes have hard deadlines and evidence requirements. Give them a dedicated queue with a deadline-driven SLA and a checklist macro, because a missed evidence window is an automatic loss regardless of who was right.
- **Buyer and seller support separated if you operate the marketplace.** Different help centers, different SLAs, different agent groups, different metrics. Zendesk's multi-brand structure is the standard answer; merging them means your sellers wait behind buyer order-status questions, which is precisely backwards from a revenue standpoint.
- **Cost per ticket measured against marketplace margin.** Marketplace fees already take a substantial cut, and a $12 average order cannot absorb an expensive human interaction. Model your fully loaded cost per contact and let that number decide how aggressively you automate — in this category, the deflection rate is the business case.
