## Who should leave Follow Up Boss

Follow Up Boss is the CRM 41 of the top 50 U.S. real estate teams run on, and the reason is narrow and real: it ingests leads from 250+ sources, routes them to the right agent in seconds, and drives relentless follow-up with calling, texting, and action plans built in. From $69/user/month on Grow (Pro runs $499/month for ten users), it is a follow-up machine. But it is *only* a follow-up machine — it deliberately does not generate leads, host an IDX website, run your ads, or do geographic farming. You bring the top of funnel; Follow Up Boss works it.

You should leave if the gap you keep hitting is one Follow Up Boss was never built to fill: you're paying separately for an IDX site and a lead source and want them under one login, your effective per-seat cost is too high for a solo book, or your business runs on listings and farming rather than inbound buyer leads. The teams who should *stay* are inside-sales teams with a working lead source who simply need speed-to-lead and accountability — that is exactly what Follow Up Boss does better than almost anyone. Don't switch away from a great follow-up tool because it isn't also a lead-gen platform; add the missing piece, or move only when the missing piece is the whole job.

## What to consider

- **Best all-in-one replacement** → BoldTrail. If you're stitching Follow Up Boss to a separate IDX site and lead source, BoldTrail (the kvCORE successor behind RE/MAX, eXp, and BHHS) folds CRM, website, 20+ lead-gen tools, and a dialer into one stack. From ~$499/month for individuals, with team tiers by quote — overbuilt for solos, natural for 10+ agent teams.
- **Best AI-first listing engine** → Lofty. For teams whose growth depends on listings, Lofty's 2026 Homeowner Agent mines your existing database for seller intent instead of only working inbound buyers. IDX, ads, and a power dialer included, from $449/month (Core), with per-user and ad fees on top.
- **Best for solo agents** → Wise Agent. Follow Up Boss is priced for teams; Wise Agent is the three-time Forbes Advisor "Best Real Estate CRM" at a flat $49/month with transaction management, marketing, and 24/7 live support. The pragmatic move when team pricing can't be justified by a solo book.
- **Best for referral and repeat business** → Realvolve. If your work is nurturing past clients and your sphere rather than buying Zillow leads, Realvolve's behavior-triggered workflows and relationship scoring go deeper than action plans, at $94/month with no forced IDX or ad stack to pay for.
- **Best for managed paid-ad lead gen** → BoomTown. When you want a vendor to *run* the Facebook and Google campaigns and feed predictively scored leads into the CRM, BoomTown does end-to-end paid funnel. From ~$1,000/month plus ad spend (realistically $3,000–$5,000+ all-in) — only worth it if paid already works for you.
- **Best for geographic farming** → Top Producer. For agents who win listings by owning a neighborhood, Top Producer pairs MLS-driven "likely to sell" farming with smart drip campaigns. Pro from $179/user/month; team tiers $399–$1,199/month, with leads and farming as add-ons.

## Match the alternative to the gap

The mistake is shopping for a "better Follow Up Boss." Its follow-up is the best in the category — the reason to leave is almost always a capability it intentionally doesn't have. So name the missing piece first, then buy for that piece, not for a marginally nicer pipeline.

Tired of paying for a separate website and lead source? BoldTrail or Lofty bring them under one roof. Is your real engine listings, not buyer inquiries? Lofty's Homeowner Agent or Top Producer's farming attack that directly. Is the per-seat cost just too high for a one-agent shop? Wise Agent does the core job for a flat $49. Living on referrals and repeat clients? Realvolve nurtures the relationship deeper than action plans do. Already winning with paid ads and want someone to run them? BoomTown owns the whole funnel.

## Trial advice

Because Follow Up Boss is genuinely excellent at follow-up, a replacement has to clearly beat it at the *one* thing driving you away — usually lead generation, price, or farming — not just match it overall. Export your contacts, load your top two finalists, and run a real week of lead intake and follow-up in each. The all-in-ones (BoldTrail, Lofty, BoomTown) take weeks to roll out, so insist on a reference call with a team your size and budget for ad spend before signing; the lighter tools (Wise Agent, Realvolve) are live in days and most offer a 14-day trial, so you can validate them well before any renewal lands.
