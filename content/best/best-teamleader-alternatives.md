## Who should leave Teamleader

Teamleader (and its newer Teamleader Focus / Teamleader Orbit products) sells one promise: run the whole back office of a small service business from a single tool. CRM, quotations, invoicing, project planning, and time tracking sit together, with strong support for European specifics — VAT handling, Peppol e-invoicing, multi-language quotes, and integrations with accounting tools like Exact and Yuki. For an agency, installer, or consultancy in Belgium, the Netherlands, or France, that local fit is the entire reason to buy, and pricing starts around €25–€35/user/month depending on the modules you switch on.

The same breadth is what eventually pushes teams out. Because Teamleader spans so many functions, the CRM layer itself is shallower than a dedicated sales platform — pipeline reporting, sequence automation, and deal forecasting feel secondary to the quoting-and-invoicing core. Pricing climbs as you bolt on modules and "boosters," and the two-product split (Focus for very small businesses, Orbit for scaling ones) means a migration looms if you grow. You should leave if you want a sales-first CRM with real pipeline analytics, if you sell mainly outside continental Europe and don't need Peppol or local VAT, or if you'd rather pay for best-in-class point tools than one suite that's adequate at everything. Teams whose invoicing and CRM genuinely have to live in one EU-aware tool have little reason to switch.

## What to consider

- **Best for a like-for-like EU all-in-one** → Sellsy. The closest direct swap: a French CRM-plus-invoicing-plus-marketing suite with quotes, e-invoicing, and subscription billing built for European compliance, from around €29/user/month. If you like Teamleader's "sales and billing in one place" model but want a deeper marketing and automation layer, Sellsy is the natural lateral move.
- **Best for a sales-first pipeline** → Pipedrive. Where Teamleader treats the pipeline as one module among many, Pipedrive makes it the whole product — visual stages, activity reminders, and clean forecasting from $14/user/month (Essential) up to $99 (Enterprise). The pick when you want reps to actually work deals, and you'll handle invoicing in a separate tool.
- **Best for configurable depth on a budget** → Zoho CRM. Multi-pipeline management, Blueprint process automation, and Zia AI from $14–$52/user/month, free for up to three users — and Zoho Invoice/Books cover the billing side if you want to keep everything in one vendor's ecosystem without Teamleader's per-module pricing.
- **Best for marketing plus sales in one free core** → HubSpot. A genuinely usable free CRM, then Starter at $20/seat/month and Professional at $100/seat/month (plus a one-time onboarding fee on Pro). The move when your growth bottleneck is lead generation and nurture, not quoting — areas Teamleader barely touches.
- **Best for an all-in-one without per-module fees** → Bitrix24. CRM, quotes, invoicing, tasks, and even telephony on a single flat per-organization price from $49/month for unlimited users, plus a real free plan. If Teamleader's appeal was breadth but the add-on pricing stung, Bitrix24 delivers similar scope without metering every feature.
- **Best for professional-services operations** → Scoro. For agencies and consultancies that bought Teamleader mainly to tie quotes to projects and time, Scoro goes deeper on work management — budgets, utilization, project profitability, and billing in one view, from about $26/user/month. The upgrade when the project side matters more than the sales pipeline.

## Match the alternative to the gap

Don't shop for "a better Teamleader" in the abstract — name which of its jobs you're actually trying to replace. Teamleader is three products wearing one badge: a CRM, a quote-to-invoice engine, and a light project tool. Each gap points somewhere different.

If you loved the EU billing-plus-CRM combo and just want it sharper, Sellsy is the closest peer and Bitrix24 is the cheaper, flatter version. If the CRM itself was the weak link, Pipedrive and Zoho give you real pipeline depth and you'll pair them with separate invoicing. If lead generation was the thing Teamleader never did, HubSpot's marketing layer is the reason to switch. And if you were really running a services business off the project and time-tracking modules, Scoro replaces that operational core far better than any sales CRM will.

## Trial advice

Teamleader's strength is that quotes, invoices, and contacts already share one database, so any replacement has to prove the seam it introduces is worth it. Before you commit, list the three Teamleader modules you actually use weekly and test each finalist against only those — there's no point grading HubSpot on invoicing or Pipedrive on VAT. Export your contacts and open deals, import them into your top two picks, and run a real week: build a live quote (or its equivalent), move two real deals through the pipeline, and check whether your accounting integration still works. Most of these tools offer a free tier or 14-day trial, so you can validate the switch — and the new tool stack around it — before moving your billing off Teamleader.
