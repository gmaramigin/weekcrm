## Who should leave Gorgias

Gorgias solves a genuine problem: ecommerce support is different from SaaS or services support. Shopify order lookups, refund processing, and edit-without-leaving-the-helpdesk matter in ways that Zendesk-style tools require workarounds to handle. For a pure-play DTC brand on Shopify, Gorgias built the right native experience and its market-share lead in that niche is earned.

The cracks appear in three scenarios. First, billing unpredictability: the ticket-based model is designed for stable volumes, but DTC businesses have seasonal spikes. A Black Friday surge or viral moment can multiply your Gorgias bill without warning. Second, multi-channel expansion: Gorgias is strong on email, chat, and social, but voice and more complex SLA routing require workarounds or add-ons. Third, teams that support both ecommerce and non-ecommerce customers (B2B accounts, wholesale, subscriptions) hit Gorgias's model limits fast — the tool was built for order questions, not complex account management.

## What to consider

- **Best flat-rate ecommerce alternative** → Reamaze. The closest feature analog to Gorgias — Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and Magento integrations, live chat, push campaigns, and chatbots — at a flat $29/user/month with no ticket-volume overage. A 3-agent team handling 5,000 tickets/month pays the same as a 3-agent team handling 500. Strong multi-brand support for merchants running multiple storefronts.
- **Best for self-service and AI deflection** → Richpanel. Purpose-built to deflect Gorgias's order-status and where-is-my-order tickets through a branded customer portal. Customers can self-serve returns, cancellations, and order edits without opening a ticket. AI handles the rest. Pricing starts at $29/month for 1,000 conversations — worth evaluating if deflection rate is a KPI for your team.
- **Best for multi-channel scale** → Freshdesk. More robust multi-channel coverage (email, phone, chat, social, WhatsApp) plus stronger SLA automation and a free plan to evaluate seriously. Freshdesk's ticketing model is per-agent, not per-ticket, which de-risks seasonal volume spikes. Shopify integration via the marketplace is solid, not native, but adequate for teams whose support mix extends beyond order questions.
- **Best for complex operations** → Zendesk. If you're outgrowing ecommerce support specialization and need deep routing, custom views, SLA management across multiple tiers, and 1,500+ integrations, Zendesk Suite at $55/agent is the next step up in sophistication. The Shopify connector is mature. The trade-off is setup complexity — Zendesk requires more admin investment than any tool on this list.
- **Best value with CRM tie-in** → Zoho Desk. At $14/agent/month with a free three-agent tier, Zoho Desk is the most affordable path out of Gorgias's per-ticket model. The Shopify integration is solid and Zia AI (sentiment analysis, auto-tagging) is included. Strong choice for teams already using Zoho CRM or Zoho Books.
- **Best for simple human support** → Help Scout. If Gorgias feels over-engineered for your volume and you primarily support via email, Help Scout's inbox-first model simplifies operations dramatically. No Shopify native integration, but Beacon live chat and a knowledge base are included from $25/user/month. Ideal for small DTC teams that want speed over features.

## Migrating from Gorgias

Gorgias exports tickets, macros, and customer data via the Settings → Export section. The most critical migration asset is your macros (saved responses) — export these first and adapt them to your new tool's template format before going live. Gorgias tags are importable to Freshdesk and Zendesk; Reamaze and Richpanel both have documented Gorgias migration guides.

The Shopify integration in each alternative re-syncs live order data independently — you don't carry data across, you reconnect. Confirm the new tool's Shopify app is installed and authenticated before decommissioning Gorgias to avoid a gap in order context during the transition. Most teams can run both tools in parallel for five to seven business days to clear the open ticket queue before hard-cutting over.

## Choosing by the real problem

If ticket overages are the pain point, go to Reamaze or Freshdesk immediately — both use per-agent pricing that doesn't punish volume spikes. If you want to reduce ticket volume rather than manage it more cheaply, Richpanel's self-service portal directly attacks WISMO and returns tickets. If you need Gorgias's ecommerce DNA but with broader multi-channel or a voice channel, Freshdesk and Zendesk are the mature paths. None of these take more than a week to evaluate on a free trial with real data — time spent there is time saved on a decision you'll live with for years.
