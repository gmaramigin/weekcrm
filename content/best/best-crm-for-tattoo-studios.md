## How we picked

A tattoo chair lives or dies on two numbers: deposits collected and no-shows avoided. A flaked four-hour session is hours of irreplaceable chair time gone, and the most valuable work — large multi-session pieces — only happens if clients actually come back for the next sitting. The right CRM locks in the booking with a deposit, cuts flakes with timed reminders, keeps in-progress pieces moving with follow-up nudges, and turns finished clients into the reviews that fill an artist's waitlist. We weighted deposit collection, no-show reduction, and repeat-session automation over anything resembling enterprise sales reporting.

## What to consider

- **All-in-one booking + deposits** → vcita. Consultations, deposits, reminders, and messaging in one app.
- **Reviews and visibility** → Thryv. Fill the book with the reviews and local presence that win new clients.
- **Repeat-session automation** → Keap. Nudge multi-session and past clients to book their next sitting.
- **Start free** → HubSpot. Track every client and project while you keep overhead low.
- **Best value** → Zoho CRM. Full automation and reminders at the lowest per-seat cost.

## Pricing snapshot

Studio-appropriate plans run $0–$60/month for a small shop or solo artist. HubSpot starts free; Zoho anchors the value end; vcita and Thryv cost more but bundle the booking, deposits, and reviews you'd otherwise pay for piecemeal.

## Trial advice

During the trial, require a deposit on one real booking and set reminders for the day before — then watch your no-show rate. Add an automation that nudges in-progress clients to book their next session. The studio that protects chair time with deposits and keeps multi-session work moving will earn more from the same calendar.
