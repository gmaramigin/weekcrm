## How we picked

Embedding a form on your site that writes directly to the CRM should be a five-minute job. The picks below ship **native forms** (no Zapier, no third-party form tool), **map fields directly to CRM objects**, and **trigger workflows** (auto-assign, send a confirmation email, notify Slack) when a submission lands. Anything that requires a Typeform-to-Zapier-to-CRM chain is the wrong shape — you'll lose leads to mapping errors and broken zaps within six months.

## What to consider

- **Inbound-led B2B SaaS, content marketing motion** → HubSpot. The forms are the best in the category — pop-ups, embedded, multi-step, with full marketing automation downstream.
- **Modern startup, AI-native CRM** → Attio. Forms are clean, fast to build, and tie into the custom data model.
- **Sales-led team that just needs lead capture** → Pipedrive. Web forms write directly into deals with auto-assignment and territory routing.
- **Tight budget, multi-product company** → Zoho CRM. Forms are included on most tiers and integrate with the rest of Zoho One.
- **Funnel-focused team that wants conversion analytics** → Freshsales. Forms include built-in conversion tracking and split-test support.

## Pricing snapshot

Form-capable CRMs typically include forms on the entry tier or one tier up. HubSpot's basic forms are free; their best forms (smart fields, A/B test, progressive profiling) are Marketing Hub Professional ($800+/mo). For most teams, the basic forms are more than enough.

## What to skip

You don't need: a separate Typeform/Tally subscription if your CRM ships forms; a "form builder SaaS" if your forms are basic lead-capture; or marketing automation that you'll never configure. Most teams pay $0–$50/mo for forms inside the CRM and that's it.

## Trial advice

Build one form, embed it on your site, and submit it 5 times with edge cases (duplicate email, missing fields, weird Unicode in name). The right CRM is the one where every submission lands cleanly in the right object with the right field mapping and the right downstream automation fires. If any of those steps drop, the integration is the wrong shape — try the next one.
