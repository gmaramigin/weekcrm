## How we picked

Automation is only valuable if it deflects work without frustrating customers, so we judged these tools on the full ladder: simple macros and canned replies, event-driven triggers, time-based escalations and SLAs, and modern AI agents that can resolve tickets autonomously. We looked at how granular the rule builder is, whether automations can act on external data (order status, account tier, custom fields), and how transparent the AI is about when it hands off to a human. Price-to-power mattered too — a deep automation engine you can't afford to staff or configure is no win. We also weighted reporting, since you can't tune deflection you can't measure.

## What to consider

- **Rule depth vs simplicity**: Zendesk and Freshdesk give you full trigger/automation/macro control with conditional logic. Help Scout keeps workflows deliberately simple — better for a small team that doesn't want to administer a rules engine.
- **AI resolution vs AI assist**: Decide whether you want AI to fully close tickets (Intercom's Fin, Zendesk AI agents) or just draft replies for humans to approve (Help Scout, Freshdesk assist). Autonomous resolution deflects more but needs a strong knowledge base and guardrails.
- **Action-taking automation**: Can a rule do something, not just route? Gorgias can issue refunds and edit orders; Zendesk and Freshdesk can hit external APIs via integrations. This is where automation moves from triage to real resolution.
- **Ecommerce vs general support**: Gorgias is built around order data and revenue attribution. If you're not ecommerce, that focus is wasted and a general tool fits better.
- **Tuning and reporting**: Look for automation-specific analytics — deflection rate, first-response time impact, macro usage. Zendesk and Intercom report on this most thoroughly.

## Pricing snapshot

Expect roughly $15-115 per agent per month, with AI features often billed separately by resolution or usage. Help Scout is the value option at around $25-50 per user per month. Freshdesk runs about $15-79 per agent per month and packs strong automation into its mid tiers, often the best power-per-dollar here. Gorgias prices around $10-360+ per month on volume-based tiers tied to ticket count rather than pure seats, which suits ecommerce. Zendesk spans roughly $25-115+ per agent per month across Suite tiers, with its AI agents adding per-resolution cost. Intercom runs about $39-100+ per seat per month plus usage-based AI fees for Fin resolutions, so model your conversation and resolution volume before committing — that's where the bill actually lands.

## Trial advice

Don't grade automation on the demo's happy path — build it against your real tickets. Export a week of recent conversations, then in each trial recreate your three highest-volume scenarios as triggers or AI flows and measure how many would have been resolved or correctly routed without a human. For AI resolution tools (Intercom, Zendesk), feed them your actual knowledge base and watch how often the bot hands off versus hallucinates; a confident wrong answer is worse than no automation. If you're ecommerce, test Gorgias against a live Shopify sandbox so you can see it act on real order data. Finally, check the reporting: you'll be tuning these rules for months, so confirm you can actually see deflection and first-response metrics before you buy.
