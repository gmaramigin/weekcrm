## How we picked

AI email writing in a CRM is only useful when the AI knows who it's writing to. A generic text generator bolted onto a compose box produces bland drafts; an AI that reads the contact's history, the deal stage, and the company context produces something a rep can actually send. So we weighted how well each platform grounds its drafts in live CRM data, the range of actions (draft, rewrite, shorten, change tone, personalize at scale), and whether the AI is genuinely native to the product or a thin wrapper around a language model. The best tools here cut the time from blank reply to sent message without making every email read like a template.

## What to consider

- **The most context-aware AI drafting** → Attio. Built AI-native, it drafts and personalizes from records it enriches automatically, so the email reflects real relationship history rather than guesses. Best for modern, ops-led teams.
- **The most proven, broadest adoption** → HubSpot. Breeze AI brings drafting, rewriting, and tone control to the largest CRM user base, tightly integrated with HubSpot's sequences and templates.
- **A sales team that wants it in the pipeline** → Pipedrive. AI-assisted email writing arrives at the Professional tier alongside the Sales Assistant, fitting a deal-focused team that lives in the pipeline.
- **An all-in-one with sending built in** → Salesmate. Its AI message composer pairs with native email, calling, and SMS, so a unified revenue team drafts and sends from one place.
- **A self-maintaining CRM** → Day.ai. It auto-captures conversation context from email and meetings, then drafts follow-ups from it — ideal for teams whose real problem is a CRM that's always out of date.

## Context is what separates good drafts from filler

The differentiator across these five isn't the underlying model — it's how much relevant context the CRM can feed it. Attio and Day.ai win on this because they continuously enrich and auto-capture data, so the AI writes with a full picture of the relationship. HubSpot leans on its mature data model and sequences to ground Breeze's output. When evaluating, send the AI a real scenario from your pipeline and check whether the draft references specifics — the last conversation, the deal stage, the company — or whether it produces something you could have generated in any standalone writing tool. The specificity is the value.

## Personalization at scale, without the slop

The promise of AI email writing is personalizing outreach across many contacts without writing each one by hand. The risk is mass-producing recognizably generic, AI-flavored messages that hurt your reply rate. The platforms that handle this well — Attio, HubSpot, and Pipedrive — let the AI draft from per-record context and then keep the human in the loop to edit before sending. Treat these tools as a fast first draft rather than a fully autonomous sender, and the quality stays high while the time savings remain real.
