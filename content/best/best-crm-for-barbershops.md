## How we picked

A barbershop lives and dies by its chair-fill rate. The CRM's job is simple but specific: let clients book the barber they want online, remind them so they show up, remember what they got last time, and nudge them to rebook before they drift to the shop down the street. We prioritized per-barber booking, automated SMS reminders, client history, and rebooking or loyalty automation. Sales pipelines and forecasting are irrelevant here — attendance and retention are everything.

## What to consider

- **You want booking, reminders, and payments in one app** → **vcita**. Per-barber calendars, automatic reminders, and card payments — the tightest all-in-one for a single shop.
- **You compete on Google reviews** → **Thryv**. Adds reputation management, campaigns, and listing sync so your shop shows up and looks good when someone searches "barber near me."
- **Most clients book by DM** → **Kommo**. Turns Instagram, WhatsApp, and Facebook messages into bookings and keeps the client thread in one place — ideal for social-first shops.
- **You want automation on a budget** → **EngageBay**. A free-friendly CRM with email/SMS automation and a clean contact record — good for a growing shop that wants sequences without a big bill.
- **Rebooking and loyalty are your focus** → **Keap**. Strong follow-up automation to bring clients back on a cadence and reward regulars — the retention engine for a shop with a loyal base.

## Pricing snapshot

Barbershop-friendly CRMs run roughly $20–$50/month for a small shop, with SMS reminders often billed as separate credits. Confirm whether per-barber calendars are included or count as extra seats before you commit.

## Trial advice

Run two for a week: turn on reminders, take a few online bookings per barber, and send one rebooking nudge. Keep the one that fills chairs with the least effort — for a barbershop, the CRM that quietly reduces no-shows pays for itself the first month.
