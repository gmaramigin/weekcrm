## Who should leave Richpanel

Richpanel made its name on ecommerce self-service: a customer-facing help center where shoppers can track orders, request returns, and resolve common issues without ever opening a ticket, backed by an agent workspace that surfaces Shopify and order context. For lean DTC teams trying to deflect repetitive "where's my order?" volume, that self-service-first design is genuinely useful and can cut agent load meaningfully.

Teams tend to look elsewhere for a few reasons. The Shopify integration, while solid, isn't as deep or as widely proven as the category leader's; the app ecosystem is smaller than the big platforms'; and pricing can feel less predictable as you scale. Stores that grow beyond pure storefront support — adding wholesale, multi-brand, or non-Shopify channels — often want a broader, more established help desk underneath their ecommerce flows. When the self-service layer is no longer the whole story, Richpanel can start to feel narrow.

## What to consider

- **Best for Shopify depth** → Gorgias. The ecommerce help desk most stores compare everything else to. Live order data in every ticket, macros that issue refunds and edit orders directly, and ticket-based pricing that suits seasonal volume. The most direct upgrade if you want the deepest Shopify integration.
- **Best for scaling** → Zendesk. Mature triggers, macros, SLA routing, and a 1,000+ app marketplace that connects to your store and the rest of your stack. The safe choice when support is growing past storefront questions and you want a platform with room to run.
- **Best general-purpose value** → Freshdesk. Multi-channel ticketing, SLAs, automation, and a knowledge base with a free plan for unlimited agents on email. A strong, affordable foundation that still integrates with Shopify for order context.
- **Best for a simpler, warmer inbox** → Help Scout. Built to feel like email rather than a ticket queue, with a knowledge base, CSAT, and an AI assistant. Ideal for stores that find full ecommerce platforms heavier than they need and want fast, friendly support.
- **Best for chat-led storefront support** → Tidio. Live chat and chatbots tuned for ecommerce, with product context and proactive messaging that nudges conversions. A good fit when most of your support happens on-site, before and during purchase.

## Choosing your next tool

Decide what mattered most about Richpanel. If it was the ecommerce focus and you want it done deeper, Gorgias is the obvious test — it's the most complete Shopify help desk and prices in a way that fits lean stores. If it was self-service deflection and you now also need a broad, scalable platform, Zendesk or Freshdesk give you that, with Freshdesk winning on price.

If the appeal was simplicity, Help Scout keeps support warm and lightweight, and if most of your conversations happen in the storefront chat widget, Tidio leans into chat and conversion. Import a real slice of order-related tickets into two finalists and work a busy day in each before committing.
