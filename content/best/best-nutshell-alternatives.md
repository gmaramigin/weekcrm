## Who should leave Nutshell

Nutshell's pitch is value: unlimited contacts and storage on every tier, email and calendar sync, and built-in email marketing — broadcasts and drip sequences — folded into one subscription from $13/user/month. That bundling is genuinely hard to beat for a growing B2B team that would otherwise pay separately for a CRM and an outreach tool. The catch is what the price buys. The $13 Foundation plan has no sales automation at all, so most teams land on Pro at $42/user/month, and even there the platform's customization, permissions, and AI depth are deliberately shallow compared with heavier CRMs.

So the trigger to leave is almost always a specific ceiling, not the price. You've outgrown Nutshell when reps need a real dialer, when your admins want custom objects and process enforcement Nutshell won't give them, when a closed deal kicks off delivery work that lives outside the CRM, or when adoption is dying because logging is still manual. Teams that should stay are 5-to-100-person B2B shops that genuinely use the bundled email marketing and value a no-surprise bill over configurability. If the all-in-one bundle is working, switching only adds cost and complexity.

## What to consider

- **Best for marketing depth** → HubSpot. Nutshell's email marketing covers broadcasts and drips; HubSpot adds landing pages, forms, full automation, and a 1,500+ app marketplace. Start free, then $20/seat (Starter) or $100 (Professional, plus a one-time $1,500 onboarding fee) when your campaigns outgrow Nutshell's lighter toolkit.
- **Best for customization at a low price** → Zoho CRM. The exact ceiling Nutshell hits — custom modules, multi-pipeline management, Blueprint process enforcement, Zia AI — Zoho clears from $14 to $52/user/month, with a free tier for three users. More configurability per dollar than Nutshell's flat bundle.
- **Best for outbound calling** → Close. Nutshell has no native dialer; Close ships a power dialer, predictive dialer, SMS, and sequences from $19/user/month (Base), with the dialers on Professional ($99) and Business ($129). The fix for phone-heavy teams.
- **Best for killing manual entry** → Salesflare. If Nutshell adoption stalled because reps stopped logging, Salesflare auto-captures contacts, meetings, and email threads from Gmail, Outlook, and LinkedIn. From $29/user/month (Growth), it attacks adoption rather than adding features.
- **Best for deal-to-delivery work** → Insightly. Nutshell stops at the sale; Insightly converts a closed deal into a project with tasks and milestones in the same system. Free for two users, then $29 (Plus) to $99 (Enterprise) — built for service firms that sell then execute.
- **Best for Google Workspace teams** → Copper. If your team lives in Gmail and Calendar, Copper runs the pipeline from the inbox and auto-builds records from email history. From $9 (Starter), though Opportunities and Leads need the $59 Professional plan.

## Match the alternative to the gap

Name the gap before you shop. Need real campaigns, landing pages, and automation? HubSpot's suite outclasses Nutshell's bundled marketing, though watch the Professional onboarding fee. Hitting walls on custom fields, modules, or permissions? Zoho gives you that depth at Nutshell-comparable pricing. Reps spend the day dialing? Close's native telephony is in a different category from Nutshell's logging-only contact records.

If the problem is people, not features, the answer changes. CRM dying from neglect points straight at Salesflare's auto-capture. A business that closes deals and then delivers — agencies, consultancies, construction — gets a second system free by choosing Insightly's project layer. And a team fully committed to Google Workspace will find Copper's inbox-native workflow removes friction Nutshell's separate app never could.

## Trial advice

Export your contacts, companies, and the active pipeline from Nutshell first, and decide upfront what happens to its email marketing — your broadcasts, drips, and engagement history may not migrate cleanly, so plan to rebuild sequences in HubSpot or pair a slimmer CRM with a dedicated outreach tool. Load your data into your top two finalists and run a real week in each, pricing the all-in monthly cost at the tier that actually unlocks what drove you away — Nutshell's value bundle is the bar a replacement has to clearly beat on the one capability you came for. Most of these tools are live in a day or two, well before your next Nutshell renewal.
