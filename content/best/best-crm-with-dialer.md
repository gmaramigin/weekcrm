## How we picked

A "CRM with a dialer" can mean two very different things: a real native dialer (build the call experience into the CRM, log everything automatically, ship call recording and voicemail drop) or a thin VoIP click-to-call integration. We picked tools that own the dialer experience — not ones that just embed a Twilio button. We weighted: **call volume the dialer can sustain**, **automatic logging and recording**, **SMS as a peer to voice**, and **whether reps can run their entire day inside the CRM**.

## What to prioritize

- **Power dial / predictive dial.** For 50+ calls/day outbound, you need at least power dial (queue-based one-click). Predictive dial (multi-line) helps but requires compliance care.
- **Voicemail drop.** Save 30–45 seconds per voicemail. Compounds fast at volume.
- **Automatic call logging and recording.** Every call should attach to the deal automatically. Manual logging is where data hygiene dies.
- **SMS in the same inbox.** Outbound is multi-channel now. SMS reply rates beat email reply rates for warm pipelines.
- **Local presence.** Caller ID matching the prospect's area code lifts pickup rates ~30%.
