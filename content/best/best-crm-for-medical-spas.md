## How we picked

Medical spas operate at the intersection of healthcare and retail. The CRMs below were chosen on five criteria: **online booking with deposits** (no-show prevention is the #1 ROI driver), **treatment and package tracking** (memberships, series packs, gift cards), **retention marketing** (rebooking nudges and birthday/anniversary campaigns drive 40% of revenue), **HIPAA-aware workflows** (PHI handling, audit logs), and **two-way SMS** (medical-spa clients respond to texts at 3–5x email rates).

## What to consider

- **Single-location spas** — Thryv and vCita are the strongest match. Both bundle online booking, payments, marketing campaigns, and reviews into one workflow with the staff-management features a small clinic needs.
- **Membership-heavy spas** — Keap's automation engine is the best for managing recurring memberships, series-package burndown, and upsell sequences. It's pricier but pays back fast on retention.
- **Multi-location chains** — HubSpot or Pipedrive become viable once you need centralized reporting across 3+ locations. You'll pair them with industry tools (Boulevard, Zenoti, Vagaro) for the booking-and-payments backend and use the CRM as the marketing + customer-data layer on top.
- **Outbound consultation pipelines** — If you sell high-ticket procedures (laser, body contouring, surgery) that require a consultation, Pipedrive's pipeline view is the cleanest way to manage the consult → procedure → follow-up funnel.

## Pricing snapshot

Med-spa-friendly CRMs cluster $30–$200/user/mo. Thryv and vCita start ~$30–$50/mo. Keap starts $199/mo (Pro) and scales with contacts. HubSpot's Starter is $20/mo per seat. Pipedrive is $14–$49/user/mo. Industry-specific booking tools (Boulevard, Zenoti, Vagaro) are typically $200–$500/mo for the booking + payments side — most multi-location spas run a hybrid stack.

## HIPAA reality check

None of the generic CRMs on this list are HIPAA-compliant out of the box. For PHI handling (treatment records, medical history), most med-spas use a separate EHR (Symplast, Aesthetics Pro, PatientNow). The CRM holds the marketing + booking layer; the EHR holds the clinical record. If you're a single-tool shop and need true HIPAA coverage, look at industry-specific platforms before generic CRMs.

## Trial advice

Pick the CRM that integrates cleanly with your booking platform (Boulevard, Zenoti, Vagaro) — if you have to manually sync clients twice a day, no amount of marketing automation makes up for it. Most CRMs above have Zapier or native integrations; test the data flow before signing an annual contract.
