## How we picked

"Email sync" is one of the most oversold phrases in CRM marketing, because there are two very different things hiding behind it. The weak version is BCC-to-CRM logging: you remember to copy a special address, and a one-way record appears. The real version is two-way sync — the CRM and your Gmail or Outlook mailbox stay continuously mirrored, so a reply you send from your phone shows up on the contact automatically, an email you send from inside the CRM lands in your real Sent folder, and the whole thread is visible from both sides without anyone copying anything.

We judged each tool on the genuine bidirectional connection: does it sync both Gmail and Outlook, can you compose and send from within the CRM, are inbound and outbound messages logged against the right contact without manual filing, and does it stay current in real time rather than on a delayed pull. We favored CRMs where the inbox is the center of gravity, because those are the ones that get email sync right rather than treating it as an afterthought.

## What to consider

- **Best automatic sync** → Salesflare. Salesflare's entire premise is killing manual entry, and email is where that shows: it captures every thread, contact, and attachment from Gmail or Outlook and files it against the right record without a click, while letting you send and sequence from inside the CRM. From $29/user/mo, it's the strongest "set it and forget it" two-way sync for lean B2B teams.
- **Best Gmail-native experience** → NetHunt CRM. NetHunt doesn't sync to Gmail — it lives inside it. Records, pipelines, and outreach all render in the Gmail interface, so two-way sync is structural rather than a connector: you're already in the inbox, and every send and reply is logged by default. From $30/user/mo, it's the most natural fit for a team that never wants to leave Gmail.
- **Best for Google Workspace shops** → Copper. The only CRM officially recommended by Google, Copper is built natively into Gmail, Calendar, and Drive, auto-populating contacts from email history and syncing threads both directions. Starter is $9/user/mo with most teams landing around $59; if your company runs entirely on Google Workspace, nothing else feels as seamless.
- **Best all-in-one with dual-provider sync** → HubSpot CRM. HubSpot's two-way sync covers both Gmail and Outlook cleanly, logs threads to the contact and deal, and lets you send tracked email from the record — inside a platform that also handles marketing and service. Free to start and paid from $20/mo, it's the safe pick when you want solid sync plus everything else.
- **Best pipeline-first option** → Pipedrive. Pipedrive's email sync (Smart Email and full two-way on its mid-tiers) connects Gmail or Outlook, threads conversations onto deals, and lets reps send from the CRM with templates and tracking. From $14/user/mo, it's the cheapest way to get real two-way sync wrapped around the cleanest sales pipeline in the category.
- **Best AI-assisted sync** → Freshsales. Freshsales bundles two-way Gmail and Outlook sync with Freddy AI for prioritization and reply suggestions, and its free tier plus $9/user/mo entry price make it the most affordable on-ramp. For a team that wants synced email and AI nudges in one inexpensive package, it's hard to beat on cost.

## Inbox-native vs. connector-based sync

The cleanest two-way sync tends to come from CRMs where the inbox is the home base. NetHunt and Copper render inside Gmail itself, so logging is automatic by design; Salesflare runs an always-on capture layer over your mailbox. HubSpot, Pipedrive, and Freshsales use robust connectors that achieve true bidirectional sync but live in their own interface — excellent if you want the CRM as the workspace and the inbox as a feed. Decide which way your team actually works before you pick, because fighting that grain is the fastest route to reps abandoning the sync.

## What to test before committing

In a trial, send an email from your phone's native Gmail or Outlook app — not the CRM — to a test contact, then check whether it appears on the record within a minute. That single test separates genuine two-way sync from one-way BCC logging dressed up in marketing copy. Then send from inside the CRM and confirm it lands in your real Sent folder, so replies thread correctly. A tool that passes both is doing sync properly.

## Bottom line

For hands-off automatic capture, start with Salesflare. For teams that live in Gmail, NetHunt or Copper feel native. HubSpot, Pipedrive, and Freshsales deliver true two-way sync with broader platform value or a lower price. Whatever you choose, verify the bidirectional test above — the gap between real sync and BCC logging is exactly the gap between a CRM that stays current and one that quietly falls behind.
