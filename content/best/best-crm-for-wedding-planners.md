## How we picked

Wedding planning is a high-touch, multi-stakeholder business: each event has a couple, a venue, 8–15 vendors, a budget, and a 6–18-month timeline. The CRMs below were chosen on three criteria: **clientflow ergonomics** (inquiry → contract → invoice in one tool), **vendor and event coordination** (project boards, shared timelines, document sharing), and **payment + contract automation** (legally binding e-signatures and integrated payment processing — most planners can't afford a separate stack).

## What to consider

- **Solo planners** — HoneyBook and Bonsai dominate. Both bundle inquiry forms, brochures, contracts, invoicing, and scheduling into one flow. HoneyBook has the polished templates; Bonsai is cheaper and adds bookkeeping.
- **Small studios (2–10 planners)** — Thryv and vCita add the multi-staff features (assigned events, calendar sharing, team inbox) you need once one person can't see everything. Both integrate scheduling, payments, and marketing.
- **Larger event firms** — Monday is the answer when you're running 50+ concurrent events and need a real project-management backend. Pair it with a contracts/payments tool (HoneyBook for the front office) and you have a system that scales.

## Pricing snapshot

Wedding-planner CRMs cluster around $29–$80/user/mo. HoneyBook is $36/mo (or $29/mo annual) and is the most popular pick. Bonsai is $25/mo and undercuts on price. Thryv and vCita start at $30–$50/mo with feature-tier pricing. Monday is per-user from $9–$24/mo but doesn't include contracts or payments — you'll layer those on top.

## What to skip

Generic sales CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot Sales Hub, Pipedrive) are the wrong shape for wedding planning. They're optimized for B2B sales pipelines, not for service-based businesses with contracts, deposits, and event timelines. You can force-fit them, but you'll spend more on setup than you would on a purpose-built tool. Spreadsheets are also tempting but break the moment you have more than 5 concurrent events.

## Trial advice

Pick two from the list, run them in parallel for one week of new inquiries, and see which one your couples respond to better. The inquiry-to-booked-call experience varies wildly between these tools — the right one for your brand is the one where your inquiry form, brochure, and proposal feel like *your* business, not the software's.
