## Who should leave Lime CRM

Lime CRM earns its place in Scandinavian B2B: pre-built data models for utilities, real estate, wholesale, and membership organizations, plus European data residency and GDPR baked in rather than bolted on. If you're a Nordic company in one of those verticals, the industry templates save real configuration work. But the appeal narrows quickly outside that profile. Pricing starts around €55/user/month and climbs to €170 at the Enterprise tier — steep for a mid-market team — and the ecosystem, partner network, and third-party integrations are thin next to the global CRMs. Support and product momentum are concentrated in Europe, which matters if your team spans other regions.

You should leave if you're outside Lime's core verticals and paying for templates you don't use, if you need a larger app marketplace and integration catalog, or if you want a lower entry price without sacrificing capability. Stay if the utilities/real-estate/membership templates and EU-first compliance posture are doing genuine work for you — that combination is hard to replicate cheaply.

## What to consider

- **Best all-round value** → Zoho CRM. The clearest like-for-like on flexibility and verticalization but far cheaper (from ~$14/user/mo), with EU data centers, a massive app ecosystem, and Zia AI. If Lime's price is the problem, start here.
- **Best for marketing-led B2B** → HubSpot. Best-in-class content, email, and automation on top of a solid CRM core, with EU hosting available. Right when growth runs through inbound and you want sales and marketing in one place.
- **Best for industry-specific + low-code** → Creatio. The most direct replacement for Lime's "configured to your vertical" philosophy — a low-code platform with industry editions you can shape to complex B2B processes without a heavy dev team.
- **Best for enterprise sales depth** → Sugar CRM. Strong forecasting, sales-automation, and on-premise/EU-hosting options for teams that want ownership of their data and a mature, configurable sales platform.
- **Best European SMB alternative** → Teamleader. A European (Belgian) work-management and CRM tool with GDPR-native hosting, built for agencies and SMBs that want quoting and invoicing alongside the pipeline.
- **Best simple pipeline** → Pipedrive. If Lime feels heavier than your sales motion needs, Pipedrive is a fast, visual, sales-first CRM with EU data residency and a gentle learning curve.

## Match the alternative to the gap

Decide what you're actually replacing. If it's the price, Zoho CRM or Pipedrive deliver most of the capability for a fraction of the seat cost. If it's the industry configuration, Creatio and Sugar CRM let you model complex vertical processes without Lime's regional lock-in. If you want to keep a European vendor and GDPR-native hosting, Teamleader is the closest cultural fit for SMBs. And if marketing is the growth engine, HubSpot consolidates that with the CRM.

## Trial advice

The migration risk with a verticalized CRM is losing the pre-built data model that mapped to your industry. Before you switch, export your contacts, companies, and any custom objects, then rebuild your two or three most important workflows in your top pick and run them with live deals for a week. Confirm EU data residency in writing if compliance is why you chose Lime in the first place — most of these alternatives offer it, but the tier and region need checking.
