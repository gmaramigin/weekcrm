## Who should leave Keap

Keap earned its reputation in the 2010s as the most powerful small-business automation platform available — campaign builder, CRM, ecommerce, landing pages, and payments in one package. For service businesses running complex nurture sequences and automated follow-ups, that breadth was worth the learning curve and the price.

In 2026, the landscape has shifted. HubSpot's free CRM and affordable Marketing Hub replicate most of what made Keap valuable, at a lower price and with a far more modern UI. EngageBay, Zoho, and Agile CRM now offer comparable automation suites for under $30/user/month. Keap hasn't stood still — the UI has improved and the automation builder is more accessible — but the value proposition struggles to compete when alternatives offer similar automation depth at one-fifth the monthly spend.

The teams who should stay on Keap are those deeply embedded in its pipeline automation and invoicing workflows, running complex multi-step campaigns that would require significant rebuilding. Everyone else should evaluate whether the renewal cost still makes sense against what the market now offers.

## What to consider

- **Best modern all-in-one** → HubSpot. The most direct replacement for Keap's breadth: CRM, Marketing Hub (email, automation, landing pages), Sales Hub (sequences, meetings), and Service Hub in one platform. The free CRM supports unlimited users, and the jump to Starter ($20/user/month) unlocks real automation. Unlike Keap, HubSpot has no mandatory annual contract lock-in at entry levels, and the product iteration pace has been consistently faster since 2022.
- **Best value for Keap features** → EngageBay. Built specifically as an all-in-one Keap/HubSpot alternative for small businesses that can't afford either: CRM, email marketing, landing pages, web forms, appointment scheduling, live chat, and a helpdesk starting at $14.99/user/month. The automation builder is the closest functional analog to Keap's campaign builder at a fraction of the price. Strong for service businesses, coaches, and agencies that need the full marketing-to-sales-to-support loop.
- **Best for depth and ecosystem** → Zoho CRM. Salesforce-level configurability — custom modules, multi-currency, territory management, workflow rules, Zia AI — at $14–$52/user/month. Pairs naturally with Zoho Marketing Automation, Campaigns, Desk, and Books if you want a Keap-like suite built from best-of-breed components rather than a single platform. Three-user free tier for initial evaluation.
- **Best pure-budget option** → Agile CRM. Genuinely free for up to ten users with CRM, email tracking, appointment scheduling, and landing pages. The paid tiers ($8.99–$47.99/user/month) unlock more automation contacts and campaign features. The UI is dated and support response times lag, but for a team leaving Keap purely on cost grounds, Agile CRM delivers surprising automation depth for the price.
- **Best for local service businesses** → Thryv. Purpose-built for appointment-based and local service businesses — the segment where Keap has historically had the strongest penetration. Business Center Plan includes CRM, appointment booking, text/email campaigns, Google and Facebook review management, and a client portal. Pricing is quote-based but typically lands well below Keap for equivalent local business use cases. Includes phone and support in a way Keap doesn't.
- **Best sales-first replacement** → Pipedrive. For Keap customers who are primarily CRM users and use the marketing automation lightly, Pipedrive delivers excellent pipeline management, email sequences, and activity tracking from $14/user/month. Pair it with Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign for email campaigns and you recover Keap's core workflow at roughly 30% of the cost for most small teams.

## Migrating from Keap

Keap allows data export from Settings → Data Export: contacts, companies, opportunities, notes, and tags all export to CSV. Email templates export individually. Campaign sequences don't export — you'll need to rebuild automations in the new tool, which is frustrating but also an opportunity to simplify workflows that accumulated over years.

The hardest Keap migration assets are its tags (which drive automation logic) and its opportunity pipelines (which tie to payment workflows). Map your tags to the new tool's equivalent before importing contacts — tags without a corresponding field in the destination tool become orphaned data. If Keap is managing invoicing or payments, confirm the new tool's payment integration before committing to the switch; HubSpot and Zoho have native payment options, while EngageBay and Agile CRM rely on Stripe integrations.

## Budget the all-in cost before switching

The most common mistake in leaving Keap is comparing base CRM price to Keap's all-in price. Model what you'll actually use: email sending volume, contact list size, automation complexity, and whether you need a helpdesk or appointment scheduling. EngageBay's All-in-One plans scale on contacts; HubSpot's Marketing Hub scales on contacts and email sends. At 10,000 contacts, both remain below Keap's $299/month Professional tier. At 50,000 contacts, recalculate before assuming the switch saves money. The value capture is real — but only for teams that model it honestly.
