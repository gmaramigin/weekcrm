## How we picked

E-commerce helpdesk selection is primarily an integration question: how deeply does order, customer, and returns data surface in the agent view? We evaluated tools on native Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce integrations, the depth of automation available for WISMO and returns processing, per-ticket and per-agent pricing structures, channel coverage (email, live chat, social, SMS), and the quality of reporting for e-commerce-specific metrics like CSAT by order type, response time, and resolution rate. Tools that require heavy custom development to show order context in tickets were ranked lower regardless of their general helpdesk capabilities.

## What to consider

- **Shopify vs. multi-platform**: If you're 100% on Shopify, Gorgias's native integration is hard to justify replacing. If you run WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento, or a custom stack, the integration quality differences narrow significantly — Freshdesk, Zendesk, and Richpanel all handle these platforms well.
- **Ticket volume and pricing model**: Gorgias charges per ticket (conversations), not per agent. At low volume ($10–50/month for under 350 tickets) this is excellent value; at high volume (thousands of tickets per month) the cost accelerates quickly. Per-agent models like Freshdesk and Zendesk become relatively cheaper as ticket volume scales.
- **Self-service vs. agent-first**: If reducing ticket volume through self-service portals is a priority, Richpanel's customer portal (where shoppers can check status, initiate returns, and edit orders without contacting support) is the most developed option. Gorgias's self-service is improving but thinner.
- **Team size**: Help Scout and Gorgias are fastest to onboard for teams under 10 agents. Zendesk's full power requires more configuration investment, which pays off for teams above 20 agents.

## Pricing snapshot

E-commerce helpdesk pricing ranges from Gorgias at $10/month (basic, up to 50 tickets) to Zendesk Suite at $115+/agent/month. Freshdesk starts at $15/agent/month with a free tier available; Help Scout is $50/month for a 3-agent workspace (roughly $17/agent); Richpanel starts at $29/month. Gorgias's ticket-based pricing is unique — very affordable for small shops, potentially expensive for high-volume operations.

## Gorgias — The Shopify-native standard

Gorgias was built specifically for e-commerce, and its Shopify integration reflects that depth. Every ticket automatically surfaces the customer's order history, current order status, tracking details, and previous support interactions — without any configuration. Agents can issue refunds, edit orders, cancel subscriptions, and apply discount codes directly from the ticket interface without ever opening the Shopify admin. WISMO automations handle the most common inquiry type out of the box: a customer asks about their order, Gorgias detects the intent, pulls the tracking number, and responds automatically. The channel coverage is comprehensive — email, live chat, social (Instagram DMs, Facebook), SMS, and WhatsApp. Pricing runs $10–900/month based on ticket volume tiers. The ceiling is real: high-volume stores sending 5,000+ tickets per month will find costs escalate, but for most direct-to-consumer Shopify brands, this is the clearest starting point.

## Freshdesk — Best per-agent value with broad platform support

Freshdesk's $15–79/agent/month pricing (with a free 10-agent tier) makes it the most cost-effective helpdesk in this list for growing e-commerce teams. Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce integrations are available through the Freshdesk marketplace, surfacing order data and customer history in the ticket sidebar — not as deep as Gorgias's native integration, but functional for most workflows. Freshdesk's automation capabilities are strong: SLA rules, round-robin assignment, canned responses, and scenario automations handle routine ticket processing without manual work. The AI-powered Freddy agent (higher tiers) handles WISMO and returns queries automatically. For e-commerce businesses on platforms other than Shopify, or teams that have outgrown Gorgias's ticket-based pricing model, Freshdesk delivers excellent per-agent value with enough e-commerce-specific functionality to satisfy most needs.

Learn more at [/vendors/freshdesk](/vendors/freshdesk).

## Zendesk — Best for large e-commerce teams

Zendesk is the enterprise-grade option in this category — more configuration investment upfront, but the deepest capabilities at scale. Its AI features (Zendesk AI, intelligent triage, suggested responses) reduce agent handle time meaningfully for teams processing thousands of tickets per day. The Shopify and WooCommerce integrations are solid, and Zendesk's marketplace has hundreds of e-commerce-specific integrations — Loop Returns, Narvar, Klaviyo, Yotpo, and more. The reporting and analytics suite is the most comprehensive in this roundup, supporting custom dashboards, SLA tracking, and customer satisfaction trending at the granularity large operations need. At $55–115/agent/month, it's significantly more expensive than Freshdesk or Help Scout, and the complexity requires a dedicated admin or careful setup. For e-commerce brands with 20+ support agents or complex multi-channel operations, it's the most scalable choice.

Learn more at [/vendors/zendesk](/vendors/zendesk).

## Help Scout — Best for DTC brands focused on email support

Help Scout takes a different philosophy from the other tools in this list: it's built around email support done exceptionally well, with a clean interface that feels like a shared inbox rather than a ticketing system. For direct-to-consumer brands where the majority of support volume is email, and where the brand voice and personal touch of communication matters, Help Scout's approach resonates. Shopify integration surfaces order data in the conversation sidebar; Beacon provides live chat and self-service help docs. The reporting is solid for team performance and happiness monitoring. At $50/month for a workspace (up to 3 users, then $20/user after), the per-agent math is attractive for small teams. Help Scout is the right choice for brands that want their support to feel human and personal, not ticket-queue transactional.

Learn more at [/vendors/help-scout](/vendors/help-scout).

## Richpanel — Best self-service portal with CX analytics

Richpanel positions itself as a Gorgias alternative with stronger self-service capabilities. Its customer portal lets shoppers check order status, initiate returns, cancel subscriptions, and edit delivery details without contacting an agent — the goal is to deflect tickets before they're created rather than resolving them faster. Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce integrations are native. The CX analytics dashboard tracks metrics that e-commerce teams actually care about: revenue recovered through support interactions, deflection rate, first contact resolution, and agent efficiency. Pricing starts at $29/month, making it competitive with Gorgias at lower volumes. For e-commerce brands where reducing ticket volume through self-service is the primary ROI driver, Richpanel's portal-first approach is meaningfully differentiated from the rest of the field.

## Trial advice

The most important test for any e-commerce helpdesk is the order context experience. Place a test order in your store, then submit a support inquiry from that customer's email address. Verify that the agent sees order status, tracking number, items purchased, and previous support history in the ticket view — without navigating away or running a manual lookup. Then test your highest-volume automation: set up a WISMO rule and confirm it fires correctly. For Gorgias specifically, calculate your expected monthly ticket volume and price that against per-agent pricing from competitors — the crossover point is typically around 2,000–3,000 tickets per month depending on team size. Finally, test mobile responsiveness for your agents; a meaningful share of e-commerce support happens outside business hours.
