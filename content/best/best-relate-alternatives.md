## Who should leave Relate

Relate is a genuinely nice tool for a specific moment: a founder or a two-to-three-person team doing their first structured CRM, who want something that feels as fast as a spreadsheet and don't want to think about configuration. The free personal plan is real, not a demo, and the Gmail/Outlook sync plus Cycles keep early pipeline moving without hiring an SDR. If you're in that phase and the free tier covers you, stay — the simplicity is the point, and trading up too early just adds overhead you'll ignore.

Leaving makes sense on two fronts. The first is depth: Relate deliberately skips advanced reporting, forecasting, and heavy automation, so a team whose sales process is getting real — multiple pipelines, handoffs, revenue targets — will feel the walls. The second is money. Team Business at $60/user/mo (or $51 annually) is steep for a startup CRM, and because enterprise and marketing features are separate add-ons, the sticker price understates what a growing team actually pays. When you're paying near-premium prices for a deliberately-limited product, you're better served by a tool that gives you more for the same or less.

## What to consider

- **Best modern upgrade with real depth** → Attio. The natural next home for Relate fans — same spreadsheet-fast feel, but with auto-enrichment, custom objects, real-time collaboration, and AI, from $29/user/mo and a usable free plan. Half Relate's team price with far more room.
- **Best for relationship-led work** → Folk. If you're managing investors, partners, and network rather than a deal pipeline, folk's one-click LinkedIn (folkX) and Gmail capture is faster than Relate, from $20/user/mo.
- **Best true sales pipeline** → Pipedrive. When the reason you're leaving is that Relate can't run a maturing sales motion, Pipedrive's visual pipeline, automation, and 300+ integrations start at $14/user/mo — cheaper and more capable for pure selling.
- **Best room to grow into marketing** → HubSpot. A genuinely free CRM with unlimited users, then a path into email, forms, and service as you scale — the safe long-term platform if you expect to add marketing later.
- **Best for outbound teams** → Close. If your startup's growth is phone- and email-driven, Close bundles a power dialer, SMS, and sequences natively from $19/user/mo — the automation Relate's Cycles only gestures at.
- **Best low-maintenance auto-CRM** → Salesflare. Keeps Relate's "don't make me log things" ethos but goes further, auto-capturing contacts, meetings, and emails from Gmail/Outlook/LinkedIn, from $29/user/mo.

## Match the alternative to the gap

Relate sits at the intersection of "modern and minimal" and "for startups," so the right replacement depends on which half you're keeping. If you loved the modern, fast, flexible feel and just want it to do more, Attio is the direct heir — you'll recognize the spreadsheet-like interface immediately, but you get custom objects, enrichment, and AI that Relate withholds by design, at less than half the seat price. If what you actually do is relationship management rather than pipeline management, don't buy a sales CRM at all: Folk is built for network-led work and its LinkedIn capture will feel like an upgrade, not a lateral move.

If you're leaving specifically because your sales process got serious, the decision is between simplicity and reach: Pipedrive gives you a clean pipeline with proper automation and reporting, while HubSpot's free tier buys you optionality if marketing is coming next. Migration is straightforward from Relate — export contacts and deals to CSV, and because Relate leans on Gmail/Outlook sync, reconnecting the same inbox to Attio, Salesflare, or Folk rebuilds most of your activity history automatically. Pick the tool that matches the workflow you're growing into, not the one that looks most like Relate.
