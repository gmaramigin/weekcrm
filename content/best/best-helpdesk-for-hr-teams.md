## How we picked

An HR helpdesk is doing two unrelated jobs at once, and the tension between them drives every selection decision. The first job is high-volume, low-sensitivity questions: how much PTO do I have, when does open enrollment close, how do I submit an expense. Those want fast intake, aggressive self-service, and automation. The second job is confidential case management: relations complaints, accommodation requests, leave administration, investigations. Those want restricted access, documentation, and an audit trail — and they want the opposite of speed and openness.

So we weighted **case-level confidentiality** first and hardest. A tool where the HR helpdesk queue is visible to the whole HR team, or worse to shared IT admins, is disqualifying for the second job no matter how good it is at the first. We looked for per-case access restriction with logged access, not just role-based permissions on a queue.

Second, we weighted **workflow depth for employee lifecycle events**. Onboarding and offboarding are the processes where HR tooling most obviously pays for itself, and they are cross-functional by nature. Third, we weighted **intake where employees already are** — Slack or Teams for most modern companies, a portal for larger and more process-formal ones. A perfect HR service catalog that employees route around by DMing the People Ops lead has not solved anything, which is the specific reason Unthread earns a place on a list otherwise full of service management platforms.

## What to prioritize

- **Per-case access control with an audit log.** Investigators see their case; nobody else does; every access is recorded. Demand a live demonstration during the trial. This is the requirement most likely to be glossed over in a sales cycle and the one most likely to matter in a deposition.
- **Onboarding and offboarding as automated multi-team workflows.** One trigger, tasks fanned out to HR, IT, payroll, and facilities with owners and due dates, and a single status view. Offboarding especially — access revocation on a deadline is a security control, not an HR nicety.
- **A knowledge base employees can search without asking.** Policy documents, benefits summaries, leave rules, and expense guidelines, with an AI agent answering from them. The majority of HR helpdesk volume is a question already answered in a PDF nobody can find, and deflecting it frees HR to do the work that actually requires judgment.
- **Intake in Slack or Teams, not only a portal.** Meet employees where they are. Unthread converts Slack threads into tracked tickets with no behavior change; Freshservice and monday service both offer chat intake as well. Portal-only intake reliably produces shadow queues in someone's DMs.
- **A service catalog with approval routing.** Equipment requests, role changes, training budget, and leave requests each need a defined form, an approver, and an SLA. Freshservice's catalog is the cleanest in this group; Wavity is the answer if you want to build custom HR workflows without engineering support.
- **Reporting HR can defend to leadership.** Volume by category, time to resolution, and repeat-question analysis. The repeat-question report is the most useful of the three, because it tells you exactly which policy to document better and turns a support metric into a communications fix.
