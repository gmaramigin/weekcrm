## How we picked

"Lead capture" only matters if the lead lands as a usable record without a human re-typing it. We weighted CRMs by how many capture surfaces they own natively — web forms, landing pages, pop-ups, chat widgets, and prospecting extensions — and by whether captured leads arrive with source attribution and routing already attached. A CRM that needs three Zapier hops to turn a form fill into a contact didn't make the cut.

## What to consider

- **Inbound web traffic** → HubSpot or EngageBay. Both bundle forms, pop-ups, and landing pages into the free tier, so a marketing site can feed the CRM on day one.
- **Lowest cost with real depth** → Zoho CRM. Web-to-lead forms, auto-assignment rules, and lead scoring start at $14/user/mo, with a free tier for up to 3 users.
- **Outbound prospecting** → Breakcold. The LinkedIn Chrome extension and unified social inbox capture leads from outreach, not just from your website.
- **Pipeline-first sales teams** → Pipedrive, but only with LeadBooster. The core CRM is excellent at moving deals; the capture tooling is a paid add-on you should price in.

## Pricing snapshot

HubSpot and EngageBay both give away capture forms in genuinely free tiers — HubSpot for unlimited users, EngageBay for up to 15. Zoho CRM is the value pick once you need rules and scoring, at $14–$52/user/mo. Breakcold runs a flat $29/mo and up for the outbound use case. Pipedrive starts at $14/user/mo but the LeadBooster add-on that supplies forms and chat is billed separately, so model the real combined seat cost before committing.

## Trial advice

Test capture, not the demo data. Embed each CRM's form on a staging page, submit a few leads through it, and watch what actually lands: did the contact get a source tag, a lifecycle stage, and an owner automatically? The CRM that turns a raw form fill into a routed, attributed record with zero manual cleanup is the one that will save your team real time.
