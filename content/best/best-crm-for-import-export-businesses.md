## How we picked

Import/export businesses face CRM requirements that most SMB tools weren't designed for: **deals measured in months or years**, **contacts split across buyer and supplier companies on multiple continents**, **multi-currency quoting**, and **documentation workflows** (certificates of origin, compliance letters, freight documents) that need to live alongside contact records. The CRMs on this list were selected for genuine multi-currency support, strong account-centric data models, and pipelines flexible enough to represent trade cycles rather than the 30-day SaaS sales cycles most CRMs optimize for.

## What to consider

- **Trading company that needs CRM + inventory + accounting in one ecosystem** → Zoho CRM. The Zoho suite covers the widest surface area: multi-currency, territory management, Zoho Inventory, Zoho Books, and Zoho Analytics give you a connected stack without enterprise pricing. The best all-around pick for most import/export SMBs.
- **Team that also needs project management, document sharing, and a built-in contact center** → Bitrix24. Unusually broad for the price — CRM pipelines, task boards, group chat, and telephony in one platform. Ideal when your team coordinates across time zones and needs more than just a deal pipeline.
- **Importer or exporter with a clean, high-volume buyer pipeline and a need for disciplined follow-up** → Pipedrive. The most intuitive pipeline view of any CRM on this list. Multi-currency is supported from Advanced; the visual deal board makes it easy to see where every buyer stands in a long cycle.
- **Trading company that runs outbound marketing alongside relationship management** → HubSpot. Strong email sequences, contact scoring, and reporting — best when you're marketing to a large buyer list (trade show leads, industry directories) and need to nurture prospects over months before they're ready to order.
- **Large trading house or distributor that needs ERP-adjacent CRM with deep compliance and reporting** → Microsoft Dynamics. The enterprise choice — integrates with finance, operations, and supply chain modules. Justified when your annual revenue and team size make the licensing cost worthwhile.
- **Trading SMB that wants a fully customizable CRM with inventory and service management hooks** → Vtiger. Strong custom module builder lets you add HS-code fields, Incoterms, and port-of-loading data without developer help. Its unified CRM (sales + support + marketing in one) suits trading companies that also manage after-sale service.

## International trade workflow considerations

The signature challenge in import/export CRM is the **dual relationship model**: you're simultaneously managing inbound supplier relationships (keeping factories and sourcing agents warm, tracking sample approval cycles) and outbound buyer relationships (pitching retailers, distributors, and wholesalers on your product catalog). A single-pipeline CRM creates confusion — build separate pipelines for each flow and link supplier and buyer records where the same commodity connects them.

**Trade shows** are a major lead source for import/export teams. Configure your CRM to capture business card data (mobile app scanning), tag leads by show and product category, and trigger a seven-day follow-up sequence automatically. HubSpot and Zoho CRM both support this out of the box; Bitrix24 does it through its built-in marketing module.

Multi-currency reporting matters more than most teams realize at the start. When deals span six months and your home currency moves against the deal currency, the CRM needs to record both the contract amount in foreign currency and the functional-currency equivalent at close. Only Zoho, Dynamics, and Bitrix24 handle this cleanly without spreadsheet gymnastics.

## Trial advice

Test your finalists with a realistic scenario: create a supplier account (factory in Shenzhen), a buyer account (retailer in Germany), a deal in EUR linked to the buyer, a deal stage that represents "sample approved," and a follow-up task 30 days out. The CRM that lets you model that cycle accurately — with the right currency on the deal and a clear activity log — is the one worth paying for.
