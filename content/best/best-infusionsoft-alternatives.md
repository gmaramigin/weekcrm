## Why look for an Infusionsoft alternative

Infusionsoft built its reputation on doing everything — CRM, email marketing, automation, e-commerce, and landing pages in one platform. That ambition is also its weakness. The product earned a reputation for being so hard to learn that a cottage industry of "Infusionsoft Certified Partners" exists just to set it up for you. The mandatory Kickstart onboarding fee, the dense interface, and pricing that climbs as your list grows push a lot of small businesses to look elsewhere once they've outgrown the honeymoon.

Keap (the rebrand) fixed some of this, but if you're starting fresh, several tools now deliver the same all-in-one promise with far less friction.

## How we picked

We weighted four things Infusionsoft users care about most: automation depth (you don't want to downgrade your workflows), total cost including onboarding and contact-based pricing, time-to-first-campaign, and whether marketing and sales actually live in one place. Every pick below gets you sending automated sequences in days, not weeks of paid consulting.

## What to consider

- **You like Infusionsoft's power but hate the UI** → **Keap**. It's literally the same company's modern product — visual automation builder, native sales pipeline, and built-in payments — but rebuilt to be usable without a certified consultant.
- **You want room to scale with a free start** → **HubSpot**. Its free CRM is the most generous in the category, and you can layer on Marketing Hub when you're ready. The trade-off is the Starter-to-Professional price jump, but you control the timing.
- **You want the all-in-one feature set for a fraction of the price** → **EngageBay**. It's the closest direct replacement: marketing automation, CRM, helpdesk, and landing pages bundled, often at 70–80% less than Infusionsoft's equivalent tier.
- **You want the best overall value** → **Zoho CRM**. Pair it with Zoho's marketing tools and you get a deep, customizable suite for a price that's hard to beat — ideal if you're already in (or willing to adopt) the Zoho ecosystem.
- **You mostly need sales automation, not marketing** → **Salesmate**. If half of Infusionsoft sat unused, Salesmate gives you sequences, a built-in dialer, and pipeline automation without paying for a marketing suite you don't run.

## Bottom line

Stay on Infusionsoft/Keap if its automation depth is the core of your business and you've already invested in learning it. Otherwise, switch to **EngageBay** for the cheapest all-in-one match, **HubSpot** if you want a free runway and room to grow, **Zoho CRM** for raw value, or **Salesmate** if you only ever used the sales half. Move your three most important automations first and confirm they fire correctly before migrating the rest.
