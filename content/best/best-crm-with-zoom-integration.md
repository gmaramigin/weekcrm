## How we picked

A Zoom integration is only worth it if it removes steps. We judged each CRM on whether reps can schedule and join Zoom meetings from a record, whether the meeting and any recording or transcript log back automatically, and how reliable the sync is in daily use. Bonus weight went to conversation intelligence — turning a call into searchable, summarized deal context.

## What to consider

- **Best overall Zoom integration** → HubSpot. Schedule from the contact card, auto-attach the link, log the meeting, and capture recordings with conversation intelligence on higher tiers.
- **Deepest enterprise tie-in** → Salesforce. Native Zoom and Zoom Phone integration, with Einstein Conversation Insights mining transcripts for deal signals at scale.
- **Best for simple scheduling** → Pipedrive. Clean meeting scheduling with Zoom links generated automatically and activity logged against the deal — no setup overhead.
- **Best value** → Zoho CRM. Native Zoom Meeting support lets you create and join meetings inside the CRM, all at Zoho's characteristically low price point.
- **Best unified calls and video** → Close. Built for inside sales, Close keeps dialing and video in one workspace so reps switch channels without switching tools.

## Pricing snapshot

Pipedrive and Zoho deliver Zoom scheduling on plans from roughly $14–$23/seat. HubSpot includes meeting tools on Starter but reserves conversation intelligence for Sales Pro and above. Salesforce's deepest Zoom and Einstein features live on Enterprise tiers near $165/seat. Remember the Zoom license is a separate line item — recording and transcription depend on a paid Zoom plan.

## Trial advice

Run a real sales call through each finalist during the trial. Schedule a Zoom from a record, take the meeting, and check what lands back in the CRM afterward — link, notes, recording, transcript. The integration that fills the deal timeline without a single copy-paste is the one your reps will actually keep using. Test with two CRMs side by side.
