## Why look for a Cloze alternative

Cloze's signature trick is automation: it quietly pulls together your email, calls, messages, and contact history, then nudges you about relationships you've gone quiet on. For people who manage a large web of professional and personal connections, that ambient intelligence is genuinely valuable. The catch is breadth — Cloze also does deals, projects, and content tracking, and stuffing all of it into one app can make the experience feel dense and busy.

Most people who leave aren't unhappy with the idea; they want a lighter, more focused version of it. Some want a stripped-down personal CRM that only does stay-in-touch reminders. Others want a more modern interface, deeper social enrichment, or tighter integration with the Google Workspace they already live in.

## What to consider

- **A minimalist personal CRM** → **Dex**. It does one thing extremely well — helping you keep up with people — with reminders and notes and none of the deal-pipeline weight Cloze carries.
- **Social-enriched contacts** → **Nimble**. It builds richer contact profiles from social and web data and adds light team and pipeline features, a good middle ground between personal and sales CRM.
- **Network-driven relationship selling** → **folk**. Contact-first and modern, with one-click LinkedIn capture, folk fits founders and agencies whose pipeline really is a relationship graph.
- **A flexible, modern foundation** → **Attio**. AI-native and as malleable as a spreadsheet, it suits people who want to design exactly how their relationships and records are structured.
- **Google Workspace-native work** → **Copper**. It lives inside Gmail and Google Calendar, automatically logging interactions — ideal for relationship-led teams who never want to leave their inbox.

## Bottom line

Stay on Cloze if its automatic, do-everything tracking is exactly what you need and the density doesn't bother you. Otherwise, choose **Dex** for pure stay-in-touch simplicity, **Nimble** for enriched contacts, **folk** for LinkedIn-driven relationship selling, **Attio** for a flexible modern model, or **Copper** if your life runs through Gmail. Import a slice of your contacts and try the auto-tracking firsthand before you switch.
