## How we picked

Contact management lives or dies on data quality and how little effort it takes to keep records current. We prioritized automatic enrichment, deduplication, flexible tagging and segmentation, and how naturally the CRM captures interactions from email and calendar. A great contact CRM should feel like a living address book that updates itself, not a spreadsheet you have to babysit.

## What to consider

- **Best overall contact CRM** → folk. Contacts sync from Gmail, Outlook, and LinkedIn, enrich themselves, and live in a clean, fast interface that teams actually enjoy using.
- **Best relationship intelligence** → Nimble. It builds a unified profile from social signals and email, surfacing context and engagement history before every conversation.
- **Best free contact database** → HubSpot. Store up to a million contacts for free, with timeline tracking that logs every email, call, and meeting automatically.
- **Best simple, tag-driven contacts** → Capsule. Lightweight, affordable, and built around tags and lists — perfect for teams that want organization without complexity.
- **Best value for growing teams** → Zoho CRM. Deep contact customization, custom fields, and segmentation at a price point that undercuts most rivals.

## Pricing snapshot

Capsule and Zoho anchor the low end, with usable plans around $9–$18/seat. folk and Nimble sit in the $20–$30 range, where the enrichment and inbox-sync features justify the step up. HubSpot remains the outlier with a powerful free contact tier, though enrichment credits and marketing tools cost extra once you scale. Watch for per-record enrichment limits across all five.

## Trial advice

Test a contact CRM with your real mess, not a clean sample. Import a few hundred genuine contacts — duplicates, half-finished profiles, and all — then see how well the CRM dedupes, enriches, and surfaces context. The winner is the one that turns a chaotic list into something you trust within an afternoon. Give two finalists the same import and compare.
