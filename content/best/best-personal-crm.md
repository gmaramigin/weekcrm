## How we picked

Personal CRMs win or lose on one thing: whether they keep working when you forget about them. We weighted **passive data capture** (does it pull contacts and history from your inbox, calendar, and LinkedIn without manual entry?), **keep-in-touch intelligence** (does it remind you to reconnect before a relationship goes stale?), and **single-user pricing** that makes sense for one person spending their own money. Pipeline features, team seats, and sales reporting were explicitly downranked — they signal a tool built for quota, not for relationships.

## What to consider

- **Networkers and founders** — you want LinkedIn sync and reconnection nudges. **Dex** is purpose-built for this; **Mesh** is the free equivalent.
- **People drowning in email threads** — pick **Cloze**, which assembles a complete per-contact timeline automatically from your messages and meetings.
- **Anyone who researches people before a call** — **Nimble** enriches profiles with social and company data as you browse the web.
- **Small relationship-led teams (2–5)** — **Folk** lets a few people share one network with light groups and reminders, bridging personal and team use.
- **Privacy-conscious users** — check what each tool ingests; full-inbox sync (Cloze, Dex) trades privacy for convenience.

## Pricing snapshot

This category is cheap by CRM standards. **Mesh** is free to 1,000 contacts (Pro $10/mo). **Dex** starts at $12/mo, **Cloze** at $17/mo, and **Nimble** at $24.90/user/mo. **Folk** has a free plan and paid tiers from $20/mo. None require an annual enterprise contract to be useful.

## Staying in touch on purpose

The feature that separates a personal CRM from a contacts app is the reconnection loop. **Dex** lets you assign people to keep-in-touch cadences — monthly, quarterly, yearly — and surfaces who's overdue each morning. **Mesh** does the same with timely prompts rather than rigid schedules. If you only adopt one habit, make it tagging your 50 most important people with a cadence; the software handles the rest, and the relationship debt you've been carrying quietly clears itself.
