## How we picked

Bakeries are two businesses in one: a retail counter and a custom/wholesale operation. The CRM matters for the second. A wedding cake, a catering platter order, or a café's weekly bread standing order all follow the same loop — inquiry, quote, deposit, delivery date, follow-up. We prioritized order-intake forms, deposit collection, scheduling, wholesale account tracking, and event follow-up automation. A point-of-sale handles the counter; the CRM handles everything that gets promised in advance.

## What to consider

- **You want one tool for orders, deposits, and reminders** → **vcita**. Custom-order request forms, card deposits, and appointment/delivery reminders in a single app — the simplest all-in-one for a custom bakery.
- **You want local marketing and reviews** → **Thryv**. Adds reputation management, campaigns, and listing sync so your bakery ranks and looks good in local search around holidays and wedding season.
- **Weddings and events are your bread and butter** → **HoneyBook**. Branded quotes, contracts, and deposits with a polished client experience — built for the higher-ticket, planned-in-advance event work.
- **You need a free way to manage wholesale accounts** → **Bitrix24**. A generous free CRM that can track wholesale clients, standing orders, and reorders — good for a bakery supplying cafés and restaurants on a budget.
- **You run everything through QuickBooks** → **Method:CRM**. Deep two-way QuickBooks sync ties orders, invoices, and customers together — ideal for a bakery that lives in QuickBooks and wants its CRM to match.

## Pricing snapshot

Bakery-friendly CRMs range from free (Bitrix24) to about $25–$50/month for all-in-one tools with payments. Watch for payment-processing fees on deposits and, if you pick Method:CRM, confirm your QuickBooks edition is supported.

## Trial advice

Test two with a real order: take a custom-cake inquiry through quote and deposit, and log one wholesale account with a reorder. Keep the one that captures orders without you re-typing them into QuickBooks or a spreadsheet — for a bakery, the fewer places an order has to be entered, the fewer that get dropped.
