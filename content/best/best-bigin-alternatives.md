## Who should leave Bigin by Zoho
Bigin is Zoho's answer to the "my spreadsheet has become a mess" problem. It is a deliberately small CRM aimed at solopreneurs and micro-teams, with a clean pipeline view, Toppings (mini add-ons), and a famously friendly price: a free single-user tier, then $9/user/month (Express) and $15 (Premier) on annual billing. For someone graduating from sticky notes to their first real CRM, it is one of the easiest, cheapest on-ramps available.

But Bigin is intentionally entry-level, and that ceiling arrives sooner than people expect. Customization is capped — limited fields, modules, and pipelines compared with full CRMs — and advanced reporting, deep automation, and marketing simply aren't part of the design. The temptation is to "graduate" upward into Zoho CRM proper, but that means a heavier tool and a different learning curve. As your team grows, hires reps, or starts wanting forecasting, sophisticated workflows, or multi-channel outreach, Bigin starts to feel like training wheels you have outgrown. If you are bumping into its limits on customization, scale, or features — and you would rather evaluate the whole market than auto-upgrade within Zoho — it is the right moment to compare.

## What to consider
- **Best for a natural pipeline step-up** → Pipedrive. Pipedrive offers the same easy pipeline feel with far more customization, automation, and forecasting headroom. Plans run $19/user/month (Essential), $34 (Advanced), $49 (Professional), and $79 (Power), so you can grow without re-platforming.
- **Best for staying simple but flexible** → Capsule. Capsule keeps Bigin's approachability while adding custom fields, multiple pipelines, and basic reporting, free for two users and then $18/user/month (Starter), $36 (Growth), and $54 (Advanced).
- **Best for built-in phone and AI** → Freshsales. Freshsales packs AI lead scoring, native calling, and proper dashboards into a still-affordable package, with a free tier and paid plans at $9/user/month (Growth), $39 (Pro), and $59 (Enterprise).
- **Best for adding marketing** → Nutshell. Nutshell pairs pipeline management with email sequences and broadcast marketing that Bigin doesn't attempt, ideal once you want to nurture as well as close. Pricing is $13/user/month (Foundation), $42 (Growth), $59 (Pro), and $79 (Business).
- **Best for an all-in-one platform** → HubSpot. If you expect to need marketing, sales, and service together, HubSpot's free CRM and unified suite give you room to scale for years. Sales Hub starts at $20/seat/month and runs to Professional at $100/seat.
- **Best for a contact-first lightweight CRM** → folk. folk is a clean, modern CRM that syncs contacts across Gmail, Outlook, and LinkedIn, good for relationship-led teams who find even Bigin a touch transactional. Plans are $20/user/month (Standard), $40 (Premium), and $80 (Custom).

## Match the alternative to the gap
First ask whether you want to leave the Zoho universe at all. If your only frustration is Bigin's ceiling and you like the ecosystem, the honest comparison is Bigin versus Zoho CRM versus a fresh start — but most teams who reach this page want options outside that box. If so, Pipedrive is the cleanest lateral move: same intuitive pipeline, far more room before you hit a wall, no jarring relearn. Capsule and Freshsales are the picks if you want more power while keeping costs near Bigin's, with Freshsales pulling ahead when you need phone and AI baked in.

If the limit you are hitting is really about capability beyond sales — marketing campaigns, service, a single source of truth — then size up deliberately. HubSpot is the long-horizon platform play, Nutshell the lighter way to add marketing without a full migration, and folk the choice for small teams who want something warmer and more contact-centric than Bigin's deal-first layout. The goal is to skip your next migration, not just your last one.

## Trial advice
Map your growth, not just today. List the two or three things Bigin couldn't do that pushed you here — more pipelines, real forecasting, a marketing email — and confirm each finalist handles them on the plan you can actually afford, not on its top tier. Import your real contacts and a few open deals, run a live week, and watch onboarding speed closely, because Bigin's whole appeal was being easy to start. The right alternative should feel nearly as quick to adopt while leaving obvious headroom above you, so you are not repeating this same search in a year.
