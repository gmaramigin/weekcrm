## How we picked

Window tinting is a booking-and-quote business. A customer calls or messages, you quote a car or a set of windows, you lock a slot, you collect a deposit, and you install. The right CRM has to run that loop from a phone — because you're in a bay or a driveway, not at a desk. We prioritized online booking, mobile quoting, deposit collection, automated reminders, and review requests. Deep sales pipelines and forecasting don't matter here; showing up and getting paid does.

## What to consider

- **You want one tool for booking, quoting, and payments** → **vcita**. Clients book online, you quote from your phone, and you collect a card deposit before the job — the tightest all-in-one for a small tint shop.
- **You also want marketing and reviews** → **Thryv**. Adds reputation management, email/SMS campaigns, and a business listing sync on top of scheduling — good if you're competing on Google visibility.
- **You want a polished quote-to-deposit flow** → **HoneyBook**. Branded quotes, contracts, and deposits in a clean client-facing flow — ideal for higher-ticket residential and commercial film jobs.
- **Lead follow-up is your leak** → **Keap**. Automates the text-and-email follow-up sequence so no quote goes cold, with a built-in pipeline for bigger commercial bids.
- **Most leads come by DM** → **Kommo**. Runs sales through WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook Messenger — the right pick if customers find you on social and expect to book by message.

## Pricing snapshot

Service-business CRMs for tint shops run roughly $20–$50/user/month for the tier that includes booking and payments. Watch for payment-processing fees on deposits and for SMS credits, which are usually billed separately from the base plan.

## Trial advice

Pick two, then run a real week: quote three jobs, take two deposits, and send the review requests from each. Keep the one you can drive entirely from your phone between installs — for a tint shop, mobile speed beats every other feature.
