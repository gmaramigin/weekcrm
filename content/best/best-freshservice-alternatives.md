## Who should leave Freshservice

Freshservice does a specific thing well: it gives a mid-size IT team proper incident management, an auto-updating CMDB, and Freddy AI deflection without the ServiceNow implementation tax. Starter runs $19/agent/month, Growth $49, Pro $99 — clean and predictable until you need the full AI feature set, which lives on a custom-quoted Enterprise plan. That opacity, plus a per-agent model that gets expensive as the service desk grows, is where the cracks usually show.

You should leave if you've hit one of three walls. Either you've outgrown Freshservice and need ServiceNow-grade process maturity, audit trails, and CMDB depth at enterprise scale — or you want the same ITIL structure for less and resent paying Freshworks rates for it. The third wall is scope: Freshservice is built for internal IT and employee service, so if your real problem is endpoint patching, managing client environments as an MSP, or asset discovery on a shoestring, you don't need a different help desk — you need a different category of tool. Teams happy with the Freddy AI deflection and multi-department ESM should stay put.

## What to consider

- **Best for ITIL without the ServiceNow bill** → Vivantio. The closest like-for-like replacement: incident, problem, change, release, and asset management with visually configurable workflows, from $59/month per agent. The move when you want Freshservice's structure but more workflow flexibility at a lower, more transparent price.
- **Best for enterprise scale** → ServiceNow. When you need thousands of fulfillers, deep CMDB dependency mapping, and auditable ITIL process maturity, this is the market leader. Budget honestly — quotes run from ~$100/fulfiller/month, contracts often start around $130k, and implementation typically costs 3–5x the license.
- **Best for cross-department service management** → Halo Service Solutions. HaloITSM, HaloPSA, and HaloCRM share one data model, so IT, facilities, HR, and customer support stop living in silos. Pricing is quote-only, but the unified platform replaces several contracts at once for organizations running service across many teams.
- **Best for endpoint and asset operations** → NinjaOne. Freshservice tracks assets in a CMDB; NinjaOne actually manages them — automated patching, real-time monitoring, and remote access across Windows, macOS, and Linux at roughly $2–3.75/device/month. The pick when your pain is keeping the fleet patched, not routing tickets.
- **Best for MSPs** → SuperOps. If you manage client environments, a help desk isn't enough — you need PSA plus RMM. SuperOps unifies ticketing, monitoring, patching, and billing with per-technician pricing (PSA from $89/tech/month; PSA + RMM from $149) that stays predictable as device counts climb.
- **Best on zero budget** → Spiceworks. Free, ad-supported, on-premises: automatic LAN device discovery, network monitoring, and an integrated help desk with tickets tied to assets. A dated UI and Windows dependency are the trade-offs, but for a solo admin with no budget it covers discovery and ticketing for nothing.

## Match the alternative to the gap

The wrong instinct is to shop for "Freshservice but cheaper." Name the actual constraint first. If it's price and you still want real ITIL processes, Vivantio is the direct swap. If it's ceiling — you've genuinely outgrown mid-market ITSM — only ServiceNow clears that bar, cost included.

If the constraint is scope, change categories. A unified IT-plus-business service desk points to Halo. Endpoint fleet management points to NinjaOne. Running a managed-services business points to SuperOps. And no budget at all points to Spiceworks. Freshservice's CMDB and deflection are good; people leave for what it deliberately doesn't do, not for a marginally nicer ticket queue.

## Trial advice

Most of these run a free trial or are free outright, so test against your real work, not a demo dataset. Import a slice of live assets and a week of actual tickets into your top two finalists, then watch where each one stalls — workflow setup, CMDB sync, agent onboarding. Weigh total cost honestly: per-agent (Vivantio, ServiceNow), per-device (NinjaOne), and per-technician (SuperOps) models diverge fast at scale, so model your headcount and fleet before your next Freshservice renewal lands.
