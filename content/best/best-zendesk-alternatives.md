## Who should leave Zendesk

Zendesk is the safe choice for scaling support: broad channel coverage, mature triggers and macros, a 1,000+ app marketplace, and the Explore analytics product. The catch is total cost of ownership. The Suite starts at $55/agent/month, but real bills routinely land 2–3x higher once AI add-ons, Explore, and premium features are layered in. Configuration complexity grows fast — getting full value usually means dedicated admin time — and smaller teams frequently find the platform over-engineered for the volume they actually handle.

You should leave if your invoice has crept far past the base rate, if you're paying for breadth your team never uses, or if Zendesk's ticket-queue model fights the way you actually talk to customers. The teams who should *stay* are large support organizations that need every channel, deep SLA and routing automation, and a mature ecosystem — and that have the admin capacity to run it. At high volume with complex operations, Zendesk's depth still earns its keep.

## What to consider

- **Best general-purpose value** → Freshdesk. The most direct like-for-like swap: multi-channel ticketing, SLA management, automation, and a knowledge base, with a free plan and paid tiers from $15/agent/month. Familiar Zendesk-style model at a noticeably lower cost.
- **Best for a human, simple feel** → Help Scout. Built to feel like email, not a ticket queue, so onboarding takes hours instead of days. Free for up to five users, paid from $25/user/month, with an AI assistant that resolves ~70% of routine requests. Ideal for teams that find Zendesk cold and over-built.
- **Best for ecommerce** → Gorgias. Purpose-built for Shopify, with live order data inside every ticket so agents process refunds and edits without leaving the helpdesk. Ticket-based pricing from $10/month suits seasonal spikes better than Zendesk's per-agent seats.
- **Best AI-first support** → Intercom. The Fin AI Agent leads the category on autonomous resolution, and you pay $0.99 per issue actually resolved — not per attempt. Strong for product-led SaaS that wants AI deeply tied to the in-product experience.
- **Best budget pick with CRM tie-in** → Zoho Desk. Context-aware ticketing across email, chat, phone, and social from $14/agent/month, with a free tier for three agents. Undercuts Zendesk meaningfully, especially if you're already in the Zoho ecosystem.
- **Best for cross-team operations** → Front. Keeps the email-like experience while adding ticketing, routing, and SLA tracking — built for requests that cross sales, fulfillment, and support before resolving. From $25/user/month, strong for logistics and operations-heavy teams.

## Cost, complexity, or model — pick your reason

These alternatives split cleanly by the problem they solve, so name yours first. Leaving purely over the bill? Freshdesk and Zoho Desk deliver Zendesk-style ticketing for materially less, and Zoho's free tier makes evaluation painless. Leaving because the platform feels cold and heavy for your team size? Help Scout's inbox model is the antidote — fast to set up, genuinely easy, and warm by default.

Leaving because your business doesn't fit a generic queue? That's where the specialists win. Gorgias is built around Shopify order data and prices on ticket volume, which fits ecommerce far better than per-agent seats. Front is built for conversations that must survive handoffs between departments. Intercom is built for AI-deflected, in-product SaaS support where Fin's usage-based pricing rewards real resolutions.

## Trial advice

Whatever pulls you away from Zendesk, model the all-in monthly cost before you commit — the same add-on math that inflates Zendesk bills applies to its alternatives. Intercom's Fin and Front's Autopilot are usage-based or paid add-ons, so estimate volume rather than reading the base seat price. Gorgias overages can spike during peak season if you don't manage them. Most of these tools offer free plans or trials, so import a real slice of your ticket history into two finalists and run a live week of support in each. The right alternative usually reveals itself the first time your team works a busy day in it.
