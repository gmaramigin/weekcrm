## How we picked

Solo founders don't need a CRM — they need **a system to stop losing track of conversations**. The bar for a solo CRM is therefore different: **5 minutes to set up**, **free or under $30/mo**, **no admin work**, and ideally **embedded in the tools you already use** (Gmail, Notion, LinkedIn). Every pick below clears that bar.

## What to consider

- **Solo founder doing partnerships, BD, or fundraising** → Folk. The contact-first design plus built-in sequences is exactly the workflow.
- **Solo founder building a startup, will hire reps soon** → Attio. Free for 3 seats, scales into a real CRM when you start hiring.
- **Solo founder who wants a free CRM that grows into marketing automation** → HubSpot. The free tier is genuinely useful and the upgrade path is clear.
- **Independent consultant or freelancer who just wants a contact list with reminders** → Capsule. Cheapest option that's still pleasant to use.
- **Freelancer who needs CRM + invoicing + contracts in one tool** → Bonsai. The all-in-one freelancer suite includes a real CRM.

## Pricing snapshot

Solo-tier CRMs range from $0 (HubSpot, Attio free) to ~$25/user/mo (Folk Standard, Capsule Pro). For a solo operator, **paying more than $30/mo for a CRM rarely pays back** — at that price you're buying features designed for teams. Stay on a free or entry tier until you hire someone.

## Trial advice

The best solo-founder CRM is the one you'll open every morning. Don't optimize for features you don't have yet; optimize for the tool that fits your existing daily workflow. If you live in Gmail, pick something that lives in Gmail. If you live in LinkedIn, pick something with strong LinkedIn capture. The CRM you actually use beats the perfect CRM you don't.
