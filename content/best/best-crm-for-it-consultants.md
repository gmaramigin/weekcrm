## How we picked

IT consultants and MSPs sell *and* deliver, so the CRM has to bridge both worlds. We prioritized tools that (1) keep a **clean, fast sales pipeline** a busy consultant will actually update between client calls, (2) connect won deals to **project or task tracking** for billable work, and (3) stay **lightweight and affordable** — no enterprise admin burden for a 1–15 person shop. Time tracking and invoicing integrations were a plus, not a requirement.

## What to consider

- **Solo consultant / freelancer** → Pipedrive or Capsule. Minimal setup, mobile-friendly, and cheap. Capsule edges ahead if you want project tracking; Pipedrive wins on pure pipeline focus.
- **Small MSP (3–10 people)** → Insightly or HubSpot. Insightly turns closed deals into delivery projects in one tool; HubSpot scales your marketing and client communication alongside sales.
- **Already on a business suite** → Zoho CRM. If you use Zoho Books, Projects, or Desk, the CRM ties billable clients, projects, and tickets together at low cost.
- **Growing toward managed services** → HubSpot or Insightly. Both handle recurring client relationships and richer reporting as you move from project work to retainers.

## How to choose

Decide first whether you need project delivery *inside* the CRM. If your projects live in Jira, Asana, or a PSA already, optimize for the cleanest pipeline (Pipedrive) and just integrate. If you're juggling proposals and project tasks in spreadsheets, pick a CRM that does both (Insightly or Capsule) so a won deal becomes a tracked engagement without re-keying client data.

## Trial advice

Run one real prospect from first email through proposal, won deal, and the first project milestone inside two shortlisted CRMs. The consultant's CRM problem is rarely features — it's whether you'll log activity while context-switching between billable work. Keep the tool you update without thinking about it.
