Kylas Sales CRM carved out a clear niche: a sales CRM for growing businesses in India and other emerging markets, sold on a flat monthly subscription with unlimited users instead of the per-seat pricing that most Western CRMs charge. For a scaling team that's adding salespeople fast, that model is genuinely appealing — you don't get punished with a bigger bill every time you hire, and the product covers the pipeline, lead management, and automation fundamentals that a growing sales org needs. Paired with onboarding and support tuned to first-time CRM adopters, it's a sensible starting point for a lot of businesses.

But the flat-fee model and emerging-market focus that define Kylas are also why some teams look around. A very small team may find per-seat plans cheaper than a flat subscription. A maturing sales org may want deeper automation, richer reporting, a bigger integration ecosystem, or AI features that more heavily-invested platforms offer. And some teams simply want a product with a longer global track record or a specific strength — built-in calling, radical simplicity, or a wider suite to grow into. Below are five alternatives worth a serious look in 2026, each chosen for a specific reason people weigh against Kylas.

## How we picked

We weighted four things. First, **value for a growing team** — total cost at realistic headcount, since affordable scaling is Kylas' whole appeal. Second, **feature depth** — automation, reporting, and AI, the most common reason to move up. Third, **specific strengths** like built-in phone or extreme simplicity that fit particular teams. Fourth, **ecosystem and track record**, since maturing teams value integrations and stability. No invented scores; what follows is opinionated analysis.

## Zoho CRM

**Zoho CRM** is the standout alternative for most Kylas users — and, like Kylas, a product built with deep roots in the Indian market that now serves customers worldwide. It delivers an enormous amount of CRM for the money: multi-pipeline sales, workflow automation, AI assistance, extensive customization, and a free tier for small teams, all at per-seat prices that stay low even as you add features.

Against Kylas, Zoho trades the flat-fee model for per-seat pricing that's often cheaper for small teams and comparable as you grow, while offering markedly more depth and a vastly larger ecosystem — plus the wider Zoho suite for email, forms, books, and dozens of other tools under one vendor. For a team that wants room to mature from a starter CRM into a serious sales operation without changing vendors, it's the most complete and best-value pick here.

**Best for:** growing teams wanting maximum depth and value with room to scale.

## Freshsales

**Freshsales** is the alternative for teams that want built-in phone and AI without a premium price. Part of the Freshworks suite, it pairs a clean sales pipeline with native calling and email, AI-driven lead scoring and insights, and solid automation — plus a free plan for small teams and low-cost paid tiers.

Where Kylas covers the fundamentals, Freshsales adds a modern, AI-assisted feel and telephony baked right in, which matters for teams that sell over the phone and don't want to bolt on a separate dialer. It's well-priced, quick to adopt, and backed by a companion support product (Freshdesk) if you later want service too. For a value-conscious team that wants calling and AI in the CRM itself, it's a strong, modern step up from a starter tool.

**Best for:** value-focused teams wanting built-in phone and AI.

## Pipedrive

**Pipedrive** is the alternative for teams that want the cleanest possible sales pipeline and nothing they won't use. It's built around one strong idea — a visual, drag-and-drop pipeline that shows every deal and its next action — with just enough automation, email sync, and reporting layered on, plus a big integration marketplace.

Against Kylas, Pipedrive is more focused and famously easy to adopt; reps tend to actually keep it updated, which is the real test of any CRM. It prices per seat, so it's especially attractive for smaller teams where a flat plan might be overkill, and it scales sensibly. It's less of an all-in-one than Zoho, but for a sales team that wants clarity and speed over breadth, it's often the more productive daily tool. If simplicity drives adoption for you, it's the pick.

**Best for:** teams that want a clean, focused, easy-to-adopt sales pipeline.

## Salesmate

**Salesmate** is the alternative that sits close to Kylas' sweet spot — an SMB-focused sales CRM strong on automation and built-in calling, priced for growing teams. It combines pipeline management, a built-in phone and SMS, email sequences, and workflow automation in one approachable package.

For a team leaving Kylas that wants more automation firepower and native telephony without moving to a heavier platform, Salesmate is a natural comparison. It's modern, reasonably priced, and covers the outbound-and-follow-up motion well, with enough customization to fit a specific sales process. It doesn't have Zoho's sprawling ecosystem, but it isn't trying to — it's a focused, well-rounded SMB sales CRM. For automation and calling on a budget, it's a strong contender.

**Best for:** growing SMB teams wanting automation and built-in calling.

## Bigin

**Bigin** is the alternative for very small teams that find most CRMs — including Kylas — bigger than they need. Made by Zoho, it's a deliberately minimal, pipeline-first CRM built for solopreneurs and small teams, with a genuinely low per-seat price and a fast, friendly setup.

Where Kylas targets growing teams that will keep adding users, Bigin targets the team that wants just enough CRM to manage deals and contacts without any weight. It covers pipelines, basic automation, and email, integrates with the Zoho and Google ecosystems, and can grow into full Zoho CRM later if you outgrow it — a clean upgrade path. For a micro-team or a business dipping its toes into CRM for the first time, it's often cheaper and simpler than a flat unlimited plan. If less is more, it's the pick.

**Best for:** solopreneurs and very small teams wanting a simple, cheap pipeline.

## How to choose

Start with why you're leaving Kylas. If you want the most depth and value with room to grow, **Zoho CRM** is the strongest all-rounder — and familiar territory if you liked Kylas' India-built pedigree. If built-in phone and AI matter, **Freshsales** delivers both affordably. If you want the simplest pipeline your reps will actually use, **Pipedrive** fits. If automation and calling on a budget is the goal, **Salesmate** is the pick. And if Kylas feels like too much for a tiny team, **Bigin** is the lean, cheap option. The key question: do you want more CRM, or less? That fork narrows it fast.

## Pricing snapshot

- **Bigin** — from single digits/seat/mo; simplest and cheapest pipeline.
- **Zoho CRM** — free tier; paid from ~$14/seat/mo; deepest value.
- **Pipedrive** — from ~$14/seat/mo; clean, focused visual pipeline.
- **Salesmate** — low-to-mid tens of dollars/seat/mo; automation and calling.
- **Freshsales** — free tier; paid from low tens of dollars/seat/mo; built-in phone and AI.

(Prices are 2026 list rates and shift with billing terms and tiers — and Kylas' flat plan may beat per-seat pricing at high headcount, so compare at your actual team size before you commit.)

## The bottom line

Kylas earns its niche with flat, unlimited-user pricing for growing teams in India and emerging markets, and it's a sensible starter CRM. The case for switching gets strong when you need more depth, a different pricing shape, or a specific strength Kylas doesn't lead on. **Zoho CRM** is the deep, value-rich all-rounder, **Freshsales** the AI-and-phone pick, **Pipedrive** the clean simple pipeline, **Salesmate** the automation-and-calling option, and **Bigin** the ultra-simple, ultra-cheap choice for tiny teams. Decide whether you want more capability or more simplicity — and compare pricing at your real headcount — and the shortlist picks itself.
