## How we picked

Solopreneur CRMs have a different bar from team CRMs. **You are the admin, the rep, and the support team** — so the CRM has to be self-explanatory on day one, fast at the actions you do every day (look up a contact, log a touch, send a follow-up), and either free or under $30/mo. Anything that needs a quarterly cleanup loses.

## What to consider

- **Founder or advisor whose business runs on personal network** → Folk. Imports your LinkedIn, Gmail, and X contacts into one searchable graph; smart groups keep relationships alive.
- **Cheapest functional CRM with a clean rolodex** → Capsule. $9/user/mo, no learning curve — the right answer when the goal is "stop forgetting people".
- **Modern solopreneur who wants a flexible system of record** → Attio. Free for solo, AI-native enrichment, the data model bends to anything from clients to investors.
- **Service business that wants a branded client portal** → HoneyBook. Built for photographers, designers, planners, coaches — contracts, invoices, and CRM in one branded experience.
- **Independent consultant or freelancer who needs proposals + contracts + invoicing** → Bonsai. The all-in-one work tool for independent professionals.
- **Personal CRM for relationship-led work (advisors, investors, executives)** → Dex. Built around staying in touch — periodic reminders, no pipeline pressure.

## Pricing snapshot

Solopreneur CRMs cluster between $0 (Folk, Attio, HubSpot free tiers) and $36/mo (HoneyBook on monthly billing). The honest budget is "what you'd pay for one decent SaaS subscription per month" — anything more and the CRM becomes overhead. The picks above all clear that bar.

## Trial advice

For solopreneurs, the right test is whether you reach for the CRM unprompted on day 7. Pick one — not two — import 100 contacts, and use it for a real week. If the answer is no, move to the next pick. Two-CRM trials work for teams; for one-person businesses they just split your attention.
