## Who should leave LiveHelpNow

LiveHelpNow is a legitimately capable platform. It puts live chat, AI chatbots, ticketing, and a knowledge base in one workspace, and its Hue AI layer adds intelligent routing and knowledge-article suggestions. For a growing or multi-brand support team that wants AI assistance without an enterprise-tier rollout, it does the job — and its multi-brand handling from a single account is a genuine strength. If it's already deployed, priced reasonably for you, and your agents are happy, there's no reason to churn for its own sake.

Two frustrations push teams to look elsewhere. The first is pricing opacity: LiveHelpNow doesn't publish its Starter, Professional, and Enterprise rates, so every evaluation and renewal starts with a sales conversation and budgeting is harder than it should be. The second is momentum — it's less well-known than Zendesk, Intercom, or LiveChat, with a thinner public review base to lean on and a smaller ecosystem, and some of its more useful AI features are reserved for the higher tiers. Teams that want to see a price before they commit, tap a bigger community and app marketplace, or ride a faster-moving AI roadmap tend to move on.

## What to consider

- **Closest chat-first swap with clear pricing** → LiveChat. If LiveHelpNow's live-chat core is what you rely on, LiveChat is the natural replacement: a polished, configurable chat widget, proactive triggers, visitor tracking, multi-channel reach (Messenger, SMS, Apple Messages), AI reply suggestions across plans — and transparent pricing from $19/agent/mo. Note ChatBot is a paid add-on if automation is central.
- **Best AI value with a real free plan** → Tidio. Tidio's Lyro AI agent automatically handles a large share of incoming questions, its no-code Flows cover lead capture and support sequences, and there's a genuinely useful free plan. For ecommerce and lean SaaS teams that want LiveHelpNow-style AI without an opaque quote, it's the value pick (paid from ~$24/mo; Lyro/advanced Flows priced as add-ons).
- **Best autonomous AI agent** → Intercom. If the appeal of LiveHelpNow was its Hue AI, Intercom's Fin is the more advanced expression of that idea — resolving tickets autonomously across chat, email, and social, billed at $0.99 per resolved issue rather than per attempt. Best for product-led SaaS that wants AI support wired into the product; model the usage-based cost before committing.
- **Best for scaling and omnichannel depth** → Zendesk. When support volume grows beyond chat into full omnichannel operations, Zendesk brings ticketing, help center, chat, and voice with mature automation and 1,000+ integrations — and, crucially, published per-agent pricing (Suite from $55/agent/mo). Watch total cost as add-ons stack.
- **Best all-round cloud desk on a budget** → Freshdesk. A free tier plus paid plans from $15/agent/mo make Freshdesk the safe, transparent, well-reviewed alternative: multi-channel ticketing, automation, SLAs, a knowledge base, and a chatbot — everything LiveHelpNow bundles, with a bigger ecosystem and no quote required.
- **Best flat-rate pricing as you add agents** → Crisp. If per-agent billing is the specific worry, Crisp bills per workspace instead — live chat, omnichannel inbox (WhatsApp, Instagram, Messenger, SMS), AI agents, and a knowledge base at a flat rate from $45/workspace/mo, with a free plan for very small teams. Predictable costs are the whole point.

## Match the alternative to why you're leaving

The right LiveHelpNow replacement depends on which frustration drove you out. If the trigger was pricing opacity, prioritize the platforms with published rates and pick by billing model: LiveChat and Zendesk if per-agent pricing is fine, Crisp if you'd rather pay per workspace so costs don't climb with every new hire, and Tidio or Freshdesk if a free tier to start matters most.

If the trigger was AI — you liked Hue but wanted more — then the decision is between Tidio's Lyro for accessible, ecommerce-friendly automation and Intercom's Fin for a more autonomous, product-embedded agent, with Freshdesk and Zendesk as the safer bets if you want AI as one feature inside a broader, better-supported help desk rather than the headline. Either way, run your actual chat and ticket volume through a trial of two finalists; with chat-first tools, the quality of the widget, the routing, and the AI deflection rate on *your* traffic is what settles it.
