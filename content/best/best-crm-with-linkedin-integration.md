## How we picked

LinkedIn is the dominant prospecting channel for B2B in 2026, and most CRMs treat it as an afterthought. The picks below ship **a real Chrome extension that adds people from LinkedIn search results in a click**, **two-way profile sync** so the CRM stays current, and **enrichment** that pulls work email and company data automatically. Anything less and your reps copy-paste from LinkedIn into the CRM all day, which is the wrong shape.

## What to consider

- **Pure LinkedIn prospecting (BD, agencies, recruiting)** → Folk. The extension is the best in the category and the contact-first data model fits the workflow.
- **AI-native team that wants enrichment as a primary feature** → Attio. AI fields auto-classify and enrich on read, and the LinkedIn extension is solid.
- **Inbound-led teams that need LinkedIn as a layer** → HubSpot. The extension is functional; the value is the rest of the platform.
- **Outbound calling + LinkedIn combo motion** → Close or Salesflare. Both surface LinkedIn alongside the dialer and email sequencer.

## Pricing snapshot

LinkedIn-capable CRMs run $19–$50/user/mo at the relevant tiers. Folk and Attio are the most LinkedIn-forward. Salesflare bundles enrichment in the base price; HubSpot tends to push enrichment to higher tiers or paid add-ons.

## What about Sales Navigator?

The CRMs above all integrate with LinkedIn's free interface and (for most) Sales Navigator. Sales Nav is worth it if you're sending 100+ connection requests a week and need advanced filters; otherwise the free LinkedIn + a good CRM extension covers the same ground for most teams.

## Trial advice

Run the Chrome extension for one week of real prospecting. The right CRM is the one where adding 20 people from a LinkedIn search takes 5 minutes total — not 30. If you find yourself copy-pasting, switch.
