## How we picked

Texting only works in a CRM when it's **two-way and logged** — outbound blasts that don't capture the reply, or texts that live in a separate app, defeat the purpose. We prioritized CRMs with native SMS where inbound and outbound messages thread against the contact automatically, then weighed automation (can you sequence texts?), channel coverage (SMS vs. WhatsApp/Instagram), and how texting fits the broader workflow — sales-led, marketing-led, or chat-first.

## What to consider

- **Outbound sales reps who text leads** → Close. Native two-way SMS alongside calling and email, included on all plans, with texts logged automatically and usable inside outbound sequences.
- **Best value, calling + SMS together** → Salesmate. Native communications in one platform from $23/user/month, with AI summaries on conversations.
- **Chat-first, WhatsApp and DMs** → Kommo. Built around messaging apps — WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok, SMS — with no-code salesbots; the right call when your pipeline lives in chat threads.
- **SMS inside a full marketing suite** → HubSpot. Text fits alongside email, forms, and automation, with a free CRM to start and SMS in the marketing workflow tools.
- **Automated SMS nurture sequences** → Keap. The strongest behavior-triggered SMS-plus-email automation, ideal for small businesses running hands-off follow-up — at a $249/month entry.

## A note on compliance

US business texting runs through A2P 10DLC: you register your brand and campaign with the carriers and must capture opt-in and honor opt-out (STOP) under TCPA. These CRMs help manage consent and unsubscribes, but the legal obligation to get consent first is yours. Build registration time into your rollout — unregistered traffic gets filtered or blocked.

## Pricing snapshot

Close includes SMS on every plan ($19–$129/user/month). Salesmate starts at $23/user/month, HubSpot has a free tier with SMS in its paid marketing tools, and Kommo starts at $15/user/month (6-month minimum). Keap is the premium option from $249/month plus a $500 onboarding fee. Carrier message fees are typically billed on top of the seat price.

## Bottom line

Match the tool to your channel. If reps text leads as part of outbound, Close is the cleanest native option; Salesmate matches it on value. If your customers message on WhatsApp, Kommo is purpose-built for it. If texting is one channel inside broader marketing automation, HubSpot (for breadth) or Keap (for hands-off sequences) fit best. Whichever you pick, sort A2P 10DLC registration before you send.
