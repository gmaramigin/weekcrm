## How we picked

A driving school runs on a leaky, seasonal funnel: inquiries surge around school terms and test windows, every enrolled student is a finite lesson package on a deadline, and a referral from a happy parent or graduate is worth more than any ad. The right CRM captures each inquiry, chases the ones that stall before enrollment, tracks each student's remaining lessons so packages actually get completed, and keeps instructor calendars booked tight rather than patchy. We weighted enrollment follow-up, package and scheduling tracking, and referral generation over reporting a small school will never need.

## What to consider

- **All-in-one booking + packages** → vcita. Enrollment, scheduling, lesson tracking, and payments in one place.
- **Enrollment and package follow-up** → Keap. Convert stalled inquiries and nudge students to book their next lesson.
- **Reviews and reputation** → Thryv. Turn graduates and parents into the reviews that drive new enrollments.
- **Start free** → HubSpot. Track every inquiry and student while you keep overhead low.
- **Best value** → Zoho CRM. Full automation and reminders at the lowest per-seat cost.

## Pricing snapshot

School-appropriate plans run $0–$60/month for a small office or solo instructor. HubSpot starts free; Zoho anchors the value end; vcita and Thryv cost more but bundle the booking, package tracking, and payments you'd otherwise piece together.

## Trial advice

During the trial, set one automation to follow up on inquiries that don't enroll within a few days, and one to remind students with lessons remaining to book their next session. The school that converts more of its seasonal inquiries and keeps packages moving toward the test will fill instructor calendars without the office chasing every student by hand.
