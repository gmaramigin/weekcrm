## Who should leave Re:amaze

Re:amaze is an all-in-one play for ecommerce: a shared help desk, live chat, chatbots, FAQ pages, and push campaigns bundled into a single, affordable tool. For small and mid-size online stores that want chat and ticketing without stitching multiple products together, that breadth-for-the-price is the main draw, and the Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce integrations cover the essentials.

Where teams start looking elsewhere is depth and pedigree. The Shopify integration is functional but not as rich as the category leader's, automation and reporting are lighter than the enterprise platforms', and as ticket volume climbs some teams want a more proven tool with a bigger ecosystem and more sophisticated routing. The same all-in-one simplicity that makes Re:amaze easy to start with can feel limiting once your store, catalog, and support team grow.

## What to consider

- **Best for Shopify depth** → Gorgias. The ecommerce help desk most stores benchmark against. Live order data in every ticket, macros that issue refunds and edit orders, and ticket-based pricing built for seasonal swings. The clearest upgrade if you want a deeper, more capable Shopify integration.
- **Best for scaling** → Zendesk. Mature triggers, macros, SLA routing, and a 1,000+ app marketplace. Broad channel coverage and the headroom to support a store that's outgrowing an all-in-one tool. The safe choice for fast-growing operations.
- **Best general-purpose value** → Freshdesk. Multi-channel ticketing, live chat, SLAs, automation, and a knowledge base, with a free plan for unlimited agents on email. A strong, affordable foundation that still connects to your store for order context.
- **Best for chat-and-bot-led support** → Tidio. Live chat and chatbots tuned for ecommerce, with product context and proactive messaging to capture conversions. A natural fit if most of your Re:amaze usage is the chat widget rather than the ticket queue.
- **Best affordable all-in-one inbox** → Crisp. The closest in spirit to Re:amaze — chat, email, and social in one shared inbox with a chatbot and a low entry price. A good swap for small teams that liked the bundled approach but want a different take on it.

## Choosing your next tool

Pin down what you valued in Re:amaze. If it was the ecommerce angle and you want it deeper, Gorgias is the obvious test — the most complete Shopify help desk, priced for lean stores. If it was breadth at a low price and you want something more scalable, Zendesk or Freshdesk give you a fuller platform, with Freshdesk the cheaper of the two.

If most of your support is happening in the chat widget, Tidio leans hard into chat and conversion; if you simply want another affordable all-in-one inbox, Crisp is the closest match. Import a real slice of order-related conversations into two finalists and work a busy day in each before you decide.
