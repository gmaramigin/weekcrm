## Who should leave NinjaOne

NinjaOne is one of the strongest unified RMM platforms you can buy — patch automation, real-time monitoring, remote access, and backup in one console, rated a G2 Grid leader for more than 23 straight quarters. But its design choices define its edges. Pricing is per-device and quote-based, typically $2–3.75/device/month, which scales cleanly for large fleets but turns expensive for MSPs managing many endpoints per technician — and it means a sales call before you see a real number. NinjaOne is also pure IT operations: there is no PSA, no ticketing-and-billing layer, and no customer relationship side. If you run a managed service business, you still need to bolt on a separate PSA.

You should leave if per-device math is working against you, if you want PSA and RMM under one subscription instead of two vendors, or if your actual need is internal service-desk ticketing or asset discovery rather than full endpoint management. Teams that should *stay* are MSPs and IT departments managing large, distributed fleets who value NinjaOne's automation depth and maturity and don't mind the per-device model. If the monitoring and patching are doing their job, switching buys you little.

## What to consider

- **Best for a modern unified PSA + RMM** → SuperOps. Where NinjaOne is RMM-only, SuperOps folds ticketing, projects, billing, and monitoring into one purpose-built platform with AI alerting that filters noise. Per-technician pricing (each license includes 150 endpoints) runs $89/tech/mo for PSA, $149/tech/mo with RMM — predictable as your device count climbs.
- **Best for flat per-tech pricing** → Syncro. The clearest answer to NinjaOne's per-device model: one flat rate of $129/user/mo (annual) with *unlimited* devices, plus built-in PSA, help desk, and M365 management. If you manage many endpoints per head, this is where the cost curve bends in your favor.
- **Best for internal IT service desk** → Freshservice. If you don't actually need RMM but proper ITSM — incident management, an auto-updating CMDB, and employee self-service — Freshservice delivers it with Freddy AI from $19/agent/mo (Growth $49, Pro $99). The move for internal IT teams drowning in email tickets, not endpoint alerts.
- **Best for enterprise, cross-department service** → Halo Service Solutions. HaloITSM, HaloPSA, and HaloCRM share one data model with codeless, ITIL-aligned automation spanning IT, HR, facilities, and customer support. Pricing is contact-vendor only and implementation is heavier, but for complex enterprise service operations it replaces several contracts at once.
- **Best for asset discovery and compliance** → Lansweeper. If the real gap is *visibility* — a live, accurate map of every device, license, and vulnerability — Lansweeper's agentless discovery sweeps on-prem, cloud, and hybrid estates and feeds ServiceNow, Jira, and Microsoft. A free tier covers small environments; larger deployments are quote-based.
- **Best for zero budget** → Spiceworks. Free and ad-supported, installed on a Windows box, it auto-discovers LAN devices, monitors uptime, and runs a help desk tied to assets. The UI is dated and it won't scale past a single small site, but for a solo admin with no budget it still works.

## Match the alternative to the gap

The mistake is shopping for "a cheaper NinjaOne." NinjaOne's RMM is excellent — the reason to leave is almost always a capability it doesn't cover or a pricing model that fights your shape. Name the gap first.

Tired of running RMM and a separate PSA? SuperOps and Syncro put both under one bill, and Syncro's unlimited-device pricing directly counters the per-device squeeze. Realize you don't need endpoint management at all, just structured ticketing? Freshservice fits internal IT; Halo fits sprawling, multi-department enterprises. Just need to know what's on the network? Lansweeper for serious discovery and compliance, Spiceworks when the budget is zero.

## Trial advice

Because NinjaOne is genuinely capable, a replacement has to clearly win on the one thing that drove you out — usually pricing model or missing PSA/ITSM — not merely match it overall. Most of these run real free trials (14 days for SuperOps and Freshservice; Spiceworks and Lansweeper have free tiers) so model your true all-in cost at your actual device-to-technician ratio, not the headline rate. Pilot your top two with a live week of tickets and patching before your NinjaOne renewal lands.
