## Who should leave Sellsy

Sellsy's pitch is consolidation: CRM, quoting, legally-compliant electronic invoicing, marketing campaigns, and cash-flow forecasting in one French-hosted product, from €35/user/month on a two-seat minimum. For a French SMB that values RGPD-compliant data sovereignty and wants its commercial and administrative workflows under one login, that's a genuinely strong package — and if local hosting and integrated billing are why you bought it, there's little reason to move.

The cracks show up at the edges. Every plan carries a 12-month commitment, so there's no month-to-month escape hatch when priorities change. Pricing and the interface are built around the French and broader European market, which gets awkward the moment you hire outside the eurozone or sell into the US. And because Sellsy spreads itself across five categories, no single module — CRM depth, marketing automation sophistication, pipeline analytics — matches a tool that does only that one thing. You should leave if your team has outgrown one of those modules, if your invoicing needs are now better served by dedicated accounting software, or if you're internationalizing and need a platform that thinks in dollars and English-first support as fluently as it does in euros.

## What to consider

- **Best for staying all-in-one in Europe** → Teamleader. The most direct swap: CRM, quotes, invoicing, and project tracking in one European product with e-invoicing and localized features across Belgium, France, Germany, Spain, and Italy. From €37/month, with a quote-to-project workflow Sellsy doesn't match for service firms that bill by project.
- **Best for configurable depth plus an invoicing ecosystem** → Zoho CRM. If you want a deeper sales engine and are happy to pair it with Zoho Books for billing, Zoho CRM runs $14–$52/user/month (free for three users), and Zoho One bundles 45+ apps — invoicing included — for $37/user/month. Multi-currency and international support beat Sellsy's eurozone focus.
- **Best for marketing-led growth** → HubSpot. Sellsy's campaigns are competent; HubSpot's are best-in-class. Free to start, then $20/seat (Starter) to $100–$150 (Professional/Enterprise, plus a one-time $1,500 onboarding fee). The move when email, landing pages, and automation are the reason you're outgrowing the suite.
- **Best for flat-rate all-in-one** → Bitrix24. Priced per organization, not per seat — from $49/month for unlimited users, with a free tier and an on-premise option for teams with their own data-residency requirements. CRM, quotes, telephony, and project management in one dense package if you can absorb the steeper learning curve.
- **Best for a focused sales pipeline** → Pipedrive. If you're decoupling invoicing and just want a clean, fast CRM, Pipedrive's visual pipeline runs $14–$99/user/month. The right call when the CRM module was always the weak link and you'd rather hand billing to dedicated software.
- **Best for killing data entry** → Salesflare. Auto-captures contacts, meetings, and email threads from Gmail, Outlook, and LinkedIn so reps stop typing. From $29/user/month (Pro $49), it's the answer when adoption — not features — is why your sales records keep going stale.

## Match the alternative to the gap

Don't replace a five-in-one tool with another five-in-one tool out of habit. Name the module that's actually failing you. If it's the whole bundle and you simply want it outside France's pricing-and-hosting frame, Teamleader is the cleanest like-for-like and Bitrix24 the cheapest at scale. If it's marketing, HubSpot is the upgrade. If it's the CRM itself, Pipedrive or Salesflare each fix a different version of that — Pipedrive the pipeline, Salesflare the data hygiene.

The subtler decision is invoicing. Sellsy's legally-compliant e-invoicing is a real asset, and only Teamleader and Bitrix24 keep billing in-house here. Choose Zoho or HubSpot and you're accepting a split stack — CRM in one place, invoicing in dedicated accounting software — which is often the better setup once each side needs to be best-in-class, but it's a deliberate trade, not a free one.

## Trial advice

Because Sellsy locks you in for twelve months, time the switch to your renewal rather than eating an early-exit cost. Export your contacts, deals, and — critically — your invoicing data, and load your top two finalists. Run a real billing cycle through each: issue a quote, convert it, send an invoice, and confirm the compliance and payment-reminder flow holds up, since that's the piece a generic CRM will quietly drop. Most of these tools are live within a day or two, so you can validate the move well before the lock-in window closes.
