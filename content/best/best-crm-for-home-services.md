## How we picked

Home-services businesses, cleaning, HVAC, landscaping, electrical, win on speed and convenience: **fast lead response** (the first to call back usually books the job), **online booking and reminders** (fewer phone tags and no-shows), and **estimate-to-invoice flow** (quote, schedule, collect payment without re-keying). We favored tools that compress that path from inquiry to paid.

## What to consider

- **Solo operators and small crews** wanting everything in one app: Thryv bundles CRM, booking, payments, and reviews.
- **Appointment-driven trades** needing client self-service scheduling: vcita's booking and reminders are the standout.
- **Growing companies** layering on marketing and nurture: HubSpot scales sales and marketing together.
- **Teams that just want a clean job pipeline**: Pipedrive moves leads from estimate to booked simply.
- **Budget-focused businesses** wanting automation and room to grow: Zoho CRM delivers the most per dollar.

## Pricing snapshot

Home-services CRMs are accessible. vcita and Zoho start in the $14–$30/user/mo range, Pipedrive around $25–$35/user/mo, and HubSpot from roughly $20 per seat upward. Thryv is typically quoted as a bundled monthly package since it folds marketing, booking, and payments into one subscription, ask for a quote based on your trade.

## Trial advice

Test the lead-to-booking path with a real scenario. During the trial, send yourself a mock inquiry, then time how fast you can respond, send an estimate, and get the job on the calendar. The platform that turns an inbound request into a scheduled, confirmed job with the fewest steps is the one that will win you the most local business.
