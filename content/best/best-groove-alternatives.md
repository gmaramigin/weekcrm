## Who should leave Groove

Groove does one thing well: it turns a forwarded support@ address into a clean shared inbox with collision detection, rule-based routing, SLA tracking, and a layer of AI for tagging and draft replies. For a startup or SaaS team that has outgrown a Gmail label but doesn't want a sprawling ticketing platform, that's often exactly enough. The friction shows up at the edges. Pricing isn't published, so you can't compare cost without a sales call. Reporting is adequate for a small team but thins out fast against operational analytics needs. And native voice and SMS are limited, so phone-heavy or omnichannel teams hit a wall.

You should leave if the unlisted pricing has stopped making sense at your headcount, if you need deeper reporting or stronger automation, or if your support has spilled into channels Groove handles weakly — phone, SMS, social, or Shopify order actions. The teams who should stay are small, email-first support groups that value the simplicity and have no reason to add channels. If the shared inbox is doing its job, switching only adds onboarding cost.

## What to consider

- **Best overall shared inbox** → Help Scout. The closest like-for-like swap: an email-style inbox that onboards in hours, an AI assistant that resolves routine requests, plus Beacon and a knowledge base. A free plan up to 5 users, paid from $25/user/month, with published, comparable pricing — the thing Groove makes you call for.
- **Best for Gmail teams** → Hiver. If your team lives in Google Workspace, Hiver layers shared inboxes, assignment, and AI agents directly inside Gmail — no new tool to learn. Free plan for unlimited users; paid from $25/user/month, with AI and omnichannel on Pro.
- **Best for scaling support** → Freshdesk. When ticket volume outgrows a simple inbox, Freshdesk brings multi-channel ticketing, strong automation, SLA management, and 1,000+ integrations. Free plan, paid from $15/agent/month — room to grow without enterprise pricing.
- **Best for cross-team work** → Front. If a request routinely touches sales, fulfillment, and support, Front keeps the email-like feel while adding routing and cross-department workflows that survive every handoff. From $25/user/month; AI Autopilot is a separate add-on.
- **Best for ecommerce** → Re:amaze. For Shopify, BigCommerce, or WooCommerce stores, Re:amaze puts order data inline and unifies email, chat, and social. A flat $59/month Starter for small teams (up to 500 conversations); per-seat plans from $29/user/month.
- **Best value with CRM** → Zoho Desk. Multi-channel ticketing, a self-service portal, and AI assistance that undercuts Zendesk on price. Free for up to 3 agents; paid from $14/agent/month, with native two-way sync if you already run Zoho CRM.

## Match the alternative to the gap

The trap is shopping for a "nicer inbox." Groove's inbox is fine — you're leaving because of a specific limit it hits, so name that limit first. Frustrated by quoting a price you can't see? Help Scout and Freshdesk publish theirs. Living in Gmail already? Hiver removes the context switch entirely.

Drowning in order-status tickets on Shopify? Re:amaze puts the store in the conversation. Need a request to move across departments without losing its thread? Front is built for exactly that. Outgrowing light reporting and want depth at a low price — especially alongside a CRM? Zoho Desk gives you more per dollar. Pick the tool that closes your gap, not the one with the prettiest queue.

## Trial advice

Because Groove is genuinely simple, a replacement has to clearly beat it at the one thing that drove you out, not just match it overall. Export your conversations, load your top two finalists, and run a real week of live tickets through each. Watch the all-in monthly cost with the channels and AI you actually need — several of these bill add-ons separately — and confirm your team adopts the new inbox without friction. Most of these tools are live in a day, so you can validate the switch long before any renewal forces your hand.
