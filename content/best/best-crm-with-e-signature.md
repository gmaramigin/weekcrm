## How we picked

E-signature inside a CRM should collapse the gap between "send proposal" and "deal won." We evaluated whether signing is native or requires a bolt-on, how cleanly signed documents attach to the deal, whether status (viewed, signed) syncs back automatically, and how well each handles quotes, CPQ, and templated contracts. Legal compliance and audit trails were table stakes.

## What to consider

- **Best overall** → HubSpot. Native quotes with built-in e-signature mean a prospect can accept and sign a proposal in one flow, with everything logged to the deal.
- **Best value** → Zoho CRM. Tight native integration with Zoho Sign delivers compliant, trackable signatures at Zoho's signature low price.
- **Deepest contract workflows** → Salesforce. With CPQ and DocuSign, it handles complex, multi-party enterprise agreements and approval chains end to end.
- **Best simple signing** → Pipedrive. Smart Docs lets reps send a signable proposal in a couple of clicks, ideal for fast-moving SMB sales.
- **Best for mid-market document automation** → Sugar CRM. Strong templating and document generation that scales contract production for larger sales teams.

## Pricing snapshot

Pipedrive and Zoho keep entry costs low, with signing available on plans around $14–$23/seat (Zoho Sign may add a small per-user fee). HubSpot bundles quotes with e-signature on paid Sales tiers, while Salesforce CPQ and DocuSign are premium add-ons that push enterprise costs well past $165/seat. Sugar's document automation lands in the mid-market band. Always check signature-volume caps.

## Trial advice

Send yourself a real contract during each trial and walk the full loop: generate the document, send it, sign as the customer, and confirm the executed PDF and "signed" status appear on the deal automatically. The CRM that closes that loop without manual filing is the one that will actually shorten your sales cycle. Compare two finalists head to head.
