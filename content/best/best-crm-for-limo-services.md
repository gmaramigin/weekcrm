## How we picked

A limo service sells time and reliability. A client reserves a car for a wedding, an airport run, or a corporate event; you confirm, take a deposit, and show up on the minute. The CRM has to make reservations reliable and keep the fleet booked without double-committing a vehicle. We prioritized reservation intake, deposit collection, calendar/booking management, corporate account tracking, and follow-up automation. Retail one-off rides need less; wedding, event, and corporate work — where the money is — needs all of it.

## What to consider

- **You want reservations and payments in one app** → **vcita**. Online booking requests, card deposits, and reminders in a single tool — the simplest all-in-one for a small fleet.
- **You compete on reviews and referrals** → **Thryv**. Reputation management, campaigns, and listing sync keep you visible for "limo near me" and wedding-vendor searches.
- **Most bookings come by message** → **Kommo**. Turns WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook inquiries into tracked reservations — good for a service discovered on social.
- **You want automation on a budget** → **EngageBay**. A free-friendly CRM with email/SMS sequences and clean contact records — a low-cost way to automate reminders and follow-ups.
- **Corporate accounts are your growth** → **Keap**. Strong account and follow-up automation for recurring executive-travel and event clients, with a pipeline for winning new corporate contracts.

## Pricing snapshot

Limo-service CRMs run roughly $20–$50/user/month, with SMS reminders often billed as separate credits. Confirm whether the tier you pick includes payment collection for deposits, since that's central to event and wedding bookings.

## Trial advice

Run two through one real reservation: take a booking, collect a deposit, and log a corporate account for reorder. Keep the one that fills the calendar without double-booking a car — for a limo service, a clean, deposit-secured schedule is the whole game.
