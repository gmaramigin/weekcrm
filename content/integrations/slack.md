## Why Slack-native customer tooling has its own category

For B2B SaaS companies whose customers live in Slack — through Slack Connect shared channels — traditional helpdesks (Zendesk, Intercom) are the wrong shape. Customers don't open tickets in a portal; they post in a shared channel. The "CRM" that works in this world is one that **runs inside Slack itself**: requests get triaged in threads, status is visible in the channel, and analytics roll up across hundreds of customer channels.

This is a different problem than just "send Slack notifications when a Salesforce deal closes" (though those integrations exist too). The vendors below specialize in turning Slack from a chat app into a customer success and support platform.

## What to prioritize

- **Native Slack thread management.** Tickets live as threads, not in a separate UI. Customers never see a portal.
- **Shared-channel support at scale.** When you have 200 Slack Connect channels, you need one inbox view that aggregates them all with SLA timers.
- **AI triage.** Auto-categorize incoming requests, suggest replies, route to the right engineer.
- **CRM/data sync.** Customer org data (plan, MRR, owner, region) should be one click away inside the Slack thread.
- **Reporting outside Slack.** First-response time, resolution time, CSAT — Slack doesn't surface these natively, your tooling has to.

## When this category is the right call

- **B2B SaaS with high-touch enterprise customers** who request Slack Connect at the contract stage.
- **Developer-focused products** where users expect synchronous, low-formality support.
- **Community-led companies** running large Slack/Discord communities and treating them as a support surface.

If your customers prefer email or your support volume is low, a traditional helpdesk is still the right shape. The Slack-native tools below are purpose-built for the case where Slack is *the* support channel, not just one of several.
