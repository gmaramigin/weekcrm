## Why Aircall-native CRM integration matters

Aircall is the dominant cloud business phone for sales-led SMB and mid-market teams — most B2B sales orgs that haven't built calling into the CRM stack are running Aircall on top. The integration is what makes the stack work: reps click-to-call from inside the CRM, calls log automatically against the contact and deal record with duration and recording attached, missed calls turn into follow-up tasks, and dialer activity feeds the same reporting that the rest of the pipeline reports against. The CRMs below ship native Aircall integrations that handle the full call loop without rep-side toggling.

The depth difference between a real Aircall integration and a fake one shows in two places: write-back fidelity (does the call summary, recording URL, and disposition write back to the deal record, or just a "call happened" event?) and dialer queue behavior (can the rep build a power-dial queue from a CRM view filter, or do they have to import lists into Aircall manually?).

## What to prioritize

- **Click-to-call from the deal/contact record.** The rep should never copy/paste a phone number.
- **Automatic call logging with disposition.** Calls log against the deal, with duration, direction, outcome, and recording URL — no manual entry.
- **Power-dial from a CRM view.** Build a filtered list in the CRM, push to the Aircall dialer, and let reps work the list without re-import.
- **Insights cards in the deal record.** Recent call activity, last-talked-to dates, and call recordings should sit on the contact/deal record where reps actually work.
- **Reporting parity.** Call activity should flow into the same CRM reports as deals — calls-to-meetings ratio, dial activity per rep, and time-to-first-call should be CRM-native, not Aircall-only.

## When Aircall integration is the right call

- **SMB-to-mid-market B2B sales orgs** that don't want native calling but still need real cloud telephony tied to the CRM.
- **Inside sales teams** running multi-rep outbound where Aircall's number management, recording, and quality features beat what most CRM-native dialers ship.
- **International sales orgs** that need local-number presence in multiple countries — Aircall's number provisioning is one of its strongest features.

## When CRM-native calling (Close), JustCall, or Dialpad might fit better

Aircall is the default for sales teams that picked it before adding a CRM. Adjacent tools fit other motions: CRM-native calling (Close, Freshsales) is one fewer vendor for high-volume inside sales; JustCall fits SMB teams wanting a slightly cheaper Aircall alternative; Dialpad fits teams that want AI conversation intelligence built into the phone system. The CRMs below are listed specifically for Aircall; many of them also integrate with the alternatives.
