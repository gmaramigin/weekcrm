## Why Zoho integration matters

Zoho One is the dominant SMB all-in-one stack outside the HubSpot ecosystem — 50+ apps under one license, and most teams that buy in end up running 8–15 of them. The challenge is that some Zoho apps are best-in-category (Zoho CRM, Zoho Desk) and some are not. The tools below either **specialize in extending Zoho with capabilities Zoho's native apps don't ship**, or **provide a clean migration path** for teams who've decided to replace one piece of the bundle (usually CRM or Desk) without ripping out the rest of Zoho. Either way, the integration matters because Zoho One customers don't want a tool that re-fragments the data model they spent two years consolidating.

## What to prioritize

- **Native Zoho CRM and Zoho Desk integration.** Sync contacts, accounts, deals, and tickets bidirectionally without Zapier glue.
- **Zoho Flow / Zoho Marketplace presence.** First-party listing means the integration is maintained alongside Zoho's quarterly releases.
- **Permission-aware.** Zoho's role-based access should be respected by the integrated tool, not bypassed.
- **Migration tooling.** If you're replacing a Zoho app, the new tool should ship a documented import path from the corresponding Zoho object model.

## When Zoho integration is the deciding factor

- **SMBs running Zoho One** as the primary stack across sales, support, and ops.
- **Mid-market teams that started on Zoho** and need to layer in specialist tools without abandoning the rest of the bundle.
- **Teams migrating off Zoho CRM or Zoho Desk** who need to preserve the rest of the Zoho integration graph.
