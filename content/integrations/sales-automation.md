## What sales automation actually means

"Sales automation" covers a range of CRM capabilities that often get conflated. The clearest breakdown:

- **Data entry automation** — automatically logging emails, calls, and meetings to the CRM without manual input. This is baseline in 2026 and should be assumed in any modern CRM.
- **Sequence automation** — multi-step email and call cadences that run without rep intervention. Rep enrolls a lead, the sequence runs, the rep handles replies.
- **Workflow automation** — trigger-based actions when a record changes (deal stage advances, field updates, record creation). Used for internal routing, notifications, and data hygiene.
- **AI-assisted actions** — next-step suggestions, email draft generation, deal scoring, and conversation summarization. Increasingly standard across mid-tier CRMs.

Most teams need all four, but the quality and pricing varies enormously across vendors.

## Where teams get automation wrong

The most common failure mode is over-automating sequences before they have validated messaging. An email sequence that sends 8 touches over 21 days is efficient — but if the messaging is wrong, it efficiently annoys a large number of prospects. Get the messaging right manually first, then automate.

The second failure mode is under-automating data entry. Manual logging is the single biggest source of CRM data degradation. If reps have to click "log this call" after every call, data completeness will be 60% at best within 90 days. Choose a CRM that captures email, calendar, and call activity automatically.

## Automation at different price points

- **Under $30/user/mo** — Expect basic workflow triggers and email templates. Real sequencing usually starts at the $40-50 tier.
- **$50–100/user/mo** — Multi-step sequences, conditional workflows, and basic AI suggestions. This is the sweet spot for most SMB and mid-market teams.
- **$100+/user/mo** — Full predictive scoring, AI deal coaching, conversation intelligence integration, and territory/quota automation.
