## Why a Salesforce integration is the deciding factor

If you already run Salesforce as the system of record, every other tool in your stack lives or dies on its Salesforce integration. A helpdesk that doesn't surface the Account, Opportunity, or Case object inside the agent view forces context-switching. A marketing tool that doesn't write back to a custom object means the campaign attribution model is broken on day one. The tools below ship **first-party Salesforce integrations on AppExchange**, **bidirectional sync with custom-object support**, and **the ability to extend Salesforce without forcing your admin team to write new Apex triggers**.

## What to prioritize

- **AppExchange-listed apps.** First-party means it survives Salesforce's quarterly releases without breaking.
- **Custom object support.** If you've modeled your business in Salesforce with custom objects, the integration must respect that — not just sync standard objects.
- **Bidirectional sync.** Tickets, conversations, and activities should round-trip cleanly without orphan records.
- **Permission-aware.** The integration should respect Salesforce sharing rules and field-level security, not blow through them.

## When Salesforce integration is non-negotiable

- **Enterprise sales orgs already on Sales Cloud** where the CRM is the source of truth for revenue.
- **Customer service teams running Service Cloud** that need a helpdesk extending the platform, not replacing it.
- **Multi-product enterprises** with a Salesforce admin team in place.

If you're not on Salesforce today, picking a tool primarily for its Salesforce integration is the wrong move — pick a CRM that fits the rest of your motion. The tools below are for teams whose Salesforce decision is already made.
