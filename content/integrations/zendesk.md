## Why Zendesk integration matters

If support runs on Zendesk and sales runs on a separate CRM, the seam between them is where revenue gets lost. A renewing customer with three open tickets shouldn't be a surprise to the AE on the QBR call. A churned account shouldn't show up in the next quarterly forecast. The tools below ship **bidirectional Zendesk integrations** that surface tickets and CSAT scores inside the CRM record, push pipeline events back to Zendesk for support context, and keep both platforms aligned on the same customer object — so a sales rep and a support agent are looking at the same source of truth.

## What to prioritize

- **Zendesk Marketplace listing.** First-party integrations stay current with Zendesk's API releases.
- **Bidirectional sync.** Tickets visible in the CRM; deals and account changes visible in Zendesk.
- **Custom field mapping.** Plan tier, MRR, account owner, region — the customer health context should be one click away from the agent view.
- **Permission-aware.** Sensitive deal data (forecast, ARR, contract terms) shouldn't leak into the support agent view unless you want it to.

## When Zendesk integration is non-negotiable

- **B2B SaaS companies** running Zendesk for support and a separate CRM for sales — the integration is what keeps both teams aligned on account health.
- **Customer success teams** who need ticket history, CSAT, and product feedback alongside renewal forecasting.
- **Sales-led companies expanding into post-sale motions** where the CRM-to-Zendesk seam is the new revenue surface.

If your support and sales motions are tightly fused, picking Zendesk Sell (Zendesk's own CRM) might be cleaner than integrating two separate platforms. The tools below are for teams who've decided to keep Zendesk for support and run a different CRM for sales.
