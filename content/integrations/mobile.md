## Why mobile-first CRMs deserve their own category

Most CRMs ship a mobile app as an afterthought — a stripped-down version of the web UI with smaller buttons and worse navigation. For field sales reps, that's the difference between a CRM that gets used in the truck and a CRM that gets ignored until Friday's pipeline review.

A genuinely mobile-first CRM treats the phone as the primary surface: fast launch times, offline support, voice-to-text logging, photo capture tied to records, route-aware visit planning, and one-tap call/text from a contact. The CRMs below either ship a native mobile experience or have a web app responsive enough to feel native on mobile.

## What to prioritize

- **Native iOS and Android apps** — not a webview, not a PWA. Real native apps launch in under a second and work without WiFi.
- **Offline support** — field reps lose connection in basements, warehouses, rural drives. The app should queue updates and sync when back online.
- **Voice-to-text logging** — speak a quick visit note while walking back to the truck. Faster than typing on a phone keyboard.
- **Photo capture to deals** — attach site photos, business cards, or whiteboard sketches with one tap. Common for roofing, construction, field service.
- **Route and visit planning** — see customers on a map, optimize a day of stops, log visits as you go.
- **One-tap call + text** — click-to-call, click-to-SMS, with the conversation auto-logged to the CRM record.
- **Push notifications** — assigned lead, replied email, deal stage change. The mobile app should pull reps in, not require them to remember to check.

## When this category is the right call

- **Field sales teams** — manufacturers reps, ag, medical device, distribution.
- **Service businesses with mobile teams** — roofing, HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, real estate.
- **Outside sales reps with route territories** — pharma, beverage, automotive, construction supply.
- **Solo founders and small teams** who do everything from a phone between meetings.

If your team is sedentary (inside sales, SaaS, agency) the mobile app matters less and a great web CRM is the right shape. The tools below are picked for the case where the phone is the primary surface and a great mobile experience determines adoption.
