## Why SMS belongs in the CRM in 2026

SMS open rates run 95%+ versus email's 20%, and reply rates can be 10x higher for transactional and high-intent outreach. The catch is operational: if reps text from a personal phone, the conversation never lands in the CRM, replies don't trigger workflows, and compliance (TCPA, opt-outs, consent records) is impossible to enforce. A CRM with native (or first-party) two-way SMS solves this — every message lands on the contact record, replies route to the assigned owner, opt-outs flow into a suppression list automatically, and managers can see SMS performance alongside email and calls.

## What to prioritize

- **Two-way messaging in the CRM UI.** Reps text from inside the contact record; replies show up in the same thread without anyone forwarding.
- **Bulk + 1:1 send.** Both blast (e.g., a customer launch announcement to a segment) and one-to-one conversational text from the rep workflow.
- **Per-rep numbers and team numbers.** Each rep gets their own number for relationship continuity; team numbers handle shared inboxes (e.g., support@).
- **Automated triggers.** SMS as a step in a sequence, an alert ("your demo starts in 15 min"), or a transactional notification ("invoice paid") — driven by the CRM, not a separate platform.
- **Compliance.** TCPA-aware opt-in capture, automatic STOP/UNSUBSCRIBE handling, consent records on the contact, and audit trail for every message sent.

## When CRM-native SMS is the right call

- **High-intent SMB and consumer sales** — real estate, mortgage, automotive, home services, financial advisors — where SMS is how prospects expect to communicate.
- **Inside sales and SDR teams** running multi-channel sequences (email + call + SMS) and needing reply detection across channels in one CRM.
- **Customer success and support teams** handling appointment reminders, status updates, and one-touch confirmations.
- **Field service** where techs and dispatchers coordinate over SMS and the conversation needs to live on the work order.

If your volume is small enough that a personal phone works, you don't need this. The moment you have more than two reps, more than one shared number, or any compliance exposure (mortgage, healthcare, insurance), CRM-native SMS is the right shape.

## Carriers and underlying infrastructure

Most CRMs on this list either ship SMS via their own short-code/long-code infrastructure (Close, HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho) or pass through Twilio for the carrier connection. Either model works — the question is whether the messaging UX, deliverability, and compliance handling are good enough that reps actually use it. Demo it on real US numbers, not a test sandbox.
