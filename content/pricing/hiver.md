## What you are actually buying per seat

Hiver bills per user, and the per-user number is the whole story — there is no ticket cap, no contact tier, no resolution meter. That makes it one of the easier help desks to forecast, and it is a genuine advantage over usage-priced tools where a busy month produces a surprise invoice.

The complication is that Hiver does not sell you seats one at a time. Plans start at 2 seats, expand to 5, and then move in increments of 5. So the pricing question is not "what does a seat cost" but "which block am I in". A nine-person support team buys ten seats. A twelve-person team buys fifteen. At Pro that rounding is $165 a month of pure air.

Because Hiver lives inside Gmail, there is also a cost that never appears on the invoice: every Hiver seat needs a Google Workspace seat underneath it. If you are already a Workspace shop that is free. If you are not, Hiver is the wrong product, and no amount of per-seat arithmetic fixes that.

## The Free plan is real, and that is unusual

Most vendors' free tiers exist to expire. Hiver's does not obviously do that — it includes shared inbox and ticketing, email, chat and help center channels, and 24x7 support, without a hard user ceiling. For a five-person team fielding a manageable trickle of support@ email, it can be the permanent answer.

What it does not include is any automation, any analytics beyond nothing, and any AI. That is the honest boundary. The free plan is a shared inbox with assignment. The paid plans are a help desk.

## Growth to Pro is the decision that matters

The $25 to $55 jump more than doubles your bill, so it deserves scrutiny. What you get for it: SLAs, CSAT surveys, a customer portal, advanced analytics, premium integrations and API access.

Read that list again and you will notice it is not a features grab-bag — it is the set of things you need the second support stops being informal. If nobody has promised a customer a response time, Growth is fine and Pro is waste. If anyone has, Pro is not optional and your real entry price for Hiver was always $55.

Elite at $85 is a different kind of purchase. Skill-based routing and custom objects are for teams with specialised queues; SSO, HIPAA and custom roles are compliance line items. Buy Elite because procurement or your security review demands it, not because the feature list looks fuller. Note too that three of Elite's headline AI features are marked coming soon — you would be paying the $30 delta partly on a promise.

## What it costs at real team sizes

Annual billing, rounded up to the nearest valid seat block:

| Team | Seats billed | Growth | Pro | Elite |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 4 people | 5 | $125/mo | $275/mo | $425/mo |
| 9 people | 10 | $250/mo | $550/mo | $850/mo |
| 22 people | 25 | $625/mo | $1,375/mo | $2,125/mo |

The 22-person row is where the block rounding stings most: three phantom seats, $165 a month at Pro, roughly $2,000 a year for nothing.

## Annual versus monthly

The annual discount is $10 per user per month on every paid tier — around 29% off Growth, 15% off Pro, 11% off Elite. That is a steeper proportional saving at the bottom than the top, which is backwards from how most vendors structure it and quietly makes Growth the best-value annual commitment on the sheet.

If you are unsure about Hiver, pay monthly for a quarter. The penalty is real but survivable, and it beats being locked into 12 months of a tier you outgrow in four.

## Who this pricing suits

It suits Google Workspace teams of 5 to 25 who want support tooling without a migration, and who can live inside Gmail's constraints. At that size the block rounding is tolerable and the per-seat cost lands well under Zendesk's equivalent tier.

It punishes two groups. Teams of 11, 16 or 21 people, who pay for a block they cannot fill. And teams that need phone as a first-class channel, since Voice is an add-on rather than an included capability — budget for it separately or look at LiveAgent, which bundles a call centre from the second tier.

List prices move, seat-block rules change, and the coming-soon features on Elite will either land or quietly disappear. Confirm the current numbers on Hiver's own pricing page before you sign anything.
