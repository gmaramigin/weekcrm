## The minimums are the pricing model

Read Deskpro's page quickly and you see $39, $59 and $99 per agent per month.
Read it properly and you see 5, 10 and 25 — the seat minimums attached to each
tier, which decide far more of your invoice than the per-agent rate does.

The consequence is that Deskpro's cost curve is not smooth. It steps. A team of
six on Team pays $234 a month. That same team deciding it needs Deskpro AI and
multi-brand support moves to Professional and pays for ten seats: $590 a month.
The per-agent price rose 51%, the actual bill rose 152%, and four of the ten
seats sit empty.

This is not a trap so much as a filter. Deskpro is telling you which tier is
built for which size of organisation, and it is enforcing it with the invoice
rather than a feature matrix. It just means the upgrade decision has to be made
against total cost, never against the headline rate.

## What the step actually costs

| Agents | Team | Professional | Enterprise |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 5 | $195/mo | $590/mo (10 seat min) | $2,475/mo (25 seat min) |
| 20 | $780/mo | $1,180/mo | $2,475/mo (25 seat min) |
| 50 | $1,950/mo | $2,950/mo | $4,950/mo |

The middle column is where the story sits. At twenty agents, Professional costs
$400 a month more than Team and every seat you pay for is a seat you use — the
minimum is behind you, and you get Deskpro AI, the AI chatbot, fifty Lite Agents,
multi-brand support and CRM sync for it. That is the best-value cell in the
table by a distance.

The Enterprise column shows why the twenty-five-seat minimum matters: a
twenty-agent team needing HIPAA-compliant hosting pays $2,475 a month regardless,
which is $99 per agent inflated to an effective $124.

## Lite Agents are the underpriced part

Most help desks make you choose between paying full freight for an engineer who
comments on three tickets a month or leaving them outside the system entirely.
Deskpro's Lite Agents — view and collaborate, cannot reply to customers — are
included from Professional at fifty seats, and a thousand on Enterprise.

For a support team of twelve backed by thirty engineers and product managers who
need ticket visibility, that changes the comparison completely. Priced against
Zendesk or Freshdesk, where those thirty people are either full seats or excluded,
Deskpro's $59 tier stops looking mid-market and starts looking cheap. Teams
without that internal collaboration pattern get nothing from it, which is why the
Professional upgrade is a much better deal for some organisations than others.

## AI in the box, hosting choice out of it

Deskpro includes its AI features and AI chatbot at Professional rather than
metering them. Against Freshservice, where Freddy AI Copilot is a separate $29
per agent per month, that is a genuine structural advantage — a Deskpro
Professional seat with AI is $59 while a Freshservice Pro seat with AI is $128,
albeit for a different category of product.

What is not in the box is the self-hosted option. Deskpro Private exists and is a
real differentiator in a market where almost everyone has abandoned on-premise,
but it is quoted separately and no price is published. If data sovereignty is
your reason for shortlisting Deskpro, nothing on the cloud pricing page tells you
what you will pay, and you should open that conversation early rather than after
you have fallen for the product.

## What the page does not tell you

Deskpro displays rates as "per agent/month" without stating whether they assume
an annual commitment or a monthly one, and there is no visible toggle. Most
vendors at this price point discount 15-20% for annual terms. Deskpro may or may
not; the page does not say, so ask, and do not assume the $39 is a
month-to-month rate you can walk away from.

Enterprise lists implementation services as a benefit without a price. That
phrasing means scope, so get the hours into the contract.

## Who it suits

Deskpro prices well for support teams of fifteen to fifty agents with a wide
internal collaborator pool, and for regulated organisations that need on-premise
or a specific hosting region and will pay for it. It prices badly for teams of
six to nine, who sit in the dead zone above Team's comfort and below
Professional's minimum, and for anyone under twenty-five agents with a
compliance requirement that forces Enterprise.

If you are a five-person team, buy Team and stay there until you genuinely have
ten agents. The minimum makes early upgrading one of the more expensive mistakes
available in this category.

Deskpro adjusts its tiers and minimums periodically, and the self-hosted product
is quoted case by case. Their pricing page and your quote are the authority on
what you will actually be charged.
