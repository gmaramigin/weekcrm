## Cheap seats, priced-out features

At $9 per user per month on annual billing, Growth is one of the lowest genuine entry prices for a full CRM. Not a free tier with a contact cap — an actual working CRM with pipeline, email, chat and reporting for less than a tenth of what HubSpot's paid tiers cost per seat.

The catch is where Freshworks drew the feature line. Sales sequences — automated multi-step outreach — sit on Pro at $39/user/mo. So does territory management, so do multiple pipelines, and so does anything resembling serious reporting. Growth is an inbound CRM: leads arrive, you track them, you close them. Any team doing outbound is buying Pro whether the demo suggested otherwise or not.

That makes the honest entry price for a sales team $39/user/mo, and the $9 figure a fair advertisement for a narrower product than most buyers imagine.

## The AI line is metered, not bundled

Freddy AI is Freshworks' marquee pitch and it is not included in your seat cost. The Freddy AI Agent bills at $49 per 100 sessions.

Sessions track visitor and inbound conversations, which means the cost curve follows your website traffic rather than your headcount. A five-person team with a busy marketing site can easily burn more on Freddy than on seats:

| Team | Plan seats (annual) | Freddy usage | Freddy cost | Total/mo |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 5 on Growth | $45 | 400 sessions | $196 | $241 |
| 20 on Pro | $780 | 1,000 sessions | $490 | $1,270 |
| 50 on Enterprise | $2,950 | 2,000 sessions | $980 | $3,930 |

In the first row the AI is four times the subscription. That is not a criticism of the rate — $0.49 a session is competitive — but it does mean "Freshsales costs $9 a seat" describes maybe half of a real invoice at small scale. Cap it or monitor it monthly.

## Growth to Pro, and Pro to Enterprise

The Growth-to-Pro jump is $30/user/mo, a 4.3x increase, and it is the one that matters. Justify it on sequences and multiple pipelines alone; the reporting improvements are a bonus rather than a reason. For a 12-person team that is $360 a month more, which one additional closed deal a quarter covers comfortably in most B2B contexts.

Pro to Enterprise is $20/user/mo for custom modules, a sandbox, audit logs and field-level permissions. This is a governance upgrade, not a productivity one. If nobody in your organisation has asked about audit trails or restricting field visibility, you do not need it yet. Regulated industries and teams above roughly 50 seats are the genuine buyers; everyone else should stay on Pro and spend the difference on the branded documents add-on if quotes matter.

## Annual commitment and the monthly premium

Freshworks advertises a 20% saving for annual prepayment, which puts the monthly-billed Growth rate near $11 and Pro near $47. That premium is standard for the category, but the 21-day trial changes how you should use it. Most CRM trials give you 14 days, which is barely enough to import data. Three weeks is enough to migrate a real subset, run live deals and find out whether your team actually logs activity in it.

Use the full trial and buy annual afterwards. Paying the monthly premium as a hedge against a bad fit is wasted money when the trial is this long.

## Suits and misfits

Freshsales fits small and mid-sized sales teams that want a modern interface without HubSpot's pricing or Salesforce's implementation project. A 15-person team on Pro pays $585/mo annually — roughly what the same team would spend on a single HubSpot Sales Hub tier with fewer seats.

It suits Freshworks customers even better. If you already run Freshdesk, the shared platform and single vendor relationship are worth real money in avoided integration work.

It punishes two profiles. Very small teams that get seduced by the $9 headline and then discover sequences cost 4x more end up feeling misled — go in knowing Pro is the real price. And organisations with heavy customisation requirements will find the Enterprise tier's ceiling lower than Salesforce or Dynamics, so paying $59 a seat for extensibility you then outgrow is the worst outcome available here.

## Check the current rate card

Figures above come from freshworks.com/crm/pricing in July 2026. Freshworks reorganises its CRM packaging more often than most vendors — plans have been renamed and re-bundled repeatedly — and the Freddy AI rate in particular is likely to move. Confirm against the vendor's own page, and ask directly about the free tier's status rather than trusting any directory on that point, including this one.
