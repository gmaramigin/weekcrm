## A free tier and then a cliff

Teamgate's Starter plan is free for two users, with one pipeline, 500 contacts and the core CRM: deals, contacts, companies, tasks, calendar sync, file tracking. No time limit.

The next plan is Team at $39.90 per user per month, or $31.92 with annual billing.

There is nothing between. A two-person team pays nothing; a three-person team pays roughly $1,150 a year. That is one of the sharpest free-to-paid transitions in the CRM market, and it defines who Teamgate is for.

## 500 contacts is the real ceiling

The two-user cap gets the attention. The contact cap does the work.

Five hundred contacts is a few weeks of list-building for any B2B team doing outbound. Import a modest prospect list and the free plan is exhausted before anyone has sent an email.

So Starter is best understood as an indefinite trial for a very small, relationship-led operation — a two-person consultancy working a warm network — rather than as a plan a startup grows on.

## What Team actually includes

At $31.92 annually, Team is more expensive than Pipeline CRM Start ($25) or comparable Pipedrive entry tiers. What it includes at that price is unusual:

- Full email synchronisation with templates
- Workflow automations
- Sales dashboards and forecasting
- In-app dialling and SMS integrations
- Mass import
- Unlimited contacts and users
- **A dedicated account manager**
- 24/7 support

The account manager on a sub-$40 plan is the outlier. Most vendors reserve named contacts for enterprise agreements. For a small sales team with nobody internally who owns CRM administration, having someone at the vendor who knows your configuration is worth a genuine premium.

## The API paywall is the tier decision

Open API access and Zapier integration are Growth features, at $47.92 annually.

That is a 50% uplift over Team, and it is triggered by a requirement almost every modern sales team has. Connecting the CRM to marketing automation, to billing, to a data warehouse, to anything — all of it needs the API.

| Requirement | Tier | Annual per user |
|---|---|---|
| Standalone CRM, one currency, up to 3 pipelines | Team | $383 |
| Any integration, multi-currency, lead scoring | Growth | $575 |

Before assuming Team is your tier, list the systems Teamgate will need to talk to. If the list is not empty, budget Growth.

## Multi-currency catches international teams

Multi-currency support sits in Growth alongside the API.

For a sales team quoting in dollars, euros and pounds — normal for any European B2B company — Team is unusable regardless of headcount or feature preference. That puts the effective price for international teams at $47.92, and it is not obvious from the tier names.

## Annual billing matters more here than usual

The discount is 20%, but the absolute gap is large because the base rates are: $8 per user per month on Team, $12 on Growth.

On a six-person team, annual billing on Growth saves $864 a year. That is not marginal, and it makes the monthly option genuinely expensive rather than a modest convenience premium.

Given there is no free trial period published beyond the free Starter plan, the sensible route is to use Starter to assess the interface and workflow, then commit annually once the decision is made.

## Where it fits

Teamgate is a mid-priced, well-featured sales CRM for small and mid-sized teams that want forecasting, automation and email sync without stepping up to HubSpot or Salesforce pricing.

Its distinctive assets are the dedicated account manager and 24/7 support at a modest price point, and the genuinely functional free tier for two-person operations.

Its distinctive weakness is the pricing shape: a free plan that ends abruptly, no low-cost entry tier, and the API behind the top tier.

## Who should buy it

Small and mid-sized sales teams of three to thirty who want a complete sales CRM with forecasting, automation and hands-on vendor support, and who will commit annually. Growth at $47.92 is fair for a team that needs integration and multi-currency.

It suits badly startups that will outgrow the free plan's 500 contacts and then face a $1,150 annual step, and cost-sensitive teams needing API access, who pay 50% more for a feature competitors include at entry level. Check teamgate.com for current rates and count your integration requirements before choosing between Team and Growth.
