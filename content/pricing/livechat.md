## You are billed on agents, but limited by visitors

LiveChat's price scales with headcount and its capability scales with traffic,
and those two things move independently. That mismatch is the defining feature
of this pricing model.

Each tier carries a hard visitor tracking limit: 100 on Starter, 400 on Team,
1,000 on Business. That number governs how many concurrent site visitors
LiveChat will watch and let you engage proactively. A four-person support team
on Team pays $196 a month and gets 400 tracked visitors — fine for a niche
B2B site, immediately insufficient for a mid-size ecommerce store on a Friday.
The store has to move to Business at $79 a head, not because its agents need
work schedulers, but because its traffic outgrew the allowance.

Work out your concurrent visitor peak before you pick a tier. It is the
constraint that will actually bite.

## The one-user Starter trap

Starter's $19 is the number in every comparison table and it applies to
precisely one person. There is no second seat available at that rate. The real
question for any team is not "Starter or Team" but "how much does Team cost
me", and the answer at list price is:

| Agents | Team ($49 annual) | Business ($79 annual) | Monthly-billed Team ($59) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 3 | $1,764/yr | $2,844/yr | $2,124/yr |
| 8 | $4,704/yr | $7,584/yr | $5,664/yr |
| 20 | $11,760/yr | $18,960/yr | $14,160/yr |

Twenty agents on Business is $18,960 a year before the ChatBot add-on. That is
where LiveChat stops being the affordable option and Crisp's flat
per-workspace model starts looking materially cheaper — Crisp's top published
tier is $295 a month regardless of how many people log in.

## Where Business earns the extra $30

Business adds work scheduler, staffing prediction, agent performance metrics,
on-demand reporting, plus SMS and Apple Messages for Business. Every one of
those is a management feature. They are worth $30 per agent per month when you
have enough agents that shift coverage is a recurring problem and someone's
job includes staring at queue metrics — realistically six people and up.

Below that, a team of three does not need staffing prediction. Stay on Team,
and revisit when you hire your fifth.

The Team-to-Business jump also buys you 1,000 tracked visitors instead of 400,
and for a lot of buyers that alone is the reason. If it is, be honest about it
— you are buying traffic capacity and getting reporting thrown in.

## Annual versus monthly

The discount is $6 per agent per month on Starter and Team and $10 on
Business. There is no seat minimum and no contract floor, so a two-person team
can pay for exactly two seats. Given the 14-day trial is genuinely
no-credit-card, the sensible sequence is trial, then a month or two of monthly
billing, then switch to annual once you know the tool has stuck.

## Who this model suits

LiveChat is priced for a dedicated support team of three to fifteen agents on a
site with predictable traffic — the pricing is transparent, there are no
consumption surprises, and you can forecast the bill exactly. Nothing on the
invoice depends on how many conversations you actually handle, which is a real
advantage over usage-billed competitors like Tidio.

It punishes two groups. Large teams, because per-agent billing at $79
compounds badly past twenty seats. And high-traffic sites with small support
teams, because the visitor cap forces an upgrade the headcount does not
justify. If you are a two-person team on a busy storefront, you are the worst
possible fit for this pricing shape.

## Check the source before budgeting

The rates above are LiveChat's published list prices as of July 2026. Visitor
tracking limits and add-on pricing get revised more often than the headline
per-agent figures — confirm on livechat.com before you commit, particularly if
the ChatBot add-on is part of your plan.
