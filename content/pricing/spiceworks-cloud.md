## The cheapest working help desk, still

Spiceworks Cloud Help Desk has been free for years, and remains so. The Core plan costs $0 a month and includes ticketing, asset tracking, alerts, reporting, a mobile app and unlimited end users — hosted, with no server to maintain.

In June 2025 Spiceworks added Premium at $6 per agent per month, which removes the advertising and adds capability.

Both numbers are far below anything else in the category. The nearest credible competitors — osTicket Cloud at around $11, HelpDesk.com Essential at $19, BoldDesk at roughly $10 to $20 — are two to three times the paid tier and infinitely more than the free one.

## How free is funded

Spiceworks does not sell the help desk. It sells access to IT professionals.

The company runs a large IT community and an advertising business, and the free help desk is both a genuinely useful product and the top of that funnel. Ads appear inside the tool, and vendor content is woven into the surrounding ecosystem.

That is a transparent arrangement rather than a hidden one, and for a small IT department it is a good trade: a working, hosted, maintained help desk in exchange for looking at vendor advertising.

It is worth being clear-eyed about it, though. Your usage is part of a commercial model, and organisations with strict data governance should read the terms rather than assume free means unencumbered.

## $6 removes the main objection

The most common complaint about Spiceworks over the years has been the advertising. Premium at $6 an agent addresses it directly.

| Team size | Premium annual cost |
|---|---|
| 3 agents | $216 |
| 5 agents | $360 |
| 10 agents | $720 |
| 25 agents | $1,800 |

Twenty-five agents ad-free for $1,800 a year is remarkable. The same team on a $19-a-seat competitor is $5,700; on a $50 competitor, $15,000.

For any IT department where the ads are the only real objection, $6 is not a decision that needs a business case.

## Where the ceiling is

The free price reflects a deliberately simple product, and it is worth knowing where it stops.

Reporting is basic. SLA management and workflow automation are shallow compared with paid competitors. Customisation is limited. There is no AI deflection layer of the kind that now defines the customer-support market.

None of that is a flaw at $0 or $6. It is the honest shape of the product. But a team whose requirements grow — formal SLAs, sophisticated routing, multi-department queues with distinct workflows — will hit the ceiling and need to move.

The good news is that moving is cheap when you have paid nothing.

## Internal IT, not customer support

Everything about Spiceworks points at a sysadmin audience: asset tracking, network alerts, the community integration, the terminology.

That makes it an excellent internal IT help desk and a poor customer-facing one. Customer support needs branding control, an experience your customers will not see advertising in, and channel breadth — chat, social, messaging — that Spiceworks does not offer.

Use it for the employee ticket queue. Buy something purpose-built for customers.

## What it competes with honestly

Within internal IT help desks, the comparison set is small:

| Product | Cost | Hosted | Ads |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spiceworks Core | $0 | Yes | Yes |
| Spiceworks Premium | $6/agent | Yes | No |
| osTicket Community | $0 | No — self-host | No |
| FreeScout | ~$50 one-time | No — self-host | No |
| osTicket Cloud | ~$11/agent | Yes | No |

The free self-hosted options are ad-free but require a server, patching and a sysadmin's attention. Spiceworks is the only genuinely free *hosted* option, and that difference is the whole reason it persists.

For a two-person IT department with no appetite for running another server, that is decisive.

## Who should buy it

Small and mid-sized internal IT departments that want a working, hosted, maintained help desk with asset tracking and no budget line at all. Nothing else in the market offers free and hosted together, and Premium at $6 is a trivial upgrade for anyone who wants the advertising gone.

It suits badly customer-facing support teams, organisations with strict governance around advertising-funded tools, and IT departments needing sophisticated SLA management or workflow depth — all of which will meet the ceiling. Check spiceworks.com for current Premium features and start with Core; there is no cost to finding out whether it fits.
