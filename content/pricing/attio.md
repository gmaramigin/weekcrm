## Two meters running side by side

Attio's pricing page reads cleanly — free, $35, $79, custom — and that clean surface hides the more interesting half of the model. Every plan carries a monthly credit allowance, and credits are what enrichment and AI actions spend. You are buying two things at once: the right for a person to log in, and the right for the system to do work on your data.

The allowances scale sharply. Free gives 100 seat credits per user and 250 workspace credits per month. Plus gives 500 and 1,500. Pro gives 1,000 and 10,000. Enterprise gives 2,500 seat credits with workspace credits negotiated. Note that the workspace pool grows far faster than the per-seat pool between Plus and Pro — 1,500 to 10,000, a 6.7x jump — because Attio expects Pro customers to be automating across the whole database, not just enriching what individual reps touch.

This is why two teams of identical size can pay very differently. A five-person team using Attio as a tidy contact database will never look at the credit meter. A five-person team running enrichment on every inbound signup and AI summarisation on every meeting will be buying top-up blocks by month three.

## What headcount does to the bill

Because Plus caps at 10 users, the seat ladder has a cliff in it rather than a slope.

| Team size | Free | Plus ($35) | Pro ($79) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 users | $0/mo | $105/mo | $237/mo |
| 10 users | not available | $350/mo | $790/mo |
| 11 users | not available | not available | $869/mo |

Going from ten users to eleven takes you from $350/month to $869/month — a 148% increase for one extra person, because the eleventh seat forces the whole workspace onto Pro. This is the single most important thing to model before you commit. If you are at eight or nine users and hiring, price Pro now rather than being surprised by it.

Add credit top-ups on top. A Plus workspace buying +10,000 workspace credits a month pays $120 on annual billing, which on a five-person team ($175/month in seats) is a 69% increase driven entirely by usage.

## Where each tier earns its place

The free plan is unusually generous and worth taking seriously. Three users with real-time contact syncing and automatic enrichment covers a founding team's actual needs. It is not a 14-day countdown; it is a plan you can sit on while you decide. The honest limit is the 250 monthly workspace credits — enough to see what enrichment does, not enough to depend on it.

Free to Plus is worth it when either the third user becomes a fourth, or when the credit ceiling starts blocking automations you want to run every day. At $35/user, Plus is priced against mid-market CRMs while offering a materially more modern data model, and for a team of four to ten it is the sweet spot in this lineup.

Plus to Pro is the expensive step and, feature-wise, a narrower one: advanced reporting, call intelligence, and the large credit increase. If you are moving because you crossed ten users, you have no decision to make. If you are still under ten and considering it voluntarily, the question is whether reporting and call intelligence are worth $44 more per person per month — for a sales-led team with a real forecast to produce, often yes; for an ops team using Attio as a system of record, usually not.

## Terms, commitment and negotiation

The annual discount is a flat 20% at both paid tiers, which is standard and worth taking once you are past evaluation. Enterprise is billed annually only, so flexibility disappears entirely at the top.

There is no seat minimum, which genuinely distinguishes Attio from much of the category. A single-person workspace on Plus costs $35/month, and that is the actual price — no phantom seats, no three-user floor. For solo operators and two-person startups, that alone can make Attio cheaper in practice than tools with lower headline rates.

## Who should and should not buy this

Attio fits technical, data-minded teams — startups, VC firms, agencies — that want to model their own objects rather than accept someone else's contact-and-deal schema. The free tier makes it low-risk to try, the single-seat pricing makes it accessible to very small teams, and the enrichment layer removes work that other CRMs make you buy a separate tool for.

It punishes two profiles. Teams sitting just above ten users pay the Pro cliff for headcount reasons alone. And workflow-heavy teams that lean hard on enrichment and AI find the credit meter turns a predictable per-seat bill into a usage bill they have to watch — the same problem Intercom customers have with Fin, expressed in a different currency.

Attio ships changes fast and has revised both its tiers and its credit allowances before. Check attio.com/pricing for current numbers before you budget against these.
