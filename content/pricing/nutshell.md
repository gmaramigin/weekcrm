## Unlimited contacts is the headline, add-ons are the story

Nutshell does something most CRMs in its price range do not: every plan includes unlimited contacts and unlimited storage. There is no record cap forcing an upgrade, no contact-tier ladder quietly compounding as your list grows. If you have 80,000 records and six salespeople, Nutshell prices you like a six-person company. HubSpot and Keap price you like an 80,000-contact one. For contact-heavy, headcount-light businesses that difference is the entire buying decision.

The catch — and it is a fair one, not a trick — is that the functionality sitting next to the CRM is unbundled. Marketing is $49/month. Engagement, which is where SMS, messaging and the unified inbox live, is $16 per user per month. Prospecting credits through Nutshell IQ are $37/month. Proposals and invoicing is $79/month. Email marketing contacts have their own tiered fee starting at $5/month and climbing with list size.

None of those are hidden — they are all on the pricing page — but they change the shape of the bill dramatically. A six-seat Foundation account is $42/month. The same six seats with Engagement, Marketing and a small contact tier is closer to $200. Still cheap, but not $42 cheap.

## The tier ladder is unusually flat, which is good news

Five tiers spanning $7 to $69 gives you fine-grained steps rather than the usual cliff. Foundation to Growth is $11 per user and buys multiple pipelines and real activity reporting — that is the upgrade almost every team makes, usually within the first quarter, once someone wants to separate new business from renewals.

Growth to Pro is $17 per user for sales automation sequences. Worth it if your reps run structured multi-touch outreach; not worth it if your motion is inbound and conversational. Pro to Business at $51 and Business to Enterprise at $69 are limit-and-governance upgrades. Most teams under 50 people have no business reason to go past Pro, and Nutshell's sales team is not especially pushy about it.

## Worked cost at three sizes

Annual billing, per month, CRM seats only:

| Seats | Foundation | Growth | Pro |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 5 | $35 | $90 | $175 |
| 20 | $140 | $360 | $700 |
| 50 | $350 | $900 | $1,750 |

Now add Engagement at $16 per user to the 20-seat Growth row and you are at $680/month — the add-on costs nearly as much as the CRM. That is the single most useful sanity check to run before signing: price your add-ons per user, not per account, and see whether the total still beats a bundled competitor.

## Annual versus monthly

The absolute discount is modest — $6 to $10 per user per month — but at the bottom of the range it is enormous in percentage terms. Foundation is $7 annual against $13 monthly, which means month-to-month nearly doubles the price of the entry tier. On Pro the gap narrows to $35 versus $42, a far more reasonable 20%.

Practical read: if you are trialling Nutshell on Foundation, the annual commitment is $84 per user for a year. That is a small enough number that the flexibility argument mostly evaporates. Higher up the ladder, take the monthly rate until you are confident.

## Fit and misfit

Nutshell is built for B2B teams of roughly 5 to 100 people who want a CRM their reps will actually maintain and who would rather not run a separate email platform. Agencies, manufacturers, professional services and anyone with a long, relationship-driven sales cycle get real value from the unlimited-contact model plus integrated campaigns.

It is a poor fit for two groups. Enterprise sales orgs with complex territory management, forecasting hierarchies and quote approval chains will find even Enterprise thin. And teams that need heavy customisation — custom objects, deep API-driven workflows — will hit the edges of what Nutshell models. It is deliberately opinionated, and the low price is partly a consequence of that.
