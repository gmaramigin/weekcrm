## One price, both deployments

HelpSpot's rate card has a property almost nothing else in this category shares: cloud and self-hosted cost exactly the same.

$899 a year for three agents. $2,199 for ten. $9,999 for fifty. $16,999 for a hundred. Whether that runs on HelpSpot's US or EU cloud or on your own server does not change the number.

Every feature is included at every band. There are no setup fees and professional onboarding is free.

That combination — no tiers, no deployment penalty, no feature gates — makes HelpSpot one of the easier help desks to budget for, and the entire decision reduces to which band you sit in.

Two observations. The discount curve is gentle through the mid-range — 25 and 50 agents cost the same per head — and then improves markedly above a hundred.

And the small end is unremarkable. At $25 an agent, a three-person team can find cheaper elsewhere; HelpSpot's argument at that size is completeness and onboarding, not price.

## Band boundaries are the expensive hires

Because you buy capacity, not seats, the marginal cost of a hire depends entirely on where you stand.

- Agent 11: $2,800 (moves you from the 10 band to the 25 band)
- Agent 26: $5,000 (25 band to 50 band)
- Agent 51: $7,000 (50 band to 100 band)

Agent 11 is the worst of them — a 127% cost increase for one person, and then fourteen agents' worth of capacity you have paid for and are not using.

The practical response is to plan hiring against boundaries. If you are at ten agents and expect to reach eighteen within a year, buying the 25 band early is the same money spent on better timing.

## Deployment parity changes the question

Most vendors charge less for self-hosted (you provide the infrastructure) or refuse to offer it at all. HelpSpot charges the same.

That removes cost from the deployment decision entirely, which is clarifying. You self-host because your data must stay on your infrastructure, or because internal policy requires it — not because it saves money. It does not; it costs more once you add the server, backups, patching and PHP/MySQL maintenance.

For regulated buyers, that clean separation is valuable. The cloud option is there when policy relaxes, at no price penalty, and migration between the two is a supported path rather than a re-purchase.

## What is not included

There is no AI deflection layer of the kind that now defines the SaaS help desk market. No autonomous resolution agent, no per-conversation deflection metric.

HelpSpot is a mature, conventional, complete help desk — good ticketing, good workflow, good reporting, a long track record. Judged against that description, the pricing is fair and the feature-completeness at every band is a real advantage.

Judged against Richpanel's $0.20-per-conversation AI or BoldDesk's bundled AI platform, it is a different product solving a different problem. Buyers whose business case is deflection should look elsewhere; buyers who need every ticket handled by a human anyway lose nothing.

## Annual-only raises the stakes

There is no monthly option. The minimum commitment is $899 up front.

For a small team that is a meaningful barrier compared with a $19-a-seat monthly competitor you can cancel in thirty days. It also means the evaluation matters more — confirm trial terms before committing, because you cannot test HelpSpot by buying one month of it.

The free professional onboarding partly offsets this. Being set up properly by the vendor is worth more than most buyers expect, particularly on a self-hosted install.

## Who should buy it

Support teams of 25 agents and up who want complete functionality at every price point, and organisations that need the option of self-hosting without a cost penalty — regulated industries, government, and companies with data residency requirements. At 100-plus agents the effective rate is genuinely competitive.

It suits badly very small teams, where $25 an agent and an $899 annual commitment compare poorly with monthly per-seat alternatives, teams sitting just past a band boundary, and anyone whose evaluation is driven by AI deflection. Check helpspot.com for current bands and plan your hiring against the boundaries at 10, 25 and 50.
