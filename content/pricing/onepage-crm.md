## Nothing here is metered by contacts — and that is the point

Almost every CRM on this site charges you more as your database grows. Agile CRM
steps you up at 10,000 contacts. EngageBay stops you at 500 on its cheapest paid
plan. OnePageCRM does none of that: unlimited contacts, deals, custom fields and
tags on every tier including the cheapest one.

So what drives the bill? Two things: **seats**, and **email throughput**.

Seats are the honest, linear part. Five reps on Professional annual is
$49.75/month. Fifteen reps is $149.25. No bundles, no minimums, no tricks.

Email throughput is where the tiers actually bite. Professional connects one
email account per user and caps sends at 250 a day. Business raises that to 450
and adds bulk send scheduling. Max allows two accounts and 1,000 sends a day.
For a CRM built around daily follow-up discipline, that cap is the feature most
likely to force your hand — a rep working a 300-touch prospecting sequence is
on Business whether they want the other features or not.

## Cost at three team sizes, annual billing

| | Professional | Business | Max |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 user | $9.95/mo | $19.95/mo | $29.95/mo |
| 6 users | $59.70/mo | $119.70/mo | $179.70/mo |
| 20 users | $199/mo | $399/mo | $599/mo |

Twenty seats of Business at $399/month is roughly what six seats of HubSpot
Sales Professional costs. That comparison is the whole pitch: OnePageCRM does
less, and charges accordingly, and if what you need is follow-up discipline
rather than revenue intelligence, doing less is not a defect.

## Where the tier jumps earn their money

**Professional to Business ($10/user/month).** Buy it for send capacity and
scheduling, not for storage. The 250-to-450 daily email jump and the ability to
queue a batch for the morning are the two things you cannot work around on
Professional. Going from 10GB to 20GB per user is nice; it is not $120 a year
nice on its own.

**Business to Max ($10/user/month).** This one has a narrower audience. Two
connected email accounts per rep matters if your people work both a personal
and a shared inbox. Unlimited automation workflows matter if you have outgrown
Business's limits, which most six-person teams have not. If you are considering
Max mainly because it is the top tier, you are probably buying headroom you
will not use.

Worth saying plainly: neither jump unlocks better reporting. OnePageCRM's
analytics are basic at $9.95 and basic at $29.95. Paying up does not fix that,
and if forecasting is your gap, this is the wrong product rather than the wrong
tier.

## Annual billing is unusually worth it here

Four months free is a 34% discount, well above the 10-20% typical in this
category. Re:amaze gives 10%. EngageBay gives 8% for a year. OnePageCRM giving
a third off is a real incentive rather than a nudge.

The trade-off is the usual one — a year of commitment on a tool you may
outgrow. But OnePageCRM's own framing is pay-as-you-go with cancellation
anytime and no long-term contract, and there are no setup fees or support
charges to recover on exit. If the product fits after a trial, the annual rate
is the sensible default.

## The teams this suits

Field sales reps, consultants, brokers and small SMB sales teams with short,
straightforward cycles — people whose actual problem is "I forgot to follow up
with sixteen people this week" rather than "I need to model pipeline coverage by
segment." At $9.95 a seat with unlimited contacts, it is one of the best value
propositions for that specific job.

It punishes complex B2B teams. Multi-stakeholder deals, custom approval flows,
territory management and revenue forecasting are all thin here at every price
point, and no tier fixes it. It also punishes heavy outbound operations, who
will find themselves on Max for send limits while paying for automation depth
that a dedicated sequencing tool does better.
