## The headline price is a single-user price

ProProfs advertises Essentials at $23.88 and Business at $47.88 per user per year. Those are extraordinary numbers — under five dollars a month for a help desk — and they come with a condition that reframes everything: both are single-user plans.

The first tier that includes a team is Business Complete Elite at $179.97 a month for three seats. That is roughly $60 per seat per month, which is a normal mid-market help desk price and about 150 times the annual rate of Business.

Neither number is dishonest. But a buyer comparing "ProProfs at $47.88 a year" against "Zendesk at $55 a seat a month" is comparing a solo licence to a team one.

## Ticket ceilings do the metering

| Tier | Annual cost | Seats | Tickets/month |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | 1 | 100 |
| Essentials | $23.88 | 1 | 1,000 |
| Business | $47.88 | 1 | 6,000 |
| Business Complete Elite | $2,159.64 | 3 | 15,000 |
| Customer Delight Suite | $5,988 | Unlimited | 60,000 |

Volume is the primary lever throughout. A solo operator handling 900 tickets a month pays $23.88 a year; the same operator at 1,100 tickets pays $47.88. Both are trivially cheap.

The cliff is between rows three and four: $47.88 a year to $2,159.64 a year. There is no intermediate tier, so a two-person team doing 8,000 tickets a month has no proportionate option and lands on a plan sized for three.

## Omnichannel is a four-tier jump

WhatsApp, Facebook and the other messaging channels appear only at Business Complete Elite.

For a consumer-facing brand in 2026, that is not an advanced feature — it is where customers actually are. So any such brand's entry price is $179.97 a month regardless of team size or volume, and the cheap tiers are irrelevant to them entirely.

The same applies to SSO and SAML, which sit at the same level. Companies with an identity-provider requirement — most above about fifty employees — are on a $2,160-a-year floor.

## The free tier deserves credit

Amid all that, the free plan is unusually good. A hundred tickets a month, one user, forever, with email ticketing, tags, canned responses, custom fields, attachments, business hours, ticket ratings and basic reporting.

Most competitors' free tiers are 14-day trials in disguise. This one is a genuine product for a freelancer, consultant or one-person support function, and it does not expire.

For that user, ProProfs is arguably the best value in the market — and the paid Essentials upgrade at under $2 a month is a rounding error if volume grows.

## The AI story tracks the tiers

AI arrives gradually: 50 response drafts a month plus categorisation and grammar checking on Essentials; a first-response generator and tone rewriting on Business; emotion detection and data redaction at Business Complete Elite.

That is a sensible progression, but note that none of it is autonomous resolution. ProProfs' AI assists agents rather than replacing them, which is a different proposition from Richpanel's per-conversation agents or eDesk's per-outcome resolution.

For a solo operator, assistive AI at $47.88 a year is excellent value. For a team building a deflection business case, it is not the same product category.

## Who should buy it

Solo operators, freelancers and one-person support functions — the free tier and the sub-$50-a-year paid tiers are close to unmatched value for that user, and the AI assistance is genuinely useful at that price.

Also organisations wanting the full ProProfs bundle — help desk, live chat, knowledge base, surveys and Qualaroo — where the $499-a-month Customer Delight Suite is efficient against buying four products.

It suits badly two- to five-person teams, who fall into the gap between a single-user tier and a three-seat tier priced 45 times higher, and any consumer brand needing WhatsApp or Facebook channels, which start at $179.97 a month. Check proprofsdesk.com for current tiers and confirm seat entitlements before comparing the headline rates to per-seat competitors.
