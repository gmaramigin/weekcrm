## A price list built around a philosophy

Most CRMs are databases you can sell from. noCRM.io is a follow-up system that happens to store contacts, and its pricing reflects that narrowness honestly: €12, €20, €32 per user per month on annual terms, with no enterprise tier and no custom quote.

The absence of a seat minimum matters more than it looks. Scoro requires five users, Sellsy two, Coevera three. noCRM will sell you one seat at €12 and pro-rate it if you leave. For a two-person operation, that difference is the entire comparison — €24/month against €99.50 at Scoro's floor.

What you give up is breadth. There is no marketing automation, no service desk, no reporting suite. If the question is "which of my leads has no next step scheduled", noCRM answers it better than almost anything at this price. If the question is anything else, you are shopping in the wrong category.

## The single-plan rule

The most consequential clause in noCRM's pricing is not a number. Every user on an account must be on the same edition.

That converts individual requirements into account-wide costs. A ten-person team on Expert pays €200/month. The moment one team lead needs the roles-and-permissions model, the whole account moves to Dream Team at €320/month — a 60% increase for one person's need.

Model this before you commit. The practical question is not "which edition do most of my team need" but "which edition does my most demanding user need", because that is the one you will pay for across every seat.

## What the three editions are for

**Starter** at €12 is one pipeline, lead capture, next-action reminders, email integration and the mobile apps. It is a complete implementation of noCRM's core idea and nothing else. For a solo seller or a pair working a single motion, it is enough and it is cheap.

**Expert** at €20 is where most teams land. Unlimited pipelines, quotes and invoices, full customisation and the 3,000+ integration catalogue. The quoting capability alone moves a lot of buyers up — it is the difference between tracking a deal and closing one inside the tool.

**Dream Team** at €32 adds advanced automation plus roles and permission management. It is a team-governance tier rather than a feature tier, and it is bought for structure, not capability.

## What it costs at scale

| Team size | Starter | Expert | Dream Team |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 user | €12/mo | €20/mo | €32/mo |
| 5 users | €60/mo | €100/mo | €160/mo |
| 10 users | €120/mo | €200/mo | €320/mo |

Even at the top, twenty-five users on Dream Team is €800/month — roughly what ten Salesforce Sales Cloud seats cost. noCRM is genuinely inexpensive at every size, and the ceiling is set by capability rather than by price.

## The missing free tier

noCRM has no permanent free plan, only a trial. In a market where Zoho, HubSpot, Bitrix24 and Attio all offer a free tier a small team can sit on indefinitely, that is a real competitive disadvantage at the moment of evaluation.

The counter-argument is that free tiers in this category are usually crippled to the point of being a demo, and that €12 is close enough to free that the distinction is academic. That holds for a funded team and does not hold for a solo founder comparing against a $0 alternative. Judge it on whether the follow-up discipline is worth €144 a year to you — for most people who actually work leads by phone, it is.

## Who should buy it

Outbound and field sales teams whose failure mode is forgetting to follow up. Agencies chasing inbound enquiries. Small teams that tried a full CRM, never populated it, and want something their reps will actually update. The no-minimum pricing makes it viable at one seat, which almost nothing else in this comparison manages.

It is the wrong tool for relationship-led businesses that need a rich contact history, for anyone wanting marketing automation, and for teams that need per-user plan flexibility — the single-edition rule makes mixed requirements expensive. Check nocrm.io/pricing for current rates before budgeting.
