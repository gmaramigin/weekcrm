## The rate card changed in May 2026

Creatio moved to a new pricing model on 1 May 2026, introducing the Unlimited tier and AI Studio alongside it. Any comparison article, review-site listing or internal quote predating that date describes a structure that no longer exists.

The current shape is three platform tiers — Growth at $25 per user per month, Enterprise at $55, Unlimited at $85 — with Sales, Marketing and Service available as $15-per-user modules on the lower two and bundled into Unlimited.

Two numbers alongside those tiers do more work than any of them: a $10,000 annual minimum and a three-year standard contract.

## The minimum is the real entry price

At $25 a user, $10,000 a year implies about 34 users. Below that headcount you are paying the floor regardless.

| Users | List cost at Growth | Actual (with $10k floor) | Effective per user/month |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | $3,000 | $10,000 | $83 |
| 20 | $6,000 | $10,000 | $42 |
| 34 | $10,200 | $10,200 | $25 |
| 100 | $30,000 | $30,000 | $25 |

A ten-person company evaluating Creatio at "$25 a user" is actually looking at $83 a user. That is not a bad price for what Creatio does, but it is a different conversation, and one worth having before the demo rather than after.

Creatio is a mid-market and enterprise platform. The $25 tier is a genuine rate for organisations of the right size, not an SMB on-ramp.

## Modules invert the tier logic

The module structure produces a counterintuitive result on the lower tiers.

| Configuration | Per user/month |
|---|---|
| Growth + 1 module | $40 |
| Growth + 2 modules | $55 |
| Growth + 3 modules | $70 |
| Enterprise (no modules) | $55 |
| Enterprise + 3 modules | $100 |
| Unlimited (all 3 included) | $85 |

Growth plus all three modules costs $70 for the least capable platform tier. Unlimited costs $85 and includes both the full platform and all three modules.

Fifteen dollars is a small premium for a substantially better foundation. Any buyer who wants Sales, Marketing and Service together should be comparing Unlimited against Enterprise-plus-modules at $100 — and Unlimited wins that comparison outright.

The genuine Growth buyer is one who wants the no-code platform with at most one module.

## Three years is an architecture decision

The standard contract is three years with a $10,000 annual minimum, so the smallest realistic commitment is around $30,000.

That is not unusual for enterprise software, but it changes what the evaluation has to prove. You are not deciding whether a CRM's interface suits your sales team. You are deciding whether to build three years of business process on a no-code platform.

Which means the proof of concept should test the thing you will actually rely on: can your team build and modify workflows without engineering help? That is Creatio's core claim and its main differentiator against Salesforce and Dynamics. If it holds for your team, the price is well justified. If your processes end up needing a partner for every change, you have bought a conventional CRM at platform prices with a three-year lock-in.

## Where it sits against the majors

Per user, Creatio undercuts its obvious competitors. Enterprise at $55 is below comparable Salesforce Sales Cloud and Dynamics 365 tiers, and Unlimited at $85 with three modules included is competitive against either at similar functional scope.

What you trade away is ecosystem. Salesforce's AppExchange, partner depth and hiring pool are far larger; a Salesforce administrator is a job title you can recruit for, a Creatio one less so.

What you gain is composability. Creatio's no-code process designer is genuinely stronger than either incumbent's, and for organisations whose competitive advantage lives in unusual internal processes, that is the entire argument.

## Implementation is the missing line

No per-user figure captures what it costs to implement a no-code business process platform.

Configuration, data migration, integration with existing systems, training, and — for most mid-market buyers — a partner to lead the work are all real and all outside the subscription. Depending on scope, they routinely match or exceed the first year of licensing.

Get an implementation quote alongside the licence quote. Evaluating one without the other is how three-year platform commitments go wrong.

## Who should buy it

Mid-market and enterprise organisations of roughly 35 users and up, with process complexity that off-the-shelf CRMs handle badly, and the appetite to build on a no-code platform over a multi-year horizon. For that buyer Creatio is well priced against Salesforce and Dynamics and genuinely differentiated.

It suits badly small businesses, who cannot reach the $10,000 minimum without paying three times the headline rate, and any organisation wanting to start small and expand — the three-year contract forecloses that. Check creatio.com for current terms, compare Unlimited directly against Enterprise-plus-modules, and quote implementation before signing.
