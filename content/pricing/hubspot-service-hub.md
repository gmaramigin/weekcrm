## Seats, not tickets, decide what you pay

Service Hub does not meter tickets, contacts or resolutions. It meters people — and specifically, which *kind* of person. HubSpot splits access into Core Seats, Service Seats and free View-Only Seats, and the edition you buy determines which of those a working agent must hold. That single design decision is the whole pricing story.

On Starter, a Core Seat at $20/month is enough. The moment you want SLAs, a customer portal, a real knowledge base or feedback surveys, you are on Professional, and every agent who touches those features needs a Service Professional Seat at $100/month. Not a $20 Core Seat with an upgrade tacked on — a different, five-times-more-expensive seat. Teams routinely budget for "Professional" as though it were a 25% step up from Starter and then discover it is a 5x step up per working agent.

The free View-Only Seat is the one piece of genuine generosity here. Executives, finance people and account managers who need to read tickets but never respond cost nothing. Auditing your seat list and demoting passive users to view-only is the single highest-leverage thing you can do to your Service Hub invoice.

## The Starter-to-Professional wall

| Team | Starter (Core Seats) | Professional (Service Seats) | Annual gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 agents | $100/mo | $500/mo | $4,800 |
| 20 agents | $400/mo | $2,000/mo | $19,200 |
| 50 agents | $1,000/mo | $5,000/mo | $48,000 |

That gap is why the interesting question is never "Starter or Professional?" in the abstract. It is "how many of my people genuinely need SLA enforcement and the customer portal?" If the answer is four out of twenty, you do not need to move everyone — you need a mixed-seat setup, and HubSpot supports that.

Professional to Enterprise is a much softer decision. $150 versus $100 per seat buys custom objects, granular permissions and playbooks. For a 20-agent team that is $12,000 a year plus the $3,500 mandatory onboarding, so the first year costs about $15,500 more than Professional. Worth it if you have a compliance requirement around permissions or genuinely non-standard data. Not worth it for better dashboards.

## Contracts, credits and the AI line item

Enterprise is billed annually — HubSpot's catalog lists no monthly option at that tier. Starter and Professional carry monthly list prices of $20 and $100 per seat, with annual prepay discounted below those figures; verify the current annual rate at checkout rather than trusting any third-party number, including this one.

The newer wrinkle is HubSpot Credits. Breeze AI features draw down a monthly credit allowance that resets and does not roll over. Light AI use is effectively free. A team that leans on AI drafting and autonomous resolution across thousands of tickets a month will find credits become a variable cost that behaves nothing like the tidy per-seat table above.

## Who this model rewards

Service Hub is priced for teams already paying HubSpot for sales or marketing. If your CRM is HubSpot, adding Service Hub means support sees full customer history with zero integration work, and the per-seat cost is easy to justify.

It punishes two groups. First, standalone support teams with no other HubSpot footprint — you are paying platform prices for a help desk, and Zendesk or Freshdesk will land closer to your actual requirement. Second, large teams where everyone needs Professional features: 50 agents at $100 is $60,000 a year for ticketing, which is real enterprise money without a real enterprise procurement conversation.

The sweet spot is a 5-to-25 person support function inside a HubSpot shop, running a mix of Professional Service Seats for frontline agents and free view-only access for everyone else.

List prices move. HubSpot restructured Service Hub seat pricing in 2024 and has adjusted credit allowances since. Treat [hubspot.com/pricing/service](https://www.hubspot.com/pricing/service) and HubSpot's product catalog as the authority, and get any onboarding fee in writing before you sign.
