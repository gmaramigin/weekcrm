## The cap that actually moves you up a tier

Read Monday CRM's pricing page and it looks like a straightforward per-seat ladder: $12, $17, $28. Model an actual sales team on it and you find the seat price is rarely what determines your plan. The record ceiling is.

Basic allows 1,000 active contacts and deals. Standard raises that to 10,000. Pro goes to 100,000. A four-person sales team importing an existing prospect list and running normal outbound will clear 1,000 records within months, sometimes within weeks of a data migration. At that point the upgrade is not a choice about features — it is a wall.

The second meter is automation actions: 250 a month on Standard, 25,000 on Pro. The gap between those two numbers is enormous, and the reason is that 250 is a taster. One automation that fires on every stage change across an active pipeline can consume the whole monthly allowance without anyone noticing until things stop firing. If automations are central to how you want to run the pipeline, Standard is a trap and Pro is the real plan.

## Three seats whether you want them or not

Monday enforces a 3-seat minimum on every paid plan. A solo consultant pays $36/month on Basic. A two-person founding team pays the same. This is easy to miss when comparing entry prices against tools like Attio or Close that will genuinely sell you one seat.

Here is what the ladder looks like at realistic team sizes, annual billing:

| Seats | Basic ($12) | Standard ($17) | Pro ($28) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 (minimum) | $36/mo | $51/mo | $84/mo |
| 10 | $120/mo | $170/mo | $280/mo |
| 25 | $300/mo | $425/mo | $700/mo |

Note the shape: Standard is only 42% more than Basic, while Pro is 65% more than Standard. The expensive step is the second one, and it is the one the record and automation caps push you toward.

## Where the money is well spent

Basic to Standard is the easiest upgrade decision in this product. Five dollars a seat buys automations, integrations, a 10x record ceiling and the standard AI agents for outreach and meeting prep. There is almost no scenario where a working sales team is better off on Basic — the 5-column board limit alone makes serious pipeline customisation impossible. Treat Basic as a price anchor rather than a plan.

Standard to Pro is a genuine decision. You are paying $11 more per seat for sales forecasting, the AI workflow builder, 100x the automation allowance and 10x the records. If your automations are simple and your database is under 10,000 records, Standard is enough and Pro is premature. If either meter is under pressure, Pro pays for itself by removing the thing that keeps breaking.

Enterprise is sales-negotiated with no published number. It buys unlimited contacts, unlimited automations, 24/7 prioritised support and the enterprise security package. Do not expect a discount on a small seat count — this tier is priced for volume and governance requirements, not for teams that merely want the higher caps.

## The commitment maths

The annual discount here is steeper than most competitors: roughly a third off, and it applies at every tier. A 10-seat Pro team pays $280/month annually versus $410 monthly. Over a year that gap is $1,560. If you are reasonably confident in the tool, monthly billing is an expensive form of optionality.

There is one wrinkle worth planning around. Because both the seat minimum and the annual discount push you toward committing early, and because the record cap pushes you upward mid-contract, teams frequently sign an annual Basic or Standard deal and then need to upgrade before it expires. Model your record growth before you pick the tier, not after.

## Who it fits

Monday CRM is a good buy for SMB teams that already think in boards and want their pipeline to look and behave like the rest of their work. The visual model is genuinely easier to onboard than a traditional CRM, and Standard at $17 is competitively priced for what it includes.

It works badly for two groups. Solo operators and two-person teams pay a 50% or 100% surcharge thanks to the seat floor. And data-heavy sales orgs — anyone with a large contact database or complex automation needs — find themselves on Pro at $28/seat, where the comparison against purpose-built sales CRMs stops flattering Monday.

List prices, caps and plan contents change; Monday has restructured its CRM tiers before. Verify against monday.com's own pricing page before you commit to a contract.
