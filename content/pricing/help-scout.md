## Seats are the base, AI is the variable

Help Scout's list price is per user per month, and that part is easy to model: headcount times tier price. What makes the bill move unpredictably is AI Answers, which is metered at $0.75 per resolution and sits outside your subscription entirely.

That structure is deliberate and, to Help Scout's credit, honestly labelled — you only pay when the AI actually closes something. But it inverts the usual budgeting exercise. A 12-seat Standard team pays $300/mo for seats. If AI Answers resolves 600 conversations that month, the AI line is $450 — more than half again on top of the seat cost. Support volume, not headcount, is what you are actually forecasting.

Set a spending cap on day one. Help Scout offers them, and without one your worst support month is also your worst invoice.

## The 10-seat wall on Pro

The gap between Plus at $45/user/mo and Pro at $75/user/mo looks like a $30 decision. It is not. Pro imposes a 10-seat minimum, so a 6-person team that wants Pro's unlimited inboxes and dedicated account manager writes a cheque for 10 seats: $750 a month instead of the $270 they were paying on Plus.

For teams already at 10+ agents the minimum is invisible and Pro's 50 included light users — read-only or occasional collaborators from other departments — are a real saving compared to buying them full seats elsewhere. Below 10 agents, Pro is a trap. Buy the extra inboxes on Plus at $10/mo each instead; you would need to add 30 of them before the maths flips.

## What 5, 20 and 50 agents actually cost

| Agents | Standard ($21) | Plus ($37.80) | Pro ($63) |

| 5 | $105/mo | $189/mo | not available (10-seat min) |
| 20 | $420/mo | $756/mo | $1,260/mo |
| 50 | capped at 25 users | $1,890/mo | $3,150/mo |

Two structural limits show up in that table. Standard caps at 25 users and Plus at 50, so growth forces a tier change independent of features. If you are hiring toward 30 agents, you are going to Plus whether or not you want custom fields.

## Annual vs monthly

The annual discount is 16%, applied to every paid tier, and there is no contract length beyond the year. Monthly billing exists on all plans with no penalty other than the higher rate. Add-ons follow the same split: extra inboxes are $10/mo annual versus $12/mo monthly, extra Docs sites $20 versus $24.

If your headcount is stable, take the annual price — 16% is at the generous end for this category. If you run a seasonal support team that doubles in Q4, monthly billing costs more per seat but lets you shed seats in January, which usually wins.

## Who this suits

Help Scout prices well for small and mid-sized teams that want one or two shared inboxes and a knowledge base, and who value the email-like interface over configurability. A 10-person SaaS support team on Plus at $378/mo is paying a fair rate for a tool their agents will not need training on.

It prices badly for two groups. Multi-brand companies get nickel-and-dimed on inboxes and Docs sites — if you support five brands, price Zendesk or Front against the add-on stack before committing. And high-volume, low-complexity queues get punished by per-resolution AI pricing; if your tickets are mostly "where is my order", a ticket-volume model like Gorgias may cost less for the same deflection.

The one genuinely underrated part of the pricing is Pro's 50 light users. Engineering and product teammates who need to read tickets and comment occasionally are usually a hidden seat tax on other platforms.

## A note on accuracy

These figures come from Help Scout's own pricing page as of July 2026. Vendors revise list prices, quietly change seat minimums, and re-bundle AI more often than any directory can track — treat helpscout.com/pricing as the authority and confirm the AI Answers rate before you build a forecast on it.
