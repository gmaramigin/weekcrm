## A small product at a small price

SupportBee charges $17 per seat annually on Startup and $21 on Enterprise. Both include unlimited email inboxes and unlimited tickets.

That is the entire pricing model. There is no AI metering, no conversation cap, no channel add-ons, no volume band. Seats are the only variable, and the two tiers are four dollars apart.

In 2026 that simplicity is unusual enough to be a feature in itself. It is also a signal: SupportBee is not competing on capability breadth, and the price reflects a deliberately narrow product rather than a discount on a broad one.

## The tier gap is structural, not scalar

| | Startup | Enterprise |
|---|---|---|
| Annual per seat | $17 | $21 |
| Monthly per seat | $20 | $25 |
| Email inboxes | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Tickets | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Knowledge bases | 1 | Multiple |
| Teams | 1 | Multiple |
| Business hours | — | Yes |
| Customer portal | — | Yes |
| Enterprise integrations | — | Yes |

Nothing in that table is about size. A fifty-person team supporting one product on one schedule with no portal is fine on Startup. A four-person team supporting two brands with an SLA is on Enterprise.

That means the tier decision is made on the first day of evaluation, from the org chart, and rarely changes. Useful for budgeting — and a reason to check the Enterprise triggers carefully before assuming $17 applies.

## Business hours is the sleeper requirement

The most consequential Enterprise-only feature is the least discussed one. Without business hours, SLA and response-time calculations run against wall-clock time, which means a ticket arriving Friday evening accrues breach time all weekend.

Any team with a contractual response commitment needs this. It is not an enterprise nicety; it is the difference between meaningful and meaningless SLA reporting.

If you have SLAs, your price is $21, not $17.

## Where the category has moved past it

SupportBee has no AI. No autonomous resolution, no suggested replies, no summarisation, no deflection metric.

Against the 2026 field that is a real gap. BoldDesk bundles a full AI platform from roughly $10 an agent. HelpDesk.com includes AI agents at $19. eDesk meters resolutions at $0.99. All three are in the same price neighbourhood as SupportBee and all three offer something it does not.

The counter-argument is legitimate but narrow: for a small team where every ticket is genuinely bespoke — technical B2B support, professional services, complex products — deflection rates are low and AI adds cost without removing work. Those teams are exactly SupportBee's market, and for them the missing AI is not a loss.

Everyone else should price a competitor that has it.

## Annual billing is worth taking

The saving is 18% to 19% — $3 a seat on Startup, $4 on Enterprise. On a ten-person team that is $360 to $480 a year.

Not transformative, but SupportBee's whole appeal is a low, stable, predictable bill, and paying 19% extra for month-to-month flexibility on a tool you have already decided to use is inconsistent with that. Trial it, decide, and commit annually.

## The honest competitive frame

Three products occupy this space, and the choice between them is about operations rather than features.

FreeScout self-hosted does substantially the same job for a one-time $50 in modules plus hosting — better value by an order of magnitude, if you have someone to patch a server.

Help Scout does considerably more — richer workflows, better reporting, a stronger knowledge base, AI features — at roughly $50 to $65 a seat.

SupportBee is the hosted, no-maintenance, no-frills middle at $17. That is a coherent position, and for a team that wants a shared inbox to just work without a server or a subscription three times larger than needed, it is the right answer.

## Who should buy it

Small to mid-sized teams wanting a straightforward hosted shared inbox with unlimited tickets and inboxes, no metering and no surprises. Especially B2B and technical support teams whose ticket mix does not lend itself to AI deflection anyway.

It suits badly anyone who needs live chat, social channels, an AI layer or deep automation — all absent — and anyone with SLAs who assumes the $17 tier applies, when business hours puts them on $21. Check supportbee.com for current rates and start with the 14-day trial.
