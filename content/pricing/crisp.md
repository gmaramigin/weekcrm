## Flat workspace pricing changes what you optimise for

Every other tool on this site makes you count agents. Crisp makes you count
almost nothing. Mini bundles four seats at $45 a month, Essentials ten at $95,
Plus twenty or more at $295, and anyone beyond the bundle is $10. Your bill
does not meaningfully respond to hiring.

The consequence is that the upgrade trigger is a feature, not a headcount. You
move from Mini to Essentials because you want a knowledge base, and from
Essentials to Plus because you want ticketing or white labeling. Nobody at
Crisp is waiting for your support team to grow.

## Cost per agent, which is where this gets interesting

| Team size | Crisp Plus ($295 + $10/extra seat) | Per agent | LiveChat Business ($79/agent) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 10 | $295/mo (seats included) | $29.50 | $790/mo |
| 20 | $295/mo | $14.75 | $1,580/mo |
| 35 | $445/mo | $12.71 | $2,765/mo |

At ten agents Crisp Plus is already less than half the per-seat alternative. At
thirty-five it is roughly a sixth. This is the entire argument for the product
and the reason it turns up on every Intercom-alternative list — the model
degrades gracefully as you grow instead of punishing you for it.

Run the same comparison at three agents and the gap narrows sharply: Crisp
Essentials is $95 while three LiveChat Team seats are $147. Still cheaper, but
not the landslide. Crisp's pricing advantage is a function of team size, and
below five people it is a preference rather than an obvious win.

## AI credits are the variable you have to watch

The flat fee is not entirely flat. Crisp meters AI usage in credits, with each
tier carrying an allowance: roughly $5 worth on Mini (about 90 automated
conversations), $25 on Essentials (about 450), $75 on Plus (about 1,350).
Automate past your allowance and you buy more credits.

For a support operation leaning hard on bots this is the line item that moves,
and it deserves a forecast. Estimate your automated conversation volume, map it
against the tier allowances, and price the overage — otherwise you have
budgeted a fixed cost for something that behaves like a usage cost. It is worth
noting the direction of travel here differs from Tidio, where AI resolution
removes conversations from the bill; on Crisp, AI resolution adds to it.

## The gap between $95 and $295

There is nothing between Essentials and Plus, and the jump is 210%. Plus adds
ticketing, unlimited task automations, white labeling, 100+ integrations,
advanced analytics and triple the AI credits. That is a coherent bundle for a
support department, and complete overkill for a team of six that wanted one
feature from the list.

If you are eyeing Plus for a single capability, price the workaround first. A
$200-a-month increase to get white labeling is worth it for an agency
reselling support; it is not worth it because you would prefer your widget
without a badge.

## Monthly, yearly, and the discounts most people miss

Yearly billing is available on every paid tier alongside monthly. Crisp also
publishes 50% off for nonprofits and students, and 20% off when you run three
or more additional workspaces on Essentials or Plus — relevant if you operate
several brands or client accounts, since the multi-workspace discount stacks on
what is already the cheapest per-agent model in the category.

## Who this model suits

Crisp is built for support teams of eight to forty people who want predictable
costs and do not want to have a budget conversation every time they hire. It is
also strong for agencies and multi-brand operators, where the workspace
discount compounds. E-commerce and SaaS teams running a mix of inbound support
and proactive messaging get the most out of the omnichannel inbox at the
Essentials tier.

It suits small teams less well than the free plan suggests. Two seats without
an email inbox is a narrow product, and a three-person team paying $95 for
Essentials is spending more per head than they would on a lightweight per-seat
tool. It also punishes anyone with heavy automation and a small team, because
the AI credits scale with bot volume while the bundled allowance scales with
tier.

## Prices move

Everything above is Crisp's published pricing as of July 2026. AI credit
allowances in particular are the kind of thing vendors revise quietly — check
crisp.chat before you build a forecast around a credit number.
