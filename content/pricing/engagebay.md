## The contact cap is the price, not the seat rate

Read EngageBay's rate card left to right and it looks like a per-seat product.
It is not really. The number that decides which row you sit on is **contacts
stored in the account**: 250 on Free, 500 on Basic, 5,000 on Growth, 50,000 on
Pro.

That is unusually aggressive at the bottom. Five hundred contacts is a few
months of a working sales pipeline, or one trade show. Once you cross it,
Basic's $14.99 becomes Growth's $64.99 — a 4.3x jump with nothing in between.
There is no $30 tier to soften the landing.

So the honest way to budget EngageBay is: pick the tier your *list* needs, then
multiply by seats. Not the other way round.

## What EngageBay actually costs at three team sizes

| | 1 user | 5 users | 15 users |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic (500 contacts) | $14.99/mo | $74.95/mo | $224.85/mo |
| Growth (5,000 contacts) | $64.99/mo | $324.95/mo | $974.85/mo |
| Pro (50,000 contacts) | $119.99/mo | $599.95/mo | $1,799.85/mo |

The table shows the model's weak spot clearly. A fifteen-person team that needs
5,000 contacts pays $974.85/month — the same 5,000 contacts a solo consultant
gets for $64.99. Contact capacity is shared but priced per head, so large teams
with small databases subsidise nothing and pay a lot.

Compare that with HubSpot's Starter tier, where you buy marketing contacts
separately from seats. Below about six users EngageBay is dramatically cheaper.
Above roughly fifteen, the all-in-one savings narrow considerably.

## Growth versus Pro

Growth to Pro nearly doubles the per-user rate for 10x contacts, web analytics,
SSO, custom reporting and a dedicated account manager. Three reasons make it
worth it, and none of them are feature FOMO.

If your list is genuinely past 5,000 contacts, you have no choice. If you need
SSO because IT requires it, you have no choice. And if you have already been
burned by a support ticket sitting for two days, the phone support and named
account manager are worth real money on a platform that runs your marketing,
sales and helpdesk simultaneously.

What is *not* worth $55/user/month is custom reporting alone. EngageBay's
analytics are the weakest part of the product at every tier; buying Pro to fix
reporting is buying a slightly better version of the thing you were unhappy
with.

## Three billing terms, three different headline numbers

EngageBay quotes monthly, annual (about 8% off) and biennial (about 15% off).
That is why you will see Basic described as $14.99, $13.79 and $12.74 across
different sites — all three are real, they are just different commitments.

On Growth the spread is meaningful: $64.99 monthly versus $55.24 biennial is
$9.75 per user per month. On a five-person team that is $585 a year. But two
years is a genuine bet on a category where SMBs routinely switch tools inside
eighteen months. Take the annual rate unless you have already run EngageBay for
a year and know it fits.

## Who this pricing rewards

It rewards small teams with big ambitions and modest databases — a three-person
startup running email nurture, deal pipelines and a support inbox for under
$200/month is getting something that would cost four figures at HubSpot. The
free tier is unusually complete and makes a real pilot possible without a
credit card.

It punishes teams that are people-heavy and contact-light: agencies, services
firms, anyone with fifteen staff and a small client list. It also punishes
anyone whose list grows in steps, because the 500-to-5,000 gap means there is
no gentle upgrade path. You are on $14.99 until the day you are on $64.99.

## Verify before you buy

These figures come from EngageBay's own pricing page as of July 2026, for the
All-in-One suite. The individual Bay products are priced separately and were not
verified here. All-in-one vendors in this bracket reprice more often than
enterprise ones — confirm at
[engagebay.com/pricing](https://www.engagebay.com/pricing) before you commit.
