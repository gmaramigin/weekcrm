## Two pricing models in one price list

Follow Up Boss does something unusual: it changes billing model between tiers. Grow is straightforward per-user pricing at $69/month. Pro and Platform are flat bundles — $499 for ten seats, $1,000 for thirty — with cheap incremental users attached.

That switch is the whole story of this price list, because it creates crossover points where adding agents makes your cost per agent drop sharply rather than rise. Most teams find them by accident, months after they should have moved.

The other half of the story is calling. On Grow it is a $39/user add-on. On Pro and Platform it is unlimited and included. For a real estate team, calling is not optional, so the honest Grow price is $108/user monthly or $91 annual — and that number changes every comparison you might make.

## Where the crossovers sit

| Agents | Grow + calling (annual) | Pro (annual) | Platform (annual) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | $273/mo | $416/mo | $833/mo |
| 6 | $546/mo | $416/mo | $833/mo |
| 10 | $910/mo | $416/mo | $833/mo |
| 20 | $1,820/mo | $826/mo | $833/mo |
| 30 | $2,730/mo | $1,236/mo | $833/mo |

Two thresholds fall out. Pro overtakes Grow at roughly six agents — and at ten, Grow costs more than twice as much for the same capability. Platform overtakes Pro at about twenty-one agents, after which every additional seat costs $17 instead of $41.

A twenty-agent team on Grow with calling is paying $1,820 a month for something Platform delivers at $833. That is not a rounding error; it is a $12,000 annual mistake, and it is the most common one in this product.

## What you get at each level

**Grow** is the working CRM: lead organisation, automated action plans, call, text and email, calendar sync, the mobile app, unlimited contacts and 250+ integrations. For a solo agent or a small partnership it is complete, provided you either add calling or genuinely do not need it.

**Pro** adds the team layer — shared team inboxes, call reporting, advanced AI — plus personalised onboarding and enhanced support. The bundled unlimited calling is the practical difference; the reporting is what team leaders actually use daily.

**Platform** is built for brokerages rather than teams: nested teams within teams, a dedicated Success Manager, priority support, enhanced onboarding. If you run multiple squads under one roof, the nesting is the feature, and the per-seat economics are the reason you stay.

## The cost that is not on the price list

Follow Up Boss is a follow-up engine, not a lead generator. For nearly every team using it, the subscription is a minority of the real spend — the majority goes to Zillow, Realtor.com, Google or Facebook to produce the leads the CRM then works.

That reframes the value question. At $416/month for ten agents on annual Pro, the software costs about $42 per agent while lead acquisition often runs ten to thirty times that. The correct comparison is not "is this CRM cheaper than the alternative", it is "does this CRM convert enough of my lead spend to justify itself" — and for teams with real lead flow, one extra closing a year settles it.

The inverse is equally true. An agent with no lead budget gains very little here, and would be better served by a $25 general-purpose CRM.

## Who should buy it

Producing teams and brokerages with a real lead pipeline and a phone-first follow-up process. The speed-to-lead automation, the shared inboxes and the call reporting are built for exactly that motion, and the flat-rate tiers make growth cheap in a category that usually punishes it.

It fits poorly for solo agents without lead spend, for referral-only businesses where a lightweight contact manager would do, and for anyone hoping the software will produce pipeline on its own. Check followupboss.com/pricing for current rates before committing.
