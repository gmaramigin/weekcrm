## The subscription is not the cost

Vendasta's published tiers — $99, $499, $999 — describe access to a platform, not the cost of running your business on it.

The model is wholesale. Vendasta gives you a white-label marketplace of marketing products (listings management, reputation, SEO, advertising, websites, social) which you resell to local businesses under your own brand at your own prices. You pay the platform fee, and separately you pay wholesale for every product a client actually consumes.

For most agencies, the wholesale product spend exceeds the platform fee substantially. An agency with twenty clients each buying listings management and reputation monitoring is spending far more on those products than the $99 or $499 platform charge, and the margin between wholesale and retail is where the business lives.

Any evaluation that compares Vendasta's $99 against another platform's $99 without modelling wholesale cost is comparing the wrong number.

## Three costs at signup

Vendasta's first invoice is larger than the monthly rate for a specific reason: each tier carries a signing fee equal to one month's subscription.

| Tier | Monthly | Signing fee | Month one |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $99 | $99 | $198 |
| Professional | $499 | $499 | $998 |
| Premium | $999 | $999 | $1,998 |

Then there is transaction processing at 2.9% + $0.30, applied to client payments running through the platform. This is standard card-processing economics rather than a platform markup, but it scales with your revenue rather than your tier. An agency processing $50,000 a month in client billing pays around $1,450 in fees — more than the Professional subscription.

## The gap between Starter and Professional

$99 to $499 is a 404% increase with nothing in between. That is unusually abrupt, and it is the structural decision that defines Vendasta's pricing.

**Starter** is for proving the model: platform access, the white-label marketplace, CRM and sales tools, basic reporting. Month-to-month, which matters — it is the only tier you can leave easily. A solo consultant or a small agency testing whether white-label resale works for their client base belongs here.

**Professional** is for running the model at volume: expanded automation, deeper branding control, task and fulfilment management, a broader catalogue. It requires a twelve-month commitment, which means $5,988 plus the signing fee before you can reconsider.

**Premium** at $999 is the full platform, also on a twelve-month term.

The practical consequence is that the upgrade decision is genuinely a business decision, not a plan change. Before signing Professional, work out how many clients at what margin justify $6,000 a year in platform cost alone, and whether you have them today rather than in your forecast.

## Making the economics work

The arithmetic that determines whether Vendasta pays off is straightforward, and worth doing explicitly.

Take your average client's monthly retail spend on resold products, subtract the wholesale cost, and multiply the margin by your client count. That is your gross platform margin. It has to cover the tier fee, the transaction fees, and your own fulfilment and account management time.

On Starter, that usually means roughly ten paying clients before the platform pays for itself with anything left over. On Professional at $499 plus a year's commitment, the threshold is considerably higher — and agencies that upgrade in anticipation of growth rather than in response to it are the ones who find the commitment uncomfortable.

## What you are actually buying

Not software, primarily. Vendasta's value is that it lets an agency sell marketing services it cannot fulfil itself, under its own brand, with billing and reporting handled. For a local marketing agency whose clients want listings, reviews, SEO and ads but who cannot staff all four disciplines, that is a genuine business model rather than a tool.

The trade-offs are the ones any wholesale arrangement carries. Your product quality is the vendor's product quality. Your margin is bounded by their wholesale price. And your clients are, at the fulfilment layer, someone else's clients — which is fine until you want to differentiate on the work itself.

## Who should buy it

Local marketing agencies, media companies and consultants who want to sell a broad marketing product line under their own brand without building fulfilment teams. Start on Starter, prove the margin with real clients, and only sign Professional when the client count justifies the annual commitment.

It fits poorly for agencies whose differentiation is the quality of their own work rather than breadth of catalogue, and for anyone treating the $99 as the total cost — the wholesale product spend and transaction fees are the larger part of the picture.
