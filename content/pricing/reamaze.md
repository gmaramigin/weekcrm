## What you are actually paying for: conversations, then seats

Re:amaze runs two pricing models side by side and picking the wrong one is the
main way teams overspend here. Starter is a flat $59/month for as many team
members as you like, metered on **responded conversations** — 500 per month.
Basic, Pro and Plus are conventional per-agent plans with no conversation cap
at all.

That means the cost driver flips depending on your shape. A five-person store
handling 300 tickets a month pays $59 total on Starter. The same store at 900
tickets a month cannot stay on Starter and jumps to $145/month on Basic. Volume
is the trigger, not headcount — which is the opposite of how most helpdesk
pricing behaves.

Worth knowing: "responded conversation" means a thread your team replied to
inside the billing month. Spam you never touch does not count. A single
customer who emails you four separate times about four separate orders counts
four times.

## A worked example at three volumes

| Monthly volume | 2 agents | 5 agents | 12 agents |
|---|---|---|---|
| 300 conversations | Starter, $59 | Starter, $59 | Starter, $59 |
| 900 conversations | Basic, $58 | Basic, $145 | Basic, $348 |
| 900 conversations + SMS | Pro, $98 | Pro, $245 | Pro, $588 |

The interesting cell is 2 agents at 900 conversations: Basic actually costs
$58, a dollar less than Starter, and removes the cap entirely. If you are a
two-person team, Starter is only worth it while you stay well under 500.

## Basic to Pro: pay it for channels, not reporting

The $20/agent gap between Basic and Pro is easy to justify if you need any of
three things — a second brand, SMS or voice in the inbox, or a custom support
domain. Those are structural; you cannot work around them.

It is much harder to justify for reporting alone. Pro's "advanced reporting"
is a step up from Basic but not a step up from what Gorgias or Help Scout give
you at similar money. If your only complaint about Basic is the dashboards,
try exporting to a spreadsheet before spending $240/year per agent.

Pro to Plus is a narrower case. Departments, custom roles, shift scheduling and
CSAT ratings are genuinely useful once you are past roughly eight agents
working in rotations. Under that, most teams are paying $20/agent for features
their org chart is too flat to use.

## Billing terms and the annual question

Annual billing knocks 10% off the per-agent tiers. There is no minimum seat
count, no setup fee and no contract — Re:amaze is explicit that plans are
month-to-month and cancellable. That 10% is genuinely modest for this category,
so if you are unsure whether Re:amaze will stick, the monthly premium is a
cheap option to keep.

Starter has no annual discount to speak of, since it is already a flat rate.

## Who this suits and who it punishes

It suits a Shopify or BigCommerce store with a small, sometimes-seasonal team
and steady ticket volume under 500 a month. $59 flat for unlimited helpers is
genuinely hard to beat — Gorgias and Zendesk both charge per seat from the
first user.

It punishes two profiles. First, low-headcount but high-volume teams: two
agents handling 2,000 conversations get no benefit from Starter's unlimited
seats and pay full per-agent rates anyway. Second, teams leaning hard on AI
deflection on the Basic plan, where five included resolutions per user runs out
almost immediately and $0.85 each compounds quietly.
