## Pricing you have to ask for

HappyFox is the unusual case in this directory: a mature, mid-market help desk that has stopped publishing what it costs. Its pricing pages explain the mechanism in detail and then hand you a demo form.

We will not print a number we cannot verify against the vendor. Third-party roundups do quote per-agent figures for Basic, Team and Pro, but they disagree with each other by wide margins — different sources report the same plan at meaningfully different rates, and at least one contradicts itself between its own body copy and its summary table. Publishing any of them would just be laundering someone else's guess. So: quote-based, and here is what to do about it.

## What HappyFox does tell you

Three structural facts are stated plainly on happyfox.com and they are enough to model your costs before you talk to anyone:

You pay a monthly price for each support agent actively managing tickets. Annual billing saves 20%. Agents are capped at 5 on the Basic plan; every higher plan includes unlimited agents. And there is a second model entirely — flat annual fee against a yearly ticket allowance — which HappyFox recommends for teams above roughly 20 to 25 agents or with very high volumes.

That last point is more useful than a price list. HappyFox is openly telling you that its own per-agent model stops making sense at a specific scale, and giving you the threshold.

## The two models pull in opposite directions

Per-agent pricing charges you for people. Ticket-allowance pricing charges you for work. Which is cheaper depends entirely on the ratio between them, and that ratio varies enormously by industry.

A 30-person support team handling low volume — internal IT, enterprise accounts with few but complex tickets — is punished by per-agent pricing and should be on a flat plan. A 6-person team drowning in a consumer ticket queue is the reverse: few seats, huge volume, and a ticket allowance would be the expensive option.

The crossover HappyFox names is 20 to 25 agents, but that is a heuristic derived from average volume. Work out your own tickets-per-agent-per-month figure before the call and you will know which model to push for rather than being steered.

## The product sprawl is the real budgeting risk

HappyFox is not one product. Help Desk, Service Desk, AI Contact Center, HappyFox CRM, HappyFox AI, Autopilot, Assist AI, Chatbot and Workflows each carry their own pricing.

This matters because the demos tend to show the assembled platform while the quote covers one piece of it. The AI capabilities that make HappyFox interesting against Zendesk or Freshdesk — Autopilot resolving tickets autonomously, Assist AI inside Slack or Teams — are separate line items, not features of the help desk licence.

Before you compare HappyFox's quote to a published competitor price, make sure you are comparing the same scope. A Help Desk quote against Freshdesk's all-in tier is not a fair fight in either direction.

## How to run the evaluation

Take the 14-day trial first, with no credit card, and build your actual workflows in it. Then go into the pricing conversation with a specific configuration rather than a headcount, because the quote you receive is shaped by what you ask for.

Ask for the per-agent rate and the flat annual rate side by side at your real numbers. Ask what renewal looks like if you add ten agents. Ask which of the AI products are in scope. And if you are a non-profit, an educational institution or an early-stage startup, ask directly — HappyFox has programmes for all three, including a year of free CRM for eligible startups, and they are not applied unless you raise them.

## Who this model suits

It suits organisations with procurement functions and a willingness to negotiate — mid-market and up, where a sales conversation was going to happen anyway and where the flat-fee option can produce genuinely good value at scale.

It punishes small teams doing self-serve evaluation. If you are five people who want to compare four help desks on a spreadsheet this afternoon, HappyFox makes that impossible, and tools like LiveAgent or Zoho Desk that publish their numbers will win the shortlist by default.

Pricing models change and HappyFox has revised both its plans and its publication policy before. Treat happyfox.com/pricing as the only authority and get every number in writing.
