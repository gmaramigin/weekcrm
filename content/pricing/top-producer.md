## Solo pricing is punishing, team pricing is not

Top Producer Pro is $179 per user per month. That is the highest solo price among mainstream real estate CRMs by a wide margin.

Then the team plans arrive and the model inverts entirely.

| Plan | Monthly | Users | Per user |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pro | $179 | 1 | $179.00 |
| Pro Teams 5 | $399 | 5 | $79.80 |
| Pro Teams 10 | $699 | 10 | $69.90 |
| Pro Teams 25 | $1,199 | 25 | $47.96 |

A 25-agent brokerage pays $47.96 per head — competitive with anything in the category. A solo agent pays $179 for the same software.

The immediate practical consequence: two agents working together should buy Pro Teams 5 at $399, not two Pro seats at $358. For $41 more they get three spare licences and shared contact access. Anyone at three or more agents buying individual Pro seats is simply overpaying.

## The bundles are advertising, priced as software

Pro + Leads starts at $479 a month. Pro + Farming starts at $599. Both vary with volume.

Subtract the $179 Pro licence and the remainder — roughly $300 and $420 respectively — is Facebook and Instagram advertising spend plus campaign management.

That is not a criticism. Managed lead generation has real value for agents who will not run campaigns themselves, and Social Connect and Smart Targeting are competent products.

But it changes the evaluation entirely. You are not comparing CRM prices; you are asking whether Top Producer generates leads more cheaply than you could by spending the same $300 directly, or by hiring a local agency.

The only honest way to answer that is cost per lead and cost per closed transaction. Ask for both, with data from agents in comparable markets, before signing. A CRM that costs $179 is a software decision; one that costs $599 is a marketing budget decision and deserves marketing-budget scrutiny.

## No annual discount changes the comparison

Every plan is quoted monthly, with no minimum and no published annual rate.

The flexibility is genuine — you can cancel any month, which matters in a business as cyclical as real estate. But most competitors offer 15% to 20% for annual commitment, so Top Producer's effective cost relative to the field is worse than the headline gap suggests.

For a brokerage confident in a multi-year choice, that lost discount is real money: 20% of a $1,199 monthly Teams 25 plan would be nearly $2,900 a year.

## The add-ons are per user, on top

Top Producer Website is $35 per user per month. FiveStreet lead routing is $25 per user per month.

On a Pro Teams 10 plan at $699, adding both for all ten agents is another $600 a month — nearly doubling the bill.

Whether that is reasonable depends on what you would otherwise pay for agent websites and lead routing separately, which for many brokerages is more. But the layering of per-user add-ons onto capped-seat plans compounds faster than most buyers expect, so price the full configuration rather than the plan.

## What justifies the premium

Three things, and they are real.

**MLS integration across 320-plus boards.** Broader coverage than most competitors, which matters if you operate across markets or in a region others neglect.

**Market Snapshot.** Automated property market reports agents send to their database. It is Top Producer's most distinctive feature and the one long-term users cite most — it drives repeat contact without effort, which is the whole game in a relationship business.

**Transaction management.** Contract-to-close workflow inside the CRM rather than a separate tool.

An agent who uses all three daily is getting good value at $179. An agent using it as a contact database is paying triple for a contact database.

## Who should buy it

Real estate teams and brokerages of five agents and up, where per-seat economics fall to $48 to $80 and the MLS breadth, Market Snapshot and transaction management justify the platform. At Teams 25 pricing, Top Producer is competitive with anything in the category.

It suits badly solo agents, who pay $179 for what a team member gets for $48, and anyone considering the Leads or Farming bundles without demanding cost-per-lead data first — those are advertising purchases and should be judged as such. Check topproducer.com for current rates, and if you have more than one agent, price the team plans before the individual ones.
