## A flat rate in a per-seat market

Almost every real estate CRM charges per agent. Follow Up Boss is $58 a user on annual Grow. Lofty starts around $449 a month. Top Producer runs $179 per user. Wise Agent charges $42 a month, annually, for up to five team members.

That is the entire story of this price list, and for a small team it is decisive.

| Team size | Wise Agent (annual) | Follow Up Boss Grow + calling (annual) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 agent | $42/mo | $91/mo |
| 3 agents | $42/mo | $273/mo |
| 5 agents | $42/mo | $455/mo |

At five agents, Wise Agent costs less than a tenth of the nearest comparison. That gap is not explained by a feature deficit large enough to match it — Wise Agent has contact management, lead automation, drip campaigns, transaction management, landing pages and reporting, all of which work.

What it does not have is the depth Follow Up Boss brings to high-volume lead operations: sophisticated routing, team inboxes, call reporting, the speed-to-lead machinery that a team spending five figures a month on portal leads actually needs. The price difference maps to that difference, not to general quality.

## The login nuance

The five-member allowance is for team members sharing access. Additional individual logins are $20/month each.

For a solo agent with an assistant and a transaction coordinator, the shared model is fine and the price is exactly as advertised. For a genuine five-agent team where everyone needs their own login and their own pipeline, the arithmetic changes — four extra logins is $80/month, taking the effective cost to $122.

That is still cheap against the category. But it is a different number from $42, and it is the single most common misreading of this price list.

## The add-on shelf

Wise Agent's add-ons are unusually small and specific, which is refreshing:

- Extra 5-member pack: $20/month
- Additional login: $20/month
- Unlimited lead enhancement credits: $5/month
- Extra landing pages: $5/month each
- WiseSocial account: $20/month
- WiseText SMS: $11/month plus $80 one-time registration

The $80 SMS registration is worth explaining because it looks arbitrary. US carriers now require businesses to register before sending application-to-person text messages, and that registration is a real cost passed through by every texting provider. It is not a Wise Agent markup, and you would pay an equivalent fee anywhere.

The unlimited lead enhancement credits at $5 are the best value on the list. The base plan includes 100 credits, which an active agent exhausts quickly, and $5 removes the ceiling permanently.

## What WiseSocial adds

The $69 monthly bundle ($59 annual) adds social media automation: automated listing posting, branded listing videos, content scheduling.

The judgement is simple. If you currently post listings to Facebook and Instagram by hand, or pay a virtual assistant to, this replaces that work for $17 a month. If your social presence runs through a marketing agency or a separate platform, it is duplication.

Branded listing videos are the piece most likely to be genuinely useful — producing them individually is either time-consuming or billable, and automated generation from listing data removes both.

## The cost that is not on the list

Wise Agent manages leads. It does not create them. For any producing agent, the portal and advertising spend that fills the CRM dwarfs the subscription — often by a factor of twenty or more.

That reframes the value question in Wise Agent's favour, actually. At $42 a month, the software is close to a rounding error against a lead budget, and the only real question is whether it converts those leads well enough. For agents whose failure mode is inconsistent follow-up rather than insufficient sophistication, it does.

The inverse also holds: an agent with no lead flow gains nothing from any CRM, and should fix that first.

## Who should buy it

Solo agents, small teams up to five, and cost-conscious brokerages that want a complete real estate CRM without per-seat pricing. The transaction management, drip campaigns and stock real estate content are genuinely useful and the 24/7 support at this price is unusual.

It fits poorly for high-volume teams running ISAs and heavy portal spend, where Follow Up Boss's routing and reporting earn their premium, and for brokerages above fifteen agents, where the flat-rate advantage gives way to a quoted Enterprise plan.
