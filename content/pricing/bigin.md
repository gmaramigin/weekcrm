## The bill scales on records and pipelines, not headcount

Almost every CRM in this directory is a seat-count multiplication problem. Bigin is not, or at least not primarily. The per-user prices are low enough — $7 to $18 — that adding a person is a rounding error on most small-business budgets. What actually moves you between tiers is structural: how many pipelines your business runs, how many automations you need, and how much data you are holding.

The tier limits make this explicit. Free gives you one pipeline and 500 records. Express gives three pipelines and 50,000 records. Premier gives five and 100,000. Bigin 360 gives fifteen pipelines and a million records. A solo consultant with one sales process and 300 contacts can genuinely run on the free plan indefinitely. A four-person agency running separate pipelines for new business, renewals and partner referrals is on Express because of the pipeline count, not because it has four people.

## Where the upgrades earn their keep

Free to Express is the easy one. The moment a second person needs a login, you are paying, and $7 per user per month annually is not a decision anyone agonises over. You also go from one pipeline to three and from 500 records to 50,000, which is a wildly disproportionate jump for $84 a year.

Express to Premier at $12 is a real decision. You get two more pipelines, 50 advanced automations instead of 30 basic ones, and 1,000 monthly AI credits. If your bottleneck is manual follow-up, the automation upgrade pays for itself. If you just want more contacts stored, buy the record add-on instead — it is cheaper than moving the whole team up a tier.

Premier to Bigin 360 at $18 is aimed at businesses that have genuinely outgrown a "small business CRM" but do not want to leave. Fifteen pipelines and a million records is a lot of headroom. Be honest with yourself here: if you need Bigin 360, you should at least price out Zoho CRM Standard at $14 per user per month, which is cheaper and considerably more capable, before paying $18 for the simpler product.

For context, twenty users on Premier costs less per year than a single Salesforce Enterprise seat. That is the entire pitch, and it is a good one.

## Who this punishes

Bigin's pricing model is unusually honest, so the failure mode is not cost — it is fit. Businesses with long, multi-stakeholder B2B sales cycles, custom modules, or reporting requirements beyond pipeline health will hit Bigin's feature ceiling well before they hit its price ceiling. Paying $18 per user for a product that cannot model your process is worse value than paying $65 for one that can.

The other group that struggles is teams that need real backup and storage guarantees. Data backup and extra file storage are separate paid add-ons, small individually but conceptually annoying if you assumed they were included.

Everyone else — solo operators, local service businesses, small agencies, consultancies under twenty people — is exactly who this was priced for, and the free tier means you can test the fit for nothing before spending a cent.

Zoho adjusts regional pricing and add-on rates periodically, and the figures above are the USD list. Check [bigin.com/pricing](https://www.bigin.com/pricing.html) with your currency selected before budgeting.
