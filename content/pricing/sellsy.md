## Two products, three ways to buy them

Sellsy's pricing is more complicated than most because it sells two distinct products — Sales and Invoicing — both standalone and bundled, each across three levels.

| | Standard | Evolution | Elite |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sellsy Sales | €29 | €49 | €79 |
| Sellsy Invoicing | €39 | €59 | €79 |
| Both bought separately | €68 | €108 | €158 |
| Sales & Invoicing bundle | €49 | €89 | €119 |
| Bundle saving | €19 | €19 | €39 |

The bundle does save money at every level, but the saving is not proportional — €19 at Standard and Evolution, €39 at Elite. And it comes with a condition the standalone products do not have: a two-user minimum.

That condition inverts the calculation for solo operators. A one-person business wanting both capabilities pays €98/month for two bundle seats at Standard, against €68 for one seat of each standalone product. The bundle is cheaper per seat and more expensive in total.

Do the arithmetic for your specific headcount and tier before assuming the bundle is the right purchase. It usually is, and it is not always.

## The VAT question

Sellsy quotes French pricing excluding VAT — HT, hors taxes. For domestic customers that adds 20%.

A €89 bundle seat is €106.80 all in. A ten-person team on Evolution is €1,068 HT, €1,281.60 including VAT. Competitors quoting VAT-inclusive figures will look more expensive than they are unless you normalise.

For VAT-registered businesses the tax is recoverable and the comparison is HT to HT. For anyone not registered, the inclusive figure is the real cost, and it matters.

## Cash Flow is a third product

Budget forecasting, multi-scenario analysis, bank synchronisation and expense tracking are not in any tier. They are a separate module from €65/month at Standard, €99 at Evolution, with Elite quoted.

The pricing structure is different too — per account rather than per user. That makes it good value for a team of ten (€6.50 per person at Standard) and expensive for a team of two (€32.50 each).

Businesses evaluating Sellsy specifically for financial visibility should price it as: bundle seats plus Cash Flow. A five-person team on Evolution with Cash Flow is €445 plus €99, or €544/month HT — a very different number from the €89 headline.

## The localisation is the product

Sellsy's argument is not that it is a better CRM than Pipedrive or a better invoicing tool than its international competitors. It is that it does quote-to-cash properly for French and francophone SMEs: compliant electronic invoicing, pre-accounting, bank reconciliation, and integration with the accounting practices those businesses actually use.

France's e-invoicing mandate makes that localisation increasingly non-optional rather than merely convenient. A generic international CRM will not handle French compliance requirements, and bolting on a separate compliant invoicing tool reintroduces exactly the data split Sellsy exists to remove.

The corollary is that outside its home markets, most of the reason to choose Sellsy disappears. International buyers comparing it on features alone are missing the point in both directions.

## The pricing has changed more than once

Sellsy has restructured from modular pricing to bundled suites over recent years, and comparison sites carry figures from several generations simultaneously. References to €35, €59 and €95 tiers describe a previous structure; the current published rates are the ones above.

This is worth stating plainly because Sellsy is one of the harder products in this category to price from public sources. Verify with the vendor directly, and be specific about which product, which level, how many users and whether the quote is HT or TTC.

## Who should buy it

French and francophone SMEs — agencies, consultancies, service businesses, small distributors — that want CRM and compliant invoicing in one system with the local accounting integration intact. The Sales & Invoicing bundle at Evolution is the natural configuration for a team of five or more.

It fits poorly for solo operators, who face the two-user bundle minimum, and for businesses outside francophone markets, where the compliance and accounting localisation that justifies the price simply does not apply. Check go.sellsy.com for current rates, and confirm whether your quote is HT or TTC.
