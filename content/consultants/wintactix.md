We work with teams using Attio who have a clear direction for how they want their CRM to support the business, but need an experienced partner to turn that direction into a concrete system.

Clients work with us to crystallize their thinking. We help them reason through how the system should work across teams, make the necessary trade-offs explicit, and decide what to standardize versus where flexibility is worth keeping.

## Our philosophy

- **We believe clients use Attio because they already trust the Attio brand.** Our role starts after that decision, by helping you get the maximum out of the platform you’ve chosen.
- **We believe most teams already have direction.** What you need is help crystallizing that direction and turning it into something concrete inside Attio.
- **We believe teams add more tools because they don’t realize what their existing tools can handle.** A big part of our work is showing you what can live inside Attio and helping you to commit to that.
- **We believe the most valuable part of consulting is thinking with the client.** That means working through your ideas together, pressure-testing them, and making trade-offs explicit.
- **We believe clients want confidence.** Confidence that you're using Attio correctly, that your approach makes sense, and that your’re not painting yourselves into a corner without realizing it.

## What we typically help with

- Turning a rough idea into a concrete Attio setup
- Defining objects, relationships, properties, and lists
- Reconciling how different teams view and use the same data
- Designing schemas that can support real, day-to-day usage
- Implementing larger, structured builds rather than lightweight configuration

## Who we’re a good fit for

- Clients who want an experienced partner to work through the system with them and make clear, opinionated decisions on the setup
- Teams using Attio who have an idea of where they want to go, but aren’t confident they’re setting it up in the best way
- Teams who want trade-offs made explicit early, rather than discovering limitations later
- Clients who want their approach sanity-checked and validated
- Teams using PostHog: we’re often a strong fit by default, as our sister agency focuses exclusively on PostHog consulting (HogHelper.com)

## Who we’re not a good fit for

- Email sequencing and outbound execution
- Outreach copywriting or campaign management
- Small, one-off configuration tasks
- Teams without clear ownership or intent on what they want to build
