Healthcare is the most regulated CRM vertical, and most general CRMs handle it badly. A serious healthcare CRM has to ship three things: HIPAA-compliant data handling with a Business Associate Agreement, a clear separation between PHI (protected health information) and the marketing/CRM record, and integration paths to the EHR-of-record (Epic, Cerner, Athenahealth) so the CRM doesn't try to replace the clinical system. Within healthcare, the right CRM also depends on the segment: pharma and biotech commercial teams have different needs from a private medical practice, and a healthcare-tech SaaS company selling into providers has different needs from either. The vendors below cover the spectrum — life-sciences-specific platforms for pharma, general enterprise CRMs with HIPAA-compliant configurations for healthcare-adjacent businesses, and lighter Apple-native or modern CRMs for small private practices.
