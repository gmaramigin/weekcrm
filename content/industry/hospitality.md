Hospitality lives or dies on guest relationships, but most properties run their guest data inside a PMS (Opera, Mews, Cloudbeds) that's built to manage rooms, not relationships. A hospitality CRM sits on top of the PMS and POS, unifies the guest profile across stays/visits, and powers the three workflows that drive repeat revenue: group sales (corporate, weddings, conferences) with multi-stakeholder pipelines and contracted-room blocks; lifecycle marketing (pre-arrival, in-stay, post-stay, win-back) tuned by guest segment; and service recovery (a complaint at check-in becomes a flagged record that the GM sees on the next reservation). The vendors below are the strongest options for hotels, resorts, restaurant groups, and event venues in 2026 — Salesforce (Hospitality Cloud and Marketing Cloud) and HubSpot anchor the marketing-led end with strong loyalty and email capabilities, Zoho CRM and Pipedrive handle group-sales pipelines for boutique and mid-scale properties, and tools like Glassix and Gladly cover the multi-channel guest service side (WhatsApp, voice, in-app messaging) that increasingly drives reviews and direct rebooking. Most successful hospitality stacks pair a CRM with the PMS and a CDP — the CDP unifies the guest record, the CRM runs sales and marketing on top, and the PMS continues to own the actual reservation.
