## What e-commerce CRM means

The "CRM" most e-commerce brands actually need is not a sales pipeline tool — it's a **customer messaging and lifecycle platform** that ingests order, cart, and product data from Shopify (or BigCommerce / WooCommerce / Amazon) and lets the brand turn that data into segmented campaigns, abandoned-cart automations, support tickets, and post-purchase follow-up.

That makes the buyer's checklist different from B2B CRM. Pipeline depth doesn't matter; deep Shopify sync, ticket SLA management, and review/return workflows do.

## What to prioritize

- **Storefront integration depth.** A "Shopify integration" can mean anything from a manual CSV upload to sub-second order sync. Klaviyo's Shopify integration syncs in under 200ms; cheaper tools batch sync once an hour. The difference shows up in cart-abandonment revenue.
- **Order context inside tickets.** Support agents need to see the customer's order history, shipping status, and lifetime value on the ticket. Tools like Gorgias, eDesk, and Re:amaze are built for this; generic helpdesks are not.
- **Marketing automation.** Welcome series, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase, win-back. The CRM should ship with these flows pre-built.
- **Reviews and UGC.** Integration with Yotpo, Okendo, Loox is now table stakes. Reviews drive conversion, and they need to flow into segmentation.
- **Multi-channel.** Email + SMS + WhatsApp + Instagram DMs. SMS open rates are 5× email; ignoring it leaves money on the table.

## When to use what

- **Marketing-led brands** (high LTV, content-driven acquisition) → Klaviyo for marketing + Gorgias for support.
- **Support-heavy DTC brands** → Gorgias, Re:amaze, or eDesk handle ticket + order context together.
- **Multi-channel sellers (Amazon + Shopify + eBay)** → eDesk specifically wins here because it consolidates all channels into one inbox with order context.
- **Lean teams** → Tidio or Richpanel bundle live chat + helpdesk + automation in one subscription.
