Agriculture is one of the most underserved CRM categories. The buyers — farmers, ranchers, ag-input retailers, equipment dealers, and co-ops — have buying cycles tied to growing seasons, relationships built over decades, and a strong preference for face-to-face and phone communication over email. The CRMs below fit either ag-tech companies selling into farms, or farm-side operations managing their own customer and supplier relationships.

## What to prioritize

- **Mobile-first** — sales calls happen in fields, equipment yards, and at farm shows. A great mobile app beats a great web app.
- **Seasonal pipeline modeling** — planting, growing, harvest, and post-harvest each generate different sales motions. Multiple pipelines and seasonal automation matter.
- **Relationship depth** — farming is generational. The CRM has to track family relationships, succession plans, and decision-maker structure across multiple farms in one operation.
- **Phone-first communication** — most ag relationships still close on phone calls and farm visits. Call logging, click-to-call, and SMS reminders matter more than email sequences.
- **Geographic context** — territories often map to crop regions or commodity zones. Geo-tagging and route planning are useful.

## When this category is the right call

- **Ag-input retailers and distributors** — selling seed, chemicals, equipment, and services to farms.
- **Equipment dealerships** — John Deere, CNH, AGCO dealers managing customer fleets and parts cycles.
- **Ag-tech SaaS companies** — farm management software, precision-ag platforms, supply-chain tools selling into agriculture.
- **Cooperatives and grain handlers** — managing producer relationships and procurement contracts.
- **Specialty crop and direct-to-consumer farms** — wineries, orchards, ranches selling direct via CSAs and farm-store programs.

For most agricultural use cases, a flexible general-purpose CRM (Pipedrive, HubSpot, Zoho CRM) configured for the seasonal model is enough. The tools below are picked for the case where ag-specific workflows or industry context add value.
