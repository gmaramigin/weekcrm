Web development agencies live in two systems: a CRM for new business and a project management tool for delivery. The handoff between them is where most agencies lose data — a "Closed Won" deal in the CRM that takes two days to manually replicate as a project in Asana, ClickUp, monday, or Notion is two days of context loss and momentum lost. The CRMs below either integrate natively with the project tool the dev team already uses, or are flexible enough to model the full lifecycle without forcing a second tool.

Three workflows define agency CRM fit: (1) **project vs retainer deal types** — a one-off website build is a different opportunity shape from a $5k/month maintenance retainer, and the CRM needs to forecast them differently without bolting on a custom field hack; (2) **scoped proposal flow** — agencies live and die by accurate scoping, and the CRM should integrate with PandaDoc, DocSend, or a similar proposal tool so estimates are version-controlled and signature-trackable; and (3) **stakeholder mapping on the client side** — even a $20k website project involves a marketing lead, a brand owner, a procurement contact, and sometimes an IT reviewer.

The picks below have been used by agencies at the 3–50 person range. HubSpot is the most common pick because of its agency partner program and marketing automation depth. Folk and Attio win on visual polish — agencies care about UI more than most buyers. Pipedrive and Copper are the leaner, lower-cost alternatives for shops that don't need marketing automation in the CRM.
