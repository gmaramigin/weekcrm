Conversational sales is a different game than email-led B2B selling. The buyer initiates contact through a messenger or chat widget, expects a fast reply, and converts inside the same conversation thread that started the relationship. The CRMs below treat messengers as first-class sales channels — not as a side integration — and ship pipelines, sequences, and AI assistants designed to live inside chat.

## What to prioritize

- **Native messenger integration** — WhatsApp Business API, Instagram DM, Telegram, Facebook Messenger, web chat. The integration should be two-way, real-time, and unified into one inbox.
- **Chat-shaped pipeline** — leads created from a chat message, stages updated by chatbot actions, deals closed inside the conversation.
- **Bot + human handoff** — bots qualify leads, humans close. Seamless handoff with context preserved is the difference between conversion and abandonment.
- **AI replies** — instant first-touch responses, suggested replies for reps, and chat summaries for handoff. Speed is the moat in conversational sales.
- **Multi-language** — conversational sales is especially strong in LATAM, MENA, and Southeast Asia, where buyers expect WhatsApp-first communication in local languages.

## When this category is the right call

- **DTC ecommerce brands** with WhatsApp/Instagram as primary channels.
- **LATAM, MENA, Southeast Asia, India SMB sales** where messenger is the default business comms channel.
- **High-velocity inbound sales** where speed-to-lead determines win rate.
- **Community-driven products** with Telegram or Discord communities as the primary acquisition channel.
- **Real estate, education, finance, travel** — verticals where buyers shop and close inside chat.

If your buyer expects email-led nurture and 30-day sales cycles, a standard CRM is the right shape. The tools below are picked for the case where the conversation IS the funnel — speed, language, and multi-channel chat are non-negotiable, and email is a fallback rather than the primary motion.
