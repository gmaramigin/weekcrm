Food and beverage sales is two different motions stitched together: **B2B wholesale** (distributors, regional buyers, retail chain accounts) and **DTC commerce** (Shopify, subscriptions, marketplaces). The CRMs that fit this category have to handle both ends — long-cycle account-based selling to Whole Foods or Sysco, plus high-velocity DTC contact data flowing in from Shopify, Klaviyo, and Amazon.

Three workflows tend to define which CRM wins for a F&B brand: (1) **sampling and trade-show pipeline** — sales is initiated through events, sample drops, and trial placements that don't fit a generic "lead → opportunity → won" model without customization; (2) **distributor and broker relationship management** — the customer-of-the-customer pattern, where the brand sells through a distributor who sells to retailers, requires either custom objects or a clean parent-account structure; and (3) **retail account management** — once you're on the shelf, the relationship is about placement, promotions, slotting fees, and reorder velocity, not "deal stages."

The CRMs below either ship features specifically for this (Salesforce Consumer Goods Cloud at the enterprise end, HubSpot with custom objects in the middle) or pair cleanly with the F&B stack — Shopify Plus, Klaviyo, ShipBob, Crisp, Repsly for field merchandising — to give a brand visibility across both channels in one system.
