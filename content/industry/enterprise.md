## What "enterprise" actually means

The line between mid-market and enterprise CRM isn't headcount — it's complexity. An enterprise CRM has to handle multiple business units with different sales motions, integrate with the ERP and data warehouse, satisfy SOC 2 / GDPR governance, and stay performant when a single account has 50,000 contacts and 10 years of history.

The features that matter shift accordingly. The question isn't "does it have a pipeline view?" — every CRM has one. It's whether the platform has the **data model**, **role-based access**, **API throughput**, and **change-management story** to survive a multi-region rollout without becoming a permanent IT project.

## What to prioritize

- **Data model flexibility.** Custom objects with permissioned fields. If your business sells through indirect channels or has a complex product hierarchy, the standard contact-account-deal model breaks fast.
- **Governance.** Role hierarchies, sharing rules, audit logs, field-level encryption. Enterprise procurement will not approve a CRM without these.
- **Integration depth.** Bidirectional sync with the ERP (NetSuite, SAP, Dynamics F&O), the data warehouse (Snowflake, BigQuery), and the marketing automation platform. Native connectors beat middleware.
- **AI on structured data.** Forecasting, opportunity scoring, churn prediction. The model is only as good as the data — clean CRM hygiene is a prerequisite, not an afterthought.
- **Total cost of ownership.** List price is usually the smallest line item. Implementation, integration, custom development, and ongoing admin headcount are typically 2–5× the license cost.

## How to choose

Run a structured RFP with three to five vendors against the workflows that actually drive your revenue, not against feature checklists. Reference calls with companies of similar size and industry are worth more than analyst reports. Plan for a 6–18 month implementation; treat anything under that with skepticism.

The platforms in this category — Salesforce Sales Cloud, Microsoft Dynamics 365, Creatio, Oracle CX, SugarCRM Enterprise — are all credible. The right answer depends on which one your existing IT investments and partner ecosystem already support.
