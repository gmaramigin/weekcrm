## How B2B CRM is different from B2C

B2C CRMs optimize around individual customers and high transaction volume. B2B CRMs optimize around **accounts**: organizations made up of multiple stakeholders, with deals that involve five-plus people and span months of evaluation. The data model has to reflect that reality — contacts roll up to accounts, opportunities have multiple participants with roles, and the pipeline tracks the procurement gauntlet (champion, economic buyer, security review, legal, signing).

## What to prioritize

- **Account-level visibility.** Every B2B CRM should show, at a glance, every contact at a target account, every email sent, and every meeting held. Without this, account executives blow deals because someone else on the team already burned the relationship.
- **Multi-threaded deal tracking.** Roles per opportunity (champion, decision maker, blocker) and the ability to track who has been engaged. This is what separates a sales CRM from a contact database.
- **Forecasting that reps trust.** Weighted pipeline, stage probabilities, and historical close rates by stage. If reps don't believe the forecast, leadership flies blind.
- **Sales engagement integration.** Cadence tools (Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo) drive most outbound motion. The CRM has to sync activity bidirectionally without losing data.
- **ABM and intent.** For larger B2B teams, integration with 6sense / Demandbase / ZoomInfo is table stakes. Prospects researching your solution before you reach out matters more than blind cold outreach.

## SMB vs mid-market vs enterprise B2B

The vendors that win at $10k ACV are not the vendors that win at $100k ACV. Below $25k ACV, optimize for rep activity and pipeline velocity (Pipedrive, Close, HubSpot Sales). Above that, prioritize forecasting, account planning, and ecosystem integrations (HubSpot Enterprise, Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics).
