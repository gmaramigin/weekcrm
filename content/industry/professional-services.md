Professional services CRM is shaped by one structural fact: **the sold deal becomes a delivered project, and the delivered project has to be profitable**. That breaks the standard CRM model in two ways. First, the deal-won stage isn't the end of the customer journey — it's the start of a project lifecycle that runs months or years, with milestones, hours logged, and a budget vs. actuals that the partner watches as carefully as the AE watches pipeline. Second, the "customer" is a long-running account where the same firm sells to the same buyer many times over a decade; the CRM has to handle a relationship graph (who at the firm sold to whom at the client, with what outcome, when) more than a deal pipeline. The vendors below are the CRMs and PSA platforms commonly deployed in services firms — **HoneyBook**, **Bonsai**, and **Sellsy** at the freelance-to-small-agency tier (CRM bundled with contracts, invoicing, and payments); **Scoro** and **Teamleader** at the European mid-market for agencies and consultancies wanting integrated PSA; **Insightly** and the larger CRMs at the enterprise tier (Salesforce, Dynamics 365) for services firms running consultative motions across multiple business units. Most firms eventually pair the CRM with a dedicated PSA tool (Kantata, Mavenlink) once project profitability tracking outgrows what the CRM can do alone — but for a services firm under 50 people, the bundled CRM-plus-invoicing tools listed here are usually enough.
