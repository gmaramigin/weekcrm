Logistics is one of the more underserved industries in CRM. The buying motion is multi-stakeholder (procurement, ops, finance), the deal isn't a single closed-won number — it's a contracted lane book that ramps over months — and the relationship continues every day for years through shipment-level operations. A general sales CRM stops at the contract; a logistics-aware CRM keeps going through volumes, on-time-delivery scores, and QBR cycles. The vendors below are the strongest options for freight brokers, asset and non-asset carriers, third-party logistics providers, and supply-chain SaaS companies in 2026 — Salesforce dominates the enterprise end (3PLs, large brokerages, carrier groups) with its custom-object flexibility and AppExchange logistics apps; Microsoft Dynamics 365 fits ERP-led shops already on Business Central or NetSuite; HubSpot, Pipedrive, and Zoho CRM cover the broker and emerging-3PL segment that needs pipeline + email + automation without an enterprise implementation; and Front and Freshdesk handle the operations-heavy customer-service surface (carrier disputes, shipment exceptions, customer chase) that traditional CRMs leave to the inbox. Most successful logistics stacks integrate the CRM with a Transportation Management System (McLeod, MercuryGate, Mavyn, Kuebix, FreightPath) so quoted lanes, tendered loads, and actual shipments flow back into the customer record — without that loop, the CRM becomes a sales-only artifact and the relationship still lives in someone's inbox.
