## TL;DR

- **Pick EngageBay** if you're a SMB or solo founder who needs CRM + marketing + support and can't justify HubSpot's per-seat math.
- **Pick HubSpot** if you have a real revenue team, a real marketing motion, and a budget — the depth, ecosystem, and reporting are categorically ahead.

## Pricing

EngageBay: Free for 15 users with limits, then Basic $13.79/user/mo, Growth $59.79, Pro $110.39 (all flat across CRM + marketing + service). HubSpot: Free CRM, then Sales Hub Starter $20/seat, Professional $100, Enterprise $150 — and you typically need Marketing Hub on top ($20 → $890 → $3,600/mo) plus Service Hub. EngageBay is 5–10x cheaper at the all-in bundled level. That's the entire pitch.

## CRM core

HubSpot's CRM is the category leader for ease-of-use, contact timeline, and pipeline UX. EngageBay's CRM is solid and covers the basics — pipeline, contacts, deals, tasks — but it's clearly catching up rather than leading. For pure sales pipeline UX, HubSpot wins.

## Marketing automation

HubSpot is the standard. Workflow builder, email marketing, landing pages, smart content, A/B testing, attribution reporting — all best-in-class. EngageBay offers similar features but at much less depth: workflows are simpler, the email builder is fewer steps but fewer options, attribution reporting is basic. For SMBs running simple drip sequences, EngageBay is enough. For real marketing teams running multi-touch attribution, HubSpot.

## Helpdesk and support

EngageBay's bundle includes ticketing and live chat, which is unique for the price. HubSpot's Service Hub does this better — but you're paying Service Hub pricing. EngageBay wins on the bundle math; HubSpot wins on individual quality.

## Ecosystem

HubSpot's app marketplace, agency network, and certification pipeline are unmatched. EngageBay has a smaller integration library and minimal agency ecosystem. If you need a third-party app installed in 48 hours, HubSpot's marketplace probably has it; EngageBay may not.

## Reporting

HubSpot's reporting depth (custom dashboards, attribution, sequence analytics) is several tiers ahead. EngageBay reports the basics well — deal flow, conversion, email opens — but doesn't go deep on attribution or revenue analytics.

## Who should pick what

- **Solo founder or 2–5 person team that needs CRM + marketing + tickets for under $100/mo** → EngageBay.
- **Marketing-led growth team that needs attribution and serious workflow tooling** → HubSpot.
- **Bootstrap SaaS replacing 4 SMB tools with one cheap bundle** → EngageBay.
- **Series A+ company with a real go-to-market budget** → HubSpot.
