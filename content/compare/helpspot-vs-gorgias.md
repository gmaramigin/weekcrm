## TL;DR

- **Pick HelpSpot** if your support is email-based B2B, IT, or internal service and you want every feature in one predictable annual fee.
- **Pick Gorgias** if you sell on Shopify and want agents resolving order issues — refunds, cancellations, edits — without ever leaving the ticket.

## Ecommerce-native vs general-purpose

**Gorgias** is not a generic helpdesk that happens to integrate with Shopify. It is built around Shopify: agents see full order history in the ticket sidebar, can cancel or modify orders, apply discounts, and trigger Shopify macros in place. Over 17,000 brands use it, and the product's revenue attribution feature — tracking which support conversations convert into sales — only makes sense for a business where support and selling are the same conversation.

**HelpSpot** is deliberately general. It converts email into trackable tickets with Power Rules automation, SLA tracking, unlimited mailboxes, and a self-service portal, and it is used by IT service desks, HR teams, and B2B support orgs in finance, healthcare, education, and aerospace. It knows nothing about your storefront, and for its customers that is fine — nobody is asking a HelpSpot agent to issue a refund mid-ticket.

The position here is simple: if you are a DTC brand on Shopify, HelpSpot will feel like it is missing half the job. If you are an internal service desk, Gorgias's headline features are dead weight you are paying for.

## Agent pricing vs ticket pricing

This is the structural difference that outlasts any feature comparison. **HelpSpot** bills a flat annual fee by agent count — from **$699/year for 3 agents**, with every feature included and no per-ticket charges. Your cost is a function of headcount and nothing else, which makes it trivially forecastable and immune to a viral moment blowing up your bill.

**Gorgias** bills by monthly ticket volume: **$10/mo** for a 50-ticket Starter plan up to **$360/mo** for Pro. This is genuinely well-suited to ecommerce, where volume swings hard between a quiet February and a Black Friday week, and where you would otherwise be sizing your seat count for the peak. The risk is the mirror image — overages during high-volume seasons accumulate quickly if nobody is watching the meter.

Layer on the AI: Gorgias's **AI Agent is billed separately at roughly $0.90–$1.00 per automated interaction**, so the tool that reduces your ticket count also adds a variable cost. HelpSpot's AI — tone adjustment, categorization, 40-language translation — is included in the flat fee. Less capable, but the marginal cost is zero.

## Channel coverage

Gorgias wins here without much contest. It unifies **email, chat, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, SMS, and voice** into one inbox, which matches how ecommerce customers actually behave — a DM about a delayed order is the same conversation as the email that follows it.

HelpSpot is email-first with a customer portal, and its omnichannel story is limited. For a B2B support desk where tickets arrive from named accounts by email, that is not a gap. For a consumer brand, it is disqualifying.

## Longevity and product philosophy

HelpSpot has been shipping since 2005, is privately held, and has explicitly avoided the feature treadmill — the interface looks dated because the company has not chased trends. Gorgias is a modern venture-backed product moving fast on AI and ecommerce workflows. Which you prefer depends on whether you want a tool that will look the same in three years or one that will have shipped features you did not ask for.

## Who should pick what

- **Shopify store drowning in order-status tickets** → Gorgias.
- **Internal IT or HR service desk** → HelpSpot.
- **Brand fielding DMs on Instagram and TikTok** → Gorgias.
- **B2B support team with email-based accounts** → HelpSpot.
- **Business with hard seasonal volume swings** → Gorgias.
- **Team that needs a fixed annual budget with no overage risk** → HelpSpot.

## Bottom line

Your storefront decides this. Gorgias is the strongest helpdesk available for Shopify merchants — the order actions in-ticket and the omnichannel inbox genuinely pay for themselves when order-status questions dominate your queue, provided you manage ticket overages and the per-interaction AI billing. HelpSpot is a quietly excellent flat-fee help desk for email-driven B2B and internal support, where predictability beats polish and nobody needs a refund button. Neither would be a good replacement for the other.
