## TL;DR

- **Pick Vendasta** if your business model is packaging and reselling white-labeled digital services to local SMBs and you need portals, a product marketplace, and billing as much as a CRM.
- **Pick Zoho CRM** if you just need a capable, cheap system to manage your own leads, deals, and clients.

## This is not a CRM comparison

Vendasta contains a CRM the way a warehouse contains a shelf. The actual product is agency infrastructure: **250+ white-label apps** you can resell across reputation, SEO, social, and websites; client-facing dashboards carrying **your brand rather than Vendasta's**; automated subscription billing and invoicing; and **Snapshot Reports** that audit a prospect's digital presence to open sales conversations. The CRM tracks prospects, proposals, and deals alongside the products you are fulfilling for them — valuable precisely because pipeline and delivery sit on one record.

**Zoho CRM** does one job and does it well for the money: lead and contact management, multi-pipeline deal tracking, workflow automation, forecasting, and Zia AI for scoring and predictions, inside an ecosystem of **50+ Zoho apps**. It has no marketplace to resell from and no white-label client portal.

So the real question is what business you are in. Agency that sells its own labor and expertise: Zoho. Agency that sells software and services under its own brand: Vendasta.

## Pricing models that don't map onto each other

**Zoho CRM** is per seat — free for 3 users, then **$14** (Standard), **$23** (Professional), **$40** (Enterprise), **$52/user/mo** (Ultimate), billed annually. Cost scales with headcount.

**Vendasta** is per platform — **$99/mo** Starter, **$499/mo** Professional, **$999/mo** Premium — plus **mandatory onboarding fees of roughly $500–$1,500**. Cost is largely fixed regardless of team size, but the jump from Starter to Professional is steep and the $99 tier has meaningful feature gaps, so treat **$499/mo** as the realistic entry point for a working reseller setup.

The break-even is not about seats, it is about resale revenue. If your white-label product margin does not comfortably exceed **$6,000/year** plus onboarding, Vendasta is not paying for itself and Zoho at **$14/user/mo** is doing the CRM job for a rounding error.

## Where each one strains

Vendasta's breadth is genuinely overwhelming for small teams — it is optimized for agencies operating at scale, and a two-person shop will pay for and configure far more platform than it uses. Its CRM, while integrated, will not satisfy a sales team that wants custom modules and enforced multi-stage processes.

Zoho's strain is the opposite: nothing in it helps you deliver or bill white-labeled products. You will end up assembling Zoho Billing, a portal, and vendor relationships yourself. That is a legitimate path — it is cheaper and more flexible — but it is a build, and the labor is not free.

## Who should pick what

- **Agency reselling white-labeled SEO, social, or reputation products** → Vendasta.
- **Agency selling its own consulting or creative labor** → Zoho CRM.
- **Media company or ISV serving many local SMB accounts** → Vendasta.
- **Small team that only needs pipeline, tasks, and forecasting** → Zoho CRM.
- **Business that needs client-facing branded portals and billing** → Vendasta.
- **Cost-sensitive team under 10 people with no resale model** → Zoho CRM.

## Bottom line

These products barely overlap, and the overlap — a deal pipeline — is the least important part of Vendasta. If you are building a recurring-revenue business reselling digital products to local businesses, Vendasta assembles the marketplace, portals, and billing you would otherwise spend a year stitching together, and its Snapshot Reports are a real sales weapon. If you are not doing that, Zoho CRM gives you a better CRM for a fraction of the money. Decide the business model first; the platform choice is downstream of it.
