## TL;DR

- **Pick Creatio** if process automation is central to your business and you want a no-code platform where analysts and admins build CRM workflows and custom apps without developers.
- **Pick Microsoft Dynamics 365** if you're standardized on Microsoft 365 and want CRM and ERP unified across sales, service, and operations with Copilot and Power Platform built in.

## Pricing

Creatio prices per user alongside platform/composable fees, generally in the mid-to-upper band, with its no-code studio and BPM engine included rather than sold as separate products — a meaningful distinction when you'd otherwise license an automation tool on top. Dynamics 365 charges per application per user (Sales, Customer Service, and so on), with costs compounding as you add modules and Power Platform components. The takeaway: Creatio bundles its automation platform into the price, while Dynamics' total cost scales with how many apps and Power Platform pieces you stack — consolidation rewards committed Microsoft buyers but penalizes piecemeal adoption.

## Core approach / Data model

Creatio's identity is the fusion of CRM with BPM — its data model and process engine are designed so that business processes are first-class citizens you orchestrate visually. The no-code studio lets you reshape the data model, build apps, and automate end-to-end without code, which is the whole pitch. Dynamics 365 is a broader business-application suite spanning CRM and ERP on the shared Dataverse model, extended through Power Platform. Both are highly customizable, but the philosophies differ: Creatio centers customization in an accessible no-code studio aimed at business users, while Dynamics centers it in a developer- and admin-friendly platform that can model the entire enterprise.

## Automation and workflows

This is Creatio's home turf. Its BPM engine lets you model, run, and monitor sophisticated business processes natively — case management, approval chains, and cross-department workflows are designed in, not added on. Dynamics 365 delivers comparable and arguably wider automation through Power Automate and the Power Platform, plus Copilot AI threaded across the apps, but automation is a layer you assemble rather than the product's core. For organizations whose competitive edge is process execution, Creatio's native, no-code BPM is the sharper instrument; for those wanting automation across a sprawling Microsoft estate, Dynamics' Power Platform reaches further.

## Email and integrations

Dynamics 365 wins on Microsoft cohesion — Outlook, Teams, Excel, SharePoint, and Power BI are native, the obvious advantage for Microsoft-centric organizations. Creatio offers email integration, an open architecture, marketplace connectors, and a developer API, and its platform neutrality means it slots into mixed environments without committing you to one vendor. The familiar trade-off applies: Dynamics is unbeatable if you're already all-in on Microsoft, while Creatio gives you a capable, automation-first CRM that doesn't assume which broader stack you run.

## Who should pick what

- **Process-driven organizations** wanting native no-code BPM: **Creatio**.
- **Teams that want business users building apps** without developers: **Creatio**.
- **Microsoft 365 shops** wanting CRM tied to Office, Teams, and Power BI: **Microsoft Dynamics 365**.
- **Companies unifying CRM with ERP** across the whole business: **Microsoft Dynamics 365**.

## Bottom line

Creatio and Dynamics 365 are both serious, customizable CRMs, but they win on different axes. Creatio leads with no-code BPM — the right choice when process automation is your differentiator and you want business users, not developers, building the system. Dynamics 365 leads with ecosystem and breadth — the right choice when you're a Microsoft shop wanting CRM and ERP fused across the business. Decide by whether process agility or Microsoft consolidation matters more.
