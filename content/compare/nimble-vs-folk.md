## TL;DR

- **Pick Nimble** if you want rich, auto-built contact profiles and a browser widget that lets you capture and enrich people from LinkedIn or any web page.
- **Pick folk** if you want a clean, modern CRM for partnerships, BD, and outreach — spreadsheet-fast UI, smart contact import from LinkedIn, and built-in email sequences.

## Pricing

Nimble is one flat plan at roughly $29.90/user/mo (≈$25 annual) with enrichment included. folk starts around $20/user/mo (Standard) and climbs to ~$40–$80/user/mo for Premium tiers that unlock more enrichment credits, larger sequence volumes, and advanced permissions. For a small team that wants enrichment baked in, Nimble's single price is simpler; folk's tiers scale better for a growing BD org.

## Design and feel

folk is the more modern product. It feels like a fast, opinionated spreadsheet — flexible views, custom fields, and a UI that a team adopts in a day. Nimble's interface is older and busier; it's competent but shows its age next to folk. If "reps will actually use it" is your top concern and your team skews younger or design-sensitive, folk wins on first impression.

## Contact capture and enrichment

Both lean on the browser. Nimble's Prospector overlays any web page and builds a living profile from social and public data — its enrichment depth is its calling card. folk's Chrome extension ("folkX") is built for bulk-importing people from LinkedIn search results and profiles into a list, then enriching emails. Nimble is stronger for deep per-contact intelligence; folk is faster for grabbing 50 prospects from a LinkedIn search and dropping them into a pipeline.

## Outreach and sequences

folk has the better outbound story. Its email sequences, merge fields, and reply tracking are built into the core product, making it a genuine light outbound tool for partnerships and BD. Nimble also offers group messaging and sequences, but folk's sequencing UX and deliverability tooling feel more current. For a partnerships or founder-led sales team running real outreach, folk is the stronger day-to-day tool.

## Pipelines and use case

folk markets itself squarely at relationship-driven teams — partnerships, agencies, fundraising, recruiting — and its pipeline views and grouping reflect that. Nimble is a more general social CRM that also serves solo consultants and small sales teams. Both keep deals lightweight on purpose; neither is a forecasting-heavy sales CRM. If you need weighted forecasts and rep quotas, look at Pipedrive or HubSpot instead.

## Integrations

folk integrates with the modern stack — Gmail, Outlook, calendar, Slack, and a growing native list plus Zapier and an API. Nimble covers the major email providers, social, and a marketplace, also bridged by Zapier. folk's integration list is newer and growing quickly; Nimble's is broad but older. Check both for the specific tools your team relies on.

## Who should pick what

- **Modern startup running partnerships, BD, or founder-led outreach** → folk. Clean UI plus built-in sequences.
- **Consultant or networker who wants the deepest auto-enriched contact profiles** → Nimble.
- **Team that bulk-imports prospects from LinkedIn search** → folk (folkX).
- **Solo user who wants one flat price with enrichment included** → Nimble.
- **Team that cares about adoption and design** → folk.

## Bottom line

Nimble and folk both reject the heavy, deal-centric CRM in favor of relationships — but folk is the newer, sharper take. folk wins on design, outreach sequencing, and modern integrations; Nimble wins on deep automatic enrichment and a single flat price. Pick folk for a modern partnerships/BD motion; pick Nimble for relationship intelligence and simple pricing.
