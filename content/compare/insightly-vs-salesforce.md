## TL;DR

- **Pick Insightly** if you're a small or mid-sized team that wants a capable, affordable CRM (with project management built in) that your team can actually adopt without a consultant.
- **Pick Salesforce** if you're scaling toward enterprise, need limitless customization, and have the budget and admin resources to run a platform rather than just a CRM.

## Pricing

Insightly is the budget-friendlier option: CRM tiers run roughly $29 to $99/user/mo with a small free plan, and it's usable out of the box. Salesforce Sales Cloud runs from about $25/user/mo (Starter) up through Pro Suite, Enterprise, and Unlimited at $165–$330+/user/mo, and the real total often climbs with add-ons, integrations, and implementation costs. For a small team, Insightly's all-in cost is dramatically lower; for an enterprise, Salesforce's pricing buys depth that smaller tools can't match.

## Core strengths

Insightly's appeal is approachability plus its CRM-and-projects combination — closed deals can flow directly into linked projects with milestones and tasks, so selling and delivery live in one tool. Salesforce's strength is near-infinite extensibility: custom objects, Flow automation, Apex code, a massive AppExchange marketplace, and the Einstein AI layer. Salesforce can model almost any business process, but realizing that power typically requires admins or partners.

## Pipeline and sales workflows

Salesforce is the more powerful sales engine by a wide margin: sophisticated forecasting, territory and quota management, CPQ, approval processes, and deep automation. Insightly covers the essentials very competently — multiple pipelines, lead routing, workflow automation, and post-sale project pipelines — and is far quicker to configure. Most SMBs will never need the ceiling Salesforce provides; enterprises with complex, multi-team sales motions will.

## Email and integrations

Both integrate with Gmail and Outlook and offer APIs. Salesforce's integration ecosystem is unmatched — thousands of AppExchange apps and connectors for virtually any system. Insightly integrates with the common tools and its own marketing and service modules, which is sufficient for most mid-market stacks. Breadth and enterprise-grade connectors favor Salesforce; simplicity favors Insightly.

## Reporting

Salesforce's reporting and dashboards are the gold standard, with custom report types, cross-object analytics, and an upgrade path to Tableau/CRM Analytics. Insightly offers strong, configurable dashboards spanning sales and project data — more than enough for SMB needs, but not at Salesforce's analytical depth.

## Bottom line

Insightly wins on cost, speed to value, and ease of adoption for small and mid-sized teams, and the built-in project management is a real bonus for service businesses. Salesforce wins on raw power, customization, and enterprise scale, at the cost of price and complexity. Right-size the decision: if you're not yet enterprise, Insightly likely does everything you need; if you're scaling into complex, multi-team operations, Salesforce is the platform built for it.
