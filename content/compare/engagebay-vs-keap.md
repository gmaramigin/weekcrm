## TL;DR

- **Pick EngageBay** if you want one affordable platform that bundles marketing, sales CRM, and a help desk together — and price is a primary concern.
- **Pick Keap** if you want best-in-class small-business marketing automation, with mature campaign sequences and a track record going back to its Infusionsoft days.

## Pricing

Price is EngageBay's loudest argument. It offers a real free tier and then climbs gently — Basic, Growth, and Pro plans that, even at the top, typically run well under $80/user/mo, with all-in-one bundles cheaper still. Keap sits noticeably higher: plans generally start around $249/mo for a base package (covering a small number of users and a contact allowance) and scale up from there. For a budget-sensitive SMB, EngageBay delivers far more functionality per dollar. Keap's pricing reflects a more specialised, automation-heavy product and the hands-on onboarding it tends to include — but the gap is large enough that cost alone steers many buyers to EngageBay.

## Marketing automation

This is where Keap earns its premium. Born from Infusionsoft, Keap has spent well over a decade refining campaign sequences, behavioural triggers, and the kind of multi-branch automation that small service businesses rely on. Its visual campaign builder and lifecycle automation are deeper and more battle-tested than most rivals at its size. EngageBay offers genuinely capable automation too — workflows, email sequences, lead scoring, landing pages — and for many teams it's more than enough. But for intricate, conditional nurture journeys, Keap remains the more sophisticated engine.

## Pipeline and sales workflows

EngageBay's CRM is a true multi-pipeline sales tool with deals, tasks, telephony, and appointment scheduling, all included in the same platform as marketing and support. Keap blends CRM with automation and adds invoicing, quotes, and payments — making it especially strong for solo operators and small service firms that sell and bill in one place. If you want a conventional sales pipeline with stages and reporting, EngageBay is the more CRM-shaped choice. If you want lead-to-cash with payments stitched into automation, Keap's bundle is compelling.

## Email and integrations

Both center email heavily. EngageBay includes email marketing, broadcasts, and templates across its tiers, plus a help desk and live chat that Keap doesn't natively match — making EngageBay the broader all-in-one. Keap focuses its integrations around its automation and commerce strengths, with a solid app marketplace, Zapier, and native payments. EngageBay's appeal is consolidation: marketing, sales, and support under one login and one bill. Keap's appeal is specialisation: do small-business automation and billing exceptionally well, and integrate the rest.

## Who should pick what

- **Budget-conscious SMBs wanting marketing, sales, and support in one tool** → EngageBay.
- **Teams that need a built-in help desk alongside CRM** → EngageBay.
- **Service businesses needing deep automation plus invoicing and payments** → Keap.
- **Marketers running complex, multi-branch nurture sequences** → Keap.

## Bottom line

EngageBay wins on breadth and price: it's the pragmatic all-in-one for a small business that wants to cover marketing, sales, and support without juggling tools or budgets. Keap wins on depth where it matters most to service-led SMBs — mature automation and integrated billing. Choose EngageBay to consolidate cheaply; choose Keap when automation sophistication and lead-to-cash flow justify the higher spend.
