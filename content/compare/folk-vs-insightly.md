## TL;DR

- **Pick Folk** if your work is relationship-led — raising money, partnerships, recruiting, agency BD — and you want a fast, modern CRM with one-click LinkedIn capture.
- **Pick Insightly** if you close deals and then have to deliver on them, and you want sales pipeline and project management living in the same system.

## Where the work ends

The cleanest way to choose is to ask when your job is done. For **Folk** users, the work is the *relationship*. Founders, agencies, VCs, and recruiters use it to organize a network — capturing contacts, tagging, segmenting, and staying in touch. There's no notion of "delivery" after a relationship warms up; Folk is deliberately light, avoiding complex pipelines and heavy reporting so it stays fast and simple for people who aren't running a sales operation.

For **Insightly** users, closing the deal is the *start*. Its defining feature is that a won deal **converts into a project** — tasks, milestones, and delivery pipelines tracked in the same CRM. That's built for professional services, consulting, and construction firms where selling and doing are two halves of one workflow, and losing the handoff between them is a real cost. Insightly also brings relationship linking (contacts, organizations, opportunities, and projects all connected) and strong analytics.

So Folk is for work that lives at the relationship layer, and Insightly is for work that runs from pipeline through project delivery. That difference should decide it before price ever enters the picture.

## Pricing

Folk has a **free plan** and paid tiers at $20 / $40 / $80 per user/month. Insightly is **free for 2 users**, then Plus $29, Professional $49, Enterprise $99 per user/month — with **annual billing required** on all paid plans, removing month-to-month flexibility. For a tiny team Folk's free plan is more generous than Insightly's 2-user cap. Across the range Folk is the cheaper relationship tool; Insightly's price buys the added project-management and analytics machinery.

## Project management: the deciding feature

This is the axis that actually separates them. Insightly's **built-in project management** means you don't need a separate PM tool after a deal closes — the same platform tracks the deliverables. Folk has **none of this**; it's not built for opportunity tracking, let alone project execution. If your business closes and then delivers — a consultancy, an agency running structured engagements — Insightly's integrated flow is the whole argument. If your relationships don't spawn managed projects, that machinery is dead weight and Folk's simplicity wins.

## Capture, automation, and UX

Folk's edge is the **front of the funnel and the experience**: folkX one-click LinkedIn capture, a modern UI, and onboarding non-technical teams can handle in a day. Insightly's automation is limited on the Plus tier (you need Professional or Enterprise for serious workflow automation), and its interface can feel dated, especially on mobile — the trade-off for a broader, older, more capable platform. Folk feels newer; Insightly does more once configured.

## Who should pick what

- **Founder or VC managing relationships and deal flow** → Folk.
- **Consultancy or agency that sells then delivers projects** → Insightly.
- **Recruiter doing LinkedIn-led candidate work** → Folk.
- **Professional-services firm needing pipeline + PM in one tool** → Insightly.
- **Small team wanting a modern UI and free start** → Folk.
- **Business that needs strong analytics and record linking** → Insightly.
