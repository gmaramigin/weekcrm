## TL;DR

- **Pick Close** if your team's day is outbound calling and emailing, and the metric that matters is how fast a lead becomes a closed deal.
- **Pick Insightly** if your business closes a deal and then has to deliver on it, and you want sales pipeline and project management in one system.

## Speed to close vs close then deliver

These CRMs are built for two different halves of the revenue journey. **Close is a speed-to-close machine.** It's an inside-sales tool for high-velocity outbound teams, with a native power dialer, SMS, and email sequences designed to move a lead from first touch to signed deal as fast as possible. Its whole design philosophy is removing friction from the sales act itself — reps spend their time talking to prospects, not navigating dashboards. Its 4.7 rating reflects how well it serves that focused mission.

**Insightly is a close-then-deliver system.** It handles the sales pipeline competently, but its defining feature is what happens *after* the win: a closed deal **converts into a project** with tasks, milestones, and delivery pipelines tracked in the same tool. It's aimed at professional services, consulting, and construction firms where selling and doing are two connected phases and the handoff between them is where balls get dropped. Insightly also links contacts, organizations, opportunities, and projects for full context, and its analytics earned it recognition as a top CRM for reporting.

So the tools barely overlap in intent. Close optimizes the sale; Insightly optimizes the arc from sale through delivery. A pure sales org will find Insightly's project layer irrelevant; a services firm will find Close missing the entire second half of its workflow.

## Pricing

Close has **no free plan**; tiers run about $19 (Base), $49 (Startup), $99 (Professional), $129 (Business) per user/month, with dialers on the upper tiers. Insightly is **free for 2 users**, then Plus $29, Professional $49, Enterprise $99 per user/month, with **annual billing required** on all paid plans. Close is cheaper to start and pricier at the top (you're buying a predictive dialer); Insightly's mid-range is comparable but locks you into annual commitments. Neither is expensive by enterprise standards.

## Calling engine vs project engine

This is the axis that decides it. Close's **native calling, SMS, and power dialer** are the point — no third-party Twilio, no add-ons, just a CRM that dials at volume. Insightly has **no comparable telephony**; it leans on integrations for calls. Conversely, Insightly's **built-in project management** is something Close doesn't attempt at all. You're essentially choosing which engine your business runs on: a calling engine that closes deals fast, or a project engine that delivers them after. Very few teams genuinely need both in one tool, which is why this rarely comes down to a feature checklist.

## Automation and reporting

Close's automation is sales-centric: Smart Views, sequences, and follow-up logic that keep reps working the right leads. Insightly's workflow automation is **limited on the Plus tier** — you need Professional or Enterprise for serious automation — but it pairs with strong analytics and reporting across the sales-and-delivery lifecycle. If your reporting needs span the whole customer journey (not just the sale), Insightly's analytics are a real draw; if you only need to know pipeline velocity and rep activity, Close covers it natively.

## Who should pick what

- **High-velocity outbound sales team on the phone all day** → Close.
- **Consultancy or agency that sells then delivers projects** → Insightly.
- **SaaS or B2B-services org running cold cadences** → Close.
- **Professional-services firm needing deal-to-project handoff** → Insightly.
- **Team that needs a power or predictive dialer** → Close.
- **Business that wants pipeline + PM + analytics in one tool** → Insightly.
