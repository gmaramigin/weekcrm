## TL;DR

- **Pick noCRM.io** if your reps live in lead-chasing mode, you've tried CRMs and seen them get ignored, and you want a tool whose entire design philosophy is "every lead has exactly one next action."
- **Pick Pipedrive** if you need a real CRM with deals, contacts, accounts, reporting, and marketing add-ons — but you still want the cleanest possible pipeline UI in that category.

## Pricing

noCRM.io starts at €12/user/mo (Starter) with the Expert tier at €19/user/mo and Dream Team at €29/user/mo — meaningfully cheaper than most CRMs. Pipedrive starts at $14/user/mo (Essential) and climbs to $99/user/mo (Enterprise). At small headcounts the prices are comparable; at growing teams Pipedrive's per-seat costs scale faster, especially once you add Campaigns or LeadBooster.

## Philosophy

noCRM.io takes a position most CRMs don't: traditional CRMs create more work for sales reps than they save. So it strips out account hierarchies, custom objects, and the rest, and forces every lead to either be "To-Do" (with a defined next action) or "Stand-By." Pipedrive is more conventional — full deal pipelines, contacts, organizations, activities — but optimizes for visual clarity rather than data depth. If a Salesforce admin would call noCRM.io "too simple," that's the point.

## Lead capture

noCRM.io is built around fast inbound. LinkedIn import, business-card scan, web forms, email-to-lead, and a Chrome extension are all first-class. Pipedrive has LeadBooster (chat, web forms, prospector) as a paid add-on, plus mature web form integrations. For high-velocity prospecting, noCRM.io's capture is faster; Pipedrive's add-ons are more comprehensive once you're paying for them.

## Pipeline and reporting

Pipedrive's drag-and-drop pipeline is the gold standard in this category. Forecasting, custom dashboards, and revenue reports are all there from the Professional tier and up. noCRM.io has a pipeline view too, but reporting is intentionally lighter — sales performance, conversion rates, and team activity are present, but you won't get deep forecasting. If reporting is a deciding factor, Pipedrive wins.

## Mobile and field sales

noCRM.io's mobile app is unusually good for the price tier — fast lead entry, voice notes, GPS-aware reminders, and offline mode. Pipedrive's mobile app is solid but more conventional. For field sales reps who genuinely live on their phone, noCRM.io's UX feels like it was designed for the road; Pipedrive's feels like a desktop CRM ported to mobile.

## Automation and integrations

Pipedrive has 300+ native integrations and Workflow Automation (from the Advanced tier). noCRM.io has 3,000+ integrations via Zapier plus native ones on the Expert plan, but its automation engine is simpler — mostly around lead assignment and follow-up scheduling. For complex automation, Pipedrive. For basic "don't let a lead fall through" automation, noCRM.io is sufficient.

## Who should pick what

- **Outbound prospecting team that wants speed of capture above all** → noCRM.io.
- **Field sales team (real estate, distribution, manufacturing reps)** → noCRM.io. The mobile experience is the reason.
- **Inside sales team that needs forecasting and pipeline reporting** → Pipedrive.
- **Team that's tried Salesforce/HubSpot and rejected it as too heavy** → noCRM.io.
- **Growing SMB that wants room to scale into deeper CRM features** → Pipedrive.

## Bottom line

noCRM.io and Pipedrive are not really direct competitors — noCRM.io is a lead manager that calls itself "noCRM" on purpose, while Pipedrive is a sales CRM optimized for visual clarity. Pick noCRM.io if your problem is "leads keep falling through the cracks because reps hate the CRM." Pick Pipedrive if your problem is "we need a real pipeline view and basic reporting without a six-month implementation."
