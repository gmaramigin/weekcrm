## TL;DR

- **Pick CommBox** if you're an enterprise running a high-volume contact center and need voice, WhatsApp, SMS, and social unified under AI routing, with native Salesforce and SAP connectors.
- **Pick Help Scout** if you're an SMB support team that answers email and chat, wants a human-feeling inbox, and refuses to run a six-week implementation project.

## Different categories, not different tiers

The instinct is to treat these as cheap-versus-expensive versions of the same thing. They aren't. **CommBox** is a contact-center platform: chat, email, voice, WhatsApp, SMS, and social routed through one agent workspace, with generative AI and bots layered on so volume grows faster than headcount. It reports **300+ enterprise customers and 100M+ conversations annually**, concentrated in telecom, financial services, retail, and insurance — industries where inbound volume is genuinely industrial.

**Help Scout** is a help desk built on a philosophy: support should feel like a personal email, not a ticket number. Shared inboxes, the **Beacon** embeddable widget, a knowledge base, in-app messaging, and an AI assistant that clears routine requests. Its natural customers are SaaS, ecommerce, healthcare, and education teams who looked at Zendesk, found it heavy and expensive, and wanted something warmer.

Ask which problem you have. "We handle enormous volume across every channel a customer might use" points to CommBox. "We want a small team to answer email really well" points to Help Scout.

## Channel coverage is the deciding factor

This is the cleanest dividing line and it usually settles the decision before pricing enters the conversation. CommBox treats **voice and WhatsApp as first-class channels**, not integrations — including outbound WhatsApp campaigns built into the platform. For a bank or telecom where customers phone in and message on WhatsApp interchangeably, that unification is the entire product thesis.

Help Scout deliberately does not go there. It covers email, chat, and in-app messaging extremely well and leaves telephony to specialists. That's a coherent choice — most SMB support is asynchronous and text-based — but it's an absolute constraint. If a phone queue is part of your operation, Help Scout doesn't compete here.

## Pricing transparency and what it implies

**Help Scout** is priced in public: a free plan for **up to 5 users**, paid tiers from **$25/user/mo**, and Plus at **$45/user/mo** where advanced workflow automation and the deeper integration library (Salesforce, Jira, HubSpot, Shopify, 100+ others) live. That per-seat model is honest but climbs — a 40-person team on Plus is a real budget line.

**CommBox** publishes no tiers at all; pricing is a sales conversation. That isn't a knock, it's a signal about the buying motion: you'll get a scoped quote, an implementation plan, and a vendor team. For a mid-market company evaluating both, the practical difference is that you can trial Help Scout tonight and won't get a CommBox number for a couple of weeks.

## AI: deflection vs orchestration

Both lead with AI, but they're solving different jobs. Help Scout's assistant is a **deflection engine** — it answers the repetitive questions, and Beacon surfaces knowledge base articles before a customer even opens a conversation, cutting inbound volume at the source. The human stays central; AI shaves the tedium.

CommBox's AI is an **orchestration layer**. It decides, per conversation, whether a bot or a specific agent should handle it, using context, history, and availability across every channel at once. That's a harder problem and only worth solving at scale — it presumes you have enough volume, enough agents, and enough channels for routing decisions to matter financially.

## Who should pick what

- **Enterprise contact center with voice and WhatsApp volume** → CommBox.
- **SMB support team of 5–40 answering email and chat** → Help Scout.
- **Already running Salesforce or SAP and consolidating channels** → CommBox.
- **Want to be live this week with no implementation project** → Help Scout.
- **Need outbound WhatsApp campaigns alongside inbound support** → CommBox.
- **Care most that support feels personal, not transactional** → Help Scout.

## Bottom line

This comparison resolves faster than most because the two products barely overlap. CommBox is enterprise infrastructure for organizations whose support volume spans every channel and justifies AI routing plus a vendor implementation team — expect a sales cycle and an enterprise price. Help Scout is one of the best-liked help desks on the market for teams who want to answer email and chat well, start in an afternoon, and keep support feeling human. Count your channels and your agents: heavy voice and WhatsApp volume at scale means CommBox, and almost everything else means Help Scout.
