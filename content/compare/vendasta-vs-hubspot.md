## TL;DR

- **Pick Vendasta** if your agency makes money reselling SEO, reputation, social, and website products to local businesses and you want it all under your own brand with billing attached.
- **Pick HubSpot CRM** if you need a CRM and marketing platform to run your own company's pipeline, with a free tier and the largest integration ecosystem in the category.

## These solve different problems

The comparison exists because Vendasta ships a CRM, but that CRM is a component, not the product. **Vendasta is reseller infrastructure**: a marketplace of **250+ white-label apps**, client-facing portals your clients log into under your logo, automated subscription billing, and Snapshot Reports that generate audit-style leads. It answers "how do I package and sell other people's software as my own?"

**HubSpot** answers "how do I manage my own customers?" It is a CRM with marketing, sales, service, and CMS hubs stacked on top, used by everyone from solo founders on the free tier to enterprise revenue teams. Nothing about it is designed for resale — it is designed for you to operate.

The honest framing: if you are an agency that only does services delivery, HubSpot is your CRM and Vendasta is irrelevant. If you are an agency building a recurring-revenue product business on top of local SMBs, Vendasta is your operating system and you will probably still want a CRM for your own pipeline.

## Pricing models point in opposite directions

**Vendasta** prices per account: **$99/mo Starter**, **$499/mo Professional**, **$999/mo Premium**, plus a mandatory **$500–$1,500 onboarding fee**. The jump from Starter to Professional is steep and most of the features agencies actually want sit above the $99 line. On top of that you pay wholesale for the marketplace products you resell — that is the business model, not a hidden fee, but it means the subscription is your floor, not your total.

**HubSpot** prices per seat: free CRM with unlimited users, **Starter at $20/seat/mo**, **Professional at $100/seat/mo** with a one-time **$1,500 onboarding fee**, Enterprise at **$150/seat/mo**. Marketing Hub layers contact-tier pricing on top, which is where HubSpot bills get away from people.

For a five-person agency, HubSpot Starter runs about **$100/mo** all-in; Vendasta Professional is **$499/mo** regardless of headcount. Vendasta gets cheaper per person as you grow; HubSpot gets more expensive.

## Whose brand is on the screen

This is the single dimension where the two are not comparable at all. Vendasta's core promise is that **your client never sees Vendasta** — the portal, the dashboards, the reports, the invoices, and the notification emails all carry your agency's brand. For agencies selling to local businesses that expect a single trusted vendor, this is the whole pitch.

HubSpot has a Solutions Partner program and lets partners manage client portals, but the product remains visibly HubSpot. Your clients know they are in HubSpot. That is fine for most agencies — HubSpot is a credibility signal in itself — but it is a fundamentally different relationship. Vendasta lets you own the customer; HubSpot shares them.

## Who should pick what

- **Agency reselling digital products to local SMBs** → Vendasta.
- **Agency that needs a CRM for its own new business pipeline** → HubSpot CRM.
- **Clients must only ever see your brand** → Vendasta.
- **Small team wanting a free, capable CRM today** → HubSpot CRM.
- **You need automated client billing and subscription management** → Vendasta.
- **Marketing-led company running email, landing pages, and lifecycle campaigns** → HubSpot CRM.

## Bottom line

Vendasta and HubSpot get compared because both are sold to agencies, but they sit at different layers. Vendasta is the wholesale, white-label, billing-and-fulfillment stack for an agency whose product is other people's software — powerful if that is your model, expensive overhead if it isn't. HubSpot is the CRM you run your own business on, with a free tier that makes it a near-default starting point and a paid ladder that gets pricey fast. If you resell, look at Vendasta. If you sell your own services, look at HubSpot. Plenty of agencies genuinely need both.
