## TL;DR

- **Pick Redtail** if you're a financial advisor, RIA, or broker-dealer who needs compliant messaging, email archiving, and custodian data feeds in the CRM itself.
- **Pick Pipedrive** if your work is linear sales and you want a cheaper, cleaner, more modern pipeline tool without financial-services baggage you don't need.

## The real decision: a regulated-industry tool vs. a sales tool

Redtail and Pipedrive aren't really competing for the same buyer — they only collide when an advisor is CRM-shopping and hasn't yet realized how different they are. Pipedrive is a sales-pipeline CRM: a beautiful Kanban of deals moving toward a close, ideal for reps and agencies. Redtail is infrastructure for a regulated practice: it exists because advisors have obligations — archiving communications, supervising messaging, keeping client account data current — that a sales CRM never contemplates.

Redtail holds the largest market share among advisor CRMs, and it earned that not by being the prettiest tool but by being the safe, compliant, well-integrated default. Its data model, workflows, and terminology are built for financial advisory. Pipedrive, by contrast, would have an advisor bending organizations-and-deals into a shape that never quite fits client relationships, and solving compliance somewhere else entirely.

If you're a solo advisor whose "pipeline" is really prospect tracking, you might briefly wonder whether Pipedrive's simplicity is enough. It isn't — the moment you need an archived email trail or a Schwab feed, the generic CRM stops being viable.

## Pricing

Pipedrive is cheaper per seat: $14/user/mo (Essential) to $99 (Enterprise). Redtail starts around $39/user/mo. On raw price, Pipedrive wins.

But Redtail's price buys things Pipedrive doesn't sell at any tier: automatic email archiving, compliant texting, and custodian integrations. Replicating even part of that around Pipedrive means bolting on separate archiving and messaging-compliance vendors — erasing the price advantage and leaving you managing three tools instead of one. For a sales team, Pipedrive's low price is a real win. For an advisor, it's a discount on the wrong product.

## Compliance and communications

This section has no contest. Redtail Email provides automatic email archiving to satisfy recordkeeping requirements, and Redtail Speak enables compliant, supervised text messaging with clients — both purpose-built for RIA and broker-dealer rules. Automated workflows handle onboarding, review scheduling, and task sequences in a compliant frame.

Pipedrive offers none of this. It's an excellent tool for logging sales activity, but it has no concept of supervision, archiving, or regulated communications. An advisor using Pipedrive would run compliance through separate systems, which is exactly the fragmentation a CRM is supposed to eliminate.

## Custodian and fintech ecosystem

Redtail's integration breadth is aimed at the advisory stack: direct custodian feeds from Schwab and Fidelity, plus eMoney, Orion, MoneyGuidePro, and Riskalyze — and, as part of Orion since 2022, deep Orion portfolio-management ties. Pipedrive's 300+ integrations and Zapier support are broad but sales-flavored, with none of the custodian or planning connectivity advisors depend on. The tradeoff to weigh with Redtail isn't capability — it's the Orion-ecosystem lock-in that some non-Orion firms flag.

## Who should pick what

- **RIA or broker-dealer needing compliance in the CRM** → Redtail.
- **General sales team living in a pipeline** → Pipedrive.
- **You need email archiving and compliant texting** → Redtail.
- **You want the cheapest, most modern deal tracker** → Pipedrive.
- **You rely on Schwab/Fidelity feeds or use Orion** → Redtail.
- **You're a non-advisory services business selling projects** → Pipedrive.
