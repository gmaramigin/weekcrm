## TL;DR
- **Pick BoldTrail** if you run a 10+ agent team or brokerage and want one login for CRM, IDX website, lead-gen ads, dialer, and back office.
- **Pick Realvolve** if you already have a lead source and an IDX site, and what you actually need is deep, behavior-triggered nurture automation at a fraction of the price.

## Scope: a platform vs a tool

The honest framing here is size. BoldTrail (the rebrand of kvCORE from Inside Real Estate) is a full real estate operating system — it bundles CRM, IDX websites, 20+ lead-gen tools, marketing automation, an AI assistant, and BoldTrail BackOffice for transactions and commissions. It's the backbone behind RE/MAX, eXp Realty, and Berkshire Hathaway HomeServices, built to run at 1,000+ agent deployments.

Realvolve is deliberately not that. It's a workflow-first CRM with no IDX website and no lead generation. It does one thing extremely well — automated client-journey sequences — and expects you to bring your own top of funnel.

## Pricing reality

Realvolve is transparent and cheap: $94/month with a 14-day free trial, for a workflow engine that overlaps with platforms charging three to five times more.

BoldTrail has no public pricing and no self-serve trial. The individual entry point is around $499/month, with team and brokerage tiers gated behind a sales demo. At the solo level that $499 is a stretch; the value curve bends sharply upward only once you have a team big enough to use the website, ads, and back office together.

## Automation depth

This is where Realvolve punches above its weight. Its workflows respond to what clients actually do — email opens, replies, property views — not just elapsed time, and they support branches, conditions, and long multi-step sequences. Relationship scoring surfaces cooling contacts before they go cold. For an agent whose problem is nurture consistency, this is the sharper instrument.

BoldTrail's Smart Campaigns are also behavior-triggered and genuinely capable, with an AI assistant that surfaces seller intent and drafts follow-ups. But that automation is one feature inside a much larger platform, so you're paying for — and learning — far more than the nurture engine alone.

## Onboarding and time-to-value

Realvolve has a non-trivial learning curve on its workflow builder, but it's a single system you can master in days. BoldTrail is a rollout, not a setup: expect 4–8 weeks before a team is getting real value, and budget for implementation as a project. Support also varies by tier — enterprise accounts report strong CSM coverage, smaller accounts report ticket-based help.

## Ecosystem

BoldTrail wins on breadth: a large integration library, dialer, IDX, and back office under one roof. Realvolve has a smaller ecosystem and a mixed mobile app — most of its power lives in the web app. If you need many connected tools, BoldTrail's surface area is an advantage; if you just need a CRM that plugs into your existing stack, Realvolve's focus is a feature, not a limitation.

## Who should pick which

Solo agents and referral/repeat-business agents who already generate leads should look hard at Realvolve — it's focused, affordable, and built for relationship nurture. Teams and brokerages that want to stop stitching together a CRM, a website builder, a dialer, and a marketing tool should evaluate BoldTrail, and insist on a reference call with a team their own size before signing.

## Bottom line

These rarely compete for the same buyer. If your problem is "I let leads go cold," Realvolve solves it for $94/month without forcing you into an all-in-one you'll half-use. If your problem is "we're a growing team running five disconnected tools," BoldTrail consolidates them — at enterprise price and enterprise rollout effort. Match the tool to your scale, not to the longer feature list.
