## TL;DR
- **Pick Wise Agent** if you are a solo agent or small team who wants transaction management, drip marketing, and a calendar bundled into one affordable, flat-priced CRM without per-seat math.
- **Pick Follow Up Boss** if you run a portal-lead team that needs instant lead routing, accountability reporting, and a large integration ecosystem, and you will pay a premium for adoption.

## Pricing
Wise Agent is built around a single, flat-rate plan that lands near $49 per month (cheaper billed annually), with modest add-on pricing for extra team members. There are no feature tiers to decode, transaction tools, drip campaigns, and the marketing suite all come in the box. For a one-to-three person operation, the all-in cost is hard to beat.

Follow Up Boss prices per seat. The entry **Grow** plan is roughly $69/user per month, with **Pro** and **Platform** tiers climbing toward $499 and $833 monthly for larger groups, and the dialer is a separate add-on. Per-seat cost falls as the team grows, but a small team will pay multiples of what Wise Agent charges.

## Core approach
Wise Agent is the affordable all-rounder: one tool that handles contacts, transactions, calendar, and marketing so a smaller shop never has to stitch pieces together. Follow Up Boss is purpose-built as the nerve center of a lead-buying team, prioritizing speed-to-lead, distribution, and accountability over being a do-everything suite.

## Lead & pipeline workflow
Follow Up Boss owns this category. Inbound leads route automatically by zip code, price point, round-robin, or first-to-claim, Action Plans drip text and email on a schedule, and team leaders can see whether each agent actually worked the lead. Wise Agent captures and assigns leads competently and runs reliable drip campaigns and call lists, but its routing logic is simpler and aimed at individuals or tight teams rather than high-volume distribution across many agents.

## Integrations
Follow Up Boss connects to 250-plus lead sources and tools, including two-way Zillow stage sync, dialers, and transaction platforms like dotloop and SkySlope, which is central to how it operates. Wise Agent offers a respectable native integration set plus Zapier, enough for most solo and small-team stacks, but it does not match Follow Up Boss's breadth or its deep portal hooks.

## Who should pick what
- **Solo agents** watching every dollar: Wise Agent, for the bundled tools at a flat price.
- **Small teams** that want marketing and transactions in one place: Wise Agent.
- **Teams buying portal leads** that need routing and accountability: Follow Up Boss.
- **Operations dependent on many third-party tools**: Follow Up Boss, for its integration library.

## Bottom line
Choose **Wise Agent** when value and an all-in-one feature set matter more than scale, you get transactions, marketing, and a calendar for one low price. Choose **Follow Up Boss** when you run a team on bought leads and need best-in-class routing, accountability, and integrations, and the higher per-seat cost pays for itself in faster, better-tracked follow-up.
