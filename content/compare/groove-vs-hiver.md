## TL;DR

- **Pick Groove** if you want a clean, standalone shared-inbox helpdesk that works across any email provider and keeps support workflows out of your personal inbox.
- **Pick Hiver** if your team is on Google Workspace and wants support tooling that lives inside Gmail — no new login, no context switching.

## What they share

Both Groove and Hiver are designed for small support teams that need structure without complexity. Both replace the chaos of a shared support@ Gmail or Outlook inbox with assigned conversations, status tracking, collision detection, canned replies, and basic reporting. Both can be set up in hours rather than weeks. Neither is trying to compete with Zendesk or Freshdesk on feature depth — they're competing on simplicity and speed.

The difference is the interface philosophy: Groove builds its own clean web app; Hiver builds inside Gmail.

## Interface and adoption

This is the axis that usually decides the comparison.

Hiver's Gmail integration means agents never leave their inbox. The Hiver panel appears in the Gmail sidebar — shared inboxes, conversation assignments, notes, tags, and SLA timers all live there. For a team where every member checks Gmail dozens of times a day, the adoption friction is near zero.

Groove is a separate web app. It's clean and fast — agents connect their email, conversations appear in Groove's inbox, and the interface is intuitive enough that new agents are productive within an hour. But it is a second tab, a second login, a second place to check. For some teams that discipline is an asset (support is separate from personal email); for others it's drag.

## Pricing

Groove: plans typically run $12–40/agent/mo depending on tier and features. The base tier is lean but covers shared inboxes, canned replies, and basic reporting. Higher tiers add automation, integrations, and more detailed analytics. Pricing is transparent.

Hiver: Lite at roughly $19/user/mo, Pro at ~$49/user/mo, Elite at ~$79/user/mo (verify at hiver.com — pricing has shifted in recent periods). One nuance: Hiver bills every Google Workspace user who handles support email, which can make the effective per-seat cost higher if support is a shared responsibility across a broad team.

For a focused 8-agent support team, the pricing is comparable. Groove may be slightly cheaper at the base tier; Hiver costs more if you're counting seats across a larger team where only some people handle support.

## Automation and workflows

Groove has rule-based automation for routing, tagging, and auto-replies — adequate for most small teams. You can set rules like "if email contains 'refund' assign to billing team and tag as urgent." Nothing more advanced than that without integrations.

Hiver's automation works similarly but runs inside Gmail's infrastructure, which means it hooks into Google Workspace's own filters and labels. For teams that already use Gmail filters extensively, Hiver's automations feel natural. For teams with more complex routing needs, both platforms will eventually feel limiting and point toward Freshdesk or Zendesk.

## Reporting

Both platforms cover the core metrics: conversation volume, response time, first reply, and CSAT. Groove's reporting interface is simple and digestible. Hiver's reporting is comparable in scope with slightly different visualization choices.

Neither platform is a fit for support orgs that need custom dashboards, cohort analysis, or executive-level BI reporting. At that point you're looking at Freshdesk Analytics, Zendesk Explore, or a dedicated BI tool regardless of which helpdesk you choose.

## Integrations

Groove integrates with Shopify, Slack, HubSpot, Salesforce, and Zapier — the standard SMB toolkit. Integration depth is functional rather than deep.

Hiver lives inside Google Workspace, which means Google Calendar, Google Meet, and Google Drive integrations are native. Third-party integrations (Salesforce, Slack, HubSpot) exist but are add-ons rather than native features.

## Verdict

If your team is fully on Google Workspace and your support is primarily email, Hiver's inbox-native experience is a genuine differentiator — try it before assuming you need a separate tool. If you're not a Google shop, or if you want your support workflow visually separated from personal email, Groove delivers a clean, affordable helpdesk experience that most small teams won't outgrow for years.
