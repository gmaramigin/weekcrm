## TL;DR

- **Pick Kommo** if your sales happen in chat — WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok, SMS — and you want a CRM that turns conversations into a pipeline with no-code salesbots.
- **Pick Bitrix24** if you want a single platform for CRM, projects, telephony, HR, and team chat, and you're willing to invest setup time to consolidate your stack.

## Pricing

Kommo starts at $15/user/month but requires a 6-month minimum commitment — there's no monthly billing — with a 14-day free trial. Bitrix24 uses per-organization pricing: a forever-free tier plus paid plans from around $49/month flat for unlimited users. For a larger team, Bitrix24's model is far cheaper because you're not paying per seat; for a small messaging-focused team, Kommo's per-user cost stays reasonable and the trade is purchasing channel-native functionality Bitrix24 doesn't match. Note Kommo's commitment structure — you're locking in half a year up front.

## Core focus

Kommo is conversational CRM done deliberately. It unifies WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok, email, and SMS into one inbox and pipeline, so leads from any channel land in the same Kanban board. Bitrix24 includes a CRM but is really a business operating system — projects, document storage, telephony, an internal social network, and HR all share the login. Kommo is narrow and deep on messaging; Bitrix24 is wide and general. If your pipeline lives in DMs, Kommo's focus is the whole point. If you want to replace several tools, Bitrix24's breadth wins.

## Messaging and channels

This is the cleanest distinction. Kommo's entire architecture is built around messenger channels and no-code salesbots that qualify and follow up automatically inside chat threads. Bitrix24 has telephony, email, and some channel connectors, but messaging isn't its center of gravity — WhatsApp-native selling is bolted on rather than core. For agencies and service businesses in markets where WhatsApp drives sales (Latin America, parts of Europe), Kommo is purpose-built; Bitrix24 will feel like a workaround.

## Automation and bots

Both offer automation. Kommo's no-code salesbots are designed for conversational flows — qualification, routing, follow-up — and pair with a built-in AI agent for lead qualification. Bitrix24's automation spans the whole platform (deals, tasks, documents) but is more general-purpose and tangled in a busier interface. For chat-driven automation specifically, Kommo is more focused; for cross-functional workflow automation, Bitrix24 reaches further.

## Usability

Kommo's interface is relatively focused because its scope is narrower — the Kanban pipeline and unified inbox are the main surfaces. Bitrix24 is notoriously cluttered; new users frequently feel lost, and onboarding without guidance takes real time, with support that's widely criticized as slow. Kommo is faster to get productive in for its specific use case.

## Who should pick what

- **Teams selling via WhatsApp/Instagram/DMs** → Kommo. It's purpose-built for conversational pipelines.
- **Agencies and service businesses in chat-heavy markets** → Kommo, for native messenger integration.
- **Companies wanting to consolidate many tools** → Bitrix24, for CRM plus projects, HR, and telephony.
- **Larger teams watching per-seat cost** → Bitrix24, whose per-organization pricing scales affordably.
- **Budget teams wanting a free starting point** → Bitrix24's free tier; Kommo has only a trial and a 6-month minimum.

## Bottom line

These tools barely compete — they just both call themselves CRMs. Kommo is the right answer when conversations are your sales channel: it brings structure to WhatsApp and Instagram pipelines without losing the chat feel, and nothing in Bitrix24 replicates that. Bitrix24 is the right answer when you want one platform to run the whole business and can absorb its learning curve and cluttered UI. Decide based on where your deals actually happen — in chat threads, choose Kommo; across many tools you'd like to merge, choose Bitrix24.
