## TL;DR

- **Pick Front** if your team's primary workflow is collaborative inbox management and you want conversations to feel human — not like tickets routed through a queue.
- **Pick HubSpot Service Hub** if your company is already on HubSpot, or you need a classic ticket pipeline with strong knowledge base, chatbot, and CRM-native reporting.

## Pricing

Front: Starter at ~$19/seat/mo, Growth at ~$59, Scale at ~$99, Premier at ~$229. Pricing is per seat with no volume floor on Starter; higher tiers have minimums. The $59 Growth plan is where most support teams land — it unlocks SLAs, analytics, and integrations.

HubSpot Service Hub: Starter at ~$15/seat/mo (2-seat minimum), Professional at ~$90/seat/mo (5-seat minimum, ~$5,400/year baseline), Enterprise at ~$130/seat/mo (10-seat minimum). Professional is where real support workflows live — the Starter tier is limited. Bundled CRM Suite pricing often changes the math if you're buying multiple Hubs.

For a 10-agent team, Front Growth costs ~$590/mo vs. HubSpot Professional at ~$900/mo. That gap narrows if you're buying HubSpot Sales or Marketing Hub alongside Service Hub, since suite discounts apply.

## Inbox model vs. ticket model

Front's defining characteristic is that it treats customer communication as collaborative email, not a queue of tickets. Every email, SMS, or chat arrives in a shared inbox. Teammates can leave internal comments on threads, @mention colleagues, assign conversations, and snooze follow-ups — all without the customer seeing any of it. It feels more like a team email client than enterprise support software.

HubSpot Service Hub is ticket-centric. Inbound contacts create tickets with properties, pipelines, and statuses. Agents work through a ticket queue. SLA timers and automation act on ticket status. This is the standard help desk model and what most support orgs expect.

Which model fits depends on your team's mental model. Operations teams, logistics coordinators, and account managers who "manage relationships via email" often prefer Front's inbox metaphor. Formal support teams with SLA accountability and routing complexity often prefer HubSpot's ticket pipeline.

## CRM integration

This is HubSpot's clearest advantage. Every ticket in Service Hub is linked to a HubSpot contact and company. Support agents see the full HubSpot timeline: marketing emails received, sales call notes, deal history, form submissions. A customer who was just in a sales negotiation and then opens a support ticket gets handled with that sales context visible.

Front integrates with CRMs — Salesforce, HubSpot, and others — via its CRM panel, surfacing contact data inside conversations. It's useful, but it's integration, not native unification. Data syncs in one direction and you're not working from the same record as your sales team.

## Automation and routing

Front's rule engine handles routing by sender, subject, keyword, tag, team, and custom attributes. Rules can assign, tag, reply, and archive. It handles standard support routing well; complex multi-condition routing across dozens of teams gets harder to manage.

HubSpot Service Hub's Workflows (Professional+) are part of HubSpot's broader automation engine — one of the most capable in the market. Workflows can enroll tickets in sequences, send internal notifications, update contact properties, trigger task creation, and branch on any ticket or contact property. If you need to trigger a sales follow-up email when a support ticket closes, that's native in HubSpot.

## Knowledge base and self-service

HubSpot's knowledge base is the stronger product. It supports article versioning, team access controls, multi-language content, and a chatbot (Breeze) that deflects using knowledge base articles before a ticket is created. At Professional+, AI-powered suggestions surface articles inside the live chat widget.

Front's help center is functional and clean. Customers can search articles; agents can share links inside conversations. The AI-powered article suggestions in conversations are a genuinely useful workflow shortcut. For teams publishing under 200 articles, it's sufficient. For large knowledge bases or multi-language requirements, HubSpot's tooling is deeper.

## Reporting

HubSpot Service Hub's reporting benefits from the full HubSpot analytics stack: custom dashboards, cross-Hub attribution (how many closed deals started as support tickets), CSAT trends, and SLA compliance reports. It's the right tool if leadership wants to see support alongside pipeline and marketing ROI.

Front's analytics covers inbox and conversation metrics — response time, handle time, team load, CSAT, SLA compliance. It's solid for a support team's operational view but doesn't connect to sales pipeline or marketing attribution.

## Verdict

Front and HubSpot Service Hub target different primary use cases despite overlapping on shared inbox and basic ticketing. Front wins when the team's work feels like managing email collaboratively — common in account management, operations, and logistics where "support" is a secondary function. HubSpot Service Hub wins when formal ticketing, knowledge base depth, and CRM-native data sharing matter — especially if you're already paying for other HubSpot products. Very few teams should choose Front over HubSpot if they're already on HubSpot; very few teams should choose HubSpot over Front if they're not and primarily want collaborative inbox management.
