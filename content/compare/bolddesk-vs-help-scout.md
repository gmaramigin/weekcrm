## TL;DR

- **Pick BoldDesk** if you need structured ticketing: SLA policies with escalation, automation rules, and 10+ channels including WhatsApp, SMS, Teams, and voice in one plan.
- **Pick Help Scout** if support is a conversation, not a queue — a shared inbox that feels like email, a free tier for 5 users, and AI that quietly handles 70% of routine requests.

## Same $25, different products

Both land at **$25 per seat per month** for the entry paid tier, which makes the philosophical split unusually clean to evaluate. You're not trading money for capability here — you're choosing a model.

BoldDesk is a **help desk** in the traditional sense. Tickets have states, SLAs, escalation paths, and routing rules. That structure exists because some support orgs have contractual response-time obligations and multi-channel intake that genuinely needs orchestration.

Help Scout is built on the opposite conviction: that ticketing systems make support feel bureaucratic to the customer. Its shared inbox deliberately looks like email, with no ticket numbers in the customer's face. That's not a missing feature — it's the product thesis.

## Channel coverage is the sharpest divide

BoldDesk consolidates email, live chat, SMS, WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram, Microsoft Teams, Slack, and web forms, plus native Twilio voice so agents take calls without leaving the platform. Ten-plus channels, no per-channel add-on pricing.

Help Scout covers shared email inboxes, the **Beacon** widget, in-app messaging, and a knowledge base. It's a tight, well-executed set — but it's web and email support, not messaging-app support.

If your customers reach you on WhatsApp or by phone, this comparison is already decided. If they email you and occasionally open a chat widget, BoldDesk's extra channels are capacity you'll pay for and never activate.

## Two different bets on AI

Help Scout's AI assistant is a **deflection** play: it resolves an average of **70% of routine requests** before a human is involved, and Beacon proactively surfaces knowledge base articles to stop tickets from being created at all. The strategy is to shrink the queue.

BoldDesk's AI is an **agent-leverage** play: generated reply suggestions and smart routing so the humans working tickets move faster. The strategy is to raise throughput.

Both are legitimate. Deflection wins when your volume is repetitive and documentable; agent leverage wins when tickets are varied enough that automated resolution is unrealistic.

## Onboarding and the free-tier question

Help Scout's onboarding advantage is real and underrated — the inbox interface is familiar to anyone who uses email, cutting ramp from days to hours. Combined with a free plan for up to **5 users**, an early-stage team can be live at zero cost with no training budget.

BoldDesk has no free tier for commercial use (nonprofits get full access free) and asks more configuration up front, because SLA policies and routing rules require decisions before they're useful. That setup cost is the price of the structure.

Worth flagging on the Help Scout side: the **Plus tier at $45/user/mo** is where advanced workflow automation and deeper integrations live, and per-seat pricing compounds. A 20-person team on Plus is a different budget conversation than the $25 entry price implies.

## Who should pick what

- **Team with contractual SLA obligations** → BoldDesk.
- **Early-stage startup with 5 or fewer support people** → Help Scout (free plan).
- **Customers arriving on WhatsApp, SMS, or phone** → BoldDesk.
- **Support that should read as personal, not transactional** → Help Scout.
- **Internal IT helpdesk needing Teams and Slack intake** → BoldDesk.
- **Ecommerce or SaaS team with high repetitive email volume** → Help Scout (AI deflection).

## Bottom line

Help Scout is one of the best-liked support tools on the market because it solves a real problem: help desks that make customers feel processed. If your support is email-first and conversational, it's fast, cheap to start, and its AI deflection genuinely works. BoldDesk isn't trying to win that argument — it's built for teams whose support has outgrown an inbox, with channel breadth and SLA machinery Help Scout doesn't attempt. At an identical entry price, pick by asking whether your bottleneck is *tone* or *structure*. Help Scout fixes tone. BoldDesk provides structure.
