## TL;DR

- **Pick Insightly** if you want a CRM that doesn't stop at the closed deal — built-in project management, workflow automation, and optional marketing and service modules let you run delivery and growth from one platform.
- **Pick Capsule** if you want an affordable, no-fuss CRM that any non-technical SMB can adopt quickly, with contacts and a simple pipeline at the centre and nothing extra to learn.

## Pricing

Capsule is the budget-friendly option with a real free tier (2 users, 250 contacts), then Starter around $18/user/mo and Growth around $36, climbing gently from there. Insightly starts higher — Plus near $29/user/mo, Professional near $49, and Enterprise near $99 — and its marketing and service products are billed as separate modules, so a full Insightly stack adds up. For a small business that only needs CRM, Capsule is materially cheaper. Insightly's price reflects that you're buying a platform, not just a contact database.

## Data model / Core approach

This is the defining difference. Insightly unifies CRM and project management: when a deal closes, it can convert into a project with tasks, milestones, and pipelines of its own, all linked back to the original opportunity. It also supports custom objects and record linking for more complex relationships. Capsule deliberately stays simple — People and Organisations, with Opportunities and Cases on top, plus custom fields and tags. Capsule will never manage your delivery work; Insightly is designed to. If sold work turns into projects you must track, Insightly fits; if your workflow ends at the sale, Capsule keeps things clean.

## Pipeline and sales workflows

Capsule's pipeline is clear and immediately usable: drag opportunities through stages, attach values and close dates, and get a milestone-based forecast a small team understands at a glance. Insightly offers multiple pipelines for both sales and projects, plus workflow automation that can trigger tasks and emails across stages. Capsule wins on time-to-value and simplicity; Insightly wins on automation and the ability to carry a record from lead all the way through delivery. A services firm benefits from Insightly's continuity; a straightforward sales team rarely needs it.

## Email and integrations

Both connect to Gmail and Outlook and log correspondence to contacts. Capsule offers templates, a Bcc capture address, and native links to Transpond (marketing), Xero, QuickBooks, and Zapier — handy for SMB accountancy workflows. Insightly provides email tracking and templates, and its real strength is the connected ecosystem of Insightly Marketing and Service plus a solid API, so larger teams can build automated cross-module flows. For simple bookkeeping integrations Capsule is convenient; for an integrated sales-marketing-service platform Insightly goes further.

## Who should pick what

- **Services firms that deliver projects after the sale** → Insightly.
- **Non-technical SMBs wanting an affordable, simple CRM** → Capsule.
- **Teams wanting CRM, marketing, and service in one ecosystem** → Insightly.
- **Businesses that need a free tier or tight accountancy links** → Capsule.

## Bottom line

Insightly and Capsule serve different ambitions. Capsule is the dependable, inexpensive CRM for a small business that wants contacts and deals tracked without overhead. Insightly is the broader platform for teams whose work continues past the sale — managing projects, automating workflows, and optionally adding marketing and service. If your process ends when the deal closes, Capsule's simplicity and price are hard to beat. If you need to deliver and grow inside one system, Insightly earns its higher cost.
