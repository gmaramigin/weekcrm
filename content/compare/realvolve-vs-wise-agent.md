## TL;DR
- **Pick Realvolve** if your problem is nurture and consistency — you want deep, branching, behavior-triggered workflows and relationship scoring to keep clients from going cold, and you'll invest time learning the builder.
- **Pick Wise Agent** if you want a dependable, flat-rate CRM with strong transaction management, a ready-made content library, and 24/7 live human support — without a steep setup curve.

## Pricing
Wise Agent is flat-rate at $49/mo covering the whole feature set, with a 14-day free trial and no credit card required. Realvolve starts at $94/mo, also with a 14-day trial. Both are dramatically cheaper than the all-in-one platforms, and neither plays the per-user upgrade games that gate essentials elsewhere. Wise Agent is the lower-cost entry and the more predictable bill; Realvolve costs more but buys a deeper automation engine.

## Workflow automation
This is where the two genuinely diverge. Realvolve is built around workflows as the main event: long-running, multi-step sequences of emails, texts, tasks, and reminders that branch on conditions and fire off client behavior or deal stage. It's the more powerful automation tool by a clear margin. Wise Agent has solid automation — drip campaigns, email/SMS marketing, landing pages — but it's broader and shallower, designed to be usable on day one rather than configured into a custom machine. If process automation is the reason you're shopping, Realvolve wins; if you want it to "just work" out of the box, Wise Agent.

## Relationship intelligence
Realvolve assigns engagement-based relationship scores that surface cooling contacts before you lose them — a feature aimed squarely at referral-and-repeat agents who can't afford to let a past client drift. Wise Agent focuses less on scoring and more on making sure the operational side (contacts, transactions, follow-up tasks) is covered. For an agent whose entire business is the strength of their book, Realvolve's scoring is a meaningful edge.

## Transaction management
Wise Agent's strength: built-in contract-to-close checklists, templates, document storage, and status tracking, so you don't need a separate transaction tool. Realvolve handles deal stages within its workflow model but isn't pitched as a transaction-management product first. If contract-to-close coordination is a daily pain, Wise Agent addresses it more directly.

## Support and onboarding
Wise Agent includes 24/7 live human support on phone and chat at the base tier — a genuine rarity at $49/mo — and has held the Forbes Advisor "Best Real Estate CRM" spot three years running. Realvolve has a non-trivial learning curve on its workflow builder; expect to invest time before it pays off, and its integration library and ecosystem are smaller. For an agent who wants help when stuck, Wise Agent is the gentler landing.

## Who should pick what
- **Referral/repeat-business agents** → Realvolve. Relationship scoring and deep nurture workflows.
- **Solo and part-time agents on a budget** → Wise Agent. $49 flat, fast to start.
- **Agents who want contract-to-close baked in** → Wise Agent. Transaction management is a core strength.
- **Process-obsessed agents who'll build custom sequences** → Realvolve. The workflow engine rewards the effort.
- **Anyone who values live human support** → Wise Agent. 24/7 phone and chat at the base price.

## Bottom line
Both are smart, affordable picks for agents who already have a lead source and don't want to overpay for an all-in-one. Choose Realvolve if your edge is relationship depth and you're willing to learn a powerful workflow builder. Choose Wise Agent if you want a proven, low-cost, well-supported CRM with transaction management included that you can use the day you sign up. Neither generates leads, so plan your top-of-funnel separately.
