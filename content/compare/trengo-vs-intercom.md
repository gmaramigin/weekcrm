## TL;DR

- **Pick Trengo** if your customers reach you across WhatsApp, Instagram, Messenger, SMS, email, and chat, and you want one shared team inbox with predictable per-team pricing. It's especially strong for European and Netherlands-based businesses where WhatsApp dominates.
- **Pick Intercom** if you're a product-led SaaS company that wants AI-first, in-product support with the industry-leading Fin AI agent and behavior-triggered messaging deeply tied to your app.

## Pricing

The models differ in a way that matters for forecasting. Trengo prices per team, not per seat — from €299/mo for 10 users (annual) — keeping costs predictable as you add agents. Intercom prices per seat: Essential $29, Advanced $85, Expert $132/seat/mo, plus Fin AI at $0.99 per resolved ticket and an extra $35/seat/mo for unlimited AI copilot access. For a growing team, Trengo's fixed-team pricing is easier to budget; Intercom's seat-plus-usage model can swing significantly with headcount and AI volume.

## Channels: WhatsApp-first vs. in-product-first

This is the clearest divide. Trengo is built around messaging channels — native WhatsApp Business API with broadcast capability, plus Instagram, Messenger, SMS, email, and live chat in one shared view. It's the better tool when customers live in messaging apps. Intercom started as in-product live chat for SaaS and excels there: it triggers targeted messages based on user behavior, usage data, and lifecycle stage, and keeps support inside the product experience. Intercom does support WhatsApp and SMS too, but its center of gravity is the web/mobile product surface, not the messaging-app inbox.

## AI agents

Both lead with autonomous AI on a pay-per-resolution model, but Intercom's Fin is the headline. Fin resolves questions autonomously across chat, email, SMS, and social with industry-leading resolution rates, billed at $0.99 per issue actually resolved. Trengo offers AI-powered routing and automated responses that triage and assign conversations to the right agent. If AI deflection of high ticket volume is the goal, Intercom's Fin is the stronger engine; if intelligent routing across messaging channels is the need, Trengo covers it well.

## Team collaboration and fit

Trengo is purpose-built for collaborative inboxes: internal notes, @mentions, and collision detection prevent duplicate replies across support, sales, and operations. It's a support-focused tool, so depth for pure CRM or sales use cases is limited. Intercom is powerful but complex — smaller teams often pay for features they don't use, and doing setup well takes time. Trengo fits SMB and mid-market teams handling high messaging volume; Intercom fits digital-first companies that can invest in configuration.

## Cost predictability

Worth calling out twice because it drives real decisions: Trengo's per-team pricing and Intercom's usage-based Fin pricing pull in opposite directions. Trengo gives you a known monthly number. Intercom's true bill is harder to predict — model your expected Fin resolution volume and copilot seats before committing.

## Who should pick what

- **Businesses where customers use WhatsApp and social messaging** → Trengo.
- **Teams wanting predictable, per-team pricing** → Trengo.
- **Product-led SaaS needing in-app, AI-first support** → Intercom.
- **High-volume support teams banking on AI deflection** → Intercom (Fin).

## Bottom line

Trengo and Intercom both tame conversation chaos, but for different teams. Trengo is the omnichannel, WhatsApp-first inbox with predictable per-team pricing — ideal for SMBs whose customers message them across apps. Intercom is the AI-first suite for product-led SaaS, with Fin and behavior-triggered messaging as best-in-class strengths and a usage-based bill to model carefully. Choose by your customers' channels: if they message you on WhatsApp, choose Trengo; if they live inside your product, choose Intercom.
