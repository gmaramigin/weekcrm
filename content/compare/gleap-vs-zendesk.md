## TL;DR

- **Pick Gleap** if most of your inbound is bug reports and feature requests from users inside a SaaS product, and you want engineers to get reproducible tickets without chasing screenshots.
- **Pick Zendesk** if you're operating a real support desk across email, phone, chat, SMS, and social, with volume that demands routing rules, SLAs, and reporting.

## Product feedback tool vs support platform

The category label hides how different these are. **Gleap** is a product intelligence tool that happens to include support chat. Its defining feature is in-app bug reporting: a user hits a problem, annotates a screenshot without leaving the product, and the report arrives with console logs and session context attached. Feature request boards and surveys feed a prioritized roadmap view. The audience is SaaS product teams and agencies running client-facing apps, and the value is measured in engineer hours not spent asking "what browser were you on?"

**Zendesk** is a support operations platform. The Suite bundles ticketing, help center, live chat and messaging, and voice into one per-agent subscription, and the whole design assumes queues, agents, shifts, and volume. Triggers, macros, SLAs, and AI-assisted routing exist because someone has to decide which of 400 open tickets gets touched next. Fortune 500 support orgs run on it.

If your support problem is *"we can't reproduce what users are reporting,"* that's Gleap's problem statement. If it's *"we can't keep up with the queue,"* that's Zendesk's.

## Pricing model, not just price

The structural difference matters more than the numbers. **Gleap** charges a flat **$119/month** for the Team plan billed annually — headcount doesn't move the bill. **Zendesk** charges **per agent**: Suite from **$55/agent/mo** annually, or **$19/agent/mo** for Support-only if you skip the bundle.

Do the arithmetic and the crossover is early. Two Suite agents cost $110/mo; three cost $165 and you're past Gleap. And Zendesk's sticker is famously not the final number — AI add-ons, Explore analytics, and premium features push real-world costs to **2–3x the base rate** for many buyers. Gleap's AI pricing, by contrast, is a published $0.02 per response.

The honest caveat: Gleap's $119 floor is real money for a two-person startup, and Zendesk's Support-only tier at $19/agent undercuts it for tiny teams that just need a ticket queue. Gleap's flat model wins on the way up, not at the very bottom.

## Channel coverage is the hard limit

This is where Gleap runs out of road. It covers **live chat, AI bot, email, and in-app messaging** in a shared inbox — genuinely enough for a product-led SaaS company whose users live inside the app. It does not do **voice, SMS, or social messaging**, and it has no equivalent to Zendesk's 1,000+ marketplace integrations or Explore analytics.

Zendesk covers all of it, plus the automation layer that makes high volume survivable. The cost is complexity: configuration grows quickly, and getting real value out of Zendesk usually means dedicated admin time. Smaller teams routinely find it over-engineered for their ticket count.

Pick by where your customers actually contact you. If the answer is "inside the app," Gleap's coverage is complete. If it includes a phone number, Zendesk is the only one of the two that answers it.

## Who should pick what

- **SaaS team drowning in vague, unreproducible bug reports** → Gleap.
- **Support org running email, phone, SMS, and social** → Zendesk.
- **Product team that wants feedback feeding a roadmap** → Gleap.
- **Team of 10+ agents where per-seat cost compounds** → Gleap.
- **Company needing SLAs, shift routing, and Explore-grade reporting** → Zendesk.
- **Agency managing bug intake for client web and mobile apps** → Gleap.

## Bottom line

Gleap and Zendesk look like competitors on a category page and rarely are in practice. Gleap is the better buy for SaaS and product teams whose support burden is really a product-feedback burden — the in-app capture of screenshots and console logs is a genuine engineering time-saver, and flat $119/mo pricing beats per-agent economics as soon as you have a few people in the inbox. Zendesk is the better buy for anyone operating support as a function: more channels, deeper automation, real reporting, and a price that climbs accordingly. Choose Gleap to shorten the distance between users and engineers; choose Zendesk to run a desk.
