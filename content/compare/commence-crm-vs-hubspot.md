## TL;DR

- **Pick Commence CRM** if you want a flat, predictable per-seat price with sales, marketing, ticketing, and projects bundled, and no contact-based billing surprises.
- **Pick HubSpot CRM** if you want the best-supported platform in the category — free to start, unmatched integrations, and marketing automation nothing at Commence's price approaches.

## Default platform vs contained alternative

**HubSpot** is the default. It is the most widely adopted CRM in SMB and mid-market, with a free tier supporting unlimited users and up to **1 million contacts**, an app marketplace with **1,500+ integrations**, and HubSpot Academy training your team can use before you buy. Any tool your business runs almost certainly has a HubSpot connector. That gravity is a real, compounding advantage.

**Commence CRM** is not competing for that position and should not be evaluated as if it were. It is a small vendor's integrated SMB system — sales pipeline, drip campaigns, help desk tickets, project tracking — sold at a flat **$29/user/mo** to companies in professional services, manufacturing, and distribution. Its argument is containment: one bill, one vendor, one bundle, no upsell path to a $100 seat.

## The pricing shape matters more than the price

This is the sharpest distinction. Commence's cost is linear — seats times price, plus roughly **$12/user/mo** if you want marketing automation. You can forecast year three today.

HubSpot's cost is a step function. Free is genuinely useful. Starter at **$20/seat/mo** undercuts Commence. Then Professional at **$100/seat/mo** plus a **$1,500** onboarding fee is a 5x jump, and Marketing Hub layers contact-tier pricing on top — roughly **$150–$250/mo per additional 5,000 contacts**, which escalates as your list grows whether or not you email it. Teams leave HubSpot over exactly this: mid-year bill increases they did not choose.

So the question is not "which is cheaper" but "which cost curve fits you." A stable 10-person SMB may pay less on Commence forever. A company that will need real marketing automation will hit the HubSpot cliff and should plan for it rather than pretend Commence is a substitute.

## Capability gap, stated plainly

On product depth this is not close. HubSpot's marketing automation, reporting, mobile experience, and interface are years ahead. Commence's UI is dated, its integration catalog is narrow, and it has no AI layer worth naming against HubSpot's Breeze. If your reps come from a modern tool, expect complaints.

What Commence actually delivers for the money is breadth of function, not quality of function. That is a defensible trade for some businesses and a bad one for others — be honest about which you are.

## Who should pick what

- **Marketing-led company that needs real automation** → HubSpot CRM.
- **Stable SMB wanting a flat, forecastable bill** → Commence CRM.
- **Team that needs help desk and projects in the CRM price** → Commence CRM.
- **Business with a long list of required integrations** → HubSpot CRM.
- **Startup that wants to begin at zero cost** → HubSpot CRM.
- **Team burned by contact-tier pricing escalation** → Commence CRM.

## Bottom line

HubSpot is the stronger product and the safer default — start free, and only confront the Professional cliff when your growth justifies it. Commence CRM is worth a trial only for a specific buyer: an SMB that wants sales, marketing, support, and delivery bundled at a fixed per-seat price and is genuinely untroubled by a dated interface and a thin integration list. If you cannot say yes to that description without hesitation, take HubSpot's free tier instead.
