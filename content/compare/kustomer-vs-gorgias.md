## TL;DR

- **Pick Kustomer** if you operate a serious omnichannel support function — email, live chat, voice, social, SMS — and need a unified CRM timeline view of every customer interaction, not just tickets.
- **Pick Gorgias** if you're a Shopify-native brand (or WooCommerce/Magento) with a support team under 20 agents that wants to resolve commerce-specific tickets fast with order context baked in.

## Pricing

Kustomer and Gorgias price in fundamentally different ways, which matters for the comparison.

Kustomer uses per-user pricing: Enterprise at $89/user/mo and Ultimate at $139/user/mo. For a team of 10 agents, that's $8,900–$13,900/month — pricing that targets mid-market and enterprise support operations, not early-stage brands.

Gorgias prices by ticket volume: Starter ~$10/mo (50 tickets), Basic ~$60/mo (300 tickets), Pro ~$360/mo (2,000 tickets), Advanced ~$900/mo (5,000 tickets). This makes Gorgias extremely accessible for small brands handling low ticket volumes, but the economics change as volume scales. A brand processing 5,000 tickets/month pays $900/mo regardless of how many agents handle them — which can be favorable or expensive depending on your support team structure.

The pricing models make direct comparison tricky. Gorgias wins on entry-level affordability. Kustomer wins for large teams where per-user pricing is more predictable than ticket-volume pricing.

## Platform Fit: E-commerce Specialist vs Omnichannel CRM

Gorgias was purpose-built for e-commerce support, and it shows. The Shopify integration is deep: customer order history, order status, refund and cancel actions, and subscription details all appear inside the ticket view without switching tabs. Agents can issue refunds, update orders, and apply discount codes directly from Gorgias. Revenue attribution tags show which tickets converted to sales. For a DTC brand on Shopify, Gorgias removes the biggest source of agent friction — looking up order data — and packages it where it's needed.

The tradeoff is that Gorgias's value is closely tied to Shopify (and to a lesser extent WooCommerce and Magento). Teams outside e-commerce, or brands running complex multi-channel retail with custom tech stacks, find Gorgias's specialization limiting rather than enabling.

Kustomer is an omnichannel customer service CRM, which is a different product category. It's not just a ticketing tool with commerce integrations — it maintains a full customer timeline: every email, chat, call, social message, purchase, and support interaction in a unified chronological view. This is genuinely powerful for brands where customer relationships span months and support context from six months ago matters to today's conversation. Kustomer integrates with Shopify and Stripe, but it's built for multi-channel operations that need CRM-level context, not just order data.

## Features and Automation

Gorgias's automation is e-commerce specific and very effective within that domain. Rules can auto-close order status inquiries, send tracking links, tag VIP customers from Shopify tags, and route tickets based on order value. The automation is opinionated and relatively easy to configure for common commerce workflows. Where it's less capable: complex multi-step automations, routing based on CRM attributes, or cross-channel escalation logic.

Kustomer's automation engine is more general and more powerful. AI-powered routing uses intent detection to classify and route conversations before an agent sees them. Workflows can span channels — a chat conversation that escalates to a phone call maintains full context. SLA management, priority queues, and team-based routing are all configurable. For support operations that handle a mix of channels with different SLAs and escalation paths, Kustomer's flexibility is a significant advantage.

## AI Capabilities

Kustomer's AI features are a core product differentiator. Intent detection automatically classifies incoming contacts by reason (refund request, shipping question, product complaint) before routing. AI-suggested responses surface relevant knowledge base content to agents. Conversation summaries compress long threads into quick context for agents picking up handoffs. Predictive CSAT scoring flags at-risk conversations before they close. These features are meaningful for high-volume operations where AI triage reduces handle time.

Gorgias's AI capabilities are more narrowly focused on commerce automation. Auto-response for common order status questions, AI-suggested replies, and intent-based routing are available on higher plans. The automation is effective for the Shopify workflow it's optimized for, but it's less sophisticated than Kustomer's intent detection and predictive features on general support operations.

## Team Size and Use Case Fit

Gorgias is a natural fit for DTC brands with 2–30 support agents, high Shopify dependency, and ticket volumes up to a few thousand per month. The onboarding is fast, the Shopify integration requires no custom work, and the pricing is accessible. It's a category leader for the Shopify brand segment precisely because it solves the right problem for that audience.

Kustomer is sized for mid-market and enterprise support teams: brands with 20–200+ agents, omnichannel support operations, and complex customer lifecycles where CRM context matters. Subscription businesses, marketplace sellers, and companies where a customer's support history directly informs commercial decisions (upsell, retention, at-risk flags) are natural Kustomer users.

## Who Should Pick What

**Choose Kustomer if:**
- Your support operation spans email, chat, voice, social, and SMS and you need unified history
- You need a full CRM timeline per customer, not just per ticket
- AI-powered intent detection and predictive routing are priorities
- Team size is 20+ agents with complex routing and SLA requirements
- You serve subscription or high-LTV customers where relationship context matters
- Shopify/Stripe integrations matter but you're not exclusively Shopify-native

**Choose Gorgias if:**
- You're a Shopify-first brand (or WooCommerce/Magento)
- Order data — status, history, refunds — needs to be in the ticket view for agents
- Your team is under 20 agents handling primarily commerce-related tickets
- You want affordable entry-level pricing that scales with ticket volume
- Fast onboarding without enterprise implementation complexity is important
- Revenue attribution from support interactions is a metric you track

## Bottom Line

Kustomer and Gorgias serve different support archetypes, and the overlap is smaller than the category overlap suggests. Gorgias has become the default choice for Shopify-native brands precisely because it integrates so deeply with the commerce stack. For a DTC brand on Shopify processing a few hundred to a few thousand tickets per month, Gorgias is likely the right call: fast to deploy, commerce-aware, and well-priced at the scale most independent brands operate.

Kustomer is a different product for a different problem. When your support team manages a customer relationship — not just resolves tickets — and when that relationship spans multiple channels, time, and purchase history that an agent needs in real time, Kustomer's CRM-first architecture justifies the premium. The acquisition history (Meta, then SupportLogic) gives some buyers pause, but the product as of 2026 is actively maintained and purpose-built for the omnichannel support use case at scale.
