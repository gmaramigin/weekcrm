## TL;DR

- **Pick eWay-CRM** if Outlook and Microsoft 365 are the center of your workday and you want CRM, communication history, and project tracking without asking anyone to learn a new app.
- **Pick HubSpot CRM** if you need marketing and sales on one platform — email automation, landing pages, forms, ticketing, and 1,500+ integrations — and you're prepared for the price curve past the free tier.

## Inbox tool vs growth platform

**eWay-CRM** is deliberately modest in ambition and that's its strength. It's a Microsoft Outlook plugin: contacts, deals, and projects appear beside your email, every message and call log files itself against the right record, and Microsoft Teams handles collaboration. It is the CRM you buy when the last CRM failed because nobody logged in.

**HubSpot CRM** is the opposite bet — a platform designed to become the operating system for go-to-market. Marketing Hub, Sales Hub, Service Hub, and CMS share one contact database, so a form fill can trigger a nurture sequence, create a deal, and open a ticket without integration work. That's genuinely hard to replicate by assembling point tools.

Neither is a compromised version of the other. eWay is not trying to do marketing; HubSpot is not trying to live in your inbox.

## The pricing models are structurally different

eWay charges **per team**. Free tier with unlimited users for basic contact management, paid from roughly **$20/mo**, Standard around **$27/mo**. Add a salesperson and your bill largely doesn't move. The tradeoff is a modular structure that's genuinely confusing to price out before you commit.

HubSpot charges **per seat, and then some**. Free forever with unlimited users and up to 1 million contacts. Starter at **$20/seat/mo**. Then a 5x cliff to Professional at **$100/seat/mo** plus a one-time **$1,500 onboarding fee**, and Enterprise at **$150/seat/mo** — with Marketing Hub layering contact-tier pricing of roughly **$150–$250/mo** per additional 5,000 contacts on top. The most common HubSpot complaint isn't the product; it's the bill arriving larger than expected mid-year.

For a ten-person team that only needs sales CRM, eWay is dramatically cheaper. For a team that would otherwise buy a CRM, a marketing automation tool, and a help desk separately, HubSpot's math looks better than it first appears.

## Ecosystem and lock-in

HubSpot's **1,500+ integration marketplace** and HubSpot Academy are real moats — most tools you'll ever want already connect, and hiring people who know HubSpot is easy. The flipside is that once marketing, sales, and service all run on one contact database, leaving means unpicking three systems at once.

eWay's ecosystem is **Microsoft's**, plus 3,000+ connections via Zapier and similar to QuickBooks, Mailchimp, and Adobe Sign. Lock-in is lower in a useful way: your email still lives in Outlook regardless of what happens to the CRM layer. But the dependency is absolute in the other direction — a team that migrates off Outlook gets nothing from eWay at all.

## Where each falls down

eWay's weaknesses are visible on the sales-management side: pipeline visualization and reporting lag modern standalone CRMs, and there's no native marketing automation to speak of. HubSpot's weaknesses are cost and bloat — Starter-to-Professional is a 5x jump, and plenty of sales teams pay for marketing, CMS, and ops modules they never open. Both criticisms are consequences of design, not bugs.

## Who should pick what

- **Outlook-first team that has abandoned a CRM before** → eWay-CRM.
- **Marketing-led business needing forms, email, and landing pages** → HubSpot CRM.
- **Consultancy wanting client projects alongside deals** → eWay-CRM.
- **Team that expects to scale into service and CMS hubs** → HubSpot CRM.
- **Cost-sensitive team where headcount grows faster than budget** → eWay-CRM.
- **Company that needs a large integration marketplace and easy hiring** → HubSpot CRM.

## Bottom line

eWay-CRM and HubSpot represent two coherent answers to "we need a CRM." eWay says the cheapest CRM is the one your reps are already inside — put it in Outlook, price it per team, add projects, and stop there. HubSpot says the CRM should be the platform everything else runs on, and charges accordingly once you pass the free tier. If your growth is marketing-driven and budget exists, HubSpot's consolidation is worth the price cliff. If it's sales- and relationship-driven inside Microsoft 365, eWay does the job for a fraction of the money.
