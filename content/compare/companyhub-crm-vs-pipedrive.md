## TL;DR

- **Pick CompanyHub CRM** if your process is genuinely non-standard and you want custom tables, objects, and relationships without hiring a developer or running a Salesforce project.
- **Pick Pipedrive** if you want the fastest path from spreadsheets to a working pipeline, with a UI reps adopt without training and a large integration marketplace behind it.

## Blank slate vs strong default

These two land in the same aisle — sales-only CRMs for SMBs, no marketing hub, no help desk — and then make opposite bets.

**Pipedrive** is built on a thesis: activity-based selling. Consistent, logged actions move deals forward, so the visual drag-and-drop pipeline is the entire centre of the UI and everything else — email, automation, the Sales Assistant's next-action suggestions — orbits around moving deals through stages. It is opinionated on purpose, and that opinion is why teams migrating off spreadsheets get productive so fast.

**CompanyHub** declines to have that opinion. Custom fields, custom tables, custom relationships, custom reports — configured without code, so an ops-minded salesperson can shape the CRM around a process rather than the reverse. Its target is the team that has outgrown a generic contact manager, has a process that doesn't look like a linear pipeline, and doesn't want to manage a Salesforce implementation to get flexibility.

The honest framing: Pipedrive is better if your process is normal, CompanyHub is better if it isn't. Be truthful about which you are, because most teams overestimate how unusual they are.

## Pricing: entry price vs effective price

**Pipedrive** starts lower — **$14/user/mo** Essential on annual billing — across five tiers: **$29** Advanced, **$59** Professional, **$69** Power, **$99** Enterprise. But the entry price is misleading in a specific way. Reporting and forecasting are thin below **Professional at $59**, workflow automation only appears at **Advanced**, add-ons (LeadBooster, Campaigns, Web Visitors) stack on top, and **monthly billing costs roughly 21% more** than annual. The realistic price for a team that wants automation and analytics is $29–$59, not $14.

**CompanyHub** runs **$21 to $64/user/mo** across three plans — a narrower ladder with less to decode. Both offer 14-day trials; neither has a free plan.

Compare like for like and they're close. Pipedrive is cheaper if Essential genuinely covers you; if you need automation and real reporting, the two converge around the $50–$64 range and the decision goes back to fit rather than price.

## Ecosystem is Pipedrive's decisive edge

Pipedrive has **300+ native integrations** plus Zapier and Make, a mature marketplace, and enough market presence that most SaaS tools ship a Pipedrive connector as standard. CompanyHub's integrations library is **noticeably smaller**, and its low brand recognition compounds the problem — fewer vendors build for it, fewer consultants know it, fewer candidates arrive already trained.

If your stack includes a specific dialer, proposal tool, or billing system, check for a native CompanyHub connector before you commit. This is the most common way a CompanyHub trial ends: the product works fine, but a critical tool needs custom API work nobody budgeted for.

Both share a weaker mobile story — each vendor's app is functional but trails its desktop experience — so neither is the pick for a field team living on a phone.

## Email and automation: closer than expected

This is where CompanyHub is more competitive than its profile suggests. It ships **two-way Gmail and Outlook sync** with tracking, templates, and scheduling, plus **bulk email and drip sequences from inside the CRM** — and that's available from the entry tier rather than gated high.

Pipedrive matches the two-way sync and adds AI-assisted email writing at **Professional and above**, with its Sales Assistant included on all paid plans to surface stalled deals and predict win likelihood. Higher tiers layer on lead scoring and call conversation intelligence.

The split: Pipedrive's AI layer is more developed, but CompanyHub gives you campaign-style email without climbing tiers or buying the Campaigns add-on. For a small team whose outbound is email-heavy, that's a real cost difference.

## Who should pick what

- **Non-standard process needing custom objects and relationships** → CompanyHub.
- **Team moving off spreadsheets that needs adoption on day one** → Pipedrive.
- **Depend on a wide stack of third-party sales tools** → Pipedrive.
- **Want bulk email and drip campaigns without add-ons** → CompanyHub.
- **Want AI next-action nudges and conversation intelligence** → Pipedrive.
- **No ops person available to design the CRM** → Pipedrive.

## Bottom line

Pipedrive is the safer default and the better bet for most SMB sales teams: a pipeline reps genuinely adopt, 300+ integrations, and a $14 entry price — provided you budget for Professional at **$59** once you want real reporting and automation, and watch the add-on creep. CompanyHub is the deliberate alternative for teams whose process doesn't fit a Kanban board, offering no-code custom tables and relationships plus campaign email from the entry tier, at the cost of a smaller ecosystem and a name nobody recognizes. Ask one question: is your sales process actually unusual? If yes, trial CompanyHub. If not, Pipedrive will get you further, faster.
