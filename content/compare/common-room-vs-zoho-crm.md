## TL;DR

- **Pick Zoho CRM** if you need the system of record — contacts, pipeline, automation, forecasting — at a price that doesn't require a budget conversation.
- **Pick Common Room** if you have a CRM already and a developer or community-led motion where buying signals appear in GitHub and Discord long before a demo request.

## One is the database, the other is the radar

The most useful framing here is that these tools sit at opposite ends of the funnel and don't substitute for each other. **Zoho CRM** is where deals live: leads, contacts, multi-pipeline deal tracking, workflow automation, Blueprint process enforcement, email campaigns, forecasting, and Zia's AI predictions — all inside the wider Zoho ecosystem of 50+ apps. It's the file cabinet, the workflow engine, and the reporting layer.

**Common Room** is the radar. It watches **50+ external sources** — GitHub commits, Slack and Discord community activity, LinkedIn engagement, product analytics, job change alerts — resolves the same human across all of them with AI identity matching, scores accounts for intent, and pushes enriched records into the CRM. It produces nothing you'd call a pipeline; it produces a ranked list of who to talk to and why now.

Confusing the two leads to a specific failure mode: a team buys Common Room, gets a beautiful stream of signals, and has nowhere disciplined to work them. The CRM comes first.

## Pricing models that aren't comparable

**Zoho CRM** is per-seat and unusually cheap for the depth: **free for up to 3 users**, then **$14/user/mo** Standard, **$23** Professional, **$40** Enterprise, and **$52** Ultimate on annual billing. Enterprise at $40 includes multi-pipeline management and advanced automation that competitors gate much higher. A ten-rep team on Enterprise runs **$400/mo**.

**Common Room** starts around **$1,000/mo** and scales with contact volume rather than headcount — which catches teams out when their community grows fast, since the bill follows the audience, not the org chart. That floor means it's simply not a candidate for early-stage companies, and the vendor doesn't pretend otherwise.

The blunt arithmetic: Common Room's entry price buys you roughly two years of Zoho CRM Enterprise for a small team.

## ICP fit is narrower than it looks for Common Room

Zoho CRM is close to ICP-agnostic. Cost-sensitive SMB and mid-market teams across services, international operations, and anyone already on Zoho Mail or Books — it fits nearly any business that sells something.

Common Room is deliberately narrow, and this is its strength rather than a defect. Its whole thesis is that for developer tools, open-source companies, and PLG SaaS, community engagement *precedes* purchase. If your buyers genuinely hang out on GitHub or Discord before they ever fill in a form, that's a real signal nobody else is capturing. If you sell through outbound to procurement teams at manufacturers, there's no community signal to mine and the platform's core advantage evaporates.

## Where the AI actually points

Zia looks **inward**. It scores the leads you already have, predicts which deals will close, flags anomalies in your numbers, and suggests when to call — all from data your team put into Zoho. It's competent and included in the price at Enterprise tier, though it isn't as polished as HubSpot's or Attio's equivalents.

Common Room's AI looks **outward**. Identity resolution across GitHub, LinkedIn, Slack, and your CRM is the hard technical problem it exists to solve, and intent scoring on top of that is what justifies the price. Neither AI replaces the other — one tells you what to do with known contacts, the other tells you which strangers to make into contacts.

## Who should pick what

- **Need a CRM at all** → Zoho CRM.
- **Dev-tools or open-source company with an active community** → Common Room.
- **Cost-sensitive SMB or mid-market team** → Zoho CRM.
- **Already on Salesforce or HubSpot and hunting earlier signal** → Common Room.
- **Traditional outbound B2B into non-technical buyers** → Zoho CRM.
- **Already using Zoho Books, Desk, or Campaigns** → Zoho CRM.

## Bottom line

This isn't really a versus. Zoho CRM is one of the best value systems of record available — enterprise-grade depth at $14 to $52 per seat, with a free tier for the first three users — and virtually every sales team needs something in that slot. Common Room is a specialist intelligence layer that earns its **$1,000/mo** floor only for companies whose buyers are visible in public communities before they're visible in a CRM. If you're choosing your first tool, buy Zoho. If you're a developer-led company already drowning in unactioned community signal and running Salesforce or HubSpot underneath, Common Room is the one worth the money.
