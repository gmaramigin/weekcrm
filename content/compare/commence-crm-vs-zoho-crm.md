## TL;DR

- **Pick Commence CRM** if you want sales, marketing automation, ticketing, and project management bundled from one small vendor and value direct support over ecosystem size.
- **Pick Zoho CRM** if you want more CRM capability per dollar, real AI features, and the option to expand into 50+ integrated apps as you grow.

## Small vendor bundle vs large ecosystem

**Commence CRM** is the product of a small, long-established vendor selling one integrated system to SMBs. The pitch is scope-in-a-box: pipeline, drip campaigns, help desk tickets, and project tracking in a single login, aimed at professional services, manufacturing, and distribution firms that track deals and delivery in the same breath. That bundling is genuine, and it comes with the practical benefit small vendors offer — you are not customer 400,000.

**Zoho CRM** comes from the opposite end of the market. It is one product inside a **50+ app ecosystem**, engineered to be deep on its own and deeper when paired with Zoho Desk, Books, Campaigns, or Sign. You get custom modules, multi-pipeline management, Blueprint process enforcement, and the Zia AI layer — capabilities Commence does not attempt.

The honest read: Zoho is the more capable CRM by a wide margin. Commence's counter is that matching its bundled scope with Zoho means running two or three Zoho products instead of one.

## Pricing

**Zoho CRM** starts at **$14/user/mo** (Standard) and tops out at **$52/user/mo** (Ultimate), with a free plan for up to 3 users. **Commence** starts at **$29/user/mo**, with marketing automation as a paid add-on at roughly **$12/user/mo** extra — so a marketing-enabled Commence seat lands near **$41**, roughly triple Zoho Standard and above Zoho Professional at $23.

Commence's price only makes sense when you count what you are not buying elsewhere: a separate ticketing tool and a separate project tool. Even then, Zoho One at **$37/user/mo** bundles 45+ apps — CRM, Desk, and Projects included — for less than a fully-loaded Commence seat. That comparison is difficult for Commence to win on numbers alone.

## Modernization and ecosystem risk

This is the dimension that should decide most evaluations. Zoho ships continuously, publishes an API surface everyone integrates against, and is not going anywhere. Commence's UI is dated by its own vendor's admission of the category's direction, and its integration catalog is narrow — if a tool in your stack does not have a Commence connector, you are building it or living without it.

That is not a reason to dismiss Commence, but it is a reason to check your specific integration list before signing. A CRM that cannot reach your accounting, e-sign, or support tooling costs more in manual work than the seat price saves.

## Who should pick what

- **SMB wanting CRM + help desk + projects from one vendor** → Commence CRM.
- **Cost-sensitive team that wants maximum CRM depth per dollar** → Zoho CRM.
- **Team that needs AI lead scoring and deal prediction** → Zoho CRM.
- **Business that values a small vendor's direct support** → Commence CRM.
- **Company already using Zoho Mail, Books, or Desk** → Zoho CRM.
- **Team with a long list of required SaaS integrations** → Zoho CRM.

## Bottom line

Zoho CRM is the better CRM and the better value for most buyers — cheaper per seat, deeper on automation, and safer on ecosystem and integration risk. Commence CRM's real case is narrow but legitimate: an SMB that genuinely wants sales, marketing, support, and delivery in one bundled system from one accountable vendor, and would rather pay a premium than administer three Zoho products. Price out Zoho One before you commit to Commence — for many teams it undercuts the bundle Commence is selling.
