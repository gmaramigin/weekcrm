## TL;DR
- **Pick Wealthbox** if you are a financial advisor or wealth-management firm that needs household records, custodian and planning-tool integrations, and compliance-ready activity archiving.
- **Pick Wise Agent** if you are a real-estate agent or team that needs transaction checklists, drip marketing, and lead follow-up tuned for buyers and sellers.

## Pricing
Wealthbox bills per user, starting around $59/user per month for its **Basic** plan, with **Pro** and **Premier** tiers adding advanced integrations, advisor reporting, and compliance archiving. Pricing is predictable and reasonable for the advisory niche, where the value is in the integrations rather than raw seat count.

Wise Agent uses a single flat plan near $49 per month (lower billed annually) with low-cost add-on seats for teammates. Everything, transactions, marketing, and calendar, comes in that one plan, which keeps budgeting simple for individual agents and small brokerages.

## Core approach
Wealthbox is a relationship and compliance hub for advisors: it organizes clients into households, logs every interaction for the record, and plugs into the financial-services tool stack. Wise Agent is an operational hub for real estate: it moves a contact from lead to closing with checklists, automated marketing, and follow-up sequences. Each is opinionated about its own industry's workflow.

## Lead & pipeline workflow
Wise Agent is structured around real-estate pipelines, capturing web and portal leads, assigning them, and nurturing with drip campaigns and call lists toward a transaction. Wealthbox models the advisory pipeline instead: prospects, opportunities, and ongoing client relationships, with workflows for onboarding and review cycles rather than fast lead conversion. Wealthbox emphasizes long-term client retention; Wise Agent emphasizes converting new business quickly.

## Integrations
Wealthbox's strength is its deep catalog of financial-services integrations, custodians, financial planning, portfolio management, and document tools, that let advisors run their day from one screen. Wise Agent integrates with real-estate lead sources, marketing tools, and Zapier, which covers the agent stack but not advisory software. The integration sets barely overlap because the audiences barely overlap.

## Who should pick what
- **Financial advisors and RIAs** needing compliance and custodian links: Wealthbox.
- **Wealth-management teams** managing households and review cycles: Wealthbox.
- **Real-estate agents** moving leads to closings: Wise Agent.
- **Small brokerages** wanting bundled marketing and transactions: Wise Agent.

## Bottom line
This comparison is decided by your profession. Choose **Wealthbox** if you advise on money and need household management, advisor integrations, and compliance archiving. Choose **Wise Agent** if you sell homes and need transaction tracking, real-estate marketing, and lead follow-up. Neither is a substitute for the other, so match the CRM to the industry it was built for.
