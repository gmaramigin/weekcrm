## TL;DR

- **Pick Breakcold** if your constraint is generating conversations, and you want a sharp tool for LinkedIn and cold-email outreach.
- **Pick Nutshell** if you want one B2B system to run pipeline, automation, and email marketing without stitching tools together.

## Top of funnel versus full cycle

The cleanest way to read Breakcold vs Nutshell is by where they sit in the funnel. Breakcold owns the very top. Its whole design is about turning strangers into replies: a Chrome extension for LinkedIn prospecting, a unified inbox across LinkedIn, email, and Twitter, a daily feed of lead activity to warm outreach, and open/reply tracking. It is deliberately lightweight and stops short of heavy pipeline machinery — that restraint is what keeps it fast for solo founders and small outbound teams.

Nutshell covers the full cycle. It is a B2B CRM that bundles contact management, pipeline tracking, sales automation, and — notably — email marketing into one subscription, with unlimited contacts and storage at every tier plus free live support. Where Breakcold hands you a way to start conversations, Nutshell gives you a place to nurture, automate, report on, and market to those contacts over months. It is the system of record; Breakcold is the outreach engine.

Many teams do not actually choose between them so much as sequence them: Breakcold to fill the top, a fuller CRM to manage what converts. But if you must pick one, ask whether your real bottleneck is getting conversations started or managing everything after.

## Pricing

Nutshell's entry point looks cheaper — Foundation around $13/user/mo — but it omits sales automation, so most teams realistically need Pro near $42/user/mo. Breakcold starts around $29/user/mo and stays lean. So the ranking flips depending on features: at the bare entry level Nutshell undercuts Breakcold, but once you want automation, Nutshell costs more. The honest read is that Nutshell wins on value-per-dollar for a full CRM (unlimited contacts, marketing, and support included), while Breakcold wins on price for a focused outbound job.

## Marketing built in — or not

A concrete dividing line is email marketing. Nutshell includes broadcast emails, drip sequences, and engagement tracking, so a small B2B team can run campaigns without a separate Mailchimp-style tool. Breakcold does not do one-to-many marketing; its email strength is personalized one-to-one outbound. If consolidating CRM and marketing into a single bill is appealing, that is a direct point for Nutshell. If you already have a marketing stack and only need a better way to prospect, Breakcold's focus is the feature, not a gap.

## Who should pick what

- **Founders and SDRs whose bottleneck is starting conversations** → Breakcold.
- **B2B teams wanting pipeline, automation, and marketing in one tool** → Nutshell.
- **LinkedIn and cold-email specialists** → Breakcold.
- **Teams that want CRM plus email marketing on one subscription** → Nutshell.
- **Solo sellers who want a lightweight, focused outbound tool** → Breakcold.
- **Growing 5-100 person B2B teams needing reporting and unlimited contacts** → Nutshell.
