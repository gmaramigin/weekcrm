## TL;DR

- **Pick Creatio** if you want to model and automate complex business processes with no-code tooling, and you'd rather own a flexible workflow platform than rent the market leader.
- **Pick Salesforce** if you need the deepest integration ecosystem, the largest talent pool, and a CRM that every vendor, consultant, and investor already supports.

## Pricing

Creatio starts at $25/user/mo for its base CRM and is generally more transparent and affordable than its enterprise rival, with composable products you bolt together. Salesforce Sales Cloud runs Starter $25, Pro $100, Enterprise $175, and Unlimited $350 per user/mo — and the headline number understates the true cost once you add Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, sandboxes, and the implementation partners most rollouts require.

## No-code vs platform

Creatio's defining feature is its no-code studio sitting on top of a real BPM (business process management) engine. Non-developers can design data models, build apps, and automate end-to-end processes by dragging through a visual editor — the workflow engine is the product, not an add-on. Salesforce is also extensible (Flow, Lightning App Builder, Apex), but serious customization typically pulls you toward Apex code and certified developers, which is power at the cost of complexity.

## Process automation

This is where Creatio is genuinely differentiated. Because every object and screen lives in the same process-driven platform, you can model approvals, case routing, and multi-step sales playbooks visually and change them on the fly. Salesforce's Flow is mature and capable, but Creatio's BPMN-native approach feels more natural for organizations whose competitive edge is operational process rather than raw pipeline volume.

## Ecosystem and integrations

Salesforce wins this category decisively. AppExchange hosts thousands of vetted apps, virtually every SaaS tool ships a Salesforce connector, and the certified-admin labor market dwarfs every competitor. Creatio has a respectable marketplace and open API, plus its own AI (Creatio AI / Copilot), but you'll occasionally hit tools that integrate with Salesforce first and Creatio later, if at all.

## Reporting and AI

Both ship capable analytics. Salesforce layers on Einstein AI and the broader Data Cloud / Agentforce stack for predictive scoring and autonomous agents — the most ambitious AI roadmap in CRM, though much of it sits in pricier tiers. Creatio embeds machine-learning scoring and generative assistance directly into its no-code flows, which is often enough for mid-market needs without the enterprise price tag.

## Bottom line

Salesforce is the safe, scalable default — unmatched ecosystem, talent, and longevity — but you pay for it in licensing and implementation. Creatio is the smarter buy for organizations whose advantage is complex, frequently-changing process and who want to own automation without a standing developer team. Choose Salesforce for ecosystem and scale; choose Creatio for no-code process agility at a lower total cost.
