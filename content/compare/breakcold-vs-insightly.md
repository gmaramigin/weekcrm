## TL;DR

- **Pick Breakcold** if your role is generating pipeline through LinkedIn and cold email, and delivery happens elsewhere.
- **Pick Insightly** if closing a deal immediately spins up a project you must manage — and you want sales and delivery in one CRM.

## Winning the deal versus delivering it

Breakcold and Insightly barely overlap once you look past the "CRM" label, and that is what makes the choice easy. Breakcold is about winning the deal. It is an outbound engine — LinkedIn prospecting via a Chrome extension, a unified inbox that folds in LinkedIn, email, and Twitter, a daily feed of lead activity to warm your outreach, and reply tracking so you know what is landing. Its horizon ends roughly at the closed-won moment; what happens after the sale is somebody else's tool.

Insightly picks up exactly where Breakcold trails off. Its defining feature is native project management: when a deal closes, it converts into a project with tasks, milestones, and deliverables tracked in the same system. It links contacts, organizations, opportunities, and projects together so a service firm can see the full arc of an account. It syncs deeply with Gmail and Outlook and leans toward consulting, professional services, and construction — businesses where the sale is the beginning, not the end, of the work.

So the question is not which is the better CRM in the abstract, but which half of the deal-to-delivery arc is your actual problem. If you cannot get enough conversations started, Breakcold. If your deals are fine but delivery is chaos, Insightly.

## Pricing

The models differ in shape. Insightly offers a genuine free plan for up to two users, then paid tiers at $29 (Plus), $49 (Professional), and $99 (Enterprise) per user — all billed annually, which removes short-term flexibility, and with meaningful automation gated to Professional and up. Breakcold starts around $29/user/mo with more flexible billing and a narrower scope. For a two-person shop testing the waters, Insightly's free tier is unbeatable on price. For a solo seller who wants outbound tooling without an annual lock-in, Breakcold is the lighter commitment.

## The workflow after "yes"

The clearest reason to choose Insightly over Breakcold has nothing to do with sales at all — it is what happens after the customer says yes. Insightly keeps the momentum inside one system: the opportunity becomes a project, tasks get assigned, milestones get tracked, and the client record stays whole. Breakcold has no equivalent; you would export the win to a separate PM tool. Conversely, Insightly's outbound and social-selling capabilities are thin next to Breakcold's, and its UI can feel dated. Choose based on where your operational pain actually lives.

## Who should pick what

- **Founders and SDRs focused on LinkedIn and cold-email prospecting** → Breakcold.
- **Consulting and professional-services firms that deliver projects** → Insightly.
- **Two-person teams wanting a free starting tier** → Insightly.
- **Solo outbound sellers avoiding annual contracts** → Breakcold.
- **Businesses where a closed deal becomes a delivery project** → Insightly.
- **Anyone whose bottleneck is starting conversations, not delivery** → Breakcold.
