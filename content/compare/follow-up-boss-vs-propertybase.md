## TL;DR

- **Pick Follow Up Boss** if your team's primary pain is converting the leads you already pay for — you want fast routing, enforcement, and a CRM that plugs cleanly into Zillow, Realtor.com, and your existing stack without paying for features you'll never use.
- **Pick Propertybase** if you need a Salesforce-grade platform that ties CRM, IDX websites, transaction coordination, and back-office compliance into one system — and you have the budget and IT resources to stand it up.

## Pricing

Follow Up Boss is one of the few real estate CRMs that publishes its prices. The Grow plan runs roughly $69 per user/month (around $58 billed annually); Pro is approximately $499/month for up to 10 users; Platform is around $833/month (annual) for up to 30 seats. Power dialer and calling features cost extra, typically $25–$50 per user/month. Propertybase does not publish a price list — you book a demo for a custom quote. Industry reports put entry-level team packages in the $1,000–$2,000+/month range, with enterprise brokerage deployments going well beyond that. They also offer a lighter product called Propertybase GO (formerly Lone Wolf Leads+) aimed at solo agents and small teams, which carries its own separate pricing. The gap matters: FUB is transparent and scalable per seat; Propertybase is a significant annual commitment suited to operations that genuinely need its breadth.

## Lead management and routing

Follow Up Boss was designed around one question: what happens to a lead in the first five minutes? Its routing engine ingests sources from 200+ portals, assigns by round-robin, first-to-claim, or direct rules, and gives team leads accountability dashboards showing exactly who called, when, and what happened. Overdue follow-ups surface automatically. Propertybase has lead routing as well, but it's part of a much larger Salesforce org — you get Salesforce's native assignment rules and workflow automation, which are powerful but require more configuration than FUB's out-of-box setup. Teams whose identity is speed-to-lead and agent performance management find FUB the sharper tool.

## IDX websites and lead capture

Propertybase includes a full IDX website builder — search portals, property detail pages, neighborhood pages, and behavioral tracking that scores leads by what they browse. This is native to the platform and a significant value driver for teams that don't want to manage a separate website vendor. Follow Up Boss has no website builder and no native IDX product. It expects you to bring a website (from Sierra Interactive, Real Geeks, Placester, or others) and pipe the leads in. Neither approach is objectively better — FUB's model lets you pick the best IDX site on the market; Propertybase's model means one fewer vendor and tighter data continuity.

## Transaction management and back office

This is where Propertybase extends well past Follow Up Boss's scope. Propertybase includes transaction coordination features — checklists, document management, milestone tracking, and in some tiers compliance and commission tracking that connects to your back office. For brokerages that also want to reduce their dependence on separate TC software, that's real consolidation. Follow Up Boss stops at the contract — once a lead converts to a client under contract, most teams export to a dedicated transaction tool like Dotloop, Skyslope, or Brokermint. If your brokerage runs a high transaction volume and needs to standardize the full deal lifecycle in one place, Propertybase's depth matters.

## Salesforce ecosystem

Propertybase's architecture is native Salesforce (Classic or Lightning, depending on the package). That means access to the full Salesforce AppExchange, custom objects, enterprise-grade reporting, and the familiarity your ops or IT team may already have with the platform. It also means Salesforce's learning curve, user license overhead, and the admin burden that comes with a highly configurable system. Follow Up Boss is a purpose-built SaaS product — easier to onboard, opinionated in good ways, and deliberately narrow. Teams with a Salesforce-literate ops team will get more from Propertybase; everyone else will likely get to value faster with FUB.

## FAQ

**Does Follow Up Boss have built-in transaction management?**
No. FUB ends at the deal stage. Most teams pair it with Dotloop, Skyslope, or a dedicated TC tool for the contract-to-close side.

**Can Propertybase replace my existing IDX website?**
Yes — Propertybase includes an IDX site builder with property search, lead capture, and behavioral tracking. Whether the site quality matches a standalone provider like Sierra Interactive depends on your design needs.

**Is Follow Up Boss hard to set up?**
Relative to most CRMs, no. Most teams are routing leads within a day or two. Propertybase on Salesforce typically requires a formal onboarding engagement, often 4–8 weeks.

**Does Propertybase work for solo agents?**
Their GO product targets solo agents and small teams. The full Propertybase Salesforce platform is generally sized for teams of 10+ agents or full brokerages.

**Which has better reporting for team leaders?**
Both have reporting, but FUB's accountability dashboards (call logs, follow-up rates, lead aging) are purpose-built for real estate team leads and require no configuration. Propertybase/Salesforce reporting is more powerful but requires building custom reports.

## Verdict

Follow Up Boss and Propertybase serve overlapping but meaningfully different buyers. FUB is the right call for teams and agents who generate leads externally and need a tight, accountable system to convert them — fast setup, transparent pricing, and a best-of-breed integration philosophy. Propertybase is the right call for brokerages that want to consolidate CRM, IDX, transaction management, and back-office into a single Salesforce-based platform and have the budget and technical capacity to run it. Buy FUB for conversion; buy Propertybase for consolidation.
