## TL;DR

- **Pick Apptivo** if you want to replace several separate SaaS tools with one low-cost suite spanning CRM, projects, invoicing, procurement, and field service.
- **Pick BlueCamroo** if you run agency- or consultancy-style client work and want CRM, projects, support, and billing tightly connected with role-based pricing.

## Two takes on the all-in-one suite

Both tools reject the single-purpose CRM in favor of doing many jobs — but they design that breadth differently. **Apptivo is a catalog.** Its 65+ modular apps cover CRM, project management, invoicing, procurement, and field service; you switch on what you need and ignore the rest. The pitch is one vendor, one login, one bill for a growing small business that's tired of stitching tools together. Breadth is the headline feature.

**BlueCamroo is a workflow.** It covers fewer domains — CRM, project and task management, support ticketing, time tracking, and invoicing — but wires them around a single client record so a lead flows into a project, into support, into an invoice without leaving the platform. It's built specifically for service businesses where client work spans sales, delivery, and support.

So the real question isn't "which does more" — it's whether you want *the most modules* (Apptivo) or *the most connected client lifecycle* (BlueCamroo).

## Pricing

Apptivo has a **functional free plan** and paid tiers from **$10/user/mo** — the lower entry point of the two. The caveat: many of the more useful features, including deeper capabilities, are gated to higher tiers, so a fully equipped setup costs more than the $10 sticker implies.

BlueCamroo offers **free CRM for up to 2 users**, then charges by role from around **$19/user/mo**, with each user paying only for the extensions their job requires. That role-based model can be more efficient for mixed teams — you're not paying a full seat for someone who only needs support tools — but it starts higher than Apptivo per role. Apptivo wins the entry price; BlueCamroo can win on value if your team's roles vary widely.

## Breadth vs integration depth

Apptivo's advantage is surface area: procurement, field service, supply chain, and more sit alongside CRM, which BlueCamroo doesn't attempt. If your consolidation need spans operations beyond client work, Apptivo covers more ground. The tradeoff is depth — any single Apptivo module is less powerful than a dedicated specialist tool, and navigation can take more clicks than it should.

BlueCamroo trades breadth for cohesion. Fewer modules, but they share one client record and a configurable workflow engine that automates processes across departments. For a service firm, that connectedness is worth more than a longer app list.

## Setup and usability

Neither is instant. Apptivo's breadth means clicking through a lot of screens, and its mobile app lags the desktop version. BlueCamroo has a steeper learning curve because the role and module system takes real configuration time, and it's less well-known, so there are fewer community resources. Budget setup time for either — more for BlueCamroo's role model, more for Apptivo's sheer number of modules.

## Who should pick what

- **You want the widest module catalog at the lowest entry price** → Apptivo.
- **You run agency or consultancy client work** → BlueCamroo.
- **You need procurement, field service, or supply chain too** → Apptivo.
- **You want leads, projects, tickets, and invoices in one record** → BlueCamroo.
- **Mixed team where roles need different tools** → BlueCamroo.
- **Small business consolidating many separate SaaS tools** → Apptivo.

## Bottom line

Apptivo and BlueCamroo both save you from running three subscriptions, but they optimize differently. Apptivo gives you the broadest app catalog at the lowest starting price — best when you want to consolidate operations across many functions and can accept shallower individual modules. BlueCamroo gives you a tightly connected client lifecycle with thoughtful role-based pricing — best for service businesses willing to invest setup time for real integration. Choose breadth or cohesion based on how your work actually flows.
