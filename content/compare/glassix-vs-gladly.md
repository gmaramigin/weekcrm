## TL;DR

- **Pick Glassix** if you want AI agents resolving conversations end to end without an enterprise contract — published pricing from **$49/user/mo** and a 30-day trial to prove it works on your volume.
- **Pick Gladly** if you're a high-volume B2C brand where a lifetime, cross-channel customer history is genuinely worth **$180–$210/user/mo** plus separately-billed voice and SMS.

## Same thesis, different tier of the market

These two agree on the argument: AI should complete customer interactions, not route them to a queue. Glassix builds around autonomous AI agents that resolve, escalate, and follow up across messaging and voice, with **CrystalVoice** handling calls and multilingual support running without per-language configuration. Gladly builds around AI that engages rather than deflects, designed to finish interactions rather than hand off.

Where they part is who they're selling to. **Glassix** targets mid-market through enterprise across e-commerce, hospitality, automotive, higher education, real estate, insurance, and SaaS. **Gladly** targets mid-to-large B2C brands in retail, e-commerce, travel, and hospitality — the Nordstrom and Ulta Beauty end of the market, where support is a brand differentiator and phone volume is enormous.

## Ticket-centric vs person-centric

The most substantive product difference is the data model. Gladly organizes everything around a **lifetime customer timeline**: voice, chat, SMS, email, and social for a given person sit in one continuous thread, so an agent picking up a call sees years of context rather than a fresh ticket. For a retailer where the same customer contacts support a dozen times across seasons, that architecture is the whole value proposition and it isn't easy to bolt on.

Glassix's contextual intelligence is aimed at a different target — handling complex, multi-step conversations within an interaction, rather than reconstructing a relationship across years. If your customers are largely one-and-done, Gladly's timeline is expensive infrastructure you won't monetize. If they're repeat buyers, it's the reason to pay more.

## The price gap is the headline

**Glassix** publishes: **$49/user/mo** (Starter), **$65** (Growth), custom Enterprise, with a **30-day free trial**. Its caveat is that advanced AI is an add-on on Growth rather than included.

**Gladly** doesn't publish. Estimates run **$180–$210/user/mo**, with a significant minimum annual commitment, and voice and SMS usage billed on top of seats. That's roughly 3x Glassix at comparable tiers before usage.

The gap is defensible only at scale. Gladly's customer base reports over **$510M in aggregate savings** and 65% CSAT improvements, which is real, but those outcomes come from operations where a few points of AI resolution translate to many headcount. A team with modest volume will never recover the seat premium.

## Maturity vs accessibility

Gladly is the more proven platform for its niche, with named enterprise logos and published resolution figures, plus plain-English **Guides** that let non-technical teams shape AI behavior without code. That lowers the practical implementation barrier despite the higher price.

Glassix is newer, with a smaller integration ecosystem than established vendors, and its AI-first approach demands strong configuration and content to produce good results. But it lets you find that out for **$49/user/mo** on a 30-day trial rather than through a procurement cycle and an annual minimum. For most buyers below true enterprise scale, that optionality is worth more than the reference logos.

## Who should pick what

- **Mid-market team wanting AI resolution on a real budget** → Glassix.
- **National retail or travel brand with heavy repeat contact** → Gladly.
- **You need to trial AI quality before committing** → Glassix.
- **Voice is your dominant channel and must be first-class** → Gladly.
- **Multilingual support without per-language setup** → Glassix.
- **Non-technical team that must configure AI behavior itself** → Gladly.

## Bottom line

Glassix and Gladly want the same thing — AI that actually finishes conversations — and disagree mainly on what that should cost and who deserves it. Gladly is the enterprise B2C standard, and its lifetime customer timeline is a genuine architectural advantage if your customers come back often and call rather than email; at roughly **$180–$210/user/mo** plus usage, it needs that volume to pay. Glassix delivers a comparable AI-autonomous ambition from **$49/user/mo** with a trial long enough to test it honestly. Unless you're operating at Nordstrom scale, start with Glassix.
