## TL;DR

- **Pick AntMyERP** if you sell, install, and maintain equipment and need dispatch, asset history, inventory, and AMC renewals in the same system as your pipeline.
- **Pick HubSpot CRM** if your revenue comes from selling rather than servicing, and you want marketing automation, a free tier, and 1,500+ integrations.

## Operations software vs revenue software

These two get compared because both call themselves CRM, but they optimize for opposite halves of the customer lifecycle.

**AntMyERP** is built for what happens *after* the sale. Its native objects are technicians, tickets, installed assets, spare parts, SLAs, and maintenance contracts. It exists for IT AMC providers, CCTV installers, elevator maintenance firms, and copier dealers — businesses where the deal is small and the ten-year service relationship is where the margin lives. The pipeline module is there because those companies need one, not because it is the product.

**HubSpot** is built for what happens *before* the sale. Contacts, deals, email sequences, landing pages, lifecycle stages, attribution reporting. Its Service Hub adds ticketing, but ticketing is a help desk, not field service — there is no scheduling board, no parts inventory, no asset-level service history.

The practical test: if a customer calls and your first question is "which unit, installed when, and by whom?", you need AntMyERP's data model. If your first question is "which campaign brought them in?", you need HubSpot's.

## Pricing philosophy

**AntMyERP** charges a flat **$50/user/mo with all modules included** — CRM, dispatch, inventory, HR, billing. There is no tier ladder and no module-by-module upsell, which is unusual in ERP and genuinely simplifies budgeting. The catch is that $50 is steep if you only wanted a contact database; the price assumes you will use the operational modules.

**HubSpot** starts at **free** for unlimited users, moves to **$20/seat/mo** Starter, then jumps to **$100/seat/mo** Professional with a one-time **$1,500 onboarding fee** — a 5x cliff that catches teams off guard. Marketing Hub adds contact-tier pricing on top, roughly $150–$250/mo per additional 5,000 contacts.

For a 20-person field service company, AntMyERP is a predictable **$1,000/mo** covering the whole operation. HubSpot Professional for the same headcount is **$2,000/mo** plus onboarding — and you still have no dispatch system.

## Ecosystem and risk

This is where HubSpot pulls decisively ahead. **1,500+ integrations**, HubSpot Academy, a huge partner and consultant network, and enough market share that hiring someone who already knows it is easy. If you pick HubSpot and it turns out wrong, migrating away is well-trodden ground.

**AntMyERP** is a narrower bet. QuickBooks integration is limited enough that accounting sync is a known friction point, the iOS app trails the browser version, and the vendor's strongest presence is in Indian and MENA markets — so references, implementation partners, and support hours are thinner elsewhere. That is the normal tradeoff for vertical software: it fits your workflow far better and leaves you more dependent on one vendor.

## Who should pick what

- **IT AMC, CCTV, elevator, or copier service business** → AntMyERP.
- **Marketing-led SMB running campaigns and lifecycle email** → HubSpot CRM.
- **You need technician scheduling and spare-parts tracking** → AntMyERP.
- **You want a capable CRM at zero cost today** → HubSpot CRM.
- **Predictable flat per-user cost covering the whole operation** → AntMyERP.
- **You need deep integrations, a partner network, and easy hiring** → HubSpot CRM.

## Bottom line

AntMyERP and HubSpot barely overlap once you look past the CRM label. AntMyERP is a field service operating system with a pipeline attached — if your business revolves around technicians, installed equipment, and maintenance contracts, its all-inclusive $50/user/mo replaces several tools and the vertical fit is worth the narrower ecosystem. HubSpot is a revenue platform with a help desk attached — unmatched for marketing-led selling, and structurally unable to run a service fleet. Pick by which half of the customer lifecycle actually generates your money.
