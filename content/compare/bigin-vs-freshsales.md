## TL;DR

- **Pick Bigin** if you're a solo operator or micro-team (under 20 people) that wants the simplest possible pipeline CRM, set up in minutes, at the lowest credible price.
- **Pick Freshsales** if you're a growing sales team that needs real AI-assisted selling, deeper automation, and reporting that won't hit a ceiling as you scale.

## Pricing

Both are aggressively priced. Bigin offers a free plan and paid tiers from $7/user/month (billed annually) — among the lowest prices for a legitimate CRM, with built-in telephony and email even on the free tier for one user. Freshsales also has a free plan, with paid tiers from $9/user/month and a 21-day trial. They're within a couple of dollars at entry, so price alone shouldn't decide it. The real cost difference shows up later: Bigin's value is in staying cheap and simple; Freshsales' is in giving you more features to grow into without re-platforming.

## Simplicity vs capability

This is the core trade-off. Bigin is deliberately stripped down — a spreadsheet-like pipeline view most users master without training, built specifically for people who find full Zoho CRM or Salesforce overwhelming. Freshsales is a fuller CRM with a 360-degree customer view, kanban pipelines with stage automation, and Freddy AI for lead scoring, deal health, and email generation. Bigin gets you organized in under 30 minutes; Freshsales does more but asks for more setup. If "simple and done today" is the goal, Bigin; if "capable and AI-assisted" is the goal, Freshsales.

## AI and automation

Freshsales wins decisively here. Freddy AI handles lead scoring, deal recommendations, email composition, and pipeline forecasting as a built-in layer. Bigin offers only basic automation by design — it's explicit that if you need workflow automation beyond the basics, custom modules, or advanced reporting, you'll outgrow it quickly. For teams that want AI to actively help them sell and prioritize, Freshsales is built for it; Bigin intentionally is not.

## The growth path

Both have escape hatches, but different ones. Bigin's is upward inside Zoho: one-click migration to full Zoho CRM when you outgrow its limits, removing the risk of choosing a dead end. Freshsales' is sideways into Freshworks: native integration with Freshdesk and Freshservice for a unified customer platform. The question is which ecosystem you'd rather grow into — Zoho's broad app suite or Freshworks' support-and-IT stack. Note also that Bigin's free plan has a single pipeline; multiple pipelines require a paid tier.

## Who should pick what

- **Solo operators and micro-teams under 20 people** → Bigin. Simplest setup, lowest price.
- **Growing sales teams that need AI-assisted selling** → Freshsales. Freddy AI is built in.
- **Local service businesses and consultants wanting basics done fast** → Bigin. Organized in 30 minutes.
- **Teams already using Freshdesk or Freshservice** → Freshsales. Unified Freshworks platform.
- **Companies expecting to scale into a full Zoho suite** → Bigin. One-click upgrade path to Zoho CRM.

## Bottom line

Both rate well (Bigin 4.3, Freshsales 4.2) and both are bargains, so this isn't about price or quality — it's about ambition. Bigin is the best pure-value pick for small teams that want simplicity and a safe upgrade path; it's honest that it has a feature ceiling. Freshsales is the better choice the moment you need real AI, automation, and reporting that scales with you. Choose Bigin if you want to start simple and migrate later, Freshsales if you'd rather buy the headroom now.
