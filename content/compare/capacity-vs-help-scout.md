## TL;DR

- **Pick Capacity** if you run a high-volume contact center in an industry with repetitive inquiry patterns and can staff a proper AI implementation.
- **Pick Help Scout** if you want support that feels human, AI that handles the routine 70%, and a free plan you can start on this afternoon.

## Different companies buy these

This isn't a close feature fight — it's two products aimed at opposite ends of the market, and the useful comparison is about which end you're on.

**Capacity** sells to mid-market and enterprise organizations in financial services, insurance, healthcare, retail, and education. Over 20,000 organizations use it, and its ROI story is built entirely on volume: **84%+ of inquiries resolved automatically**, **36+ billion automated interactions** processed. The platform is explicit that smaller teams may find it oversized.

**Help Scout** sells to SMBs — SaaS, ecommerce, healthcare, education — that specifically dislike the complexity and cost of enterprise platforms. Its free plan supports **up to 5 users**. Its entire design premise is that support should feel like a personal email, not a routed ticket.

If you can identify which of those descriptions is your company, the decision is largely made.

## Published pricing vs the sales call

Help Scout tells you what it costs: free to 5 users, **$25/user/mo** for the entry paid tier, **$45/user/mo** for Plus where advanced workflow automation and the deeper integration catalog live. You can budget it in a spreadsheet in five minutes.

Capacity publishes nothing. Enterprise contracts are negotiated and sized by usage and seats, meaning budget discovery requires a sales cycle before you know whether it's viable.

Neither approach is wrong — usage-priced AI deflection genuinely resists a public price list. But it's a real friction difference. A 12-person support team can trial Help Scout for free this week. Getting to a Capacity number takes longer than that.

Worth noting on the Help Scout side: per-seat pricing compounds. At $45/user/mo on Plus, a 30-person team is spending over $16,000 a year, which is the scale at which enterprise alternatives stop looking absurd.

## Channel reach: the one hard capability gap

Capacity runs a single AI layer across **voice, chat, email, and SMS** — one system, not a separate bot per channel. Combined with **250+ prebuilt connectors** that let the AI pull live data rather than recite scripted answers, it can resolve inquiries that require looking something up in a core business system.

Help Scout covers shared email inboxes, the **Beacon** widget, in-app messaging, and a knowledge base. No voice. No SMS.

That's not an oversight — Help Scout is deliberately a customer-facing web and email support tool, and it's also explicitly not built for ITSM or internal service desks. But if you need phone deflection or your customers text you, this is a hard stop rather than a tradeoff.

## Two philosophies of AI in support

Help Scout's AI is designed to **get out of the way**. It resolves the routine 70%, and when a human takes over the handoff is seamless and the customer never feels processed. Beacon proactively surfaces knowledge base articles so tickets aren't created at all. The goal is preserving warmth while removing drudgery.

Capacity's AI is designed to **be the front line**. Virtual agents handle the inquiry end to end, and real-time agent assist backs up the humans who do get involved. The goal is handling cost reduction at scale.

Both work. But Help Scout's approach only scales as far as its channel coverage, and Capacity's only pays for itself above a volume threshold most SMBs never cross.

## Who should pick what

- **High-volume contact center in insurance, banking, or healthcare** → Capacity.
- **SMB support team that wants to start free today** → Help Scout.
- **Need AI deflection on phone calls and SMS** → Capacity.
- **Support brand built on sounding human, not automated** → Help Scout.
- **Have implementation resources and a knowledge base to train on** → Capacity.
- **Under 25 agents, mostly email and chat volume** → Help Scout.

## Bottom line

Capacity and Help Scout barely overlap in practice, and pretending otherwise wastes an evaluation cycle. Capacity is a credible enterprise bet when repetitive inquiries are burying a large support org and you have the bandwidth to implement properly — the deflection rates and live-data integration depth are real advantages, but they're advantages that only cash out at volume. Help Scout is one of the best-liked support tools available for exactly the teams Capacity tells to look elsewhere: fast to set up, honest about its pricing, and genuinely good at keeping support personal while AI absorbs the routine. Start with your ticket volume and your appetite for a procurement process. Those two facts will answer this for you.
