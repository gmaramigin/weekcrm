## TL;DR

- **Pick Pipeliner** if your sales process is genuinely complex — multi-line products, account hierarchies, channel partners, opportunity rooms — and you want a visual CRM that handles that complexity without resorting to Salesforce.
- **Pick Pipedrive** if you sell direct, your pipeline fits on one screen, and you want the team productive in a week instead of a quarter.

## Pricing

Pipeliner: Starter $65/user/mo, Business $85, Enterprise $115, Unlimited $150 — billed annually. Pipedrive: Essential $14/user/mo, Advanced $29, Professional $59, Power $79, Enterprise $99. Pipeliner is roughly 2–4x the price at equivalent tiers. The premium is real but it's buying a different category of product.

## Visualization

Pipeliner's signature feature is its "navigator" — a multi-dimensional pipeline view that lets you slice deals by stage, owner, account, product line, or any custom dimension, with drag-and-drop across all of them. Pipedrive's pipeline is excellent but it's still a Kanban — flat, single-dimension. For sales teams running multiple parallel motions, Pipeliner's view is the killer feature.

## Account management

Pipeliner ships true multi-level account hierarchies (parent–child–grandchild) and org charts as a first-class entity. Pipedrive treats accounts as a flat list of organizations. Enterprise sales teams with complex buying committees and account trees will hit Pipedrive's ceiling fast; Pipeliner is built for that motion.

## Customization

Both are highly customizable. Pipeliner offers more enterprise-grade configuration (custom entities, multiple currencies, complex permission models). Pipedrive customizes fast at the SMB level but slows down past mid-market — fields and workflows yes, custom objects no.

## AI

Both ship AI features in 2026. Pipedrive's "AI Sales Assistant" focuses on deal-coaching suggestions and email drafting. Pipeliner's Voyager AI does forecast assistance and account intelligence. Neither is best-in-class; both are useful, table-stakes additions.

## Mobile

Pipedrive's mobile app is one of the best in the CRM market — field reps actually use it. Pipeliner's mobile is functional but more limited; reps will hit the web app more.

## Who should pick what

- **Mid-market sales orgs with complex deal structures, channel partners, or account hierarchies** → Pipeliner.
- **SMB sales teams that want pipeline visibility and nothing else** → Pipedrive.
- **Field-sales teams whose reps live on mobile** → Pipedrive.
- **Sales orgs running multiple parallel pipelines that need multi-dimensional reporting** → Pipeliner.
