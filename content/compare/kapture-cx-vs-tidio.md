## TL;DR

- **Pick Kapture CX** if you're running a high-volume support organization that needs omnichannel coverage including voice, SLA enforcement, and connectors into SAP, Oracle, or Salesforce.
- **Pick Tidio** if you're an ecommerce store or small SaaS team that wants AI to handle the majority of chat volume without hiring more agents.

## Two different buyers entirely

This is not a close comparison — it's a comparison of tools bought by different people for different reasons.

**Kapture CX** is sold to support leaders at mid-to-large enterprises in retail, e-commerce, and BFSI, where ticket volume is measured in thousands per day and consistency across channels is an operational requirement. Its design assumptions show it: a unified agent workspace, automation-first routing, SLA rules, and enterprise connectors that pull order and account data in so agents aren't tab-switching. That architecture only pays off if you have enough volume and enough agents to feel the savings.

**Tidio** is installed by a founder or a marketing lead. It starts as a chat widget on your storefront and grows into a help desk. Its flagship feature, the **Lyro AI agent**, handles up to **67% of inquiries** without a human — which is the whole pitch. It's about avoiding a second support hire, not optimizing a floor of fifty agents.

The mistake to avoid is buying Kapture for a five-person team (you'll pay for infrastructure you never use) or Tidio for a large operation (you'll hit its B2C-shaped ceiling).

## Channel coverage is the hard dividing line

Kapture CX unifies **email, chat, phone, and social** in one workspace. Tidio unifies **email, live chat, Messenger, Instagram, and WhatsApp** — a strong messaging spread, but no voice.

If phone support is part of your service model, this comparison ends here: Tidio doesn't do it. If your customers only ever reach you through the website and social DMs, Kapture's voice capability is dead weight you're funding.

## Pricing shapes and traps

**Tidio** starts free and paid from **$24.17/mo** — priced per account, not per seat, which is why it's so cheap at small scale. Two traps: Lyro AI and advanced Flows are **add-ons**, so the AI you're buying it for isn't fully in the base price; and the Plus tier starts at **$749+/mo**, a steep cliff once you need real team features.

**Kapture CX** starts at **$39/user/mo** with enterprise configurations custom-quoted. Per-seat pricing means costs scale linearly with headcount, and the custom-quote model means you can't size the deal without a sales conversation. That's normal for enterprise support software, but it makes Kapture impossible to trial-and-decide the way you can with Tidio.

## Ecommerce depth vs enterprise integration depth

Both integrate with Shopify, but they mean different things by it. **Tidio's** ecommerce integrations are deep and consumer-facing — order lookups, cart context, product answers surfaced inside the chat so Lyro can resolve without escalation. **Kapture's** integrations are back-office: SAP, Oracle, Twilio, FreshBooks, Salesforce, reducing manual data entry for agents working inside enterprise systems of record.

One more practical note: Kapture is **less established on English-language review platforms** than Zendesk or Freshdesk, so peer validation is harder to come by. Tidio's 300,000+ user base makes it easy to find someone running your exact stack.

## Who should pick what

- **Shopify or WooCommerce store with a small support team** → Tidio.
- **Enterprise support floor needing SLA enforcement and routing** → Kapture CX.
- **Phone support is part of your service model** → Kapture CX.
- **You want AI to deflect the majority of chats before a human sees them** → Tidio.
- **You need SAP, Oracle, or Salesforce data inside the agent workspace** → Kapture CX.
- **You want to be live this afternoon without a sales call** → Tidio.

## Bottom line

Kapture CX and Tidio are both good at what they do, but they're not competing for the same budget. Kapture is enterprise support infrastructure — worth evaluating if you run high volume across channels including voice and need automation and enterprise integrations as first-class features, with the caveat that you should verify its support footprint in your region. Tidio is the most accessible AI support tool in the category, and for an ecommerce team trying to hold headcount flat while volume grows, Lyro is a genuinely practical answer. Pick by scale: a support organization, or a support function.
