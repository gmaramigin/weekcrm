## TL;DR

- **Pick Kylas Sales CRM** if you're scaling a sales team and want unlimited users on a flat subscription instead of paying per seat.
- **Pick HubSpot CRM** if you need marketing automation, a large app marketplace, and a platform with room to grow into enterprise use.

## The pricing model is the actual decision

Most CRM comparisons come down to features. This one comes down to how you're billed, because the two models produce wildly different bills at scale.

**Kylas** charges a **flat monthly subscription for unlimited users**. This is unusual, and it's the product's central claim. A 15-rep team and a 60-rep team pay the same. The strategic effect is that you stop rationing seats — sales assistants, field reps, and junior BDRs all go on the CRM, which means your data is more complete because nobody is working outside the system.

**HubSpot** charges **per seat**, from **$20/seat/mo** on Starter to **$100/seat/mo** on Professional (with a one-time **$1,500 onboarding fee**), plus contact-tier pricing on Marketing Hub. At 40 reps on Professional you are into serious money before you've added a single marketing contact.

The honest counterweight: HubSpot's **free CRM supports unlimited users** too, with up to a million contacts. So at the very bottom of the market HubSpot is free and Kylas is not. The flat-rate advantage shows up in the middle — when you're too big for HubSpot Free but not ready to pay per seat for Professional.

## What you give up going flat-rate

Kylas is a **sales CRM**, full stop: pipeline, lead capture and assignment, activity tracking, mobile apps. It's designed for teams replacing spreadsheets who don't have a dedicated CRM administrator.

HubSpot is a **platform**. Marketing automation, service hub, CMS, custom objects, a **1,500+ app marketplace**, and HubSpot Academy for onboarding. If your go-to-market is marketing-led — inbound forms, nurture emails, lifecycle stages — Kylas doesn't have an equivalent, and you'd be pairing it with a separate marketing tool.

Kylas also has a **narrower integration ecosystem** than HubSpot. This is the practical thing to check before signing: list the five tools your sales process actually touches and verify each one.

## Market fit and support reality

Kylas is built for **India and emerging markets** and targets SMBs scaling their sales teams there. That's not a limitation so much as a focus — the pricing model, the onboarding assumptions, and the support footprint are all tuned to that buyer. A US or EU team evaluating Kylas should validate local support coverage and time zones the same way an Indian team would validate a Western vendor.

HubSpot is globally distributed with local support, extensive documentation, and a partner network in most markets. If you want a CRM that any consultant you hire will already know, that's HubSpot.

## Why teams leave HubSpot

Worth naming honestly, because it's the case for looking at alternatives at all. HubSpot's three most common exit reasons are **contact-tier pricing surprises** that bump the bill mid-year, the roughly **5x price cliff between Starter and Professional**, and feature bloat — sales teams paying for marketing, CMS, and ops modules they never open.

Kylas is essentially the anti-thesis of all three: one price, no tier cliff, no modules you aren't using. Whether that's a feature or a limitation depends entirely on whether you needed those modules.

## Who should pick what

- **Scaling a 20+ rep sales floor on a tight budget** → Kylas Sales CRM.
- **Marketing-led go-to-market with campaigns and nurture** → HubSpot CRM.
- **Field sales team where every rep needs a seat** → Kylas Sales CRM.
- **You need a specific app integration or custom objects** → HubSpot CRM.
- **SMB in India or a similar emerging market** → Kylas Sales CRM.
- **You want a platform you won't outgrow for years** → HubSpot CRM.

## Bottom line

Kylas and HubSpot answer different questions. Kylas answers "how do I get my whole sales team on a CRM without the bill scaling with headcount?" — and its flat-rate unlimited-user model is a genuinely good answer for growing teams whose need is pipeline visibility, not marketing orchestration. HubSpot answers "what platform can my whole go-to-market run on for the next five years?" — with the ecosystem, depth, and price tag to match. If your constraint is seats, look hard at Kylas and confirm the integrations you need. If your constraint is capability, HubSpot is still the safe bet.
