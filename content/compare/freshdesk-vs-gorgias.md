## TL;DR

- **Pick Freshdesk** if you need a full-featured helpdesk that handles email, chat, phone, and social across any industry — especially if predictable per-agent pricing and a self-service knowledge base matter to you.
- **Pick Gorgias** if you run a Shopify, WooCommerce, or Magento store and want support agents to see and act on order data directly inside every conversation.

## Pricing

Freshdesk uses per-agent pricing with a genuinely useful free tier. The Free plan covers up to 10 agents with basic ticketing. Growth runs $15/agent/month, Pro $49/agent/month, and Enterprise $79/agent/month (all billed annually). A 10-agent team on Pro pays $490/month — predictable no matter how many tickets come in.

Gorgias flips the model entirely: you pay per ticket, not per agent. Starter is roughly $10/month for 50 tickets, Basic ~$60/month for 300 tickets, Pro ~$360/month for 2,000 tickets, and Advanced ~$900/month for 5,000 tickets. Agents are unlimited on every plan. That's a bargain if your ticket volume is consistent, but a DTC brand running a Black Friday sale can blow through its monthly allotment in a weekend, triggering overage charges or a forced plan upgrade.

For stable ticket volumes under 2,000/month with a large agent team, Gorgias often wins on cost. For teams that expect volume spikes or need to keep budgets locked, Freshdesk's per-agent model is safer.

## E-commerce integration

This is where the products diverge most sharply. Gorgias was purpose-built for commerce — the ticket sidebar shows order status, shipping tracking, past purchases, and lifetime value without any configuration. Agents can trigger refunds, cancellations, and loyalty point adjustments inside the ticket without switching tabs. Macros (saved response templates) are pre-loaded with Shopify variables like `{{order.shipping_address}}`.

Freshdesk supports Shopify and WooCommerce via marketplace apps, but order data appears as a widget rather than being woven into the core interface. It works, but agents feel the seams. If 80% of your tickets are "where is my order?" queries, that difference matters every minute of every shift.

For non-commerce businesses — B2B SaaS, agencies, healthcare, education — Gorgias's commerce focus is irrelevant, and Freshdesk's neutral architecture is a better fit.

## Channel coverage

Freshdesk supports email, live chat, phone (via Freshcaller integration), social media (Facebook, Twitter/X, WhatsApp), and a customer portal with a knowledge base. All channels funnel into a unified inbox. Phone support on the Growth plan requires a Freshcaller add-on, but it's available.

Gorgias covers email, live chat, social media (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok comments), and SMS. There is no native voice channel. If phone support is a requirement, Gorgias is not an option without a separate voice tool and manual workflow bridging.

For omnichannel teams that take calls, Freshdesk has a clear structural advantage. For DTC brands where support happens over chat, email, and Instagram DMs, Gorgias covers all the real channels.

## Automation and AI

Freshdesk's automation engine (called Automations) handles ticket routing, SLA management, and canned responses. AI features — including Freddy AI for suggested replies and article suggestions — are gated to the Pro plan and above. The free and Growth tiers get rule-based automation only.

Gorgias leans on commerce-specific automation: automatically closing "where is my order?" tickets when tracking confirms delivery, routing VIP customers (flagged by Shopify order history) to priority queues, and triggering post-purchase flows. Its AI is more narrowly tuned to commerce intent than Freshdesk's broader Freddy AI, which means higher accuracy for WISMO queries and lower accuracy for everything else.

If your team handles varied query types — returns, product questions, billing, technical support — Freshdesk's automation library is more flexible. If WISMO and order actions are the lion's share of volume, Gorgias's specialized automation genuinely reduces handle time.

## Self-service and knowledge base

Freshdesk includes a full-featured self-service portal with a searchable knowledge base, community forums, and a widget that surfaces articles before a ticket is submitted. It's one of the strongest knowledge base tools among mid-market helpdesks and is available starting at the Growth plan.

Gorgias has a basic FAQ feature but no proper knowledge base. Merchants typically pair it with a separate tool (Helpcenter.io, Zendesk Guide standalone, or a Shopify page) for self-service. If deflection through documentation is part of your support strategy, Freshdesk is the more complete solution.

## Who should pick what

**Pick Freshdesk if:**
- You support customers across industries beyond retail
- You need phone support built in
- A self-service knowledge base is part of your deflection strategy
- You have a small team that benefits from the free tier
- Predictable per-agent billing matters to finance

**Pick Gorgias if:**
- Your store runs on Shopify, WooCommerce, or Magento
- The majority of tickets are order-related (WISMO, returns, exchanges)
- You want agents to process refunds and cancellations inside the ticket
- Your agent count is large relative to ticket volume
- You sell through Instagram or TikTok and need social comment management

## Bottom line

Freshdesk is the safer default for any support team that isn't exclusively e-commerce. Its breadth of channels, solid knowledge base, and predictable pricing make it a strong generalist helpdesk.
