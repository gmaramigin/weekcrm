## TL;DR

- **Pick eDesk** if you sell across marketplaces and need Amazon, eBay, Walmart, and Shopify messages plus review management in one inbox.
- **Pick Help Scout** if you want the fastest, most human-feeling shared inbox and your support arrives mainly through email, chat, and one storefront.

## Two very different definitions of "help desk"

**eDesk** treats support as a **channel consolidation problem**. Its premise is that a modern seller has messages scattered across Amazon Seller Central, eBay, Walmart, Shopify, WooCommerce, WhatsApp, and Instagram — each with its own response-time expectations — and that the win is pulling **300+ sources** into one queue with native connectors and ecommerce-shaped automation on top.

**Help Scout** treats support as a **conversation quality problem**. Its premise is that ticketing systems make companies sound like companies, so it builds around a shared inbox that looks and behaves like email: no ticket numbers shouting at customers, no queue jargon, just replies that feel written by a person. Beacon, its embeddable widget, surfaces knowledge base articles before a customer even opens a chat.

Both are correct about their own buyer. The question is which problem you actually have.

## Where each one breaks down

eDesk's weakness is that its advantage evaporates outside retail. Strip away the marketplace connectors and review automation and you are left with a competent but unremarkable help desk at an above-market price — which is why it is a poor fit for B2B SaaS or services teams.

Help Scout's weakness is the mirror image. It is genuinely delightful and one of the highest-rated tools in the category, but it has **no marketplace message handling and no seller-feedback tooling**. A seller running Amazon and eBay alongside Shopify will keep a Seller Central tab open forever, which defeats the point of a unified inbox.

## Review management is eDesk's structural edge

Worth isolating, because it is not a feature Help Scout competes on at all. eDesk automates **feedback collection and reputation monitoring** across selling platforms. For a marketplace seller, review velocity and seller rating gate Buy Box eligibility and account standing — they are operational metrics, not marketing ones. There is no Help Scout equivalent; you would buy a separate reputation tool.

If you sell only on your own Shopify store, this matters much less and the argument swings back toward Help Scout.

## Pricing and how it scales

Help Scout is meaningfully cheaper to start and simpler to predict: **free for up to 5 users**, paid from **$25/user/mo**, with advanced automation and deeper integrations gated to the **$45/user/mo** Plus tier. Cost scales with headcount only.

eDesk starts at **$39/agent/mo** and adds **$0.99 per AI resolution**. That is defensible — you pay for deflection you receive — but it means costs track ticket volume, and a seasonal spike shows up on the invoice. High-volume sellers should model peak months, not average ones.

## Who should pick what

- **Multi-marketplace seller on Amazon, eBay, and Walmart** → eDesk.
- **DTC Shopify brand with email and chat support** → Help Scout.
- **Team of 5 or fewer wanting to start free** → Help Scout.
- **Business where seller rating drives revenue** → eDesk.
- **SaaS, healthcare, or education support team** → Help Scout.
- **Seller drowning in cross-channel message volume** → eDesk.

## Bottom line

Help Scout is the better product for most support teams — cheaper, faster to adopt, and consistently loved by the people who use it every day — and a single-storefront DTC brand should probably start there. eDesk earns its higher price only in one scenario, but earns it convincingly: a seller operating across marketplaces gets connectors and review automation that Help Scout will never build, because it is not Help Scout's business. Count your channels; that number decides this.
