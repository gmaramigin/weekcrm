## TL;DR

- **Pick Gladly** if you're an experience-led B2C brand (retail, travel, hospitality) where customers contact you repeatedly, voice matters, and you want AI that completes interactions rather than just deflecting.
- **Pick Kustomer** if you need a customer-service CRM with custom objects and deep data modeling to represent complex products, orders, and high-volume omnichannel queues.

## Pricing

Neither vendor publishes rate cards. Gladly is estimated around $180-210/user/mo with a significant minimum annual commitment, and voice and SMS usage billed on top of seat fees. Kustomer is quote-only on standard annual contracts, with voice and WhatsApp billed pay-as-you-go. Both are firmly enterprise-tier investments — expect a sales conversation, a setup project, and usage-based channel costs layered onto seat licenses. Smaller teams will find both hard to justify.

## Data model: timeline over tickets

Both reject the ticket as the unit of work in favor of a lifetime customer timeline — every channel and interaction unified per person. That's the shared DNA. The difference is emphasis. Gladly's timeline is built around the conversation and the relationship: agents see a continuous history designed to make repeat contacts feel known. Kustomer's timeline doubles as a CRM record — purchases, orders, and support events sit alongside custom objects you define, so the data model can mirror a complex catalog or subscription structure. If you need to model the *business object* (the order, the policy, the booking), Kustomer's flexibility is the edge.

## AI approach

Gladly markets AI that engages rather than deflects — designed to complete interactions end-to-end, with plain-English "Guides" letting non-technical teams define brand voice without code. It claims 76% of conversations fully resolved by AI. Kustomer offers customer-facing bots plus agent-assist tools focused on reducing handle time and automating routine inquiries. Gladly's pitch is resolution and brand experience; Kustomer's is operational efficiency at volume.

## Channels

Kustomer covers chat, email, voice, SMS, Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp in one queue — broad social coverage for omnichannel B2C. Gladly spans voice, chat, SMS, email, and social, with voice engineered as a native, first-class channel. For brands where phone support is central — high-touch retail, travel, hospitality — Gladly's voice-native design is a meaningful differentiator.

## Who they're built for

Gladly's reference customers (Nordstrom, Crate & Barrel, Ulta, UGG) signal its sweet spot: mid-to-large consumer brands competing on experience. Kustomer's (Turo, SKIMS, Everlane, Sweetgreen) point to high-volume retail and marketplace operations where context and speed both matter and product/order data is complex.

## Bottom line

These two are closer in philosophy than most help-desk comparisons — both bet on the customer timeline over the ticket. Choose Gladly if your brand competes on experience and voice is core; its AI-resolves-everything pitch fits high-touch consumer support. Choose Kustomer if you need CRM-grade data flexibility and custom objects to model complex orders at high volume. Budget for enterprise pricing, usage-based channels, and a real implementation either way.
