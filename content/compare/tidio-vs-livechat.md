## TL;DR

- **Pick Tidio** if you're a small store or SMB that wants live chat plus an AI agent (Lyro) and visual chatbots to automate FAQs and recover sales — with a usable free tier.
- **Pick LiveChat** if you want a polished, agent-first chat platform with best-in-class operator tooling, routing, and a deep app marketplace for sales and support teams.

## Pricing

Tidio offers a free plan and affordable paid tiers (roughly $29–$59/mo per workspace ranges), with Lyro AI conversations metered separately. LiveChat prices per seat (around $24–$59/agent/mo) with no permanent free tier after the trial. For a tiny team automating chat, Tidio's free/low-cost entry plus bundled bots is cheaper; for a staffed team that wants premium agent tools, LiveChat's per-seat pricing buys a more refined operator experience.

## AI and automation

Tidio leads here for small teams. Lyro (its AI agent) answers customer questions from your content, and its visual chatbot builder handles lead capture, FAQ, and cart-recovery flows out of the box — automation is central to Tidio's pitch. LiveChat's AI (and its sibling product ChatBot) is capable but historically sold as an add-on/companion product rather than baked in. If you want bots and AI included and simple, Tidio; if you want chat first with AI added deliberately, LiveChat.

## Agent experience

LiveChat is the more refined tool for human agents. Canned responses, message sneak-peek, smart routing, tags, file sharing, and a fast, reliable agent app are best-in-class — it's a product built around making operators efficient. Tidio's agent panel is good and improving but is optimized for small teams juggling chat alongside other work. For a dedicated chat/support team, LiveChat's operator tooling is noticeably more polished.

## Ecommerce fit

Both serve ecommerce, but Tidio leans into it — Shopify integration, product cards in chat, cart-based triggers, and visitor-tracking aimed at converting and supporting shoppers. LiveChat integrates with ecommerce platforms too and shines when chat is a sales channel staffed by people. Small Shopify stores often find Tidio's bundled automation a faster path; sales teams that want operators closing via chat lean LiveChat.

## Channels and scale

LiveChat scales cleanly to larger teams with routing, groups, and reporting, and it's part of the broader LiveChat/HelpDesk/ChatBot ecosystem if you want a fuller suite. Tidio covers chat, email, and Messenger/Instagram and is excellent for small-to-mid teams but isn't aimed at running a large contact center alone. Match to team size: Tidio for lean teams, LiveChat for staffed chat operations.

## Integrations

LiveChat has the larger, more mature marketplace (200+ apps) and a strong developer platform. Tidio covers the key SMB and ecommerce integrations plus Zapier. For a complex stack or a team that wants to extend chat heavily, LiveChat's ecosystem is the stronger foundation.

## Who should pick what

- **Small store wanting AI + chatbots + live chat in one affordable tool** → Tidio.
- **Staffed sales/support team that wants premium agent tooling** → LiveChat.
- **Team that needs a real free tier to start** → Tidio.
- **Operation that runs chat as a serious sales channel with routing** → LiveChat.
- **Shopify store automating FAQ and cart recovery** → Tidio.

## Bottom line

Tidio and LiveChat both do live chat well but optimize for different buyers. Tidio is automation-first and SMB-friendly — AI agent and bots bundled at a low entry price. LiveChat is operator-first and premium — the most refined human-agent chat experience with a deep ecosystem. Pick Tidio to automate chat cheaply on a small team; pick LiveChat to run polished, staffed chat at scale.
