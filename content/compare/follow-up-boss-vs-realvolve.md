## TL;DR

- **Pick Follow Up Boss** if you lead a team that buys leads and lives or dies on speed-to-lead — automatic routing, native calling/texting, and accountability reporting are its whole reason for being.
- **Pick Realvolve** if your business runs on referrals and past clients and your real problem is nurture and consistency, not generating new inquiries.

## Pricing

**Follow Up Boss** starts at **$69/user/month (Grow)** with a **Pro plan at $499/month for 10 users** and a 14-day free trial, on month-to-month terms with no long-term contract. The catch: it's priced for teams, and the Pro plan's per-user calling add-ons can push the true monthly cost up meaningfully.

**Realvolve** starts at **$94/month** with a 14-day free trial — notably affordable for a feature set that overlaps with platforms charging 3–5× more, since you're not paying for an IDX website or managed ad stack you may not need.

## Lead routing vs. relationship workflows

These tools optimize for different moments in the funnel. Follow Up Boss is a **speed-to-lead machine**: it pulls from 250+ lead sources, routes each lead to the right agent automatically, and fires Action Plans — drip sequences triggered by lead source, status, and behavior. Built-in calling, texting, and email mean reps work leads without bolting on third-party tools.

Realvolve is a **workflow-first relationship engine**. You design detailed multi-step sequences of emails, texts, tasks, and reminders that fire off client behavior or deal stage, and the system assigns engagement-based relationship scores that surface cooling contacts before you lose them. The workflows support branches and conditions — powerful, but with a real learning curve.

## Team accountability vs. solo consistency

Follow Up Boss is built for team leads and brokerages. Performance dashboards by agent, lead source, and deal stage make it a coaching and accountability tool, not just a CRM — which is exactly why top inside-sales teams reach for it.

Realvolve's center of gravity is the individual agent or small team who already has leads and wants to stop letting them go cold. There's no managed lead routing across a large roster; the depth is in the nurture automation.

## Communication and ecosystem

Follow Up Boss's native calling and texting are a genuine edge for high-volume outbound teams. Realvolve leans on behavior-triggered email/text workflows rather than a built-in dialer, and its integration library is smaller. Realvolve's mobile app reviews are mixed — most of the power lives in the web app.

## Bottom line

If you run a **lead-buying agent team**, Follow Up Boss is the obvious answer — it's purpose-built for routing and follow-up at scale, and the ecosystem proves it. If you're a **referral-driven agent** whose problem is staying consistent with the relationships you already have, **Realvolve** is the more focused and less expensive fit. Match the tool to your funnel, not the other way around.
