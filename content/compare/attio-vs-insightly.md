## TL;DR

- **Pick Attio** if you want the fastest, most flexible modern CRM and you manage delivery in a separate tool.
- **Pick Insightly** if closing a deal kicks off a project, and you want sales pipeline plus task/milestone tracking in one system.

## Positioning

Both are SMB-friendly CRMs, but their center of gravity differs. Attio is an AI-native sales CRM that auto-builds contacts and companies from your inbox and lets you customize everything like a spreadsheet. Insightly is a CRM-plus-project-management platform: when a deal closes, it converts into a project with tasks, milestones, and deliverables tracked in the same place — purpose-built for businesses that sell and then have to execute.

## Pricing

The two overlap closely on entry pricing. Attio has a free plan and runs $29 (Plus), $59 (Pro), and $119 (Enterprise) per user/mo, with monthly or annual billing. Insightly is free for 2 users, then $29 (Plus), $49 (Professional), and $99 (Enterprise) per user/mo — but all paid plans require annual billing, removing the flexibility to commit month-to-month. If you want to start cheap and stay flexible, Attio's monthly option matters.

## Project management

This is the clearest dividing line. Insightly's headline feature is native project management — deals become projects, with pipelines, tasks, and milestones, so professional-services and construction teams don't need a separate PM tool. Attio has no built-in project management; you'd link it to Linear, Asana, or ClickUp via integrations. If post-sale delivery lives inside your CRM, Insightly is built for that and Attio isn't.

## Data model and customization

Attio is the more flexible and modern platform. Custom objects, views, filters, and templates are easy to build without a developer, and the data auto-enriches from email and calendar. Insightly supports relationship linking (contacts, organizations, opportunities, projects) and custom objects — but custom objects are gated to the Enterprise tier, and the overall experience is more rigid. For teams that want the CRM to bend to an unusual workflow, Attio wins.

## Interface and mobile

Attio's spreadsheet-like UI is fast, clean, and feels contemporary. Insightly's interface is functional but can feel dated by comparison, particularly on mobile. If day-to-day pleasantness and speed matter to adoption, Attio has the edge.

## Email and integrations

Both integrate with Gmail and Outlook with two-way sync. Attio's email capture and enrichment shine most for Google Workspace users and feed its automatic record-building. Insightly's email sync keeps contact records current from your inbox and pairs with strong reporting — Insightly earned 2026 recognition as a top CRM for analytics, an area where Attio's forecasting and sales analytics are still maturing.

## Who should pick what

- **Startups and ops-led tech teams** → Attio. Modern, flexible, fast to deploy.
- **Consultancies, agencies, and construction firms** → Insightly. Deal closes, project begins, all in one tool.
- **Teams that need deep reporting today** → Insightly. Analytics are a recognized strength.
- **Teams escaping Airtable or shared sheets** → Attio. Familiar UI, real CRM power.
- **Buyers who want month-to-month flexibility** → Attio. Insightly's paid plans are annual-only.

## Bottom line

If your business is sales-led and you keep delivery in dedicated tools, Attio is the better, more modern, more flexible CRM. If your business closes deals and then has to deliver on them — and you'd rather not bolt on a separate project tool — Insightly's combined pipeline-plus-projects design is a genuine, practical advantage that Attio can't match. Choose based on whether post-sale execution belongs inside your CRM.
