## TL;DR

- **Pick Freshsales** if you want an AI-forward CRM backed by a large platform (Freshworks), with Freddy AI for lead scoring and a deep app ecosystem you can grow into.
- **Pick Salesmate** if you want a flexible, lower-cost CRM with strong built-in calling, generous automation on mid tiers, and a team that values responsive support and customization.

## Pricing

Freshsales starts free for tiny teams, then runs ~$11/user/mo (Growth), ~$47 (Pro), and ~$71 (Enterprise) billed annually. Salesmate starts around $23/user/mo (Basic) and climbs to ~$63/user/mo (Business/Enterprise), often bundling more automation and calling minutes at the mid tier. For very small teams, Freshsales' free and Growth tiers are hard to beat on price; for teams that want automation without jumping to an enterprise plan, Salesmate's mid tier delivers more.

## AI and lead scoring

Freshsales' biggest edge is Freddy AI — built-in contact scoring, deal insights, next-best-action suggestions, and generative assists, all maintained by Freshworks at platform scale. Salesmate has added AI (Sandy AI assistant, smart suggestions) but it's newer and lighter. If AI-driven prioritization is central to how your reps work, Freshsales is the more mature option today.

## Calling and communication

Both ship a phone system, which is unusual at this price. Freshsales includes a built-in phone (buy numbers, log calls, record) tied to its CRM. Salesmate's "Smart Connect" telephony and Power Dialer are a particular strength — many teams pick Salesmate specifically because its calling, SMS, and sequencing feel like a sales-engagement tool bolted onto a CRM. For phone-heavy teams, evaluate Salesmate's dialer closely; it punches above its price.

## Automation and customization

Salesmate is the more flexible product for the money — workflow automation, sequences, and custom layouts are available without forcing you onto the top tier. Freshsales automation is solid and scales, but the deeper workflow and journey features sit on Pro/Enterprise. If you want maximum configurability per dollar, Salesmate; if you want automation that's part of a broader Freshworks suite (Freshdesk, Freshmarketer), Freshsales.

## Ecosystem and scale

Freshsales benefits from the Freshworks platform — shared data with Freshdesk and Freshmarketer, a large marketplace, and enterprise-grade infrastructure. Salesmate is a focused, independent CRM with a leaner marketplace but a reputation for fast, hands-on support. Choose Freshsales if you expect to adopt other Freshworks products; choose Salesmate if you want a nimble standalone CRM and direct vendor access.

## Reporting

Both offer customizable dashboards and pipeline reporting. Freshsales' reporting is broad and ties into Freddy AI insights. Salesmate's reporting is capable and improving, with good activity and sales-performance views. For most SMB teams the difference is marginal; Freshsales pulls ahead only at the analytics-heavy enterprise tier.

## Who should pick what

- **Team that wants the most mature built-in AI** → Freshsales.
- **Phone- and sequence-heavy sales team on a budget** → Salesmate.
- **Company already using or planning to use Freshdesk/Freshworks** → Freshsales.
- **Team that wants the most automation and customization per dollar** → Salesmate.
- **Tiny startup that needs a genuinely free starting point** → Freshsales.

## Bottom line

Freshsales and Salesmate compete hard in the affordable-AI-CRM-with-calling segment. Freshsales wins on AI maturity and platform ecosystem; Salesmate wins on calling depth, mid-tier value, and flexibility. Pick Freshsales if you want AI and an ecosystem to grow into; pick Salesmate if you want a flexible, phone-strong CRM that does more before you hit the enterprise tier.
