## TL;DR

- **Pick Helpshift** if you're a game studio that wants players supported inside the game, community sentiment watched across Discord and forums, and trust and safety handled by the same vendor.
- **Pick Tidio** if you're an ecommerce store or small business that wants AI-powered live chat on your site this week, starting free.

## Vertical specialist vs horizontal SMB tool

This is not a close-features comparison — it's a fit test, and the answer is usually obvious within one sentence of describing your business.

**Helpshift** is an end-to-end player engagement platform built exclusively for game studios. It ships **in-game support SDKs for mobile, PC, web, and console**, so a player hitting a problem never leaves the game to get help. Around that sits community sentiment monitoring across Discord, forums, and social; live-ops engagement tooling; and trust and safety. Its customers are studios from indies to EA, Ubisoft, and Supercell. Helpshift is explicit that non-gaming companies should look elsewhere, and that's honest advice rather than modesty.

**Tidio** is a horizontal AI live chat and help desk for **ecommerce, SaaS, and service businesses** — 300,000+ of them. Its center of gravity is a chat widget on a storefront, Lyro AI answering "where is my order," and native Shopify and WooCommerce integration. It's B2C-leaning by design and admits it's weaker for B2B account management.

The overlap between "studio shipping a live-ops game" and "Shopify merchant with a small support team" is essentially nil. Identify which one you are and the comparison ends.

## Pricing transparency is a real differentiator

**Tidio** publishes everything: a **genuinely useful free plan** with live chat and basic ticketing, paid from **$24.17/mo**. You can be running by lunch without talking to anyone.

**Helpshift** publishes nothing. **No tiers, no estimates, contact vendor** — which means a full sales cycle before you know whether it fits your budget, and typically enterprise-shaped contracting. Add **SDK integration into your game client**, which is engineering scope that has to be planned and shipped, not a snippet you paste.

That gap has consequences beyond convenience. An indie studio with three people can evaluate Tidio in an afternoon and can't evaluate Helpshift without a meeting. Tidio's model suits self-serve buyers; Helpshift's assumes a procurement process and a technical integration project.

Tidio's own cost trap is worth flagging: Lyro AI and advanced Flows are **priced as add-ons**, so AI-heavy usage climbs faster than the $24.17 entry suggests, and the Plus plan at **$749+/mo** is a steep jump for teams needing proper team features.

## What each one uniquely covers

Helpshift's gaming-native capabilities have no general-purpose equivalent. **70+ language support** with built-in AI translation handles a global player base without hiring per language — a genuine problem for any game with international reach. **Unified player context** across every touchpoint enables VIP handling for whales and high-value accounts. **Guard AI** covers trust and safety, which for a game with chat or UGC isn't optional. And the **AI trilogy** — Care, Engage, Guard — maps to resolution, retention, and safety as separate jobs.

Tidio's coverage is shaped for a storefront: **Lyro** resolving up to **67%** of inquiries, **no-code Flows** for lead capture and discount offers, and a multi-channel inbox pulling in email, live chat, Messenger, Instagram, and WhatsApp. That last set matters — Instagram and Messenger are where D2C customers actually message brands, and Helpshift's channel focus is players, not shoppers.

## Who should pick what

- **Mobile or console game studio with live players** → Helpshift.
- **Shopify or WooCommerce store with a small support team** → Tidio.
- **Studio needing trust and safety plus community sentiment monitoring** → Helpshift.
- **Business that wants to start free today, no sales call** → Tidio.
- **Global game with players across dozens of languages** → Helpshift.
- **D2C brand fielding DMs on Instagram and Messenger** → Tidio.

## Bottom line

Helpshift is the most specialized player support platform available, and for a game studio that specialization is worth the opaque pricing and the SDK integration work — nothing horizontal replicates in-game support, community sentiment monitoring, and trust and safety in one place. Tidio is one of the most accessible AI support tools going, with a free plan that's actually usable and an AI agent that meaningfully cuts ecommerce ticket volume. Neither is a fallback for the other. Ship games, call Helpshift. Sell products, install Tidio.
