## TL;DR

- **Pick Kylas Sales CRM** if you're scaling a sales team and want every rep licensed under one flat subscription rather than paying per seat.
- **Pick Pipedrive** if you want the most polished pipeline CRM available, with mature automation, reporting, and a 300+ integration marketplace.

## Same philosophy, different billing

Unlike most CRM matchups, these two agree on what a CRM should be. Both are **pipeline-first**: a visual deal board is the central interface, activities drive deals forward, and neither tries to bolt on marketing automation or a service desk. If you compared feature lists at the concept level they'd look similar.

Where they diverge is the invoice. **Pipedrive** is per seat — **$14/user/mo** (Essential, annual) through **$29** (Advanced), **$59** (Professional), **$69** (Power), and **$99** (Enterprise). **Kylas** charges a **flat monthly fee for unlimited users**.

Do the math on your own team and the crossover point becomes obvious. At five reps, Pipedrive Essential is trivially cheap. At thirty reps on Professional, you're paying a real monthly number, and Kylas's flat fee starts looking like the structural answer rather than a discount.

## Where Pipedrive earns its price

Being honest about the trade: Pipedrive is the more developed product.

Its **visual pipeline UI** is the benchmark the category is measured against — most reps are productive on day one without training. **Activity tracking** logs calls, emails, tasks, and meetings automatically against deals. **Two-way email sync** with Gmail and Outlook is solid, with AI-assisted email writing from Professional up. The **Sales Assistant** ships on all paid plans and surfaces stalled deals and win-likelihood predictions. And the **300+ native integrations** plus Zapier and Make mean your stack probably already connects.

Kylas covers the fundamentals well — pipeline with customizable stages, lead capture and assignment, activity tracking, and mobile apps — and is explicitly designed for teams without a dedicated CRM administrator. But its **integration ecosystem is narrower**, and feature depth is thinner for complex workflows. That's the trade you accept for flat-rate pricing.

## The tier trap on both sides

Pipedrive's headline **$14/user/mo** is misleading in a specific way: reporting and forecasting are limited on lower tiers, and workflow automation doesn't appear until Advanced. The features most teams actually evaluate Pipedrive for live on **Professional at $59/user/mo**. Add-ons like LeadBooster, Campaigns, and Web Visitors push the effective seat price higher still.

Kylas has the opposite problem — not tiers, but **transparency**. Full pricing isn't published on the site, so you need a trial or a sales call to confirm what you'll pay. That's a smaller issue than tier-cliff surprises, but it does make side-by-side budgeting harder, and you should get the number in writing before committing.

## Market focus

Kylas is built for **SMBs in India and emerging markets** that are scaling sales headcount. The flat-rate model, the onboarding assumptions, and the support footprint all reflect that. Pipedrive is globally distributed with a mature partner ecosystem, and scales reasonably to a few hundred users before organizations typically move to Salesforce or HubSpot.

If you're outside Kylas's core geography, weigh support coverage and time zones. If you're inside it, the local fit is a genuine advantage that a spec-sheet comparison won't show.

## Who should pick what

- **20+ reps and per-seat cost is the blocker** → Kylas Sales CRM.
- **You want the best pipeline UX in the category** → Pipedrive.
- **Field sales team where everyone needs a login** → Kylas Sales CRM.
- **You need workflow automation and forecasting** → Pipedrive.
- **SMB in India or a similar emerging market** → Kylas Sales CRM.
- **Your stack depends on specific native integrations** → Pipedrive.

## Bottom line

Pipedrive is the better product and Kylas is the better economics — that's the whole comparison. If you have a small-to-mid sales team and can absorb per-seat pricing, Pipedrive's pipeline UX, automation, and integration marketplace justify the cost, though you should evaluate Professional rather than the entry tier to see what it really does. If you're licensing thirty or more reps and mainly need clean pipeline visibility and lead tracking, Kylas's flat fee removes a cost ceiling that otherwise shapes how you run your team. Count your seats first; the answer usually falls out of that number.
