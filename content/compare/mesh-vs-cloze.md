## TL;DR

- **Pick Mesh** if your professional network lives primarily on LinkedIn and you want a clean, visual way to track relationships, set follow-up reminders, and understand how your contacts connect to each other — without a steep learning curve.
- **Pick Cloze** if you want your CRM to automatically build itself from your existing email, calendar, phone, and document activity — surfacing relationship gaps and priority contacts through AI without you manually logging anything.

## Pricing

Both tools are priced for individuals rather than teams, but there's a gap. Cloze's Pro plan runs approximately $17/month (billed annually), making it one of the more affordable AI-powered personal CRM options. Business plans with additional team features are available at higher tiers. Mesh is positioned in a similar individual-productivity price range; check their current pricing page as it has evolved since launch. Both are meaningfully cheaper than business CRMs like Salesforce or HubSpot, and neither requires an annual contract commitment at the individual tier. If you're evaluating purely on price, the difference is unlikely to be the deciding factor — evaluate instead on which approach to relationship intelligence actually fits how you work.

## Contact intelligence and automatic enrichment

Cloze's defining capability is passive data collection. Connect your Gmail or Outlook, your calendar, your phone (call logs), and your cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive, Box), and Cloze builds a unified timeline for every contact — every email thread, every meeting, every document shared — without you doing anything. Its relationship-strength algorithm scores contacts by recency and frequency of interaction, surfacing people who are "at risk" of going cold. Cloze essentially converts your existing communication infrastructure into a CRM automatically. Mesh takes a different approach, with stronger emphasis on LinkedIn as the primary data source — importing your connections, surfacing mutual relationships, and helping you understand your network graph. Mesh requires more intentional input; Cloze requires more integration permissions.

## Relationship reminders and follow-up

Both tools address the core personal CRM problem: you know you should follow up, but you forget. Cloze's "agenda" view surfaces contacts you haven't engaged with recently and recommends outreach based on its relationship-strength scoring, with pipeline stages you can assign to any contact regardless of whether they're a deal. You can set explicit follow-up reminders, but the AI layer also proactively surfaces who needs attention. Mesh lets you set follow-up reminders directly on contacts and surfaces relationship prompts — people you've met recently who might be worth following up with, or contacts you've been meaning to reach. Mesh's prompts tend to be more network-graph oriented (second-degree connections, warm intros); Cloze's are more communication-frequency oriented (who went quiet).

## LinkedIn integration

Mesh's LinkedIn integration is a central feature — it's designed to pull in connection data, surface contact bios, mutual connections, and career changes in a way that makes LinkedIn a live data layer for your contact records. For professionals who live in LinkedIn (recruiters, founders, investors, consultants), this is a meaningful differentiator. Cloze has LinkedIn as one of many data sources but doesn't build its core UX around it. Cloze is more agnostic about where relationship data comes from — email and calendar drive most of its intelligence. If your networking is primarily LinkedIn-first, Mesh's integration depth is valuable. If you build relationships primarily through email and meetings, Cloze's automatic email/calendar synthesis will serve you better.

## Email and calendar integration

Cloze's email and calendar integration is deep and bidirectional. It reads your full inbox history to reconstruct relationship timelines, identifies follow-up commitments you've made in email, and attaches emails directly to contact and deal records without any manual linking. The calendar integration means every meeting is automatically associated with the attendees' contact records. Mesh integrates with email and calendar as well, but the experience is lighter — you get meeting context and some email linking, but the depth of automatic inbox mining that Cloze does is a significant differentiator for users with rich email histories.

## Mobile and daily use

Personal CRM tools live or die by whether you actually use them, and mobile experience matters. Cloze has a well-regarded mobile app — many users manage their daily agenda and contact follow-ups entirely from mobile. Mesh has a mobile presence as well, with the network visualization and contact management accessible from the app. Both are designed as tools you interact with daily rather than weekly; the one that earns daily use is the one that matches your existing habits. If you check LinkedIn daily, Mesh's flow fits naturally. If you process email and calendar daily, Cloze's automatic surfacing of relationship priorities in your inbox flow fits better.

## FAQ

**Does Cloze work if I use Microsoft 365 instead of Google Workspace?**
Yes. Cloze integrates with both Gmail and Outlook/Microsoft 365 for email and calendar. The full intelligence layer works with either platform.

**Can Mesh replace LinkedIn for networking, or does it require LinkedIn?**
Mesh works best as a layer on top of LinkedIn, not a replacement for it. The LinkedIn integration is central to the product's value; without it, the network intelligence features are more limited.

**Is my email data private if I connect it to Cloze?**
Cloze processes your email to build contact timelines and relationship data. Their privacy policy describes how data is handled — worth reviewing if you handle sensitive communications. Cloze does not sell contact data to third parties.

**Which is better for investors or VCs tracking deal flow?**
Cloze is popular in the investor community for its deal pipeline features and automatic communication logging across a large contact base. Mesh's network graph is appealing for mapping relationships between founders, funds, and co-investors. Both have users in this segment; Cloze's deal tracking is more structured.

**Can I share contacts or notes with a small team in either tool?**
Cloze has team plans with shared contact records and communication visibility. Mesh is primarily designed for individual use, though they have explored team features. If you need a shared personal CRM for a small partnership or team, Cloze's team tier is more mature.

## Verdict

Mesh and Cloze are both well-designed personal CRM tools solving the same fundamental problem — helping people maintain meaningful professional relationships — through different lenses. Mesh is network-visual and LinkedIn-native, best for professionals whose relationship graph is most legible in a LinkedIn context and who want to understand connection paths and warm introductions. Cloze is communication-native and AI-passive, best for professionals whose relationships are conducted primarily over email and calendar and who want a CRM that builds itself from existing behavior. Choose Mesh if you navigate your network through LinkedIn; choose Cloze if you want your inbox to drive the intelligence.
