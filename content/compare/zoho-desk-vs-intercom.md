## TL;DR
- **Pick Zoho Desk** if you want predictable per-agent pricing, already run Zoho CRM, or need solid multi-channel ticketing without an enterprise budget.
- **Pick Intercom** if you're a SaaS or app company that wants in-product messaging and an autonomous AI agent (Fin) carrying a big share of your volume.

## Pricing
These two price on different philosophies, and that's the whole decision in miniature. Zoho Desk runs a conventional per-agent ladder — free for up to 3 agents, then roughly $14/agent/mo at Standard up to $40/agent/mo at Enterprise, billed annually. Your bill is knowable a year in advance. Intercom starts at $29/seat/mo (Essential) and climbs to $85 (Advanced) and $132 (Expert), but the headline number isn't the real number: Fin AI is billed at $0.99 per resolved ticket and the unlimited Copilot is another ~$35/seat/mo. Intercom can be cheaper per human if AI resolves most tickets, or much more expensive if volume spikes. Zoho is the safer line item; Intercom is a bet on deflection economics.

## Channels
Both cover email, chat, and social, but they lean different ways. Zoho Desk unifies email, live chat, phone, social, and web forms into one queue, and phone/telephony is a first-class citizen — useful if you still take calls. Intercom is messaging-native: its chat widget, in-app messenger, and mobile SDK are the strongest part of the product, with email, SMS, and WhatsApp layered on. If your customers live inside a web or mobile app, Intercom meets them there. If they email and call, Zoho Desk fits the workflow more naturally.

## AI and automation
This is where Intercom pulls ahead on raw capability. Fin is one of the best autonomous agents on the market and the pay-per-resolution model means you only pay when it actually closes an issue. Zoho Desk's AI (Zia) does sentiment analysis, ticket summarization, and reply suggestions, but the generative features sit at the Enterprise tier and aim to assist agents rather than replace them. If "AI handles 50%+ of tickets unattended" is your goal, Intercom is built for it. If you want AI to make human agents faster, Zoho Desk delivers that at a fraction of the cost.

## Ticketing and context
Zoho Desk's pitch is context-aware ticketing: every ticket shows the customer's full history and, with Zoho CRM connected, the sales record alongside it. That two-way CRM sync is its strongest moat — support and sales genuinely share one customer record. Intercom organizes around conversations more than tickets, though it has added a proper ticketing layer; it shines when support data needs to tie back to product usage and lifecycle stage rather than a CRM deal.

## Knowledge base and self-service
Both ship a built-in help center. Zoho Desk's knowledge base builder produces branded FAQs and guides for deflection and is included on paid tiers. Intercom's help center is tightly wired into Fin — articles directly feed the AI's answers, so good documentation compounds your deflection rate. If self-service is mostly a static FAQ, Zoho is plenty. If self-service is the fuel for an AI agent, Intercom's loop is more powerful.

## Ecosystem and fit
Zoho Desk's gravity is the Zoho suite; outside that ecosystem its differentiation narrows and you're comparing it to Freshdesk. Intercom's gravity is the product-led SaaS stack — Jira, segmentation, behavioral triggers — and it's overkill for a traditional phone-and-email shop.

## Who should pick what
- **Zoho ecosystem teams** → Zoho Desk. Native CRM sync removes data silos for free.
- **Budget-conscious SMBs** → Zoho Desk. Predictable per-agent pricing and a real free tier.
- **Product-led SaaS** → Intercom. In-app messaging and Fin map to how you support users.
- **High-volume support betting on AI** → Intercom. Pay-per-resolution can beat headcount math.
- **Phone-heavy or mixed support** → Zoho Desk. Telephony is built in, not bolted on.

## Bottom line
Zoho Desk and Intercom rarely compete for the same buyer once you look closely. Zoho Desk is the disciplined, value-driven choice — predictable cost, strong CRM integration, capable multi-channel ticketing — and it's a no-brainer if you're already in the Zoho world. Intercom is the modern, AI-forward platform for software companies that want support fused into the product and are willing to manage usage-based pricing to get class-leading deflection. Decide whether you're buying a help desk or buying an AI support engine, and the rest follows.
