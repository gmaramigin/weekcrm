## TL;DR

- **Pick Nutshell** when outbound selling and email marketing are the engine of your business. The Foundation tier ($13/user/mo) includes more than most CRMs charge Pro-tier prices for, and the built-in email marketing removes the need for a Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign subscription alongside your CRM.
- **Pick Insightly** when the real pain isn't outbound selling — it's keeping track of what happens after the deal closes. Service businesses, agencies, and consultancies benefit from Insightly's deal-to-project structure more than they'd benefit from a built-in email tool.

## Pricing

Nutshell's pricing is transparently inclusive: Foundation at $13/user/mo covers unlimited contacts, email sync, form builder, live chat widget, and web forms — features that are add-ons elsewhere. Pro at $42/user/mo adds sales automation, sequences, and advanced reporting. Power AI at $52/user/mo adds AI meeting transcription and smarter forecasting. Insightly's free tier (2 users) is generous for solo founders; Plus at $29/user/mo is the practical starting point; Professional at $49/user/mo is where real workflow automation unlocks. Annual billing is required for both. For a 10-person B2B team doing regular email outreach, Nutshell Pro (~$420/mo) competes with Insightly Professional + Insightly Marketing ($490 + $299/mo+), a significant total cost difference.

## Email Marketing

This is Nutshell's clearest advantage. Every Nutshell plan includes email broadcast capability, contact segmentation by CRM attributes, and drip sequence automation at Pro and above. You can build an outreach sequence, attach it to a pipeline stage, and have follow-ups fire automatically — without leaving the CRM or paying for a separate marketing platform. Insightly's core CRM has basic email logging but no campaign or sequence tooling; Insightly Marketing is a real product but requires a separate (and more expensive) subscription. For B2B teams where the SDR sends sequences and the marketing team sends newsletters, Nutshell covers both use cases in one bill.

## Pipeline Management

Both platforms offer multi-pipeline support, drag-and-drop kanban views, and customizable stages. Nutshell's pipeline is specifically designed for B2B — with lead routing rules, territory management at Pro, and team visibility controls. Insightly's pipeline UI is clean and functional, with a visual drag-and-drop editor and strong filtering for multi-segment businesses. Neither lags significantly here; both are capable for teams managing 50–500 active deals. Nutshell's advantage is the tighter connection between pipeline stage and automation trigger; Insightly's is the link from pipeline stage to the project that follows close.

## Project Delivery

Insightly's unique strength. When a deal is marked Won, Insightly can automatically generate a project — copying contact details, ownership, and custom fields — and attach a task template with pre-assigned milestones and owners. This is invaluable for businesses that sell a service and then deliver it: implementations, renovations, consulting engagements, custom software builds. The project and the deal share a record history, so client-facing teams have full context without digging through two systems. Nutshell has no equivalent; once a deal closes, you'd need Asana, Monday, or a similar tool to track the work.

## Reporting and Analytics

Both offer dashboards, funnel conversion tracking, and activity reporting. Nutshell Pro includes team leaderboards, goal tracking, and rep activity breakdowns that sales managers value. The Power AI plan adds AI-generated forecasting insights. Insightly's reporting is flexible — the report builder lets you filter across contacts, opportunities, and projects in the same view, which is rare and useful for businesses where deal metrics and delivery metrics are related. G2 acknowledged Insightly for analytics strength in 2026. If you want a single report that says "how many deals closed and are the resulting projects on time," Insightly can build it; Nutshell cannot.

## Support and Onboarding

Nutshell includes free live support (chat and email) with every plan — no tiered support, no waiting for Enterprise to get a human. This is unusual and genuinely appreciated by small teams without dedicated IT. Insightly offers documentation, webinars, and email support across tiers, with priority support on Enterprise. Neither has the partner ecosystem of a HubSpot or Salesforce, but both are responsive for SMB-scale needs.

## Verdict

Nutshell and Insightly are both solid, honestly-priced SMB CRMs — but they're solving different extension problems. Nutshell extends the CRM forward into marketing: outreach, sequences, and list management without a separate tool. Insightly extends the CRM forward into delivery: task management, milestones, and project tracking without a separate tool. The right answer almost always comes from answering one question: does your team's biggest headache happen before the deal closes (go with Nutshell's automation and email tools) or after (go with Insightly's project layer)?
