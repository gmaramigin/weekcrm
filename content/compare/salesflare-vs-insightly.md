## TL;DR

- **Pick Salesflare** if your team is a lean B2B sales unit and the real problem is nobody keeps the CRM updated.
- **Pick Insightly** if closing a deal starts a delivery project, and you want sales and that project living in one system.

## Adoption problem versus delivery problem

Salesflare and Insightly are both sensible SMB CRMs, but they are answering different questions. Salesflare answers "how do I get my reps to actually use the CRM?" Its entire design fights manual entry: contacts, companies, meetings, email threads, and attachments sync automatically from Gmail, Outlook, and LinkedIn, so the record is current whether or not anyone maintains it. Add a built-in Lead Finder, email sequences, and relationship intelligence that flags at-risk accounts, and you get a sales CRM optimized for teams under 50 where adoption is the make-or-break factor.

Insightly answers "how do I manage what happens after the deal closes?" Its signature is native project management — a closed opportunity converts into a project with tasks, milestones, and deliverables tracked in the same platform, with relationship linking tying contacts, organizations, opportunities, and projects together. It is aimed at consulting, professional services, and construction firms where the sale is the start of the real work.

So this is not a features arms race. If your pain is a stale, unloved sales CRM, Salesflare. If your pain is deals closing and then delivery scattering across spreadsheets and PM tools, Insightly.

## Pricing

The entry tiers nearly match — Salesflare Growth and Insightly Plus both around $29/user/mo, and both lean annual. Insightly differentiates at the edges: a free plan for up to two users at the bottom, and Professional ($49) and Enterprise ($99) above, though serious automation is gated to Professional and up. Salesflare mirrors the ladder ($29/$49/$99) but attaches a five-user minimum to Enterprise, a barrier for solo sellers wanting premium features. For a two-person shop, Insightly's free tier is the cheapest start. Beyond that, the price difference is small enough that fit should decide.

## Where the CRM's effort goes

The deepest difference is where each product spends its energy. Salesflare pours it into ingestion and low-maintenance upkeep — the CRM works so you can sell. Insightly pours it into structure and continuity across the deal-to-delivery arc — the CRM is the system of record for the whole account lifecycle, not just the sale. That also shapes their weak spots: Salesflare's reporting and forecasting are solid but shallow versus enterprise tools, and it is not built for post-sale workflows at all; Insightly's UI can feel dated and its Plus-tier automation is limited. Pick the strength that matches your actual bottleneck.

## Who should pick what

- **Lean B2B sales teams fighting CRM adoption** → Salesflare.
- **Consulting and professional-services firms that deliver projects** → Insightly.
- **Teams that want records to build themselves from the inbox** → Salesflare.
- **Two-person teams wanting a free tier** → Insightly.
- **Businesses where a closed deal becomes a delivery project** → Insightly.
- **Agencies and SaaS teams selling deals with no heavy post-sale work** → Salesflare.
