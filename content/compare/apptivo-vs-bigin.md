## TL;DR

- **Pick Apptivo** if you run a small business and want one vendor for CRM, invoicing, project management, and field service instead of stitching separate tools together.
- **Pick Bigin by Zoho CRM** if you want a focused, pipeline-first CRM that is genuinely simple, cheap, and live in under 30 minutes.

## Breadth vs focus

Both are affordable small-business tools, but they answer opposite questions. **Apptivo** answers "how do I run my whole operation from one login?" It is a modular suite of **65+ integrated apps** — CRM, invoicing, procurement, project management, field service — where you switch on the modules you need. For a business currently paying for a separate CRM, a project tool, and invoicing software, Apptivo's pitch is consolidation under one subscription at a reasonable price.

**Bigin** answers "how do I track my deals without the overhead of a real CRM platform?" It is a deliberately **stripped-down, pipeline-centric CRM** from Zoho, built for solo operators and teams under 20 people who find full Zoho CRM or Salesforce overwhelming. It does contacts, pipeline stages, outreach, and light automation — and nothing it does not need to.

The tradeoff is classic: Apptivo gives you breadth at the cost of depth in any single module, while Bigin gives you focus at the cost of scope beyond sales. Neither is wrong; they fit different needs.

## Pricing

**Apptivo** offers a functional free plan and paid tiers from **$10/user/mo**, though many of its more useful features are gated to higher tiers, so a fully-equipped setup costs more than the entry price implies. **Bigin** also has a genuine free plan (single pipeline, one user, with built-in telephony and email) and paid plans from **$7/user/mo billed annually** — among the lowest prices for a legitimate CRM. Bigin is cheaper per seat, but Apptivo's price buys far more than CRM.

## Simplicity and setup

Bigin's whole identity is **simplicity**: a spreadsheet-like pipeline view most users master without training, and setup measured in minutes. Apptivo trades some of that ease for breadth — reviews note it can take more clicks than expected to move around, and its mobile app lags its desktop version. If frictionless onboarding matters most, Bigin; if you accept a learning curve in exchange for range, Apptivo.

## Growth path

The two scale differently. Bigin offers **one-click migration to full Zoho CRM**, so a team that outgrows it steps up without re-platforming — ideal if you expect to need advanced automation, custom modules, or enterprise reporting later. Apptivo scales **sideways**, by activating more of its 65+ modules as your operations broaden. Choose Bigin if your future is a deeper sales system; choose Apptivo if your future is more business functions under one roof.

## Who should pick what

- **Small business consolidating CRM + invoicing + projects** → Apptivo.
- **Solo operator or micro-team needing a simple pipeline** → Bigin.
- **Team that wants field service and procurement too** → Apptivo.
- **Team that wants to be organized in 30 minutes** → Bigin.
- **Business expecting to grow into full Zoho CRM** → Bigin.
- **Business wanting fewer SaaS vendors overall** → Apptivo.

## Bottom line

Apptivo and Bigin both serve small businesses well but solve different problems. Apptivo is remarkable breadth for the price if you want to reduce the number of tools you run, accepting that each module is shallower than a specialist. Bigin is one of the best pure-value CRMs going — simple, cheap, and with a safe upgrade path — if all you need is a pipeline. Pick by scope: one tool for everything, or the best small tool for sales.
